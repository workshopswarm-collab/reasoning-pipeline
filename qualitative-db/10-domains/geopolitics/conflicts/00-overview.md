# Overview summary

Conflicts is the subdomain of wars, armed confrontations, proxy struggles, ceasefires, force posture, territorial contests, and escalation dynamics. It matters because geopolitical outcomes are often shaped less by headline rhetoric than by the structure, tempo, and incentives of organized conflict itself.

## Why this area matters

Conflicts matter because they reshape state behavior, alliance commitments, sanctions risk, energy exposure, and global political expectations. Researchers need a dedicated orientation note for conflict structure so they do not collapse military, diplomatic, and informational developments into one undifferentiated crisis narrative.

## Common conflicts or failure modes

- treating rhetoric as equivalent to operational change on the ground
- overweighting single dramatic events without checking force posture or sustainability
- confusing temporary de-escalation with durable conflict resolution
- ignoring logistics, replenishment, and external support when reading battlefield momentum

## Missing coverage

- conflict-tracking source hierarchy
- territorial-control and force-posture templates
- escalation and ceasefire monitoring guidance
- better notes on proxy warfare and external support structures
- more explicit integration with sanctions and energy spillover analysis

## Most fragile assumptions

- that direct military action is the only meaningful conflict signal
- that ceasefire talk implies durable de-escalation
- that tactical gains automatically translate into strategic resolution
