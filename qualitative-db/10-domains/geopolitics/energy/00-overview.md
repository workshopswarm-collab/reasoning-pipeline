# Overview summary

The geopolitics-of-energy subdomain is the structural layer of producers, transit routes, chokepoints, refining, LNG, pipelines, sanctions exposure, and state dependence. It is distinct from the `energy` driver note, which explains how energy pressure affects outcomes in a particular case.

## Why this subdomain matters

This subdomain matters because energy is both a commodity system and a power system. Researchers need a place to orient around producer concentration, route vulnerability, state dependence, and infrastructure before interpreting any current supply shock or policy move.

## Core conclusions

- Energy should be treated as strategic infrastructure, not just a price series.
- Producer concentration, transport routes, and state dependence often matter more than headline spot-price moves.
- Energy systems interact tightly with sanctions, trade, defense posture, and industrial policy.
- Supply security and spare capacity are often more informative than nominal production rhetoric.

## Main evidence clusters

- producer concentration and spare capacity
- pipeline, LNG, shipping, and refining infrastructure
- chokepoints and transport vulnerability
- sanctions exposure and rerouting
- import dependence and substitution capacity
- state policy and cartel coordination

## Important recurring objects

- producer states and energy ministries
- OPEC-style coordination bodies
- pipelines, LNG terminals, and shipping lanes
- refiners, traders, and state energy companies
- sanctions regimes and maritime enforcement actors

## Important recurring drivers

- energy
- sanctions
- trade
- operational-risk

## Common conflicts or failure modes

- reading energy solely through price without route or capacity context
- assuming nominal output claims translate into available supply
- ignoring refining and transport bottlenecks while focusing only on production
- treating energy transition rhetoric as immediate geopolitical insulation

## Missing coverage

- cleaner source maps for shipping, LNG, and refining constraints
- stronger notes on chokepoints and rerouting behavior
- more explicit linkage between producer politics and capacity reality

## Most fragile assumptions

- that supply is fungible enough to erase route risk
- that headline production targets equal operational capacity
- that import dependence can be adjusted quickly during stress

## Recommended next research steps

- keep time-sensitive disruptions and price responses in `40-research/`
- use this note for structural energy-security orientation, not rolling commodity commentary
- deepen route- and chokepoint-specific notes only when repeated use justifies them
