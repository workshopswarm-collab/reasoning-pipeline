# Overview summary

Social media is a platform-and-distribution subdomain, not just a generic attention theme. It should be used to orient around platform mechanics, creator incentives, recommendation systems, audience migration, and how distribution infrastructure shapes visibility across culture, politics, sports, and tech.

## Why this subdomain matters

This subdomain matters because many high-salience narrative swings are downstream of platform design rather than pure public sentiment. A good social-media overview should help researchers distinguish the infrastructure of amplification from the broader `media-narratives` driver that later moves expectations.

## Core conclusions

- Platform design and recommendation systems are first-class variables in attention outcomes.
- Social-media distribution is cross-domain infrastructure, not just a culture niche.
- Audience scale and audience quality should be separated from short-run virality.
- Platform policy, creator incentives, and moderation shifts can materially change narrative propagation.

## Main evidence clusters

- recommendation and feed mechanics
- creator incentives and monetization
- audience migration across platforms
- moderation, policy, and algorithm changes
- engagement metrics and conversion quality
- platform-specific distribution bottlenecks

## Important recurring objects

- social platforms
- creators and celebrity accounts
- recommendation systems
- advertiser and monetization systems
- moderation and safety policies
- audience segments and fandom clusters

## Important recurring drivers

- media-narratives
- sentiment
- partnerships
- product-launches

## Common conflicts or failure modes

- treating virality as durable audience conversion
- assuming cross-platform attention translates cleanly from one network to another
- confusing platform-native amplification with broad cultural consensus
- ignoring policy or algorithm changes that altered the distribution environment

## Missing coverage

- better platform-specific metrics and measurement notes
- source hierarchy for engagement and audience-quality data
- stronger coverage of creator monetization and recommendation-system changes

## Most fragile assumptions

- that raw reach equals durable influence
- that attention quality is similar across platforms
- that narrative momentum is independent of distribution infrastructure

- keep platform-specific, time-sensitive algorithm or policy changes in `40-research/`
