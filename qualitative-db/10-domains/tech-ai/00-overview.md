# Overview summary

Tech & AI is a domain spanning model capability, infrastructure, software platforms, hardware, governance, productization, and adoption. Good research here should separate technical capability from deployment, distribution, regulation, and commercial integration rather than collapsing them into one progress narrative.

## Why this area matters

Tech & AI matters because major outcomes in the space depend on the interaction between research progress, infrastructure constraints, product design, enterprise adoption, regulation, and platform distribution. It is one of the clearest domains where technical quality alone does not determine market relevance.

## Core conclusions

- Tech & AI should be analyzed as a stack: models, compute infrastructure, regulation, products, and distribution all matter separately.
- `models`, `chips-compute`, and `regulation` currently justify standalone subdomains because they have distinct evidence and institutional structures; other topics should stay in the parent domain or research layer unless they earn more separation.
- Capability gains do not automatically translate into product success, adoption, or durable market position.
- Infrastructure bottlenecks and compliance constraints are often as important as frontier-model progress.

## Common conflicts or failure modes

- confusing benchmark or capability gains with durable product adoption
- treating enterprise experimentation as equivalent to durable rollout
- underweighting distribution, infrastructure, and workflow integration
- overgeneralizing from frontier-model headlines to the whole stack

## Missing coverage

## Most fragile assumptions

- that model capability alone drives market outcomes
- that enterprise experimentation is equivalent to durable rollout
- that frontier-model leadership automatically converts into product dominance
