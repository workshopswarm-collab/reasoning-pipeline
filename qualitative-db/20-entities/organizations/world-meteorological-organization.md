# Summary

The WMO is a high-yield institution because global weather coordination, climate-warning systems, and hazard communication increasingly affect physical and economic risk expectations.

## What this entity is

An international organization focused on weather, climate, and hydrological coordination and standards.

## Why it matters

The WMO matters because global weather and climate information is increasingly central to disaster preparedness, supply-chain planning, and public-risk interpretation.

## Current state

Research should focus on warning systems, coordination quality, hazard framing, climate communication, and whether global meteorological cooperation is improving resilience under mounting volatility.

## Key strengths

- useful bridge between scientific coordination and real-world operational risk
- strong relevance to climate and weather-linked disruption analysis
- can shape cross-border preparedness and information quality

## Key weaknesses

- indirect influence may be underappreciated outside hazard events
- outputs can be generalized or underused in political and market discourse

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful institution for weather-risk and climate-resilience analysis

## Open questions

- how much future resilience will depend on global coordination of weather intelligence versus national response capacity alone
