# Summary

The ICC is a high-yield institution because war-crimes legitimacy, international law, and leader accountability narratives often converge around it.

## What this entity is

An international legal institution focused on prosecution of severe international crimes.

## Why it matters

The ICC matters because even when enforcement power is limited, legal legitimacy and leader-targeting can materially affect diplomatic narratives and reputational risk.

## Current state

Research should focus on warrant activity, legitimacy debates, enforcement constraints, geopolitical reactions, and whether international-law claims are shaping state behavior or mainly symbolic conflict.

## Key strengths

- strong relevance to legitimacy and accountability in conflict analysis
- useful bridge between legal framing and geopolitical narrative
- can alter diplomatic and reputational terrain even without hard enforcement

## Key weaknesses

- enforcement is limited and politically uneven
- symbolic legal action can be overread relative to material coercive capacity

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful institution for conflict-legitimacy and international-law analysis

## Open questions

- how much practical geopolitical weight the ICC can retain under growing major-power contestation of international legal norms
