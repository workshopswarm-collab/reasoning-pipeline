# Summary

Andrés Manuel López Obrador is a high-yield political entity because Mexican executive politics, institutional change, and U.S.-Mexico spillovers give him lasting prediction-market relevance.

## What this entity is

A major Mexican political leader whose significance comes from executive power, movement-building, and reshaping domestic political expectations.

## Why it matters

AMLO matters because leadership legacy, institutional reform, and U.S.-Mexico policy spillover can alter migration, trade, and governance narratives.

## Current state

Research should focus on legacy effects, institutional durability, movement cohesion, economic and social-policy signaling, and how much successor politics preserve or alter his model.

## Key strengths

- strong relevance to Mexican governance and North American spillover analysis
- useful benchmark for executive-populist durability in a major neighboring state
- can shape politics beyond formal office through legacy and movement effects

## Key weaknesses

- legacy effects may be overgeneralized across changing political contexts
- personal salience can obscure institutional and regional variation
