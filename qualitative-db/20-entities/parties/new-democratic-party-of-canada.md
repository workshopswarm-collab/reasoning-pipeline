# Summary

The NDP is a high-yield political entity because coalition leverage, vote splitting, and parliamentary balance can make it disproportionately important in Canadian election outcomes.

## What this entity is

A major Canadian federal party whose significance comes from center-left coalition dynamics and parliamentary leverage.

## Why it matters

The party matters because smaller but durable parties can strongly affect winner/coalition probabilities even without dominating national vote share.

## Current state

Research should focus on seat-efficiency, coalition leverage, leader dependence, and whether national support is durable enough to keep the party pivotal.

## Key strengths

- can be disproportionately important in minority-government scenarios
- useful institutional anchor for understanding non-binary Canadian competition

## Key weaknesses

- vote share can matter less than seat distribution and bargaining leverage
- third-party relevance can erode quickly under strategic-polarization pressure

## Important recent changes

- its importance remains tied to whether Canadian parliamentary competition stays genuinely three-sided

## Open questions

- whether the party can preserve leverage as the main contest intensifies between Liberals and Conservatives
