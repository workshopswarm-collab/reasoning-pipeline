# Summary

Mexico is a high-yield prediction-market entity because it connects migration, cartel violence, trade integration, manufacturing shifts, and U.S. electoral spillover.

## What this entity is

A major North American state deeply integrated with the United States economically and politically salient in migration, security, and nearshoring narratives.

## Why it matters

Mexico matters because changes in border policy, trade, domestic security, or U.S.-Mexico relations can drive simultaneous movement across politics, geopolitics, and economics markets.

## Current state

Research should focus on migration pressures, security/cartel dynamics, trade and manufacturing integration, domestic political leadership, and U.S. policy spillover.

## Key strengths

- strong cross-domain relevance to politics, trade, and security
- major role in North American supply-chain and migration questions
- high event sensitivity from U.S. political spillovers

## Key weaknesses

- narratives can overconcentrate on border issues at the expense of broader state capacity and economic context
- security noise can obscure structural trade importance

## Important recent changes

- remains one of the most important neighboring-country entities for U.S.-centric prediction markets

## Open questions
