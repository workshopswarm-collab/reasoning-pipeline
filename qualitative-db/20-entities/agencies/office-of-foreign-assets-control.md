# Summary

OFAC is a high-yield institution because sanctions design, enforcement, and designation actions are direct tools of U.S. geopolitical and economic statecraft.

## What this entity is

The U.S. Treasury office responsible for sanctions administration and enforcement.

## Why it matters

OFAC matters because sanctions actions can change market structure, geopolitical pressure, and commercial access quickly and sometimes more concretely than diplomatic language.

## Current state

Research should focus on designation activity, enforcement posture, sectoral restrictions, evasion pressure, and whether sanctions tools are expanding, tightening, or losing effectiveness.

## Key strengths

- direct relevance to sanctions event markets
- highly actionable institutional layer for economic coercion analysis
- useful bridge between law, geopolitics, and commercial consequences

## Key weaknesses

- enforcement impact can vary substantially by target and coalition support
- symbolic sanctions can be overread relative to practical effect

## Important recent changes

- remains a core institution in sanctions and fragmentation analysis

## Open questions

- how much marginal geopolitical leverage future sanctions packages will retain under adaptation and evasion
