# Summary

OSTP is a high-yield institution because AI governance, science policy, and executive coordination on emerging technology often run through it.

## What this entity is

A White House policy office focused on science, technology, and research strategy across the executive branch.

## Why it matters

OSTP matters because emerging-tech governance often depends on how executive priorities are framed and coordinated before formal legislation arrives.

## Current state

Research should focus on AI governance guidance, science-policy priorities, cross-agency coordination, and whether technology strategy is becoming more coherent at the executive level.

## Key strengths

- strong relevance to AI and science-policy coordination
- useful bridge between executive priorities and implementation across agencies
- can influence high-salience emerging-tech norms before law solidifies

## Key weaknesses

- influence is partly indirect and can be hidden behind other institutions
- policy guidance may matter more symbolically than operationally in some contexts

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful institution for executive technology-governance analysis

## Open questions

- how much durable AI and science policy will be shaped by executive coordination versus legislative and judicial constraint
