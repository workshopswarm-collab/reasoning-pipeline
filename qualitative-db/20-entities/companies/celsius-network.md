# Summary

Celsius is a high-yield crypto entity because lending-platform failure, contagion memory, and trust breakdown remain important parts of crypto's institutional history.

## What this entity is

A failed or distressed crypto-lending platform whose significance comes from collapse-era contagion and lessons about risk, leverage, and transparency.

## Why it matters

Celsius matters because market memory of lending-platform failures still shapes trust, due diligence, and regulation debates in crypto.

## Current state

Research should focus on legal aftermath, institutional memory, contagion lessons, and whether the platform's legacy is still influencing risk perception and policy.

## Key strengths

- useful reference point for crypto trust and lending-risk analysis
- strong relevance to collapse-era contagion memory
- helps anchor discussions of platform opacity and retail risk

## Key weaknesses

- primarily historical rather than active as an operating business
- scandal memory can crowd out newer structural developments
