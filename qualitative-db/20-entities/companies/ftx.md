# Summary

FTX is a high-yield historical crypto entity because its collapse remains one of the defining failures for exchange trust, market structure, and regulatory memory.

## What this entity is

A failed crypto exchange and trading company whose collapse became a central scandal and institutional memory anchor for the crypto industry.

## Why it matters

FTX matters because many later discussions about custody, exchange risk, regulation, fraud, and reputational damage are still structured in relation to it. Even as an inactive company, it remains a durable reference object in crypto trust and policy analysis.

## Current state

Research should focus on legal aftermath, institutional memory, regulatory consequences, and how strongly the FTX collapse still shapes exchange due diligence and market structure thinking.

## Key strengths

- strong historical anchor for crypto trust and failure analysis
- useful bridge between scandal narrative and structural regulatory consequences
- recurring retrieval value in post-crisis crypto interpretation

## Key weaknesses

- historical salience can crowd out newer structural developments
- scandal-centric framing can simplify broader ecosystem weaknesses

## Important recent changes

- remains one of the most important historical failure objects in crypto regulatory and trust-memory analysis
- current relevance is more institutional and retrospective than operational

## Open questions

- how long FTX remains the dominant cautionary reference point for crypto market structure
- whether its policy legacy fades as new market infrastructures replace the crisis generation
