# Summary

Anchorage Digital is a high-yield crypto infrastructure entity because custody, regulated access, and institutional onboarding are central to the mainstreaming of digital assets.

## What this entity is

A crypto-financial infrastructure company focused on custody, institutional access, and regulated digital-asset services.

## Why it matters

Anchorage matters because institutional participation in crypto often depends less on tokens themselves than on whether trusted custody and compliance infrastructure exists.

## Current state

Research should focus on custody positioning, institutional partnerships, regulatory standing, product breadth, and whether regulated infrastructure demand is widening across asset classes and counterparties.

## Key strengths

- strong relevance to institutional crypto access and infrastructure quality
- useful bridge between traditional finance expectations and crypto-native asset exposure
- custody is a core prerequisite for many larger allocators and partners

## Key weaknesses

- infrastructure players can be under-observed despite strategic importance
- business value depends heavily on trust, regulation, and institutional adoption speed

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful watchlist entity for whether regulated crypto infrastructure is becoming durable institutional plumbing

## Open questions

- how much of institutional crypto growth will be captured by custody-first intermediaries versus exchanges or large incumbents
