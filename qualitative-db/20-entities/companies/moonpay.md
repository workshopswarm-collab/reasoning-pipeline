# Summary

MoonPay is a high-yield crypto infrastructure entity because onramps, payment partnerships, and consumer access to digital assets remain recurring bottlenecks in adoption.

## What this entity is

A crypto-financial infrastructure company focused on fiat-to-crypto onramps, consumer access, and partnership-led distribution.

## Why it matters

MoonPay matters because adoption often depends less on protocol design than on whether ordinary users can access digital assets through trusted payment rails and interfaces.

## Current state

Research should focus on partnership distribution, payment acceptance, regulatory exposure, geographic reach, and whether onramp infrastructure is becoming more commoditized or more defensible.

## Key strengths

- direct relevance to crypto adoption friction and access
- useful bridge between crypto-native ecosystems and mainstream payment behavior
- partnership model can create strong distribution leverage

## Key weaknesses

- onramp services can face margin pressure and commoditization risk
- access-heavy businesses remain exposed to regulation and banking dependencies

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful infrastructure entity for understanding where crypto adoption still depends on simpler user access

## Open questions

- how defensible consumer onramps are as exchanges, wallets, and payment networks integrate more native crypto access
