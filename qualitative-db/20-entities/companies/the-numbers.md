# Summary

The Numbers is a useful box-office data entity because it provides title-level theatrical performance and comparison surfaces used in film-performance research.

## What this entity is

A box-office and film-data platform used for theatrical grosses, title comparisons, and release-performance context.

## Why it matters

The Numbers matters because it is often part of the practical public-source stack for evaluating title-level box-office questions. It can provide useful cross-checks or alternative framing relative to Box Office Mojo when weekend performance or title-specific context is in question.

## Current state

Research should focus on data presentation clarity, title-page usefulness, comparability with other box-office sources, and whether its surfaces are helping resolve or merely reframing title-performance ambiguity.

## Key strengths

- useful public source for title-level theatrical performance checks
- strong comparison value alongside other box-office surfaces
- recurring relevance in movie-performance verification workflows

## Key weaknesses

- public box-office surfaces can differ in framing and timing details
- source availability does not always resolve title-level ambiguity cleanly

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful comparison and cross-check surface for theatrical-performance research
- current relevance is elevated where source-surface differences are themselves part of the interpretive problem

## Open questions

- how often The Numbers provides genuinely clarifying evidence versus simply another public framing of similar data
- when disagreement between public box-office surfaces is meaningful enough to change confidence
