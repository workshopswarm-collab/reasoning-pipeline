# Summary

Stephen Curry remains the defining offensive identity of the Warriors and one of the strongest single-player spacing and gravity effects in basketball.

## What this entity is

An elite guard whose shooting, off-ball movement, and defensive attention reshape how opponents must play.

## Why it matters

Golden State's ceiling and public market perception are still highly tied to Curry's availability and offensive load.

## Current state

Research should emphasize health, conditioning, shot-creation burden, and whether team context is still strong enough to leverage his gravity.

## Key strengths

- historic shooting gravity
- scalable offensive fit across lineups
- championship-tested late-game value

## Key weaknesses

- age and injury management now matter more
- supporting roster quality is essential for converting his gravity into winning at scale

## Important recent changes

- continued late-career relevance and ongoing injury/availability sensitivity

## Open questions

- whether current Warriors structure can still maximize his unique offensive effect
