# Summary

Aaron Judge is one of baseball's clearest franchise-carrying bats and a major determinant of Yankee offensive ceiling and public market sentiment.

## What this entity is

An MVP-caliber outfielder with elite power, high visibility, and strong centrality to team identity.

## Why it matters

Yankees evaluation changes materially with Judge's availability and form.

## Current state

Research should track his health, offensive production, and how much the broader lineup insulates or depends on him.

## Key strengths

- elite power and production profile
- enormous franchise and league visibility
- proven award-level impact

## Key weaknesses

- team-level optimism can overconcentrate around one star bat
- health matters disproportionately because of lineup centrality

## Important recent changes

- remains one of the most visible and valuable player-level drivers in MLB research

## Open questions

- how much true team resilience exists when Judge is absent or below peak form
