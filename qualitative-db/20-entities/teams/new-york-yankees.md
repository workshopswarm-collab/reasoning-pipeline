# Summary

The Yankees are one of the highest-salience baseball entities because contention, payroll expectations, star-player dependence, and media narrative intensity all converge around them.

## What this entity is

A flagship MLB franchise with exceptional brand power, high expectations, and constant competitive relevance.

## Why it matters

The Yankees matter because they are both a baseball team and a narrative institution; changes in health, stars, or underperformance can ripple far beyond ordinary team coverage.

## Current state

Research should focus on Judge/Cole-driven top-end strength, roster depth, AL East competition, injury sensitivity, and whether current results match underlying expectations.

## Key strengths

- huge information visibility and media coverage
- star-led upside with strong institutional commitment to contention

## Key weaknesses

- public narrative can exaggerate both upside and crisis
- contention outlook is very sensitive to superstar health and depth quality

## Important recent changes

- remains one of the most scrutinized and forecast-relevant MLB franchises

## Open questions

- how much underlying team strength persists when top-end stars miss time
