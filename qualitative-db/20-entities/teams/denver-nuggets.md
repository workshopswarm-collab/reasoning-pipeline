# Summary

Denver remains one of the clearest NBA title-race teams because Nikola Jokić gives the Nuggets a championship-caliber offensive center of gravity that few opponents can match.

## What this entity is

An NBA contender whose offensive identity and title viability are tightly linked to a singular superstar hub.

## Why it matters

Few teams are easier to read structurally: Jokić creates the ceiling, and support quality determines how much of that ceiling is realized. That makes Denver one of the cleanest teams for thinking about NBA championship odds through star concentration plus support quality.

## Current state

Research should focus on support-cast health, defense, and whether non-Jokić minutes remain stable enough in playoff settings. In the current NBA title race, Denver remains a direct benchmark for whether more balanced contenders can actually outperform a singular elite engine.

## Key strengths

- top-end offensive engine
- proven championship core logic
- strong identity continuity

## Key weaknesses

- high concentration around one star engine
- margin can narrow quickly if support pieces underperform

## Important recent changes

- still viewed through MVP-level Jokić lens in current contention discussions

## Open questions

- whether the supporting ecosystem remains championship-caliber around the star hub
