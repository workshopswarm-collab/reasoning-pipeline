# Summary

The Toronto Blue Jays are a core MLB entity whose evaluation depends on AL East competition, roster quality, health, and how much top-end talent translates into durable team strength.

## What this entity is

An American League East franchise with long-term relevance, strong market visibility, and recurring playoff aspirations.

## Why it matters

The Blue Jays are a natural anchor for baseball-market research because they combine strong division competition, notable star power, and high fan/information visibility.

## Current state

Research should focus on lineup quality, rotation/bullpen depth, AL East context, player health, and whether team structure supports sustainable contention.

## Key strengths

- high information visibility
- meaningful star-player presence
- competitive division context sharpens evaluation quality

## Key weaknesses

- division strength can make even good underlying teams look fragile in standings terms
- baseball outcomes are highly sensitive to roster health and rotation quality

## Important recent changes

- recent division and pennant relevance keep the club highly forecast-relevant

## Open questions

- how much true contention margin exists relative to AL East rivals
- whether star-led upside is supported by enough depth and health
