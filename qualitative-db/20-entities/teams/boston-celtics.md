# Summary

The Celtics are one of the clearest anchors of the NBA title race because roster depth, two-way versatility, and star wing play give them both a high floor and a real championship ceiling.

## What this entity is

An NBA contender built around two-way versatility, lineup strength, and Tatum-centered star scalability.

## Why it matters

Boston is a canonical NBA contender reference point: deep, talented, and structurally strong enough that small changes in health, shooting variance, or late-game creation can materially shift NBA Finals and championship probabilities.

## Current state

Research should focus on playoff execution, shot-quality sustainability, late-game offense, and whether roster balance remains intact under health stress. In the current NBA title-race frame, Boston is usually one of the first teams any contender comparison has to clear.

## Key strengths

- deep roster quality
- strong two-way framework
- credible championship-level floor and ceiling

## Key weaknesses

- late-game star-shot questions can still matter more than regular-season depth in extreme leverage spots

## Important recent changes

- remains a high-end contender benchmark in current league context

## Open questions

- whether depth and balance remain sufficient against more heliocentric elite teams in postseason settings
