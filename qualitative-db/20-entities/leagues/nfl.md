# Summary

The NFL is the core professional-football league entity because quarterback leverage, coaching, roster construction, and postseason structure all derive meaning through it.

## What this entity is

The premier American football league and the main context-setting institution for football markets.

## Why it matters

NFL structure determines how schedule, postseason format, quarterback scarcity, and media intensity shape football outcomes.

## Current state

Research should track quarterback health, coaching, roster turnover, standings/playoff context, and how a small number of high-leverage variables drive team evaluation.

## Key strengths

- very high information density and media attention
- clear structure for contender and quarterback-centric analysis

## Key weaknesses

- short seasons and playoff format create high variance
- public narratives can move faster than underlying team quality

## Important recent changes

- remains the central football framework for sports prediction and public attention

## Open questions

- how much current NFL parity is real versus schedule/injury noise
