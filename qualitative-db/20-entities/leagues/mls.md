# Summary

MLS is a high-yield sports entity because growing U.S. soccer attention, Inter Miami/Messi spillover, and expanding public-market relevance make it a useful base context object.

## What this entity is

A professional soccer league whose significance comes from North American growth, star-driven visibility, and shifting attention economics.

## Why it matters

MLS matters because leagues can become market-relevant before their full competitive level changes, especially when star concentration expands attention dramatically.

## Current state

Research should focus on star-driven attention, club quality dispersion, international crossover interest, and whether North American growth translates into durable market depth.

## Key strengths

- benefits from strong narrative crossover between sports, culture, and celebrity attention
- club and player markets can spike sharply when global stars or tournaments are involved

## Key weaknesses

- competitive quality and international relevance are less stable than in top European leagues
- attention can be concentrated in a few clubs or personalities

## Important recent changes

- global attention has risen materially as star signings and North American soccer relevance have increased

## Open questions

- whether league-wide strength is deepening or whether visibility remains too concentrated to support broad durable market relevance
