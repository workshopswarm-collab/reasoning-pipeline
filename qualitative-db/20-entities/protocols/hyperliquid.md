# Summary

Hyperliquid is a high-yield crypto protocol because onchain trading infrastructure, derivatives-style activity, and exchange/protocol convergence are central structural trends.

## What this entity is

A crypto protocol/platform associated with trading infrastructure and exchange-like functionality in an onchain form.

## Why it matters

Hyperliquid matters because it helps test whether more of crypto's trading stack can migrate onchain without losing speed, liquidity, or user relevance.

## Current state

Research should focus on liquidity, trading adoption, product depth, user retention, and whether onchain trading architecture is becoming competitive with centralized venues.

## Key strengths

- strong relevance to exchange-protocol convergence in crypto
- useful bridge between DeFi and high-frequency trading-style infrastructure
- can reveal whether onchain markets are maturing beyond simple spot activity

## Key weaknesses

- infrastructure novelty may outpace sustained liquidity depth
- platform-specific enthusiasm can distort broader structural interpretation

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful protocol for onchain trading-infrastructure and market-structure analysis

## Open questions

- how much of future crypto market structure will migrate toward onchain execution without sacrificing the advantages of centralized venues
