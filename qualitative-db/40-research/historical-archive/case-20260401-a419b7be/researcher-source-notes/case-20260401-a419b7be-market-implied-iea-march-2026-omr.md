# Summary

IEA's March 2026 report strongly validates the market's core bullish mechanism: a near halt in tanker movements through Hormuz, roughly 20 mb/d of disrupted crude/product exports, at least 8 mb/d of curtailed crude production, and benchmark crude around $92/bbl after a roughly $20 spike since Feb. 28.

## Key facts extracted

- The Middle East war led to a near halt in tanker movements through the Strait of Hormuz.
- The IEA says nearly 20 mb/d of crude and product exports are disrupted.
- It estimates at least 8 mb/d of crude production is currently curtailed, plus additional condensates/NGLs.
- Benchmark crude prices surged about $20/bbl to around $92/bbl since the outbreak of hostilities.
- Consumer countries hold large inventories and IEA member countries agreed to release 400 mb from emergency reserves.
- The IEA also cut 2026 demand growth expectations, citing both higher prices and weaker economic conditions.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The source directly states that the current oil market is under severe physical and logistical stress, and that the bullish move is not merely speculative sentiment. It also directly notes countervailing buffers: large inventories, emergency reserve releases, and demand destruction from higher prices.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the disruption remains severe through the market's March resolution date.
- How much emergency stock release and demand weakening cap front-month prices.
- Whether the physical stress described for benchmark crude translates one-for-one into the exact CME active-month settlement used for resolution.

## Why this source may matter

This is the strongest public case for why a high market-implied probability is not crazy. It shows the market is responding to a genuine chokepoint shock with real supply losses, not just narrative momentum.

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports the market's bullish prior and explains why a $90+ settlement can be favored. But because it also documents buffers and demand destruction, it does not by itself justify treating $90+ as almost locked in.

## Reliability notes

High-signal institutional report on global oil balances and disruptions. Strong for mechanism validation, but still dependent on fast-changing wartime conditions and incomplete visibility.
