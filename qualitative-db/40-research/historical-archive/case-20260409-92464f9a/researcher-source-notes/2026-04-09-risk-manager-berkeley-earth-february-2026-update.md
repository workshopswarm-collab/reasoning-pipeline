# Summary

Berkeley Earth’s February 2026 update provides independent contextual climate evidence: February 2026 was warm at 1.55 ± 0.12°C above the 1850-1900 baseline, but the report also flags unusually high uncertainty from NOAA data disruptions and notes that revisions remain possible as more station data arrives.

## Key facts extracted

- February 2026 global average was reported at 1.55 ± 0.12°C above 1850-1900.
- February 2026 was nominally the second warmest February on record.
- Berkeley Earth says conditions remained modestly cooler than during much of 2023 and 2024.
- The report explicitly says February 2026 has larger uncertainty than preferred due to ongoing upstream data-provider disruptions and reduced station coverage.
- The report discusses NOAA archive degradation and says revisions are normal, but current disruptions are larger than typical.
- ENSO context: La Niña was fading, with stronger El Niño risk shifted into mid-to-late 2026 rather than immediate March 2026.
