# Assumption

The market's 95% price is assuming that the next Netflix US TV Top 10 update for **3/30/26 - 4/5/26** will preserve or elevate XO, Kitty Season 3 to the #1 slot rather than being displaced by another title before the official Tuesday posting.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is priced near certainty before the decisive chart is published, so most of the residual risk is timing and displacement risk rather than title existence risk.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability YES view.
- Acceptance that current pre-update confidence can remain above 85% despite lack of direct settlement data.
- The judgment that only a narrow set of failure modes still matters.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Netflix's own Tudum ecosystem clearly references XO, Kitty Season 3 as active current content.
- There is no sign during this run that the governing chart has already published a contradictory result.
- Extreme market pricing often reflects consensus around a dominant release-week performance expectation for headline Netflix series, especially when the governing source is a simple official chart.

## What would falsify it

- The Netflix Tudum US TV weekly update for **3/30/26 - 4/5/26** posts with a different title at #1.
- A Netflix-controlled pre-release or adjacent chart surface indicates XO, Kitty materially underperformed relative to another contemporaneous US title.
- The update fails to post by the market deadline and resolution mechanics force "Other."

## Early warning signs

- Evidence of another US Netflix title dominating current viewership or social/platform placement close to the reporting-window end.
- Delay or anomaly on the Netflix Top 10 publishing cadence.
- Any authoritative Netflix text suggesting the target week or category differs from what traders assume.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market should fall sharply from near-certainty to NO/Other depending on the failure mode, because a 95% price leaves little cushion for even a modest timing or rank-order surprise.
