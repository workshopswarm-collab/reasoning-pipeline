# Assumption

The decisive catalyst for this market is the scheduled Netflix US TV Top 10 publication on April 7, 2026, and there is no credible pre-publication signal available in this run that should move the probability by more than a few points away from the market’s already-extreme baseline.

## Why this assumption matters

This case is almost entirely about timing. If there were a strong pre-release signal showing XO, Kitty had clearly overtaken the field for the unpublished 3/30/26 - 4/5/26 window, then a 95% market price could be justified; if not, the price is mostly confidence in an upcoming chart publication rather than confidence from already-visible evidence.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly-bearish versus market view rather than a full contrarian call.
- Treating the April 7 chart publication as the highest-information catalyst.
- Framing current conviction as limited by pre-publication observability.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The authoritative Netflix page still shows the prior completed week on 2026-04-06.
- The contract explicitly says the resolving update is expected on April 7 at 3:00 PM ET.
- The available authoritative evidence during this run constrains mechanics and timing, but does not yet disclose the target week’s winner.
- Because the market is already at 0.95, the burden for maintaining or increasing confidence should be a stronger pre-publication signal than was available here.

## What would falsify it

- A Netflix-controlled pre-publication surface or official release note revealing the upcoming chart winner.
- A clearly visible Netflix app or Tudum signal specifically tied to the 3/30/26 - 4/5/26 US views week.
- A direct rule clarification showing the title mapping makes the market almost mechanically true despite the apparent season-number mismatch.

## Early warning signs

- Evidence that `XO, Kitty Season 3` is mislabeled and should map to a different Netflix title entry than assumed.
- A late-breaking Netflix editorial push or homepage prominence signal tied specifically to the target window.
- A contract interpretation update that weakens the relevance of the April 7 publication timing.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a credible pre-publication signal appears, the market’s 95% price could become more defensible. If the timing assumption fails the other way—e.g. publication timing slips or title mapping is ambiguous—the confidence should drop further because resolution risk rises.
