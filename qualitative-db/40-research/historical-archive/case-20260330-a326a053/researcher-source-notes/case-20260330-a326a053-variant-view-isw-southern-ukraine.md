# Summary

Recent ISW reporting cuts against an easy, near-certain Russia-captures-all-of-Huliaipole path. ISW describes Ukrainian counterattacks in the Huliaipole direction as materially disrupting Russian plans, tying down forces, and even helping Ukraine retake meaningful territory in southern Ukraine during early 2026.

## Key facts extracted

From ISW March 9, 2026:
- Ukrainian forces were **successfully counterattacking** in the Oleksandrivka and **Hulyaipole directions**.
- ISW said these counterattacks were generating **tactical, operational, and strategic effects** that may disrupt Russia's spring-summer 2026 offensive plan.
- ISW wrote that Russia likely hoped advances in the Hulyaipole direction would support broader advances toward Orikhiv and later Zaporizhzhia City.
- ISW said Ukrainian counterattacks in late January **greatly complicated Russian plans** to advance rapidly from the east.
- ISW assessed that Russian forces were facing a **far more difficult battlefield situation** in southern Ukraine in early March than at the start of 2026.

From ISW March 26, 2026:
- Ukrainian counterattacks continued to make gains in southern Ukraine.
- ISW said Ukrainian officials reported liberation of **over 400 square kilometers** in the Oleksandrivka and Hulyaipole directions from late January to mid-March.
- ISW said Russian command had to **redeploy elite airborne and naval infantry units** to southern Ukraine in response to Ukrainian counterattacks.
- ISW assessed it is **unlikely** Russian forces will be able to make significant efforts to advance in the Fortress Belt area while also contending with Ukraine's recent successes in the Hulyaipole and Oleksandrivka directions.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- ISW is not describing an uninterrupted Russian glide path toward full municipal capture.
- Huliaipole is framed as part of a contested and disrupted operational theater, not as a nearly wrapped-up positional gain.
- Ukrainian activity in the relevant direction has recently been positive enough to force Russian reallocations and complicate offensive planning.

## What is uncertain

- ISW toplines do not by themselves tell us the exact current municipal shading state on the ISW map for every parcel of Huliaipole municipality.
- Operational disruption does not prove Russia cannot still capture the municipality by April 30.
- ISW's conservative mapping methodology may lag or understate some tactical changes.

## Why this source may matter

The market's 0.86 price suggests a very high-confidence path. ISW's own recent writing makes that level of confidence look fragile because the same source class used for resolution is simultaneously emphasizing Ukrainian counterpressure and Russian disruption in the Huliaipole direction.

## Possible impact on the question

This evidence should push against treating April-30 capture as close to inevitable. The more plausible bearish variant is not that Russia cannot ever take Huliaipole, but that the path is contested enough that 0.86 may be too high.
