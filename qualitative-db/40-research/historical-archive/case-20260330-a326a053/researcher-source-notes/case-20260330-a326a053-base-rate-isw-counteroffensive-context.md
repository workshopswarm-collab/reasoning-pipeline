# Summary

ISW's March 2026 reporting cuts against an 86% near-certainty view. The Huliaipole axis is active and strategically important, but recent battlefield direction is not one-way Russian progress. ISW repeatedly describes Ukrainian counterattacks in the Huliaipole and Oleksandrivka directions as materially disrupting Russian plans.

## Key facts extracted

- ISW March 9: Ukrainian forces were "successfully counterattacking" in the Hulyaipole direction and these actions may disrupt Russia's spring-summer 2026 offensive plan.
- ISW March 9: Russian plans had aimed to use gains in the Hulyaipole direction to support a broader push toward Orikhiv and eventually Zaporizhzhia City.
- ISW March 9: Ukrainian counterattacks deprived Russian forces of some of the starting offensive positions they hoped to use for a summer offensive.
- ISW March 16: Ukrainian forces had reportedly liberated more than 400 square kilometers in the Oleksandrivka and Hulyaipole directions from late January to around March 10.
- ISW March 16: Russian advances west of Huliaipole were slowed to less than roughly 1.2 to 1.5 km per week.
- ISW March 16: Russian elements fighting northwest and west of Huliaipole had not fully restored the prior battlefield situation after Ukrainian counterattacks.
- ISW March 26: Ukrainian forces had made significant tactical gains in southern Ukraine since late January 2026 and had retaken more territory than Russian forces seized in February 2026.
- ISW March 26: Russian command had to redeploy elite airborne and naval infantry units to southern Ukraine in response to Ukrainian counterattacks.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The source directly supports the following picture:
- Huliaipole matters as part of a wider Russian operational design in southern Zaporizhzhia.
- Russia is still trying to press in southern Ukraine, but faces real friction from Ukrainian counterattacks.
- The local battlefield around Huliaipole is contested rather than cleanly collapsing in Russia's favor.
- Russian offensive tempo and options are being constrained by the need to respond to Ukrainian actions elsewhere on the same southern front.

## What is uncertain

- ISW does not by itself tell us the exact current municipality-level control state required by the market's resolution rule.
- It remains unclear how much Huliaipole municipality still needs to be captured for full-coverage map resolution.
- The reported rate of Russian advance may change materially if local defenses crack or if Russia reallocates more force.
