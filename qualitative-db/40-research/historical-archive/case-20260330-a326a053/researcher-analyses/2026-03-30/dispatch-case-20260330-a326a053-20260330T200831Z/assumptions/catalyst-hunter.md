# Assumption

The current April-30-favored market price only holds if Russian pressure converts into visibly accelerating municipality-level map closure within the next several ISW/DeepState cycles rather than remaining a contested attritional front.

## Why this assumption matters

This market is mostly a timing market now. If the next map cycles do not show clear closure, the market has less reason to stay extremely confident in the April 30 bucket.

## What this assumption supports

- A still-positive probability of capture by April 30.
- The idea that traders are right to price Huliaipole as a live offensive objective.
- A view that the next few map cycles are the highest-information catalysts.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Huliaipole is a real Russian operational objective in ISW's framework.
- The spring-summer offensive is active.
- The market page extract shows traders are pricing timing buckets, not just raw terminal truth.

## What would falsify it

- Continued ISW/DeepState map stagnation or only tiny gains over several cycles.
- More evidence of effective Ukrainian counterattacks that keep forcing Russian redeployments.
- Clear contract interpretation issues that reduce confidence in the apparent April 30 price.

## Early warning signs

- Headlines keep saying "pressure near Huliaipole" without map closure.
- ISW keeps emphasizing contestation and Ukrainian disruption rather than rapid Russian progress.
- Traders rotate confidence away from the earliest timing bucket.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The catalyst case weakens materially. The market can still end Yes eventually, but the sharp April-30 confidence should compress if closure is not visible soon.
