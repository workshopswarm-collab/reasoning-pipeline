# Summary
The most predictable recurring catalysts around the March 2026 contract window are CPI on March 11, the March 17-18 FOMC meeting, and late-month PCE/income plus Q1 GDP advance on April 30 only after this market resolves.

## Key facts extracted
- BLS lists February 2026 CPI for release on March 11, 2026 at 8:30 AM.
- BLS lists March 2026 CPI for release on April 10, 2026, which is after this market resolves.
- BEA lists Personal Income and Outlays for February 2026 on April 9, 2026 and GDP advance for Q1 2026 on April 30, 2026, both after resolution.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- CPI schedule: February 2026 CPI releases March 11, 2026; March 2026 CPI releases April 10, 2026.
- BEA schedule: Personal Income and Outlays for February 2026 releases April 9, 2026; Q1 2026 advance GDP releases April 30, 2026.

## What is uncertain
- This note does not include jobs, retail sales, ISM, or earnings dates.
- Market sensitivity to any given CPI print depends on the prevailing inflation narrative and positioning at the time.

## Why this source may matter
It sharply narrows the true high-information calendar during the final month: the market will not have March CPI, March PCE, or Q1 GDP before resolution. That makes the March 11 CPI print and March 17-18 Fed meeting disproportionately important because they are among the last major macro releases available to trigger a repricing inside the contract window.

## Possible impact on the question
If SPX is still below 6300 entering March, the contract likely needs either a benign/soft CPI on March 11, a dovish Fed on March 17-18, or both, to generate a fast enough rally. If those events disappoint, there are fewer remaining macro catalysts with similar information value before March 30 resolution.

## Reliability notes
Official calendars are high confidence for release timing. They are useful for catalyst sequencing but not for directional inference by themselves.
