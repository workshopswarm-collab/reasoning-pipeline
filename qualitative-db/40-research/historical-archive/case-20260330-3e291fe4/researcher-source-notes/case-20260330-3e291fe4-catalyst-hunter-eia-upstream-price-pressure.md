# Summary

EIA's March 30 daily prices page still showed strong upstream pressure heading into the final day: WTI up 5.3%, Brent up 7.1%, New York Harbor gasoline up 5.6%, Gulf Coast gasoline up 6.7%, and Gulf Coast crack spreads up 14.9% on the latest close shown. This does not resolve the market directly, but it supports the idea that retail pass-through pressure has not vanished.

## Key facts extracted

- WTI: **101.26**, up **5.3%**.
- Brent: **121.47**, up **7.1%**.
- NY Harbor gasoline: **3.26**, up **5.6%**.
- Gulf Coast gasoline: **3.30**, up **6.7%**.
- Gulf Coast 3:2:1 crack spread: **49.35**, up **14.9%**.
- EIA notes daily prices are updated on weekdays and republishes AAA retail data with permission.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Upstream commodity pressure remained elevated late in the window.
- Refining margin pressure was also strong.
- The environment for continued retail strength remained supportive.

## What is uncertain

- Retail pass-through timing is lagged and may not fully reach the AAA national average before the deadline.
- The EIA snapshot includes prior-close wholesale data, so it is indirect for the contract's final day.
- Strong upstream signals alone cannot guarantee the last penny of national retail movement.

## Why this source may matter

This is the main non-AAA catalyst source. If the market is right, it is partly because traders believe high upstream pressure is still feeding into the final retail print.

## Possible impact on the question

Moderately bullish for Yes, but less important than AAA itself. Best used as confirmation that the final AAA step still has causal support.
