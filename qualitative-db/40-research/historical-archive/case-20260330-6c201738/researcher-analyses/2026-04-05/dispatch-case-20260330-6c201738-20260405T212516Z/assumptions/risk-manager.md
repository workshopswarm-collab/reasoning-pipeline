## Core fragile assumption

The working NO view assumes the market will resolve using a stacked “general tariff rate” logic that counts both:
1. the China-wide opioid-order tariff (20% after Mar. 3, 2025), and
2. the broad reciprocal baseline tariff imposed on all countries (10% from Apr. 5, 2025),
while excluding item-specific exceptions and paused/unimplemented announcements.

## Why this assumption matters

If stacked, the countable general tariff rate on China is already at least 30%, comfortably above the 5%-15% band. If not stacked, or if one layer is later withdrawn/suspended for China before March 31, 2026, the market could become more live.

## Why this assumption looks reasonable

- The market description itself gives a stacking example: a general tariff on all imports plus a tariff on top of that on Chinese imports should be added.
- EO 14195 states its China duty is “in addition to any other duties, fees, exactions, or charges applicable.”
- The reciprocal tariff fact sheet explicitly presents the Apr. 5 tariff as a general all-country baseline.

## Main failure modes

1. **Non-stacking administrative treatment**: later CBP or official guidance could clarify that one layer supersedes rather than stacks for purposes of the market’s “general tariff rate.”
2. **Later rollback**: Trump administration could reduce or terminate the China opioid tariff, or materially revise the reciprocal framework before March 31, 2026.
3. **Source-of-truth ambiguity**: resolution could rely on a narrower official statement that names a lower “general tariff rate” for China notwithstanding overlapping executive orders.
4. **General-vs-special carveout dispute**: some special pathways (especially de minimis/postal treatment) could confuse the practical headline rate and create settlement controversy.

## What would most weaken this assumption

- A later official Trump administration statement or Federal Register notice explicitly saying China’s general tariff rate is in the 5%-15% range as of the resolution timestamp.
- A later CBP/HTSUS implementation notice showing one of the broad China-wide tariff layers was terminated, paused, or absorbed before March 31, 2026.
- Official non-stacking guidance directly relevant to the market’s contract language.

## Current judgment

This assumption is still strong enough to support a NO lean, but it is the main interpretive hinge and therefore the primary place to watch for later invalidation.
