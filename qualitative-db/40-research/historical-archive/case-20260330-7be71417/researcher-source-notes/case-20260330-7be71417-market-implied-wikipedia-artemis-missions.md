# Summary
Wikipedia's Artemis mission list shows Artemis II with a listed launch datetime of April 1, 2026 UTC/EDT, implying that widely available contextual references are aligned with a very-near-term launch rather than a delay past April 30.

## Key facts extracted
- Artemis II is listed as launching April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC / 6:35:12 pm EDT.
- It is presented as the first crewed Artemis flight on SLS Block 1 from LC-39B.
- The same page lists later Artemis missions as NET 2027 or later, which is directionally consistent with Artemis II being the near-term active mission in the program sequence.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- The page explicitly lists a concrete April 1, 2026 launch datetime for Artemis II.
- The page functions as a launch-manifest style contextual source, though not an authoritative one.

## What is uncertain
- Wikipedia is not a governing source of truth and may lag or inherit errors from other reporting.
- The page does not itself resolve whether the market counts liftoff only, scrubs, or official launch-status edge cases.

## Why this source may matter
It is a strong contextual check for what publicly aggregated information currently says the schedule is. If even public reference layers show an April 1 launch date, an 81% market price is easier to understand as the market largely pricing a scheduled near-term launch with some normal launch-risk discount.

## Possible impact on the question
This source supports the view that the market is not inventing an aggressive timeline out of thin air. It makes a by-April-30 launch look like the base case absent contrary official NASA schedule changes.

## Reliability notes
