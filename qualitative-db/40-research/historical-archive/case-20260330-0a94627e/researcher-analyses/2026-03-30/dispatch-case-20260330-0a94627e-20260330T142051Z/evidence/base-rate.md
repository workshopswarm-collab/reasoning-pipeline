# Summary

Current lean: **YES, well above the market-implied 79.25%, because the commercial result already appears to have landed around $54.5M and the remaining risk is mostly source-finalization mechanics.**

## Question being evaluated

Will `Project Hail Mary` gross **more than $54M** domestically on its **second weekend** (March 27–29, 2026)?

## Current lean

Strong lean **YES**.

## Prior / starting view

Outside-view baseline before post-opening data: a film opening at **$80.5M** needs a second weekend of at least **67.1% of opening** (equivalently, a drop no worse than about **-32.9%**) to clear $54M. For a non-franchise, adult-skewing sci-fi/event film, that is a demanding threshold. A disciplined base-rate prior would start materially below the current market price absent evidence of exceptional word of mouth and favorable calendar/competition conditions.

## Evidence supporting the claim

1. **The Numbers lists an exact second weekend of $54,537,595.**  
   - Source: `...the-numbers-weekend-data.md`  
   - Why it matters: this is already above the threshold by **$537,595**.  
   - Directness: direct box-office data.  
   - Weight: very high.

2. **Daily figures shown by The Numbers are internally consistent with a >$54M weekend.**  
   - Fri $14.67M, Sat $22.78M, Sun $17.09M.  
   - Why it matters: reduces the odds that the weekend number is a typo or flimsy estimate.  
   - Directness: direct.  
   - Weight: high.

3. **Independent coverage converged around $54.5M / -32%.**  
   - Sources: AP, Deadline.  
   - Why it matters: cross-source convergence lowers idiosyncratic-source risk.  
   - Directness: direct weekend reporting, though Sunday-estimate based.  
   - Weight: high.

4. **The film had unusually strong retention conditions.**  
   - A CinemaScore, 95% RT score, little meaningful new competition, premium-format retention, and favorable spring-break / Easter runway.  
   - Why it matters: these are exactly the types of factors that can justify moving far above a normal big-opener fade prior.  
   - Directness: partly direct, partly interpretive.  
   - Weight: medium-high.

5. **Comp framing suggests this is an exceptional hold, not an ordinary one.**  
   - Deadline compares the second weekend favorably with `Oppenheimer` ($46.7M, -43%) and `Dune: Part Two` ($46.2M, -44%).  
   - Rotten Tomatoes points to `The Martian` as a high-leg sci-fi comp and notes the film’s strong reception profile.  
   - Why it matters: supports the argument that this case belongs in the “special conditions” bucket rather than the generic big-opener decay bucket.  
   - Weight: medium.

## Evidence against the claim

1. **The threshold was structurally demanding from the start.**  
   - A >$54M second weekend off an $80.5M opening requires about a **-32.9%** hold or better.  
   - Why it matters: that is not a normal path for a large-opening live-action film.  
   - Weight: medium.

2. **Sunday reporting can revise on Monday actuals.**  
   - Why it matters: this market resolves to final-ish source data, not Sunday headlines.  
   - Weight: low-medium.

3. **The market description’s Box Office Mojo URL appears mismatched.**  
   - Why it matters: introduces small operational ambiguity around the exact page used for resolution.  
   - Weight: low.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- `Project Hail Mary` is a non-franchise film, which usually argues for more uncertainty, but its post-launch reception and competition environment were unusually favorable.
- Some comp framing can be cherry-picked; however, in this case the raw weekend number itself matters more than narrative spin.

## Conflict between inputs

There is little factual conflict on the central number. The main disagreement is about **how much residual source-finalization risk** remains:
- raw weekend reporting and The Numbers imply the answer is effectively already yes;
- the only material caution is that the market’s contractual source is Box Office Mojo final daily data.

This is mostly a **source-mechanics** disagreement, not a disagreement about demand.

## Key assumptions

- Box Office Mojo final daily data for the correct `Project Hail Mary` page will be broadly aligned with The Numbers / AP / Deadline.
- Monday revision, if any, will be much smaller than **$537k**.
- The market resolves against the intended film page despite the apparent bad URL in the market description.

## Key uncertainties

- Whether the exact Box Office Mojo final 3-day figure is already posted in a machine-readable way.
- Whether any operational/resolution handling issue arises from the mismatched URL in the market description.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Box Office Mojo final daily tab prints a 3-day total **below $54.0M**.
- Resolution staff uses the wrong title page or another inconsistent source interpretation.

## What would increase confidence

- Direct capture of the correct Box Office Mojo Domestic Daily table for March 27–29 showing a final 3-day total above $54M.
- Official Monday actuals matching The Numbers and AP reporting.

## Net update logic

The outside view starts skeptical because the threshold required an exceptional hold for a large-opening live-action film. But the specific evidence here is strong enough to overwhelm that prior: the exact weekend total already appears above the line, multiple outlets converge on the same number, and the film had the structural supports needed for such a hold. So the base-rate stance moves from “this is hard” to “this has very likely already happened.”
