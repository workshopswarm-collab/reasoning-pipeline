# Summary

NOAA is not the settlement source, but it provides independent official confirmation that March 2026 was unusually warm and near the top of the historical March distribution.

## Key facts extracted

- NOAA says March 2026 had a global surface temperature **1.31°C above the 20th-century average**.
- NOAA says March 2026 tied with 2024 as the **second-warmest March on record**.
- NOAA links to its full March 2026 global climate report and climate-at-a-glance series.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- March 2026 was 1.31°C above the 20th-century average in NOAA's framework.
- Warm anomalies covered much of land and ocean surfaces.
- March 2025 was only 0.01°C warmer in NOAA's March ranking language.

## What is uncertain

- NOAA uses a different baseline and methodology from NASA GISS, so the 1.31°C figure is not mechanically the same as the NASA settlement figure.
- This source cannot settle the bracket directly because the market names NASA GISS specifically.

## Why this source may matter

It independently reduces the risk that the NASA result is a one-off extraction error or that March 2026 was plausibly much cooler than the contract bracket threshold.

## Possible impact on the question

While not dispositive for settlement, NOAA's report supports the view that March 2026 was warm enough that the 1.25-1.29°C bracket was at least at risk of being exceeded, making an extreme `Yes` price less comfortable than it first appears.

Strong as an official contextual source and reasonably independent from the Polymarket/NASA contract text, but weaker than NASA for settlement because it uses different definitions and baselines.
