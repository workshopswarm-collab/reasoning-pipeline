# Summary

A ~95% Yes price for this market requires much stronger assumptions than “regional tensions remain elevated.” It effectively assumes that direct Iran-Israel escalation not only occurs, but does so in the exact qualifying form and on the exact listed date.

## Core assumptions

1. **Exact-date assumption**
   A qualifying direct Iranian strike is likely to occur on March 27, 2026 specifically, not merely sometime around that period.

2. **Direct-action assumption**
   Iran would choose direct state action rather than proxy retaliation, covert activity, cyber action, or threats.

3. **Impact assumption**
   At least one projectile or drone would impact Israeli ground territory, rather than being fully intercepted or falling outside qualifying territory.

4. **Attribution/confirmation assumption**
   Reporting and/or official confirmation would clearly establish Iranian state responsibility within the market’s confirmation window.

5. **Catalyst visibility assumption**
   There is, or will be, a sufficiently strong calendar-bound catalyst making that exact-day strike materially more likely than a generic background conflict prior.

## Why these assumptions matter

The first and fifth assumptions do most of the work. Elevated hostility alone can support a nontrivial risk of crisis, but it does not support near-certainty for a narrow exact-date event without a visible timing mechanism.

## What would falsify these assumptions

- Absence of any concrete pre-March-27 countdown catalyst, ultimatum, or announced retaliation window.
- Continued use of proxies or indirect escalation channels instead of direct Iranian strikes.
- Diplomatic or deterrent signals that preserve confrontation but reduce direct state-to-state exchange risk.
- Any evidence that the market price is mechanically distorted or stale rather than information-rich.

## Why this assumption note is useful

It separates a generic “Iran-Israel risk is high” narrative from the much harder proposition actually being priced by the market.
