# Summary

The market resolves Yes only for a narrowly defined direct Iranian strike: drones, missiles, or aerial bombs launched by Iranian military forces that impact Israeli ground territory on the listed date in Israel time. Proxy attacks do not count. Intercepted missiles or drones do not count unless they impact Israeli ground territory. If date/time cannot be confirmed by consensus of credible reporting within three calendar days, the market resolves No.

## Key facts extracted

- The resolution requires a direct Iranian military aerial attack, not proxy action.
- The strike must impact Israeli ground territory.
- Interceptions alone are insufficient for Yes.
- The event is date-specific rather than a broad escalation market.
- Consensus confirmation of timing is required within a short window.
- The market page currently indicates a final outcome of Yes and the assignment metadata shows current_price 0.9505, implying roughly 95.1%.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Yes requires Iran to initiate a drone, missile, or air strike on Israel’s soil on the listed date in Israel Time.
