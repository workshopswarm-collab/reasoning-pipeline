# Summary

The relevant base-rate backdrop is that direct Iranian missile/drone strikes that clearly originate from Iranian territory and impact another state's territory are rare events, and direct Iranian strikes on Iraq appear especially infrequent. The obvious recent precedents are the January 2020 strike on U.S. forces in Iraq and the January 2024 strike in Erbil; by contrast, Iran's larger direct attacks in 2024 were directed at Israel, not Iraq.

## Key facts extracted

- The 8 January 2020 Iranian attack on U.S. forces in Iraq was a direct IRGC ballistic-missile strike on Al-Asad and Erbil airbase locations in Iraq, launched from Iran in response to the killing of Qasem Soleimani.
- A January 2024 Iranian missile strike hit Erbil in Iraq, showing that direct Iran-on-Iraq strikes are possible in extremis, but not routine.
- April 2024 and October 2024 direct Iranian strikes were aimed at Israel, indicating that when Iran chooses direct overt state-on-state strikes, Israel has recently been the more common target class than Iraq.
- The Polymarket contract for this market is narrow: it requires a drone, missile, or air strike by Iranian forces that impacts Iraqi territory, excludes proxy attacks, and excludes merely intercepted projectiles.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The 2020 operation page says Iran launched ballistic missiles at Al-Asad Airbase and Erbil in Iraq in response to Soleimani's assassination.
- The April 2024 strike page says Iran directly launched drones/cruise missiles/ballistic missiles at Israel in reprisal for the Damascus embassy strike.
- The October 2024 strike page says Iran launched about 200 ballistic missiles at Israel, the second direct Iran-on-Israel attack of 2024.
- The market page says only attacks by Iranian forces explicitly claimed by Iran or confirmed to have originated from Iranian territory count; proxy attacks do not count.

## What is uncertain

- Full recent count of all historical direct Iran-on-Iraq qualifying strikes beyond the most salient 2020 and 2024 examples.

This source bundle is enough to anchor the outside view: qualifying direct Iran-on-Iraq strikes exist but appear rare, clustered around exceptional retaliation episodes, and are not the modal expression of Iranian regional coercion.
