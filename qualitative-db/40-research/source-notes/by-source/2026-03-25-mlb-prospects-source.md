# Summary

MLB Pipeline is the official league source for prospect rankings, team farm-system context, and promotion-relevant player development signals.

## Key facts extracted

- Prospects and farm systems are first-class baseball research objects because promotion timing can affect roster strength and narrative quickly.
- Team prospect context helps explain medium-term upside that may not yet be visible in current major-league performance.
- Prospect coverage is especially useful for teams whose contention path depends on internal development rather than only current roster quality.

## Why this source may matter

Prospect quality matters for long-horizon team strength, trades, and organizational upside even when the current MLB roster looks thin.

## Possible impact on the question

Useful when a baseball question depends on future roster reinforcement, trade leverage, or long-run organizational quality rather than current standings alone.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly provides prospect rankings, scouting context, and organizational pipeline information.
- It supports claims about farm-system quality and top prospect visibility.

## What is uncertain

- Prospect rankings are not guaranteed MLB outcomes, and timelines to impact can shift quickly.
- Public lists can lag behind internal development or role changes.

## Reliability notes

High-value official prospect context source; use alongside team and player baselines rather than as a standalone measure of current major-league strength.
