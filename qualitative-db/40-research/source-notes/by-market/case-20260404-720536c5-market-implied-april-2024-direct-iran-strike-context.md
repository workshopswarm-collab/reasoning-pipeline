# Summary

The strongest public evidence supporting a very high market probability is that Iran has already crossed the direct-attack threshold twice in the modern cycle: a large direct strike in April 2024 and another direct missile attack in October 2024 per BBC background reporting. That makes a qualifying direct Iranian strike on Israeli territory no longer a tail event in principle. The market appears to be pricing persistence of this new direct-conflict regime rather than the older assumption that Iran would stay confined to proxies.

## Key facts extracted

- BBC reported on 15 April 2024 that Iran carried out strikes against Israeli territory for the first time ever.
- BBC reported Iran launched more than 300 drones and missiles in April 2024; Israeli military figures cited by BBC said a small number of ballistic missiles reached Israel and struck Israeli territory.
- BBC reported one missile lightly hit Nevatim air base and cited US officials saying five ballistic missiles impacted Israeli territory.
- BBC background reporting on 26 October 2024 said the 1 October 2024 missile attack was Iran's second-ever direct attack on Israel.
- The Polymarket market rules require impact on Israeli ground territory by Iranian military drones/missiles/air strike; intercepted weapons do not count, but impacts do.
- The fetched Polymarket page indicates this listed market ultimately resolved Yes, consistent with the idea that consensus reporting later supported a qualifying event.
