# Summary
The weekend chart for March 27, 2026 shows *Project Hail Mary* holding the **#1 domestic slot** with **$54.54M**, far ahead of the #2 film at **$12.2M**. The film kept premium scale and broad distribution, which supports the thesis that audience demand held up well into weekend two.

## Key facts extracted
- *Project Hail Mary* ranked **#1** on the Mar 27 weekend chart.
- Weekend gross: **$54,537,595**.
- The #2 film, *Hoppers*, grossed **$12.2M**.
- The film played in **4,077 theaters**, with a **$13,377 per-theater average**.
- Total reported weekend box office for all reporting films was **$95.87M**, so *Project Hail Mary* represented a very large share of weekend demand.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- The chart reports a **-32%** change week-over-week for *Project Hail Mary*.
- No new wide release appears close to challenging it for the top spot.

## What is uncertain
- Relative chart dominance does not eliminate threshold risk when the contract cutoff is tight.
- This source does not isolate how much of the hold came from audience reception versus weak competition.

## Why this source may matter
This note helps separate two questions: whether the film was structurally strong enough to be a likely YES, and whether the margin above the specific $54M cutoff was large enough to justify very high confidence.

## Possible impact on the question
Supports YES on directional strength grounds. At the same time, the contract was not asking whether the movie would have a good hold or stay #1; it was asking whether it would beat a very specific line. The source therefore supports the bullish thesis but also reinforces that **strength in narrative terms can still sit uncomfortably close to the strike in contract terms**.

## Reliability notes
The Numbers chart is a standard industry-facing summary source for domestic weekend performance. Good for relative ranking and scale context; still secondary to Box Office Mojo for resolution under the contract wording.
