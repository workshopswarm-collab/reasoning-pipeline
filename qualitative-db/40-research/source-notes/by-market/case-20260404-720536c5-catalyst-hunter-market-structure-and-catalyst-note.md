# Summary

The market is priced at 0.9505, implying about a 95.1% chance that Iran will carry out a qualifying direct drone/missile/air strike on Israeli soil on one specific date nearly a year in the future. Given the resolution criteria, that price embeds an extremely strong assumption that a direct state-to-state aerial strike will occur on that exact date rather than merely that Iran-Israel tensions remain high or that proxy conflict continues.

## Key facts extracted

- Resolution is narrow: only a direct Iranian military drone, missile, or air strike impacting Israeli ground territory on the listed date qualifies.
- Proxy attacks do not count.
- Intercepted missiles/drones do not count unless they impact Israeli ground territory.
- The date must be confirmed by credible reporting within a limited window or the market resolves No.
- Current market price is 0.9505, or roughly 95.1% implied probability.
- Relevant reusable drivers in the vault are conflicts, escalation, deterrence, diplomacy, and sanctions.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The market prompt defines a narrow qualifying event and excludes a broad set of military actions that might otherwise sound escalation-like.
- The vault’s conflict/escalation/deterrence guidance emphasizes that markets often overread tension/rhetoric and under-separate broad confrontation from specific operational escalation.

## What is uncertain

- No single calendar-bound catalyst is presently identified that would make a strike on March 27, 2026 specifically highly likely.
- There may be future catalysts not yet visible, but without a concrete trigger they should not be priced near certainty for an exact date.
- The market may be reflecting either stale crisis salience, misunderstanding of the resolution criteria, or a pure data/price anomaly.

## Why this source may matter

This note defines the key catalyst problem: the market is not asking whether Iran and Israel remain adversaries, but whether a very specific kind of direct strike happens on one exact day. That sharply raises the burden of proof for any Yes-leaning thesis.

## Possible impact on the question

Unless a concrete date-linked trigger emerges — for example a known deadline, planned operation, announced retaliation window, or sharply escalating crisis immediately preceding March 27, 2026 — a price near 95% looks too high. The default catalyst view should be that broad conflict risk does not translate into near-certainty for an exact-date direct strike.

## Reliability notes

The market specification is authoritative for resolution. The vault materials are internal causal guidance rather than external evidence, but they are highly relevant for avoiding common reasoning errors in conflict markets.
