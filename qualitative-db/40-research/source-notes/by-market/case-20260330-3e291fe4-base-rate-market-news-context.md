# Summary

Late-March news context broadly supports continued upward pressure rather than an abrupt unwind. Reuters search snippets reported on March 19 that U.S. pump prices had jumped more than 30% since the Middle East war began and were headed toward $4 per gallon, while Reuters snippets on March 27 said brokerages had raised oil-price forecasts as the Iran conflict drove oil prices sharply higher this month.

## Key facts extracted

- Reuters snippet dated 2026-03-19: U.S. pump prices had jumped more than **30% this month**, moving toward **$4 a gallon**.
- Reuters snippet dated 2026-03-27: major brokerages had **raised 2026 oil price forecasts** as the Iran conflict drove oil prices sharply higher that month.
- AAA's March 26 public note also tied the gasoline spike to **high crude prices** and rising spring-break demand.
