# Summary
AAA said on March 26 that the national average had risen to $3.98 after jumping one dollar in a month and explicitly warned that the average "could reach $4/gallon in the coming days" for the first time since August 2022.

## Key facts extracted
- AAA described an extremely rapid rise from $2.98 on Feb. 26 to $3.98 on Mar. 26.
- AAA said crude oil prices remained high as the Iran conflict continued.
- AAA explicitly said the national average could reach $4 in the coming days.
- AAA also highlighted rising gasoline demand during spring break season.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- “The national average for a gallon of regular gasoline is up 10 cents from last week and one dollar since last month.”
- “The line graph below shows the steep rise from $2.98 on February 26 to $3.98 today.”
- “The national average could reach $4/gallon in the coming days for the first time since August 2022.”

## What is uncertain
- Whether the path to $4 continues smoothly into March 31 rather than stalling just below the threshold.
- Whether crude/oil retreat could interrupt the final move.
- Whether AAA's “could” is being overinterpreted by traders as near-certainty.

## Why this source may matter
It is strong pro-YES evidence because it comes from AAA itself and directly addresses the specific threshold in question.

## Possible impact on the question
Pushes probability upward meaningfully. But the risk-manager caution is that “could reach $4 in the coming days” is not the same as “will definitely print $4.00+ in the exact AAA resolution field by the deadline.”

## Reliability notes
High relevance because AAA is both a key information source and the contract's resolution source. Still, this is guidance/commentary, not the actual resolving print.
