# Summary

The market resolves only on a narrow class of events: a directly Iranian drone, missile, or air strike that impacts Bahraini territory or an official Bahraini embassy/consulate during March 2026. Proxy actions, interceptions, and ambiguous attribution do not count. Bahrain is strategically relevant in Gulf security and hosts major US military presence, but background material does not itself show a current consensus expectation of an imminent direct Iranian strike on Bahrain.

## Key facts extracted

- The market requires direct Iranian military action, not proxy action.
- Intercepted drones or missiles are insufficient for a Yes resolution.
- Bahrain is strategically important in Gulf security architecture and closely tied to Saudi and US security alignment.
- Iran-Bahrain relations are historically strained, including over Bahrain’s hosting of US Fifth Fleet assets and broader sectarian/regional rivalry.
- Bahrain’s strategic salience could make it a symbolic target in an extreme escalation scenario, but also raises deterrence costs because any direct Iranian strike would likely broaden regional conflict.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The Polymarket event page states that only military actions by Iranian forces explicitly claimed by Iran or confirmed to originate from Iranian territory count, and that proxy attacks do not count.
- The Bahrain entity note describes Bahrain as a Gulf state with high strategic relevance because of alliance posture and regional security alignment.
- The Iran entity note emphasizes Iran’s centrality to regional conflict and proxy dynamics.
- Background relations material indicates long-running strained Iran-Bahrain relations and Iranian criticism of Bahrain for hosting US Fifth Fleet facilities.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the word “again” in the market title reflects a prior qualifying incident that traders are anchoring to.
- Whether there is fresh, case-specific reporting or order-flow information behind the extreme 94.5% price that is not easily visible via the currently available tools.

This source set is enough to frame the main mechanism: Bahrain is a strategically meaningful but escalation-sensitive target, and the contract definition is narrow enough that many Iran-adjacent hostile acts would still resolve No.
