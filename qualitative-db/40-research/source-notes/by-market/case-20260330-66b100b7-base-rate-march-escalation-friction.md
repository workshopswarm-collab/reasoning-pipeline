## Why this source may matter

This source cluster is the main reason not to over-extrapolate from February progress headlines. The pathway exists, but it is not linear. Structural friction, public denial, coercive leverage, and mediator dependence all lower the probability that an in-person qualifying meeting is actually completed and acknowledged by the deadline.

## Possible impact on the question

For a base-rate view, this is bearish versus the market. Rare diplomatic meetings between adversaries often fail because multiple constraints must all line up: active channel, acceptable venue, empowered delegates, domestic political cover, and enough de-escalation to make public acknowledgment possible. March reporting suggests those constraints remain binding.
