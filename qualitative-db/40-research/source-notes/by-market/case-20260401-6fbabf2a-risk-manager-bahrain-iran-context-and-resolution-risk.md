# Summary

The resolution criteria are unusually strict and create a gap between broad regional escalation risk and this market's specific yes-condition. Bahrain is a strategically plausible Iranian pressure target because of hosting the U.S. Fifth Fleet and its Gulf alignment, but an Iran-on-Bahrain qualifying strike still appears to require a relatively extreme escalation choice compared with other regional targets.

## Key facts extracted

- The market resolves Yes only for a drone, missile, or air strike by Iranian military forces that impacts Bahrain's territory or official diplomatic premises.
- Proxy attacks do not count.
- Intercepted missiles/drones do not count.
- Attribution must be explicit Iranian claim or credible confirmation of origin from Iranian territory.
- Bahrain hosts the U.S. Fifth Fleet and is deeply embedded in Gulf/U.S. security architecture.
- Bahrain-Iran relations are historically strained, making Bahrain a symbolically available target in a Gulf escalation scenario.
- CFR's March 26 conflict tracker describes Iran retaliating against U.S. facilities, Israel, and Gulf energy/civilian infrastructure in a broader regional war context, which keeps Bahrain inside the broad risk envelope even if not specifically identified.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Polymarket description states the exact strike, attribution, interception, and reporting requirements.
- Background references state Bahrain's hosting of the U.S. Fifth Fleet and longstanding tension with Iran.
- CFR states the conflict had broadened to Gulf infrastructure and U.S.-linked regional targets by late March.

## What is uncertain

- Whether there was already one earlier qualifying Iran strike on Bahrain this month; the market title says "again," but current run evidence here does not independently verify the prior incident.
- Whether Bahrain specifically, rather than other Gulf or Israeli/U.S. targets, is in Iran's active target set.
- Whether any future Iranian action would be direct and attributable enough to satisfy resolution, rather than proxied, intercepted, ambiguous, or falling short of impact.

## Why this source may matter

This source bundle defines the main risk-manager issue: the market can look almost certain only if one assumes both that Bahrain is a serious direct target and that any further Iranian action would satisfy the market's narrow resolution criteria.

## Possible impact on the question

It pushes toward a high probability relative to ordinary geopolitics because the broader war environment is real, but it pushes against an ultra-high probability because the yes condition is narrower than "regional danger" and requires a specific, attributable, successfully impacting Iranian strike on Bahrain.

## Reliability notes

- Polymarket resolution text is authoritative for what counts.
- CFR is a credible secondary framing source for regional conflict context, but not a dispositive event feed for Bahrain-specific targeting.
- Wikipedia/background material is useful for structural context only and should not be overweighted for live operational claims.
