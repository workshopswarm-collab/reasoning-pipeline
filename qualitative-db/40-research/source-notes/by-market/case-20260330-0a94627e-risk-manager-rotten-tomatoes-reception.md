# Summary
Rotten Tomatoes presents *Project Hail Mary* as a well-received theatrical release, with a strongly positive critics consensus and enthusiastic audience blurbs. This supports the case for good week-two retention, but it is an indirect input rather than direct proof of weekend gross.

## Key facts extracted
- Critics consensus calls the film “**a visually dazzling space odyssey**” and “**a near-miraculous fusion of smarts and heart**.”
- Audience summary is also strongly positive.
- The page identifies the distributor as **Amazon MGM Studios** and lists a wide theatrical release date of **Mar. 20, 2026**.
- Runtime is **2h 36m**, which is long for a mainstream sci-fi release and can somewhat constrain show-count throughput.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- The page shows positive review excerpts from multiple critics and strong audience commentary.
- The film is framed as a notable theatrical release rather than a mixed-reception curiosity.

## What is uncertain
- The fetched page did not reliably expose clean numeric critic/audience percentages in the extracted text.
- Aggregated reception does not directly quantify second-weekend conversion.
- Positive reception can coexist with threshold risk if the line is set near realized outcomes.

## Why this source may matter
Reception strength is one of the main reasons a large opener can avoid a steep second-weekend collapse. It helps explain why a drop to the low-30s was plausible.

## Possible impact on the question
Moderately supportive of YES because positive word of mouth and review framing improve holdover odds. The main risk-manager caveat is that even strong reception does not justify ignoring a **narrow contract buffer** when the line is $54M and the eventual final is only about $0.54M above it.

## Reliability notes
Rotten Tomatoes is useful for public reception aggregation and critical-consensus framing, but it is not a box-office measurement source. Best treated as indirect evidence about week-two hold quality rather than as a resolving source.
