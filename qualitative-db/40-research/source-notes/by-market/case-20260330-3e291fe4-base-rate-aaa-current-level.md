# Summary

The direct resolution source is sitting essentially on the threshold already. AAA shows the U.S. national average regular price at **$3.990 on 2026-03-30**, up from **$3.980 yesterday**, and AAA said on March 26 that the national average could reach **$4/gallon in the coming days**.

## Key facts extracted

- AAA Fuel Prices page for 2026-03-30 lists:
  - Current Avg. regular: **$3.990**
  - Yesterday Avg. regular: **$3.980**
  - Week Ago Avg. regular: **$3.956**
  - Month Ago Avg. regular: **$2.982**
- AAA Newsroom on 2026-03-26 stated:
  - national average regular gasoline was **$3.98** that day
  - regular gasoline was up **10 cents from last week** and **$1.00 since last month**
  - crude oil prices remained high amid the Iran conflict
  - the national average **could reach $4/gallon in the coming days**
  - spring-break gasoline demand was also rising

## Evidence directly stated by source

The source directly tells us that the market does **not** need a large move. It needs only a **one-cent** increase from the 2026-03-30 AAA national average to reach the $4.00 bracket on the resolution source.

## What is uncertain

- Whether AAA's 2026-03-31 update will print **$4.000+** or stall at **$3.99x**.
- Whether intraday/local changes not yet incorporated into AAA will be enough to push the national average over the line by the next posted reading.
- Exact timing of the final AAA update relevant for resolution.

## Why this source may matter

This is the most important source in the case because AAA is the resolution source. From a base-rate standpoint, once the tracked value is one cent below the target and still rising, the event class changes from "large price shock needed" to "small continuation move needed."

## Possible impact on the question

This pushes the probability materially upward versus any generic prior about gasoline reaching $4. The remaining task is tiny relative to the recent trend: one cent versus ten cents in the prior week and roughly one dollar in the prior month.
