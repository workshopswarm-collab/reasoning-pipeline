# Summary
Euronews reports Oman confirmed another round of U.S.-Iran nuclear talks in Geneva on Feb. 23, 2026. The piece also says Araghchi expected to meet Steve Witkoff in Geneva and still saw “a good chance” for diplomacy, but simultaneously stresses U.S. military buildup, zero-enrichment disagreement, and war preparation language.

## Key facts extracted
- Oman publicly confirmed a next round of U.S.-Iran talks in Geneva.
- Iran’s foreign minister said he expected to meet U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff in Geneva.
- Araghchi said a diplomatic solution still had a “good chance.”
- Iran insisted enrichment rights remained non-negotiable while the U.S. position was that Iran could not enrich uranium.
- The source emphasized parallel military buildup and both sides preparing for war if talks failed.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- “The United States and Iran will hold their next round of nuclear talks Thursday in Geneva, facilitator Oman confirmed...”
- “Araghchi told CBS ... that he expected to meet US envoy Steve Witkoff in Geneva on Thursday, and said a ‘good chance’ remained for a diplomatic solution...”
- “The US has said Iran cannot have nuclear weapons ... and that it cannot enrich uranium.”
- “Araghchi, however, told CBS that Iran has the right to enrich uranium.”
- “Both Iran and the US have signalled they are prepared for war if the talks ... fail.”

## What is uncertain
- Whether the expected Witkoff-Araghchi meeting occurred in the exact in-person, acknowledged form required by market rules.
- Whether any such meeting has already happened and been reported clearly enough for resolution standards.
- Whether military escalation after late February made another qualifying meeting more or less likely by June 30.

## Why this source may matter
This is one of the strongest affirmative pieces for the market because it suggests named principals expected to meet and that the mediator publicly confirmed another round. But it also contains the main fragility: the substantive gap remained wide and war risk was rising at the same time.

## Possible impact on the question
Pushes probability upward because diplomatic machinery and named interlocutors were active. Pushes against overconfidence because the path to a qualifying meeting still depended on unresolved enrichment disputes and avoiding military derailment.
