# Summary

DeFi is one of crypto's main application layers because it turns blockchains into lending, trading, and collateral systems without traditional intermediaries.

## Key facts extracted

- DeFi uses smart contracts to recreate or extend financial functions.
- It is heavily concentrated in lending, exchanges, collateral, and stablecoin-linked activity.
- Smart-contract and governance risk are central, not secondary.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a market question turns on whether a protocol fits into a real DeFi usage pattern or just markets itself that way.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains the basic logic of decentralized finance, including onchain lending, trading, and smart-contract-based financial services.
- It supports orientation-level claims about what DeFi encompasses.

## What is uncertain

- It does not by itself show which protocols are gaining durable traction or which activity is high quality.
