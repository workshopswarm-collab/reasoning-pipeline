# Summary

Crypto exchanges are core market-structure institutions because they control fiat access, custody assumptions, trading liquidity, and increasingly regulatory exposure.

## Key facts extracted

- Exchanges connect crypto assets to fiat and other digital assets.
- Market structure differs between custodial, brokerage-style, and decentralized exchange models.
- Regulation, withdrawals, custody, and liquidity structure matter materially.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when the question depends on understanding the role of centralized exchange plumbing rather than just token prices.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly describes what crypto exchanges are, how custodial trading venues work, and how exchange models differ.
- It supports orientation-level claims about custody, fiat access, and trading structure.
