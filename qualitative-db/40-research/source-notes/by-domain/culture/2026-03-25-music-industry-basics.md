# Summary

The music industry is an attention-and-distribution system shaped by streaming, touring, ownership, fan intensity, and release cadence.

## Key facts extracted

- Recorded music, publishing, touring, and branding interact.
- Streaming and platform distribution changed revenue and attention structure.
- Artist-level fandom can be a major economic and narrative driver.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a culture market depends on whether artist attention, release timing, or platform distribution is the key driver.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains the basic structure of the music industry, including recording, distribution, labels, and consumption channels.
- It supports orientation-level claims about how music becomes a business and an attention market.

## What is uncertain

- It does not reveal which current artists, platforms, or release strategies have the strongest live edge.
- Revenue structure and cultural relevance can diverge.

## Why this source may matter

Useful as baseline framing because music-related culture markets often depend on distribution and release mechanics as much as artistic popularity.

## Reliability notes

Good conceptual baseline; later pair with chart/streaming/touring metrics.
