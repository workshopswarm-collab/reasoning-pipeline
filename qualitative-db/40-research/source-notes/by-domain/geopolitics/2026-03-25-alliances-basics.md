# Summary

Alliances are one of the main geopolitical coordination mechanisms because they aggregate deterrence, burden sharing, and signaling.

## Key facts extracted

- NATO is a military alliance with collective-defense structure.
- Alliance credibility depends on both formal commitments and member willingness.
- Alliances are strategic signaling systems as much as military arrangements.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a conflict or deterrence market depends on whether alliance ties will be activated in practice rather than just in theory.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains what alliances are, why states form them, and the general logic of collective security or strategic cooperation.
- It supports baseline claims about alliance behavior and obligations.

## What is uncertain

- It does not determine how strongly any specific alliance member will act in a live crisis.
- Formal commitments and actual political willingness often diverge.

## Why this source may matter

Useful as a reminder that alliance questions are about both legal structure and political credibility, not just membership lists.

Useful baseline framing; later pair with alliance-specific country and burden-sharing notes.
