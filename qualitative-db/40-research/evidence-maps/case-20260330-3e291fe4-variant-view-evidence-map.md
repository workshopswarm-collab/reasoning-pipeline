# Summary

The strongest variant case is that the market is paying too much for **narrative proximity** to $4.00 and not enough attention to the exact resolution mechanics. AAA is at **$3.990** today, which is tantalizingly close but still not a qualifying print. The latest pace of increase also appears to be slowing after the big March shock already happened.

## Question being evaluated

Is 0.775 too high for AAA national regular gasoline to print **$4.000 or above** by March 31, 2026?

## Current lean

Lean **below market**.

## Prior / starting view

A 0.775 market price with gas prices surging from Middle East conflict initially suggests the crowd may simply be right: one more daily move and the threshold hits. The variant task is to ask whether the market is overconfident because the remaining hurdle is smaller in magnitude but harsher in contract form than headline framing implies.

  - Why it matters: the most relevant industry source viewed the threshold as plausible near-term.
  - Directness: direct.
  - Weight: high.

  - Why it matters: crude/oil disruption and spring demand still point upward directionally.
  - Directness: indirect to exact threshold timing.
  - Weight: medium-high.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- The market may be right that one last cent is likely, but still too confident about it.
- Earlier March velocity was enormous, yet the most recent daily and weekly changes are much milder.
- The same facts support both a bullish “one more push gets there” story and a bearish “the move is tiring just below the line” story.

## Conflict between inputs

The disagreement is mainly **timing-based and threshold-based** rather than directional.

- Market-style thesis: price momentum plus war/seasonality likely finishes the job.
- Variant thesis: the market is overpaying for closeness and underweighting that the exact printed threshold still has not occurred.
- What would resolve it: the March 31 AAA print.

## Key assumptions

- Retail pass-through is slowing enough that the final penny is not better than even-money.
- Traders are partially anchoring on oil/geopolitical headlines rather than the exact AAA measurement rule.

## Key uncertainties

- exact March 31 AAA print timing
- whether wholesale and retail pass-through still have enough residual momentum overnight
- whether any late-day reporting artifacts can nudge the national average over 4.000

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- March 31 AAA posts exactly $4.000 or higher
- late-March local/state averages continue broad one- to two-cent gains into the final print
- any AAA update or commentary implying national pass-through remains strong despite recent slowing

## What would increase confidence

- evidence that late-March daily national moves had flattened below +1 cent per day
- confirmation that today’s $3.990 reflected a slowing or exhausted transmission from earlier oil shock
- additional market evidence that traders are treating $3.99 as de facto resolved-Yes rather than a still-open hurdle

## Net update logic

The main update is from a naive "gas is almost $4, so Yes is probably right" frame to a more mechanical "this is a one-day exact-threshold contract and the threshold still has not printed" frame. What mattered most was the direct AAA number of **3.990** plus reporting that the pace has slowed after the big March jump already occurred.
