## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- Russian strategic intent toward the sector is real, but intent is easier to observe than successful endpoint conversion.
- News headlines about offensives may reflect broad operational pressure without implying rapid municipality capture.
- Mixed media coverage can mean the front is fluid, but fluidity cuts against near-certainty unless the map already shows near-complete control.

## Conflict between inputs

This is mainly a **weighting-based conflict**:
- the bullish case weights Russian offensive intent, initiative, and prior autumn/winter gains
- the bearish base-rate case weights attritional friction, recent Ukrainian counterattacks, and the strict map endpoint

What would resolve it:
- up-to-date map confirmation showing how much of Huliaipole municipality remains outside qualifying Russian control
- repeated corroborated reports of rapid local Russian advances over multiple consecutive updates

## Key assumptions

The key assumption is that the market is underweighting endpoint friction and over-extrapolating from strategic pressure. See the linked assumption note.
