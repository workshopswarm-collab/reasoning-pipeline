# Summary
Diplomacy is clearly active enough that YES cannot be dismissed, but the current market price looks too confident because it compresses multiple fragile steps into one smooth path.

## Question being evaluated
Will there be a deliberate, publicly acknowledged, in-person diplomatic meeting between authorized U.S. and Iranian representatives by June 30, 2026 under this market’s rules?

## Current lean
Lean YES, but at meaningfully lower confidence than the market.

## Prior / starting view
Starting from the 0.76 market price, the implied view is that active mediation and recent talks make a qualifying meeting more likely than not by a wide margin.

## Evidence supporting the claim
- Oman publicly confirmed another round of U.S.-Iran nuclear talks in Geneva. High weight; direct signal that mediation is real and public.
- Araghchi reportedly expected to meet U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff in Geneva. High weight if accurate because named principals imply possible qualifying format.
- Pakistan publicly said it would soon host U.S.-Iran talks. Medium weight; shows diplomatic channel remains open even after March deterioration.
- Historical precedent exists for secret backchannels and even face-to-face Oman talks in 2009/2012. Medium weight; proves this is operationally possible.

## Evidence against the claim
- The central substantive issue appears unresolved: Iran says zero enrichment is a non-starter while the U.S. publicly insists Iran cannot enrich. High weight; talks can exist without producing a qualifying meeting.
- Late-March reporting shows conflicting public narratives: Trump cited progress while Iran rejected the idea that meaningful talks were happening. High weight; this is a classic warning sign that “diplomacy” may be more signaling than execution.
- The region is simultaneously under military pressure/buildup. High weight; escalation risk can cancel, postpone, or politically poison an in-person meeting.
- Market rules are stricter than generic diplomacy chatter: indirect or planned talks are not enough unless the meeting is in-person and publicly acknowledged. High weight; execution risk is being underpriced.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence
- “Talks” language can refer to indirect, mediated, or anticipated contacts rather than a resolution-qualifying bilateral encounter.
- Hosting offers from Pakistan/Oman are bullish for process but not dispositive for a completed meeting.
- Historical precedent cuts both ways: there have been face-to-face contacts before, but also long periods of breakdown after active mediation.

## Conflict between inputs
- The main conflict is interpretive and timing-based.
- Bullish inputs say mediation is alive and named officials may meet.
- Bearish inputs say the substantive gap and escalation environment make a clean, public, in-person meeting much less automatic than the price suggests.
- Best resolving evidence would be a dated official announcement naming participants, venue, and meeting format, followed by public acknowledgement afterward.

## Key assumptions
- Mediator-announced talks become actual in-person meetings.
- Hard substantive disputes do not block basic diplomatic staging.
- Escalation does not overtake diplomatic scheduling.
- Public acknowledgement occurs clearly enough for market resolution.

## Key uncertainties
- Whether any late-February/late-March meetings already met the market standard.
- Whether future contacts will be direct, mediated in-person, or still effectively indirect.
- Whether June 30 is enough time given conflict volatility.

## Disconfirming signals to watch
- Another breakdown story focused on enrichment deadlock.
- Fresh threats or military action that make face-to-face diplomacy politically costly.
- Continued vague “progress” rhetoric without a concrete meeting readout.
- Reports that mediation remains only shuttle-style or remote.

## What would increase confidence
- Official U.S., Iranian, Omani, or Pakistani confirmation of a scheduled in-person meeting.
- Credible reporting that named authorized representatives will attend in the same room.
- Post-meeting acknowledgement from either government.

## Net update logic
The evidence keeps YES above 50 because real diplomatic infrastructure exists and multiple mediators are visibly active. But it moves me down from the market because the price seems to treat mediation intent, hosting offers, and progress claims as near-equivalent to a completed, public, in-person meeting. They are not equivalent under this market’s wording.
