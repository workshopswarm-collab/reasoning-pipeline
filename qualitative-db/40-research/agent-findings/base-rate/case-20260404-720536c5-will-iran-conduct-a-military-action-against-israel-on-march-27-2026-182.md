# Claim

I disagree with the market. The market-implied probability is about **95.1%**, while my base-rate estimate for a qualifying **Yes** is **~70%**.

That is still a high probability, because the current context appears to be active Iran-related regional war rather than peacetime. But 95% is too close to certainty for an event defined this narrowly: a direct Iranian military strike, from Iranian territory, on the exact date, with projectiles that actually impact Israeli ground territory, not just interceptions or proxy action.

## Implication for the question

The outside-view says traders are probably collapsing three different things into one bucket:

1. broad belief that Iran and Israel are already in major conflict,
2. broad belief that Iran will keep attacking in some fashion,
3. the much narrower contract requirement that a direct-from-Iran strike successfully impact Israeli territory on that exact date.

Those are not the same event. In a war context, 70% can be defensible. But 95% implies almost no meaningful pathway to miss, and there are several:

- Iran attacks indirectly via proxies instead of directly.
- Iran launches but projectiles are intercepted, which does **not** count.
- Iran targets US assets, Gulf shipping, or regional infrastructure instead of Israel proper that day.
- Iran and Israel remain in conflict but the direct-strike tempo pauses or shifts on the exact date.
- reporting ambiguity prevents a clean confirmation within the market’s time rules.

## Supporting evidence

- The market’s own resolution criteria are extremely restrictive: only direct Iranian military aerial munitions from Iranian territory that impact Israeli ground territory count; interceptions and proxy attacks do not.
- The relevant reference class is sparse. The best-known modern overt direct Iranian strike on Israel is the April 2024 episode, which underscores rarity relative to the volume of Iran-Israel tension rhetoric over time.
- Current April 2026 open reporting indicates an active Iran-related war environment, which justifies moving far above a normal peacetime prior.
- Even in active war, not every day produces the exact combination required by the contract. Daily event contracts are structurally fragile because timing, targeting, interception, and attribution all matter.

## Counterpoints

- The strongest pro-market argument is simple: if Iran and Israel are already in open high-tempo war, the base rate should be anchored to ongoing campaign behavior, not to the long-run prewar history.
- If direct Iranian launches at Israeli territory have become routine by late March 2026, then a 95% price could be closer to fair than my outside-view estimate.
- I am intentionally discounting salience and momentum; if the current operational pattern is effectively daily direct impact, this estimate is too low.

## Key assumptions

- The contract’s narrow wording matters more than the broad war narrative.
- Even under severe escalation, there remains substantial daily variance in whether Iran itself, from its own territory, lands qualifying impacts in Israel.
- Deterrence, interception, operational choice, and target substitution still create real miss paths on any single day.

## Why this is decision-relevant

The key decision question is not “are Iran and Israel in conflict?” It is whether the market has overcompressed uncertainty in a narrowly specified daily event. My view is yes.

A 95.1% market price means the No side is priced as almost negligible. For that to be right, one must believe there is almost no realistic chance of any of the exclusion pathways above. The base-rate lens says that is too aggressive unless there is direct evidence of an already-established pattern of qualifying impacts on most days.

## What would falsify this interpretation

I would move materially upward toward the market if I found credible evidence that by late March 2026:

- direct Iranian launches from Iranian territory into Israel proper were already occurring routinely,
- at least several recent days had qualifying impacts rather than mostly interceptions,
- official or highly credible reporting indicated an announced or clearly ongoing Iranian direct-strike cycle targeting Israel on a near-daily cadence.

I would move downward if evidence showed the conflict was being expressed mainly through proxies, US-Iran exchanges, maritime disruption, or intercepted launches rather than successful direct impacts in Israel.
