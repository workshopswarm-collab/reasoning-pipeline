# Claim
My risk-manager view is **YES 62%**, versus the market-implied **76%**. I still lean YES because active mediation, named envoys, and recent Pakistan/Oman hosting signals imply real diplomatic machinery. But I **disagree with the market by about 14 points** because it appears to underprice execution risk, format risk, and escalation risk.

## Implication for the question
The market question is not “are there talks?” It is whether there will be a **deliberate, in-person, publicly acknowledged diplomatic meeting** between authorized U.S. and Iranian representatives by June 30. That is a narrower and more failure-prone path than the current price seems to assume.

## Supporting evidence
- PBS/AP and Euronews both indicate active diplomatic channels and late-February Geneva talks.
- Euronews says Oman confirmed a Geneva round and Araghchi expected to meet Steve Witkoff, which is strong bullish evidence if it maps to an actual in-person encounter.
- AP-syndicated late-March reporting says Pakistan will soon host talks, suggesting diplomacy is still alive even after deterioration.
- Historical precedent exists for Oman-facilitated backchannels and even face-to-face U.S.-Iran talks.

## Counterpoints
- The core substantive dispute still looks severe: U.S. zero-enrichment posture versus Iran’s insistence that enrichment rights are non-negotiable.
- Late-March public narratives conflict sharply: Trump cites progress, while Iran rejects that meaningful talks are even taking place.
- Military buildup/escalation risk can derail scheduling or make public bilateral diplomacy politically toxic.
- Resolution wording is strict: indirect contacts, phone calls, vague mediation, or planned-but-unheld talks do not count.

## Key assumptions
- Mediator activity converts into an actual in-person meeting rather than indefinite indirect diplomacy.
- Authorized representatives will be physically present in a way that clearly qualifies under the market rules.
- Either government or a consensus of credible media will publicly acknowledge the meeting.
- Escalation does not overwhelm logistics and politics before June 30.

## Why this is decision-relevant
This looks like a market that may be directionally right but too smooth about the path. The key underpriced risk is **confidence compression**: several contingent steps are being priced almost as if they were already completed.

## What would falsify this interpretation
- A clearly reported official in-person meeting between named authorized U.S. and Iranian representatives would force me up toward or above market.
- Conversely, another visible breakdown over enrichment, or more evidence that contacts remain only indirect/mediated without a same-room encounter, would push me further below market.

## Recommended follow-up
- Verify whether any late-February Geneva contact already satisfied the exact resolution criteria.
- Track official Omani, Pakistani, U.S., and Iranian statements for explicit same-room meeting confirmation.
- Watch for whether the market is mistakenly upgrading from “host talks / mediator optimism” to “resolution-compliant meeting likely imminent.”
