# Assumption

A market price near 95% appears to assume not just severe regional tension, but that a direct Iranian attack satisfying the contract’s exact attribution, impact, and timing rules either already happened or was overwhelmingly likely to happen on that specific date.

## Why this assumption matters

Without that stronger assumption, a 95% probability is hard to defend. General escalation risk is not equivalent to a qualifying direct strike on the exact calendar day.

## What this assumption supports

- A near-certain Yes view.
- Confidence that proxy activity, threats, interceptions, or ambiguous attribution will not be the dominant path.
- Confidence that date/time and impact will be clearly documented by credible reporting.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Middle East conflict environment in late March 2026 appears heated.
- Prior Iran-Israel cycles create a plausible direct-strike tail.
- Traders may possess additional reporting not available in this research pass.

## What would falsify it

- Credible reporting that late-March action remained proxy-centered rather than directly Iranian.
- Evidence of launches/interceptions without impact on Israeli ground territory.
- Reporting ambiguity on exact timing or launch origin.
- Absence of major credible-source confirmation despite the market’s extreme price.

## Early warning signs

- Headline flow focused on Hezbollah or other proxies rather than Iranian forces.
- Reports emphasizing interceptions only.
- Reporting framed around threats, alerts, or preparations rather than confirmed impacts.
- Market participants treating broad war risk as if it were the same as the contract.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move materially lower than the market, because the main hidden leap from elevated regional conflict to a contract-valid direct Iranian strike would be broken.
