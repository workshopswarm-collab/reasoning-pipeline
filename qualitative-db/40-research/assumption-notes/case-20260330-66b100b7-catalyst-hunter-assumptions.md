# Assumption

The market's current price only makes sense if the active indirect mediation channel converts into a publicly acknowledged in-person qualifying meeting on a relatively short timetable rather than remaining stuck at the message-passing stage.

## Why this assumption matters

The difference between indirect message relay and a market-qualifying diplomatic meeting is the whole contract. A lot of apparent bullish evidence is really evidence for contact, not yet evidence for resolution-grade meeting logistics.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability.
- A view that Pakistan's announcement is a real repricing catalyst rather than just geopolitical noise.
- Confidence that near-term headlines will move the market further toward Yes.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Pakistan publicly said it would host talks in the coming days.
- Trump said negotiations are ongoing and progressing.
- AP and ABC both describe real mediator activity and active diplomatic signaling.

## What would falsify it

- Public clarification that contacts are only indirect and no in-person meeting is planned.
- Continued mediator chatter without venue, participant, or format confirmation.
- Military escalation that hardens positions and interrupts the diplomatic path.
- Confirmation that the operative market deadline is much sooner than the title suggests, leaving no realistic window to arrange a qualifying meeting.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated headlines about proposals and responses but no meeting logistics.
- Iranian officials publicly rejecting the mediation venue or format.
- U.S. rhetoric emphasizing coercion without any process detail.
- Contradictions between market metadata and official market wording remaining unresolved.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The catalyst case weakens sharply. The market could remain elevated because diplomacy exists in principle, but the repricing path toward confirmed Yes would flatten or reverse if the channel stays indirect.
