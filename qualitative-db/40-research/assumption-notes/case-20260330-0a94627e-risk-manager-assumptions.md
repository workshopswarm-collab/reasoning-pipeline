# Assumption
The bullish market view implicitly assumed that strong holdover demand would survive final counting and revisions with **more than a minimal buffer** above the $54M line.

## Why this assumption matters
A market price near 79% is not merely saying YES is more likely than NO; it is also saying confidence is fairly high. That confidence only makes sense if the expected finishing margin over $54M is not razor-thin.

## What this assumption supports
- A high-confidence YES view at market close.
- The interpretation that strong reception and a #1 hold were enough to make threshold risk modest rather than material.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- The film opened very strongly at $80.5M.
- Reception appears strong enough to support a healthy second-weekend hold.
- The final realized drop was only -32%, which is objectively good for a large opener.

## What would falsify it
- Final reported second weekend landing only marginally above the line.
- Evidence that a normal Sunday softness or small downward revision could easily have moved the number below $54M.

## Early warning signs
- Any estimate coming in near $54M to $55M rather than comfortably above.
- Lack of cushion versus the strike despite otherwise bullish narrative signals.
- Over-reliance on qualitative strength (#1 rank, good reviews, good hold) without checking exact contract geometry.

## What changes if this assumption fails
If the margin above the line is thin, the correct response is not necessarily to flip bearish; it is to lower confidence materially. The risk-manager view becomes: YES remains favored, but the market is probably **overpricing certainty**.
