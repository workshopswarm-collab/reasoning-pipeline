# Assumption

The market's 0.76 price only makes sense if traders are assuming an authorized, publicly reportable mediated diplomatic encounter is more achievable than a clean direct bilateral summit and that the true practical deadline leaves enough runway for such an encounter.

## Why this assumption matters

Without that assumption, the market would need unusually strong evidence of a scheduled direct meeting to justify 76%. With it, the bar drops because mediated and indirect formats become valid resolution paths.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability materially above 50%.
- A view that the market is not merely hallucinating diplomacy but pricing broad resolution wording and active mediation.
- A conclusion that the market could be directionally right even if confidence is somewhat high.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market language explicitly allows meetings through designated mediators or interlocutors.
- Pakistan publicly says it is prepared to host and facilitate U.S.-Iran talks.
- Trump publicly says negotiations are ongoing directly and indirectly.

## What would falsify it

- Confirmation that only a direct bilateral sit-down would count in practice.
- Confirmation that the March 30 timestamp is the actual binding deadline and no meeting is scheduled.
- Public statements from either side rejecting an in-person mediated encounter.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated references to indirect contact without any movement toward in-person engagement.
- Contradictory official messaging about who is authorized to attend.
- Continued wartime escalation crowding out diplomacy.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If this assumption fails, the market-implied probability should move down materially because the viable resolution paths become much narrower.
