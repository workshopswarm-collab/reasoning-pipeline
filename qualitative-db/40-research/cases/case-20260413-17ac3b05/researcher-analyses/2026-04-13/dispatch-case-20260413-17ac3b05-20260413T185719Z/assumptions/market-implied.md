# Assumption
The market's 0.74 price is mainly assuming that the initial NBS Q1 2026 GDP print will land in Beijing's familiar policy-comfort zone around low-to-mid 5% growth, with March weakness or strength unlikely to push the first print outside the 5.0-5.5 bracket.

## Why this assumption matters
If this assumption is wrong, the apparent edge disappears: the market could be underestimating downside from weak demand/property drag or upside from policy-supported front-loading and favorable base effects.

## What this assumption supports
- A roughly market-consistent probability for the 5.0-5.5 bracket.
- The view that this is more an official-print calibration question than a radical macro surprise question.
- The decision to treat settlement mechanics as low ambiguity once the governing release is identified.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- NBS January-February activity data show broad stabilization/improvement across industry, services, retail, investment, and trade.
- The market bracket itself is centered on the range most observers would expect policymakers to prefer and the official first print to plausibly show.
- The contract uses the initial official release, not later revisions, which favors market pricing around the expected first official number rather than around private estimates of "true" growth.

## What would falsify it
- March data or late-breaking macro developments implying a clear slump or a clear upside acceleration.
- Evidence that NBS is likely to print outside the bracket despite January-February resilience.
- Resolution guidance or release mechanics showing a different governing statistic than the initial Q1 press release.

## Early warning signs
- Sharp deterioration in March PMI, trade, retail, property, or labor indicators.
- New evidence of major statistical/reporting irregularity relevant to the initial GDP print.
- Market repricing away from the bracket despite no contract-rule change.

## What changes if this assumption fails
I would cut confidence in the bracket materially and re-evaluate whether the market is over-anchored to a politically comfortable range.
