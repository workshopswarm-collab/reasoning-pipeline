# Assumption

The working view assumes the initial NBS Q1 2026 GDP print will preserve a managed, target-consistent headline growth figure in the 5.0%-5.5% band rather than surprise materially below 5.0% or above 5.5%.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire probability estimate depends on whether official high-frequency strength plus policy-management incentives are enough to keep the first-release headline inside the bracket despite weak property and uneven domestic demand.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below, but near, the market price.
- A view that the most likely miss risk is confidence overstatement rather than strong directional error.
- A recommendation to treat settlement mechanics and release timing as secondary but real tail risks.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- January-February official activity data were firm enough to support a mid-5s style headline.
- China’s published annual growth target and communication incentives generally favor stability around target-consistent prints.
- Property weakness and soft consumption argue against a clearly higher upside breakout.

## What would falsify it

- March activity data or trusted previews showing a clear deterioration relative to January-February.
- Credible reporting that the official release may be delayed, reformulated, or operationally irregular.
- The actual initial NBS Q1 release printing below 5.0% or above 5.5%.

## Early warning signs

- Weak March PMIs, exports, retail, or credit impulse indicators.
- Sharper signs of property drag or labor-market softening.
- Official language turning noticeably more defensive before release.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move materially lower if growth momentum clearly rolls over or if operational/timing risk rises. It should move modestly lower-to-neutral if the evidence suggests greater source ambiguity around what exact figure the contract will use.
