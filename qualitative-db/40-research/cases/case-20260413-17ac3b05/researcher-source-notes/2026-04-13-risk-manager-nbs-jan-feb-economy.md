# Summary

This official NBS activity release gives the strongest direct pre-Q1-close read on how China’s economy was tracking in January-February 2026. It supports a base case of growth staying in a solid expansion range, but the details also show why confidence should not be too high.

## Key facts extracted

- Industrial value added in January-February rose 6.3% y/y.
- Services production rose 5.2% y/y.
- Retail sales rose 2.8% y/y, faster than prior December but still softer than headline industrial momentum.
- Fixed-asset investment rose 1.8% y/y, while real-estate development investment fell 11.1% y/y.
- Officials explicitly acknowledged rising geopolitical risks and continuing domestic problems/challenges despite the upbeat headline.

## Evidence directly stated by source

Directly stated by NBS in the release text.

## What is uncertain

- This is not the Q1 GDP release itself and does not mechanically map one-for-one into reported quarterly GDP growth.
- The tone is official and positive, so interpretation should discount for presentation bias.
- March data are still missing, and March can matter materially for the final quarterly print.

## Why this source may matter

It is the best official high-frequency contextual read available before the market resolves. The mix of strong industrial output plus weak property and only moderate consumption is important for range analysis around the 5.0%-5.5% bracket.

## Possible impact on the question

Net supportive of a result inside the 5.0%-5.5% bracket, because the economy appears firm enough to avoid a low print while still carrying enough drag from property and uneven domestic demand to cap upside. The main risk implication is that the market may be directionally right but somewhat too confident because the evidence is still partial and politically filtered.

## Reliability notes

Primary official source for current macro conditions, but not independent. Strong on direct factual indicators; weaker on interpretation because official Chinese macro releases can embed optimism and because Q1 GDP is not yet published.
