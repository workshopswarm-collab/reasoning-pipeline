# Summary

This official NBS release provides the closest high-quality partial-quarter read on Q1 2026 momentum before the Q1 GDP print.

## Key facts extracted

- Industrial value added in January-February 2026 rose 6.3% y/y.
- Services production rose 5.2% y/y.
- Retail sales rose 2.8% y/y after a weak December comparison.
- Fixed-asset investment rose 1.8% y/y; real-estate investment still fell 11.1% y/y.
- Exports and imports both showed strong growth in the first two months.
- NBS characterization was that the economy had a good start, while also acknowledging external environment pressure, geopolitical risk, and continued domestic difficulties.

## Evidence directly stated by source

Directly states current official activity indicators, including both stronger industrial/trade momentum and continued real-estate weakness.

## What is uncertain

- These are partial-quarter indicators, not GDP itself.
- NBS framing is politically filtered and can sound more upbeat than the underlying private interpretation.
- March data could still shift the quarter-level GDP print enough to move the contract across the bracket boundary.

## Why this source may matter

It is the strongest directly relevant pre-release official snapshot for whether Q1 2026 growth is plausibly around the government's growth target range rather than clearly below it.

## Possible impact on the question

Supports a moderate base-rate lean toward a print near 5%+, but not a lock: property weakness and one remaining month of data leave room for a miss below 5.0 or a print above 5.5.

## Reliability notes

High credibility because it is official and current, but medium interpretive reliability because it is an upstream partial-quarter indicator set rather than the settling GDP release itself.
