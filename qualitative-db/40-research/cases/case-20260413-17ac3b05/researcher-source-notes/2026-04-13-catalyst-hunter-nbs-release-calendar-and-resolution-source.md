# Summary

The governing source of truth for settlement is the NBS of China English-language "Preliminary Accounting Results of GDP" release for Q1 2026, with the market rules additionally clarifying that the initial release controls and later revisions do not.

## Key facts extracted

- The market description specifies settlement according to China's Y/Y GDP growth rate in the Q1 2026 "Preliminary Accounting Results of GDP" release.
- The market description points to the NBS English press-release page as the release location.
- The market description says if the value falls exactly between two brackets, the market resolves to the higher bracket.
- The market description says if no data for the specified quarter is released by the time the next quarter's data is scheduled, the market resolves using the last available quarter.
- The NBS 2026 regular press-release calendar says annual and quarterly national economic performance will be released in January, April, July, and October, and notes release dates are preliminary and subject to adjustment.
- The NBS latest releases page shows the March 2026 flow of data and confirms the English press-release page is active and current.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Quarterly national economic performance is an April release item in 2026.
- The source-of-truth page for the release family is the NBS English Press Release page.
- Initial release timing matters more than later revision cycles.

## What is uncertain

- The exact April day for the Q1 2026 GDP release is specified in the market description as April 17, 2026; the release calendar itself gives the month cadence and warns dates can be adjusted.
- The English page structure shows latest releases but does not itself pre-publish the Q1 GDP value.

## Why this source may matter

This is the settlement mechanics anchor. For this market, catalyst timing is dominated by one official release rather than a stream of soft interpretation.

## Possible impact on the question
