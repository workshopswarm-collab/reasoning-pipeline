# Summary

This official NBS calendar is the strongest direct source for verifying the timing and publication cadence relevant to settlement.

## Key facts extracted

- NBS says annual and quarterly national economic performance will be released in January, April, July, and October.
- Release dates are preliminary and subject to adjustment.
- If the specified quarterly release were delayed, the next quarter's scheduled release window provides the fallback timing reference mentioned in the market contract.
- The national statistical database updates after releases, but only after the primary release itself.

## Evidence directly stated by source

Direct official description of the release cadence for quarterly national economic performance and the caveat that dates may adjust.

## What is uncertain

- The calendar text shown in extraction does not enumerate the exact April day in the captured snippet, though the contract and current market context point to April 17, 2026.
- Because dates are preliminary, an administrative shift remains possible even if unlikely.

## Why this source may matter

It materially reduces ambiguity around whether the market is keyed to a standard recurring NBS quarterly release cycle rather than an ad hoc publication.

## Possible impact on the question

Supports the view that the market is likely to resolve on a routine official quarterly GDP release rather than on a later revision or informal source.

## Reliability notes

High reliability for timing/cadence. Less informative for the expected numerical growth rate itself.
