# Assumption

The market will be driven mainly by whether BTC can hold an existing price cushion above 72,000 through ordinary volatility, rather than by a still-unpriced major scheduled catalyst before noon ET on April 15.

## Why this assumption matters

My leaning toward Yes depends on treating this as a hold-the-line volatility question with limited remaining scheduled macro information value, not as a setup with a large known event still ahead.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate moderately above the market-implied baseline.
- The view that current spot above 72,000 deserves substantial weight.
- The view that catalyst risk is real but not obviously asymmetric against Yes.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was above 74,000 during the run, leaving a cushion of more than 2,000 above the threshold.
- Recent daily data show BTC already absorbed a sharp dip toward 70.7k and then rebounded above 74k.
- BLS schedule shows the March CPI release was already on April 10, so one obvious macro catalyst is no longer ahead of the market.
- Accessible sources did not reveal a comparably large, clearly scheduled event still pending before resolution.

## What would falsify it

- Discovery of a known, high-impact macro release or crypto-specific event set for the morning of April 15 before noon ET.
- A fast deterioration in BTC spot that erases the current cushion and reestablishes trade near or below 72,000.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing 73k decisively before the U.S. session.
- Rising intraday volatility with repeated failures to reclaim higher levels.
- Risk-off macro headlines or exchange-specific disruptions hitting crypto overnight.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a meaningful pending catalyst exists or the cushion collapses, the thesis should move from modest Yes lean to roughly market or outright No lean, because the narrow contract timing would dominate.
