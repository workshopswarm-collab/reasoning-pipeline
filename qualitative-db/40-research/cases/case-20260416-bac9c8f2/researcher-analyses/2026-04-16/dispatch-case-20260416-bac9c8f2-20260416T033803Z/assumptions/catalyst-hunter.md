# Assumption

The best working assumption is that no major macro, policy, or exchange-specific shock arrives before April 17 noon ET, so BTC remains in the recent 73.5k-75.4k trading regime and the current above-threshold spot meaningfully informs the noon-ET close probability.

## Why this assumption matters

This market resolves on one exact 1-minute close, so a near-term catalyst or volatility shock matters more than broad medium-term Bitcoin conviction. The estimate depends heavily on whether the current regime persists for another half day.

## What this assumption supports

- A modest Yes edge rather than a near-certain Yes.
- The view that the market is roughly right but a bit optimistic.
- The claim that timing/catalyst risk, not long-run Bitcoin thesis, is the central uncertainty.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance recent daily and hourly candles show BTC spending multiple sessions clustered around the threshold rather than breaking into a clearly new range.
- Current spot is above 74k, but only by about 1.3%, which is small enough that routine crypto volatility still matters.
- No single authoritative scheduled catalyst was identified in this short run that obviously dominates the next 12 hours, which makes regime persistence a reasonable base case.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp macro headline or risk-off move that pushes BTC decisively below recent support before noon ET.
- Exchange-specific disruption, settlement ambiguity, or Binance price dislocation.
- A strong upside continuation that cleanly re-establishes 75.5k+ and makes the threshold less relevant.

## Early warning signs

- BTC trades back below 74k for sustained periods during the European or US morning session.
- Funding/liquidity stress or sudden cross-asset risk-off conditions emerge.
- Binance-specific operational issues affect the reference market.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a catalyst breaks the recent regime, the current spot snapshot loses much of its predictive value and the estimate should be repriced quickly toward whichever side the new regime favors.
