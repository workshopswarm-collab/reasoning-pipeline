# Assumption

Absent a fresh negative catalyst before Apr 17 noon ET, ETH/USDT is unlikely to fall more than roughly 6.5% from the currently observed Binance spot level to below 2200 at the exact resolution minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The bullish Yes view depends less on long-run Ethereum fundamentals than on the short-term burden of proof for any bearish catalyst. If no meaningful negative catalyst arrives, the existing cushion above 2200 likely holds through the specific one-minute settlement window.

## What this assumption supports

- A high but not absolute probability on Yes.
- A view that the market is directionally right but perhaps a bit complacent at 95% because crypto can gap on macro or exchange-specific shocks.
- A catalyst-centered framing in which the main question is whether any credible near-term shock can produce a sharp drawdown before noon ET.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot ETHUSDT was about 2353-2354 during verification, well above 2200.
- The contract resolves on a single one-minute close, so the market needs a sizable downside move that persists into that exact minute, not just a momentary wick elsewhere.
- No specific scheduled catalyst was identified in the collected evidence that obviously dominates the next ~14 hours.

## What would falsify it

- A macro, regulatory, or exchange-specific shock that drives ETH below 2200 into the Apr 17 12:00 ET close minute.
- Evidence of a known high-impact event before resolution that the current market is underpricing.
- A rapid overnight deterioration in crypto risk sentiment that compresses the existing price cushion.

## Early warning signs

- ETH losing the 2300 area quickly with broad crypto weakness.
- Exchange disruption, liquidation cascade, or major risk-off macro headline before noon ET.
- Persistent basis/flow deterioration suggesting repricing is underway rather than random noise.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case becomes materially less about contract mechanics and more about downside catalyst intensity. A break of this assumption would justify cutting the Yes probability sharply and could make the market's 95% look overconfident.
