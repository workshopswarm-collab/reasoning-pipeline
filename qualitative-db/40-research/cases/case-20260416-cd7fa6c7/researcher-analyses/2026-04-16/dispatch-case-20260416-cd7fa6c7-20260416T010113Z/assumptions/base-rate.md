# Assumption

The current BTC/USDT regime near 74.6k is informative for the Apr 17 noon ET settlement minute, meaning no major overnight macro, crypto-specific, or exchange-specific shock drives price materially away from the current threshold neighborhood.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case for Yes depends heavily on the fact that BTC is already above 74,000 shortly before resolution. If the next ~15 hours are unusually volatile, the current spot advantage becomes a weak signal.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly favorable Yes probability above 50%
- Treating current Binance spot and recent realized range as the main outside-view anchor
- Rough agreement with the market rather than a large contrarian move

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTC is currently above the strike on the same exchange/pair family relevant to settlement.
- Recent closes have clustered around the threshold rather than far below it.
- In many short-horizon threshold markets, present level relative to strike is the dominant base-rate input unless a known catalyst is imminent.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp macro or crypto risk-off move before noon ET Apr 17
- Exchange-specific disruption or unusual divergence on Binance BTC/USDT
- A renewed move that pushes BTC sustainably below 74,000 before the settlement window

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the 74,000 level for a sustained block of trading before the morning of Apr 17
- Cross-market crypto weakness accelerates during Asian or European hours
- Binance trades materially weaker than other major BTC/USD venues

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes lean should drop quickly toward or below 50%, because this contract is settled on one specific minute and a relatively small adverse move is enough to flip the outcome.
