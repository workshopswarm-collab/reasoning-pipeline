# Assumption

The main assumption is that absent a fresh macro or crypto-specific shock, BTC can hold roughly the 74k area through noon ET on April 17 because current Binance spot is already modestly above the strike.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC must stage a major breakout; it only needs to remain above a nearby threshold at a specific minute. That makes level-holding behavior more important than directional medium-term conviction.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly bullish probability estimate above 50%
- The view that the market is roughly right to price Yes as favored
- The idea that the most relevant catalysts are negative shocks that could knock BTC back below the line rather than positive catalysts needed to push it far higher

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was trading around 74.5k at the time of research.
- Recent Binance daily closes were already above 74k on Apr 13, Apr 14, and Apr 15.
- The strike sits inside the recent 24-hour range but not at an extreme edge of it.
- For a one-day horizon, inertia and current level matter unless a new catalyst arrives.

## What would falsify it

- BTC decisively loses the mid-74k area and trades back into the 73k handle ahead of the resolution window.
- A macro risk-off catalyst, sharp equity selloff, or crypto-specific deleveraging event emerges before noon ET.
- Binance-specific data or market microstructure shows unusual weakness relative to broader BTC pricing.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated rejection below or near 74k in the hours before US morning trading.
- Overnight move back under 74k accompanied by rising downside momentum.
- Cross-market risk-off tone strong enough to challenge support before the noon ET print.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If BTC loses the level cleanly before the resolution window, the case flips from "can it hold?" to "can it reclaim quickly enough by noon ET?" That would materially lower the Yes probability and make No the more likely outcome.
