# Assumption

Vitality's outright win probability is materially path-dependent on whether it beats G2 in the April 14 Group A upper-bracket semifinal and secures the shorter, lower-variance route toward the title.

## Why this assumption matters

My estimate is not just about whether Vitality is the best team in the field; it depends on how many elite matches the team must survive. A semifinal-bye path preserves energy and reduces upset exposure, while a lower-bracket or quarterfinal-heavy path increases variance.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate slightly below the market despite agreeing Vitality is the rightful favorite.
- The claim that the G2 match is the highest-information near-term catalyst.
- The claim that a single group-stage result can reprice the market meaningfully without resolving it.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ESL/Liquipedia format surfaces show group winners advance directly to semifinals, while lower finishers must play additional playoff rounds.
- Liquipedia's live bracket shows Vitality already won its opener and now faces G2 in the upper-bracket semifinal, making this match the cleanest immediate branch point in path quality.
- In elite CS2, each extra knockout-caliber series adds nontrivial upset risk even for the best team.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that the bracket path is less important than assumed because the field quality is meaningfully weaker on Vitality's side.
- Evidence that Vitality's win probability barely moves after a G2 loss because the market views the lower path as nearly equivalent.
- Unexpected roster/injury/news shocks to key rivals that dominate bracket-path effects.

## Early warning signs

- Vitality looks vulnerable even in wins, implying underlying form is weaker than recent trophy history suggests.
- A rival such as Spirit or Falcons shows clearly superior event form, making path advantages less decisive.
- Market price remains sticky after major bracket-state updates, implying catalyst relevance was overstated.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If path dependence is weaker than assumed, then the current market price near 70.5% is easier to justify, because Vitality's team-quality edge would dominate bracket sequencing. My view would move closer to the market.
