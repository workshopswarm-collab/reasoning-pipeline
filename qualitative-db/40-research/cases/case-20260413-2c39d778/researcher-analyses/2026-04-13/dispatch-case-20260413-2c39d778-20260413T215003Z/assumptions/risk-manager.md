# Assumption
Vitality is probably the strongest single team in the field, but not so far ahead of the elite pack that a 70.5% outright title probability is robust once bracket and single-elimination path risk are included.

## Why this assumption matters
The final probability call depends on whether the market price mostly reflects true dominance or whether it is overconfidently compressing normal tournament variance.

## What this assumption supports
- A below-market estimate on Vitality outright win probability.
- Emphasis on path risk over directional anti-Vitality contrarianism.
- The claim that uncertainty, not just opponent strength, explains much of the disagreement with market price.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- ESL confirms a 16-team event with group stage plus single-elimination playoffs.
- Liquipedia confirms a deep field with multiple elite brand-name teams.
- In stacked S-tier events, even best-team status rarely eliminates upset and bracket risk.
- This runtime could not access HLTV ranking/form pages, which prevents a stronger pro-Vitality case from being independently verified here.

## What would falsify it
- Reliable independent evidence that Vitality entered Rio with an overwhelming current-form edge versus the field.
- Live tournament evidence showing a much easier bracket path than expected.
- Multiple strong opponents being absent, compromised, or already eliminated early.

## Early warning signs
- Vitality cruising through group play without map pressure.
- Other top contenders showing visible instability or early exits.
- New verified rankings/odds indicating the field was materially weaker than this run assumed.

## What changes if this assumption fails
If Vitality’s edge over the field is materially larger than assumed, the fair probability moves closer to the market and the current underweight-to-market stance weakens.
