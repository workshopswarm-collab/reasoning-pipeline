# Assumption

Vitality can be the best team in the field and still have only roughly a 55-60% chance to win the whole event because the bracket requires converting elite status through several high-leverage matches against other top teams.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is pricing Vitality at 70.5%, which looks closer to a dominant head-to-head or a much softer event path than to a deep S-tier tournament with multiple elite rivals and playoff variance.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below market.
- The claim that the main variant view is about overconfidence in championship conversion, not disbelief in Vitality’s quality.
- The argument that field depth and path compression matter more than broad reputation.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Liquipedia shows a deep field including other elite teams such as G2, Team Spirit, Team Falcons, NAVI, and MOUZ.
- The event format includes group stage plus playoffs, with late-stage Bo3/Bo5 pressure rather than a single direct final against a weak field.
- Championship driver logic in the vault notes that title-winning contexts compress matchups and raise the importance of depth, resilience, and repeat execution.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that multiple top rivals are materially weakened by stand-ins, health issues, or other disruptions, leaving Vitality with an unusually soft path.
- Evidence from stronger direct ranking / odds sources that Vitality’s edge over the field is historically extreme for this event.
- Tournament results that rapidly remove the strongest rivals from Vitality’s side of the bracket.

## Early warning signs

- Vitality continues winning comfortably while the strongest rivals look unstable or are eliminated.
- Official or high-quality contextual sources show the bracket becoming materially easier than implied at market open.
- Additional verification shows the market has already incorporated most path-risk adjustments.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the field is softer or the path materially easier than it presently appears, Vitality’s fair probability would move closer to or above the current 70.5% market level.
