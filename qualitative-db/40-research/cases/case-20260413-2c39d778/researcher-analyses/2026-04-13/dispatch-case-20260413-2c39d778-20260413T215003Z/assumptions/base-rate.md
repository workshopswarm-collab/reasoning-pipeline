# Assumption

Even if Vitality is the best team in the current field, best-team status in a 16-team S-tier CS2 event does not usually translate into true tournament win probability above roughly the low-60s unless the field is unusually weak or the event is nearly complete.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is pricing Vitality at 70.5%, which is extremely high for a tournament winner market with multiple elite opponents still alive or present. The final probability estimate depends heavily on whether that price should be accepted as a justified exception or discounted as favorite overpricing.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below the market price.
- A view that structural tournament variance remains meaningful even for a dominant team.
- A recommendation to treat vivid recent-team-form narratives as insufficient on their own to justify a 70%+ title probability this early.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Multi-stage tournaments compress several elimination risks into one title probability.
- Liquipedia shows a 16-team S-tier field including several recognized elite organizations rather than a thin field.
- The market is not directly settled already; title still must survive bracket path, opponent quality, map variance, and pressure moments.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that the tournament is already in a very late stage with Vitality needing only one favorable final match.
- Evidence that most credible top rivals have already been eliminated or are materially weakened.
- Evidence from strong current rankings or bookmaker markets showing Vitality at or above the low-70s before adjusting for vig.

## Early warning signs

- Vitality reaches the grand final with a clearly inferior opponent.
- Reliable pre-match markets heavily favor Vitality over every remaining team.
- The field quality deteriorates unexpectedly through upsets or roster issues.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If reliable external pricing or tournament state shows Vitality's path is much easier than the generic outside view assumes, the estimate should move upward toward the market and the disagreement should shrink materially.
