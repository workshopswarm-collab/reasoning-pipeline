# Assumption

The market's 70.5% price assumes Vitality is not just the best team in the field, but sufficiently more reliable than other elite contenders that single-event upset variance is unusually low.

## Why this assumption matters

The difference between “best team” and “70% to win a 16-team S-Tier tournament” is large. If the field remains deep and live, even a clearly best roster may still have much lower title odds than the current price implies.

## What this assumption supports

- A thesis that the market is pricing substantial crowd confidence in Vitality's current form and roster stability.
- A market-efficient interpretation in which recent dominance and star power justify a very large favorite premium.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Vitality is explicitly present in the event with its star-heavy roster.
- The market is likely aggregating broad public belief that Vitality with ZywOo/ropz/apEX/flameZ/mezii is the strongest team in CS2 at this moment.
- Stable roster continuity and brand confidence can justify favorite status.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that several peer teams enter in comparable form.
- Early tournament results showing the bracket is more fragile than price implies.
- Any roster or health disruption for Vitality.
- Evidence that historical single-event CS2 title conversion for even dominant favorites rarely approaches 70% in comparable fields.

## Early warning signs

- Tight or messy wins against lower-seeded opponents.
- Multiple rival teams looking playoff-ready simultaneously.
- Map-pool weakness or fatigue concerns surfacing during the event.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The fair price should move materially lower, because the market would be over-converting “best team in the world” into “near-certain tournament winner.”
