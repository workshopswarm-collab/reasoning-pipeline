# Summary

Liquipedia’s Team Vitality page supports the market’s bullish case: the roster core is stable, elite, and highly decorated. It also helps frame the variant view more honestly, because the bearish angle is not “Vitality is weak,” but rather “even a clearly elite team may be overpriced in a deep tournament.”

## Key facts extracted

- Vitality’s active core listed on the page includes apEX, ZywOo, flameZ, mezii, and ropz, with XTQZZZ as coach.
- The page describes Vitality as one of the most successful Counter-Strike organizations, with more than 20 S-tier trophies and multiple majors / grand-slam level achievements.
- Contract/continuity notes suggest roster stability rather than immediate internal disruption.
- The page also shows Vitality actively present in IEM Rio 2026 competition coverage.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Current roster names and coach.
- Historical achievement framing.
- Ongoing event references linking the team directly to IEM Rio 2026.

## What is uncertain

- This page is contextual rather than a formal competitive ranking or official tournament result source.
- Historical accolades do not automatically translate into event-win probability in a single tournament.

## Why this source may matter

It establishes the strongest market argument: Vitality is not just a popular brand; it is an elite, stable roster with a long record of top-tier success. Any variant thesis must clear that bar rather than ignore it.

## Possible impact on the question

This source pushes against an overly bearish view. It supports keeping Vitality as the single most likely winner, but does not itself justify a probability above 70% given tournament variance and field strength.

## Reliability notes

Good contextual roster source, secondary not primary. Best used to establish roster continuity and the strength of the consensus case, not to settle the market on its own.
