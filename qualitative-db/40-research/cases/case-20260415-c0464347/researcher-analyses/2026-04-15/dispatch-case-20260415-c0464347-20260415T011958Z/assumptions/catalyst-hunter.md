# Assumption

There is no remaining scheduled catalyst before April 20 noon ET likely by itself to drive a sustained Binance BTC/USDT move of more than roughly 6% downward from the current mid-74k range to below 70k at the exact settlement minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is mostly a path-and-timing judgment rather than a deep terminal Bitcoin valuation view. If a high-information scheduled catalyst were still pending, the market's 88% Yes price could be too complacent.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability close to but slightly below market.
- The judgment that current distance above the threshold is meaningful over a five-day window.
- The idea that unscheduled shock risk, not known calendar risk, is the main threat to Yes.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BLS CPI schedule shows the obvious inflation release already occurred on April 10.
- CME FedWatch page confirms the next FOMC meeting is still upcoming, but not inside the immediate settlement window in a way that creates a discrete rate decision before April 20.
- Current Binance spot is materially above 70k, so absent a fresh shock the threshold is not tight.

## What would falsify it

- Discovery of a major scheduled macro event, exchange-specific operational issue, or legal/regulatory announcement set before April 20 noon ET with credible downside impact.
- A sudden deterioration in ETF flow or macro-risk sentiment that breaks BTC back below 70k before the settlement window.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses 72k and fails to reclaim it quickly.
- A sharp risk-off move in equities/rates/crypto over the next several sessions.
- Binance-specific disruption or divergence versus other major exchanges.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move notably lower because a sub-70k print at the exact settlement minute becomes more plausible than the current spot cushion suggests.
