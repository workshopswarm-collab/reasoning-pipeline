# Summary

BLS's official calendar shows the March 2026 Employment Situation is due **Friday, 2026-04-03 at 8:30 a.m. ET**, which puts the main U.S. macro event of the week directly in front of this market's resolution window. The latest published Employment Situation (for February 2026) showed payrolls down **92,000** and unemployment at **4.4%**, a mixed-to-soft backdrop that keeps the jobs print capable of moving rates and equities materially.

## Key facts extracted

- BLS schedules the **March 2026 Employment Situation** release for **Apr. 03, 2026 at 08:30 AM ET**.
- The latest available BLS Employment Situation release (for **February 2026**) reported **nonfarm payroll employment edged down by 92,000**.
- The same release reported the unemployment rate changed little at **4.4%**.
- BLS said employment in health care decreased reflecting strike activity, while information and federal government employment continued to trend down.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The week's biggest scheduled macro catalyst lands on Friday morning, late in the market-resolution window.
- The last payroll report was not a clean "everything is strong" print; it had enough softness that the next jobs release could materially shift rate expectations and equity sentiment either way.

## What is uncertain

- The February weakness may have been noisy or partly distorted by strike effects.
- I do not yet have consensus expectations for the March payroll report in this note.
- A soft jobs print can be interpreted bullishly or bearishly depending on how it shifts growth vs. Fed expectations.

## Why this source may matter

For a weekly S&P question, a Friday 8:30 a.m. jobs report is exactly the kind of event that can erase or extend an early-week move. That makes an 81.5% implied probability look aggressive unless the market is already very comfortably above last week's close.

## Possible impact on the question
