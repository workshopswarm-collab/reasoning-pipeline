# Assumption

An official Strategy purchases-page update and linked 8-K posted on April 6, 2026 counts as a qualifying company announcement for market resolution.

## Why this assumption matters

The market resolves on official information from MicroStrategy or Michael Saylor, so the entire thesis depends more on source qualification and timing than on broader interpretive inference.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability "Yes" estimate.
- Confidence that the market's extreme pricing is justified.
- The claim that no further non-company confirmation is needed to cross the evidence floor.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market description explicitly names official company information as the resolution source.
- Strategy's official purchases page is a company-controlled surface dedicated to reporting Bitcoin purchases.
- The page links the corresponding April 6, 2026 8-K and includes company-authored acquisition text.
- This is stronger than relying on third-party reporting or inference from holdings changes alone.

## What would falsify it

- A resolution clarification stating the purchases page does not count, and only some narrower source class counts.
- Discovery that the April 6 entry was posted outside the designated window despite the dated row.
- Retraction/correction by Strategy showing the entry was erroneous or not an announcement of additional Bitcoin acquired in the title window.

## Early warning signs

- Market operator delays resolution despite the page update.
- Conflicting official timestamps between the page, 8-K, and any company social post.
- Removal or correction of the April 6 purchase row.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The confidence in "Yes" would drop materially, and the case would require narrower timestamp/attribution verification from the 8-K itself or from an official Michael Saylor post.
