# Assumption

The market counts an official company-controlled website update on April 6 ET as a qualifying announcement even if the underlying BTC purchases were executed earlier than the announcement date.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is explicitly about announcement timing, not trade execution timing. The final probability estimate depends on treating the official purchases-page update as the decisive event inside the March 31-April 6 window.

## What this assumption supports

- A near-certain YES interpretation.
- The view that the key catalyst already occurred rather than remaining pending.
- The conclusion that additional late-window catalysts are no longer required for resolution.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market description says it resolves YES if MicroStrategy announces that it acquired additional Bitcoin between the dates in the title.
- The description further clarifies that announcements made within the designated time frame count regardless of when the actual purchases were made.
- Strategy's official purchases page includes an April 6, 2026 reported purchase entry and company-authored summary text.

## What would falsify it

- A clarifying rule interpretation that excludes the official purchases page as a qualifying announcement surface.
- Evidence that the page update was posted outside April 6 ET.
- A contrary official correction from Strategy or Michael Saylor withdrawing or re-dating the announcement.

## Early warning signs

- Resolver commentary claiming only an SEC filing or only a Michael Saylor social post counts.
- Discovery that the page metadata publish time converts to a date outside the market window under ET.
- A mismatch between the page entry and any linked SEC filing date.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability would drop materially because the case would then depend on whether a separate official press/X/SEC announcement clearly landed in-window and satisfied the exact resolution wording.
