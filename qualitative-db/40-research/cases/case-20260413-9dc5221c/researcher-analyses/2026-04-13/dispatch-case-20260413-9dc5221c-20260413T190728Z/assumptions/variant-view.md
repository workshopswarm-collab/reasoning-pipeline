# Assumption

A two-point lead with two classical rounds left in an elite double round robin is usually converted into at least a match-winning tie or outright win, but not often enough to justify near-certainty above 97-98% without official live standings and tiebreak verification.

## Why this assumption matters

The whole variant view depends on resisting the temptation to equate "dominant position" with "already won." If this assumption is too conservative, the market near 95% is cheap; if it is too aggressive, the market is overconfident.

## What this assumption supports

- A modest under-market estimate rather than a hard disagreement.
- The claim that the main remaining risk is not current form but the nonzero path from late stumble or playoff variance.
- The decision to keep the estimate in the low-90s instead of mid/high-90s.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Sindarov's contextual standing is overwhelming: 9/12 and playoff-safe with two rounds left.
- But the tournament has not yet officially ended, and the contract resolves on an actual winner declared by FIDE.
- In chess, two late bad games, a pursuer hot streak, or rapid/blitz tiebreak variance can still matter once the event has not yet settled.

## What would falsify it

- Official FIDE standings or regulations showing Sindarov has already clinched outright regardless of remaining results.
- Official pairings/tiebreak math showing no realistic path except Sindarov victory.
- Conversely, evidence that the standings feed was inaccurate or missing a material fair-play/appeal complication.

## Early warning signs

- Official FIDE reporting lagging behind or contradicting secondary standings.
- News of fair-play review, protest, withdrawal, or scoring dispute.
- A round-13 loss narrowing the lead and materially increasing playoff risk.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If he has already mathematically clinched, the estimate should move sharply toward resolution confidence. If the standings are less secure than secondary sources imply, the estimate should fall materially and source-quality concerns should dominate.
