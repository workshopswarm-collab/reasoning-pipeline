## Why this assumption matters

The entire bullish thesis depends on the remaining schedule not containing hidden structural fragility beyond the obvious round-13 showdown with Giri and final-round variance.

## What this assumption supports

- A win probability moderately below, but still close to, the market's 95.05% implied probability.
- The view that round 13 is the decisive catalyst and round 14 is mostly a mop-up risk unless Sindarov loses first.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- FIDE reports show Sindarov built and maintained a two-point lead through multiple rounds.
- FIDE reports indicate Giri missed a major chance in round 12 and now faces a must-win game against Sindarov.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that tiebreak or playoff rules make a two-point cushion much less protective than it appears.
- A round-13 loss by Sindarov that reopens the event materially.
- A correction to current standings or pairings from FIDE.

## Early warning signs

- Official FIDE language indicating the standings margin is smaller than currently understood.
- Independent credible coverage highlighting a hidden route where Sindarov can still lose despite avoiding defeat in round 13.
- Signs of collapse in form or a risky opening strategy from Sindarov in the next round.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market should be marked down materially; the final two rounds would become a genuine toss-up or at least much more open than current pricing implies.
