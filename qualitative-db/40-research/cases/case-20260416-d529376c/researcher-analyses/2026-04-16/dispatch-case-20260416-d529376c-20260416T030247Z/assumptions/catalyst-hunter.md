# Assumption

The key assumption is that no near-term negative catalyst strong enough to knock SOL from the mid-80s to below 80 will arrive before the exact Binance noon ET print on 2026-04-19.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is less about long-run Solana fundamentals and more about whether the next ~3 days remain free of a sharp repricing shock.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-but-not-certain Yes probability.
- A view that current distance above the threshold is meaningful.
- A timing-based conclusion that the main risk is abrupt downside volatility rather than gradual drift.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot data shows SOL already above 85 at assignment time.
- Recent noon-ET-equivalent minute candles on Binance have also closed above 80.
- With the threshold only a few days away, the set of plausible market-moving catalysts is narrower than over a multi-week horizon.

## What would falsify it

- A broad crypto risk-off move that takes SOL below 80 before or at the relevant minute.
- A Solana-specific outage, exploit, or major exchange/liquidity disruption.
- A macro or regulatory shock that materially hits altcoin beta over the next several sessions.

## Early warning signs

- SOL losing the 82-83 area and failing to recover.
- BTC/ETH broad drawdown with altcoins underperforming.
- New reports of Solana reliability incidents or exchange-specific execution problems.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case would move from a high-probability Yes to a genuinely live coin-flip or No-risk scenario because the contract is pinned to one exact minute rather than a broader average.
