# Summary

Ukrainska Pravda reported President Zelenskyy's public warning on 14 April that Russia could launch another overnight attack using Shahed drones and possibly missiles. This is not Kyiv-specific, but it is the clearest near-term catalyst signal found in this run because it points to an active strike window during the market period.

## Key facts extracted

- Zelenskyy said that on the night of 14-15 April there could be another Russian attack.
- He said many Shaheds were already in Ukrainian skies and missiles could also be used.
- The remarks came during a joint press conference with Norwegian Prime Minister Jonas Gahr Støre.
- The warning followed a same-day Russian missile strike on Dnipro.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- A top Ukrainian official publicly warned of an imminent overnight Russian air attack.
- The expected attack mode included drones and possibly missiles, which are qualifying weapon classes under this market if directed at Kyiv municipality.
- The source does not say the attack would target Kyiv specifically.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the warned-about attack actually occurs.
- Whether any such attack is directed at Kyiv municipality rather than another region.
- Whether later official reporting would clearly place a qualifying strike within Kyiv municipality during the market window.

## Why this source may matter

This is a useful catalyst source because it identifies a concrete, immediate time window in which repricing could happen. It raises the chance of a qualifying event before resolution even though it does not itself satisfy the contract.

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports a higher-than-base-rate probability of some Russian air action somewhere in Ukraine during the relevant window, but only modestly increases probability for Kyiv municipality specifically because the geographic target is unstated.

## Reliability notes

The report is recent and based on public remarks by the Ukrainian president, which makes it strong for imminent-risk signaling. It is weaker as settlement evidence because it is predictive, not confirmatory, and is filtered through a media summary rather than an official Air Force strike bulletin.
