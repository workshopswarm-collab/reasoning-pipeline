# Assumption

The next roughly 48 hours will look more like a typical recent Ukrainian nationwide strike cycle than like a specially concentrated campaign against Kyiv municipality.

## Why this assumption matters

The probability estimate depends on whether recent broad strike tempo should be translated into a very high Kyiv-specific short-window risk, or whether municipality-specific targeting remains meaningfully less frequent than national strike headlines suggest.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below the market-implied 73%.
- A view that broad national attack activity is not enough by itself to justify a very high Yes probability for a single municipality over a narrow window.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Russia continues regular aerial attacks across Ukraine, which raises the background risk.
- But the contract is narrower than "any strike in Ukraine" and narrower than "any Kyiv Oblast alert/report." It requires a qualifying strike against Kyiv municipality itself.
- The accessible contextual reporting reviewed in this run confirmed broad overnight attacks and multiple strike locations, but did not cleanly confirm Kyiv municipality as one of them.
- When a market is both location-specific and date-sensitive, base rates should be discounted relative to broad war-intensity headlines.

## What would falsify it

- Clean official or major-media reporting that Kyiv municipality itself was targeted or struck in the relevant window.
- Evidence of a current Russian campaign pattern specifically concentrating aerial attacks on Kyiv city over consecutive nights.

## Early warning signs

- Air Force or Kyiv city official statements naming Kyiv as a target during the relevant window.
- Repeated independent media reports specifically saying drones or missiles were directed at Kyiv city/municipality.
- Market-moving headlines tying overnight salvos directly to Kyiv rather than to Ukraine broadly.

## What changes if this assumption fails

My probability should move materially higher, likely toward or above the market, because the contract permits intercepted projectiles aimed at Kyiv to count.
