# Assumption

The main uncertainty-carrying assumption is that elevated nationwide Russian overnight air-attack activity does not automatically translate into a Kyiv municipality-directed strike before the market closes.

## Why this assumption matters

The market resolves on a narrow geography and weapon definition, so the difference between "Russia attacks Ukraine tonight" and "Russia strikes Kyiv municipality tonight" is the entire trade.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below the market's 73% implied level.
- A view that the most important catalyst is official/local confirmation of Kyiv targeting, not just generalized strike warnings.
- Caution against over-weighting nationwide barrage headlines.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The clearest imminent warning found in this run was national in scope, not Kyiv-specific.
- Official-style reporting reviewed here confirms large-scale drone/missile activity against Ukraine, but the accessible summary did not place Kyiv municipality among the targets.
- The contract explicitly allows intercepted attacks to count, but only if clearly directed against Kyiv municipality.

## What would falsify it

- Ukrainian Air Force, Kyiv City State Administration, or the Mayor of Kyiv reporting that drones or missiles were directed at Kyiv during the market window.
- Broad credible media consensus specifically describing a Russian drone or missile strike on Kyiv municipality within the time window.

## Early warning signs

- Public alerts or official statements singling out Kyiv as the destination of inbound Shaheds or missiles.
- Local Kyiv administration posts about air defense activity or falling debris tied to a Russian aerial attack.
- Major media specifically datelining a Russian strike in Kyiv rather than elsewhere.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The yes probability would jump sharply because the contract accepts intercepted attacks aimed at Kyiv municipality even without impact damage on the ground.
