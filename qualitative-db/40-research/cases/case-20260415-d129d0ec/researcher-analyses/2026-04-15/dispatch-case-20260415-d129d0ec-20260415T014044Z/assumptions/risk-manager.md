# Assumption

The market price is implicitly assuming that recent generalized Russian aerial pressure on Ukraine translates into a better-than-even chance of at least one clearly attributable, contract-qualifying strike on **Kyiv municipality itself** before the deadline.

## Why this assumption matters

A market can overprice the broad thesis ("Russia keeps attacking Ukraine / Kyiv is often targeted") while underpricing the narrower contract requirements around geography, weapon type, timing window, and confirmation standard.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability near the current 0.73 market price.
- Confidence that broad alert activity is likely to convert into a qualifying event before deadline.
- Confidence that if a strike occurs, it will also be confirmed cleanly enough for resolution.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Kyiv remains a recurring high-value Russian target.
- Official channels still show aerial-threat activity and Kyiv alerts.
- Intercepted drones/missiles can still count, which lowers the physical-damage threshold for Yes.

## What would falsify it

- The remaining window passes with alerts but no clear Kyiv-municipality strike confirmation.
- Reported Russian aerial activity is concentrated in other regions rather than Kyiv municipality.
- Ambiguous or late reporting prevents confirmation within the contract’s allowed reporting window.

## Early warning signs

- Kyiv alerts continue but official channels do not convert those alerts into confirmed strike reports.
- Reporting emphasizes Kyiv oblast / approaches / interceptions elsewhere rather than Kyiv municipality.
- Major media do not produce convergent reporting despite social-media chatter.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The correct view should move materially lower than the market, because this contract is not about generalized escalation or Ukraine-wide attack frequency; it is about one narrow, auditable event in one municipality by a near-term cutoff.
