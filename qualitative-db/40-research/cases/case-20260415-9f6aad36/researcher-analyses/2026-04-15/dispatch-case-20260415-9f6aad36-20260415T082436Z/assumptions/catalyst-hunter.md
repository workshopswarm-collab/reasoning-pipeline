# Assumption

No major negative macro, policy, or exchange-operational catalyst will hit BTC hard enough before April 16 noon ET to drag Binance BTC/USDT below 72,000 at the exact settlement minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The current Binance spot reference is comfortably above the threshold, so the bearish path mainly requires a fresh downside catalyst or a sharp risk-off move within a short window.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability above 50%.
- A view that the market is directionally right to keep 72,000 above-even odds.
- A catalyst analysis centered on downside shock risk rather than gradual drift.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance spot and recent one-minute closes are around 73,971, leaving roughly a 2.7% cushion versus 72,000.
- For a one-day horizon, absent a clear scheduled bearish trigger, spot often needs either a broad macro shock, sudden deleveraging, or venue-specific disruption to lose that cushion by a fixed minute.
- The contract uses Binance spot specifically, so exchange-specific operational or liquidity dislocations matter more than slower narrative developments.

## What would falsify it

- A verified macro shock, hawkish policy surprise, or major risk-off headline that produces a fast BTC selloff through 72,000.
- A Binance-specific outage, price dislocation, or abnormal candle behavior near settlement.
- Sustained intraday weakness on April 15-16 that compresses the cushion well before noon ET.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the 73k handle and fails to reclaim it.
- A sharp rise in cross-venue volatility or headlines implying risk-off contagion.
- Any sign Binance data availability or pricing integrity is impaired near the relevant window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case shifts from roughly agreeing with market pricing to a much tighter or even bearish view, because this contract depends on one exact minute rather than average daily price.
