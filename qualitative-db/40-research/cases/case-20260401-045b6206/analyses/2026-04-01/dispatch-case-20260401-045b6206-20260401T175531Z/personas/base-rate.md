# Claim

The base-rate view is that **a qualifying direct Iranian strike on Iraq in March is materially less likely than the market price implies**. My estimate is **~35%**, versus a market-implied probability of **85.4%** from the current price of 0.854.

## Implication for the question

I **disagree with the market**. The outside-view anchor for this contract should start from the fact that:

- qualifying events are **narrowly defined**
- direct overt Iranian missile/drone strikes from Iranian territory are **rare**
- direct Iran-on-Iraq strikes appear **especially rare and episodic**, not routine
- Iran more commonly expresses pressure through proxies, gray-zone activity, deterrent signaling, or strikes on other target sets than through overt, contract-qualifying impacts on Iraqi soil

To justify an 85%+ probability, I would want strong current evidence that Iraq is the specifically likely venue for another direct overt Iranian strike this month, not just that Iran is regionally active or that tensions are high.

## Supporting evidence

- **Historical rarity:** the most salient direct qualifying Iran-on-Iraq precedent is the January 2020 ballistic missile strike on U.S. forces in Iraq after Soleimani's killing. There was also a January 2024 Iranian strike in Erbil. That is enough to show feasibility, but not enough to make such strikes look common.
- **Reference-class mismatch:** the stronger recent precedent for direct overt Iranian strikes is actually **Iran-on-Israel** in April and October 2024. That suggests that even when Iran chooses direct overt retaliation, Iraq is not obviously the default target.
- **Contract narrowness matters:** proxy attacks do not count; intercepted missiles/drones do not count; there must be impact on Iraqi territory and credible reporting tying it to Iranian forces or Iranian territory. This is materially narrower than “regional escalation involving Iran.”
- **Structural friction:** direct strikes on Iraq create diplomatic and escalation costs with Baghdad, the U.S. footprint, and broader regional signaling. Iran has often had lower-cost alternatives to impose pressure.

## Counterpoints

- **Nonzero precedent exists:** Iran has directly struck Iraq before, so the event is not remotely impossible.
- **Iraq is a plausible theater:** Iraq sits inside the broader Iran/Israel/U.S. confrontation web and contains targets or geographies that could become relevant in retaliation dynamics.
- **Market may know live trigger details:** if traders are pricing off fresh, specific intelligence or an ongoing sequence of events pointing at imminent Iraq-based retaliation, my base-rate estimate could be too low.

## Key assumptions

- March 2026 does **not** contain a uniquely strong Iraq-specific trigger comparable to the most extreme retaliation episodes.
- The relevant reference class is **direct overt Iran-attributed impacts on Iraqi soil**, not all Iran-linked violence in or around Iraq.
- Structural incentives still favor Iran using alternatives to a direct, overt, contract-qualifying strike unless provoked into a narrow retaliation path.

## Why this is decision-relevant

The market price appears to be treating “Iran is in active regional confrontation” as close to equivalent to “Iran will directly and overtly hit Iraq this month.” The base-rate view says those are very different propositions. Iraq-specific, contract-qualifying direct strikes are rare enough that the burden of proof should be high before accepting an 85% probability.

## What would falsify this interpretation

I would move materially upward if credible new reporting showed any of the following:

- an imminent Iraq-specific retaliation trigger involving Iran directly
- visible operational preparation for missiles/drones aimed at Iraqi territory from Iran
- authoritative reporting that Iraqi territory is now the intended overt venue for Iranian retaliation
- a recent direct Iranian strike pattern expanding from Israel/other theaters into Iraq specifically

Conversely, absent such evidence, the safer outside-view remains well below the market.

## Recommended follow-up

- Check whether the 0.854 market price is driven by fresh March-2026 Iraq-specific reporting rather than generic regional-war salience.
- If such reporting exists, quantify how much it truly narrows the path to a contract-qualifying impact on Iraqi soil.
- Otherwise, treat this market as a likely case of narrative/extrapolation outrunning the historical base rate.
