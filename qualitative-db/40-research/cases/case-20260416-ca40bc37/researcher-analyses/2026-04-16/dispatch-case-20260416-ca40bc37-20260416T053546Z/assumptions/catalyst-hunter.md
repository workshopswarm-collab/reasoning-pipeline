# Assumption

The base case assumes no major macro or crypto-specific shock hits before April 20 noon ET that is large enough to knock Binance BTC/USDT more than roughly 4% lower from current levels.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC is structurally cheap or guaranteed to remain above 72k forever; it is that the remaining time window is short and the current cushion is meaningful. That makes near-term shock risk the central assumption.

## What this assumption supports

- A yes probability above the market-implied baseline only slightly, not dramatically.
- The view that path-to-resolution is mainly about avoiding a sharp risk-off catalyst.
- The claim that the most informative thing to watch is macro-risk scheduling rather than long-run BTC fundamentals.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot is already above 75k.
- Recent daily closes show BTC has held above 72k for several sessions.
- No clearly scheduled FOMC rate decision appears before April 20 from the Fed calendar surface checked here, reducing obvious calendarized macro event risk inside the window.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp global risk-off move, exchange-specific problem, or new crypto policy shock that pushes BTC decisively back below 72k before or at the settlement minute.
- Evidence of a scheduled high-impact macro release before noon ET on April 20 that the market is underpricing.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing 74k quickly and failing to reclaim it.
- Weekend risk sentiment deterioration or liquidation-driven selloff.
- Binance-specific operational issues that could distort the relevant close.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a real shock catalyst emerges, the market should be marked materially lower because the contract only needs one specific minute below 72k to resolve no.
