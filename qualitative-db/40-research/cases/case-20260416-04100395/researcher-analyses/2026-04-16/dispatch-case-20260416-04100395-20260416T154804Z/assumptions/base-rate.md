# Assumption

The recent Binance spot regime around the low-2300s is a better anchor for tomorrow's noon ET settlement minute than any one short-term narrative shock.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case depends on treating recent realized price distribution near 2300 as the main prior, instead of assuming a continued directional breakout just because current spot is slightly above the line.

## What this assumption supports

- A moderate, not extreme, Yes probability.
- Skepticism toward a market price that implies a clearer edge than the recent price distribution alone supports.
- Use of recent threshold-crossing frequency as contextual evidence.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The line is close to current price rather than far in the money.
- Recent Binance daily closes show repeated movement on both sides of 2300.
- One-day crypto price forecasting is noisy enough that nearby thresholds should usually be priced with meaningful uncertainty unless there is a strong catalyst or settled structural edge.

## What would falsify it

- A large, durable move well above 2300 before settlement, with supporting market-wide momentum or catalyst evidence.
- New contract-relevant information showing that noon ET pricing behaves systematically differently from the broader 24-hour distribution used as context.

## Early warning signs

- ETH holding materially above 2360-2400 for several sessions.
- A strong market-wide breakout led by BTC/ETH with sustained risk-on follow-through.
- Clear macro or crypto-specific catalyst evidence not visible in the current base-rate framing.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The correct estimate would shift higher and the market's current bullish lean would look more justified than overstated.
