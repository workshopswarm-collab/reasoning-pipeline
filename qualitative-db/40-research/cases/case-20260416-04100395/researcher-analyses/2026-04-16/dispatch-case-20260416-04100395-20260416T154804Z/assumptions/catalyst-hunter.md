# Assumption

No discrete Ethereum-specific negative catalyst or exchange-specific disruption is likely to hit before 2026-04-17 12:00 ET with enough force to push the Binance ETH/USDT 1-minute close back below 2300.

## Why this assumption matters

The case is short-dated. The thesis depends less on long-run Ethereum fundamentals and more on whether the current above-strike spot level can survive the next sub-24-hour event window.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly bullish Yes lean.
- An own probability estimate above the market-implied baseline.
- The view that the most relevant catalyst is the absence of a new bearish trigger rather than the arrival of a new bullish one.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot and recent 1-minute candles show ETH already trading comfortably above 2300.
- Contextual source checks did not reveal a single obvious imminent ETH-specific binary catalyst.
- In a calm or ordinary-news regime, short-horizon continuation from a spot level already above strike is plausible.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp macro risk-off move in crypto overnight.
- A Binance-specific trading disruption, data issue, or liquidity shock.
- Material Ethereum-specific negative news before noon ET on 2026-04-17.

## Early warning signs

- ETH losing the 2320-2300 zone and failing to reclaim it.
- Bitcoin-led crypto weakness spreading across majors.
- Exchange incidents or abnormal spread/liquidity deterioration on Binance.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case would move from a moderate Yes lean to a roughly even or No-leaning setup, because the margin over strike is not large enough to absorb a meaningful volatility shock with confidence.
