# Assumption

The market counts the April 13, 2026 Strategy purchases-page/8-K disclosure as an official company announcement inside the April 7-13 ET window, regardless of when the BTC was actually bought.

## Why this assumption matters

The contract resolves on announcement timing, not trade execution timing. If the April 13 disclosure counts as official information from MicroStrategy/Strategy, the market is effectively settled YES.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-confidence YES lean.
- The view that the main catalyst was the expected Monday company disclosure cycle.
- The conclusion that repricing risk before midnight ET is minimal after the official post appears.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market description explicitly says announcements within the window count regardless of when purchases were made.
- Strategy's official purchases page posted a dated April 13 entry for 13,927 BTC and linked an 8-K.
- Strategy's historical press practice shows official BTC purchase disclosures on company-owned investor relations surfaces.

## What would falsify it

- Polymarket clarifies that the purchases page does not count unless a separate press release or Michael Saylor post exists.
- The linked 8-K or company page is withdrawn/corrected before resolution.
- The timestamp is shown to be outside the ET market window.

## Early warning signs

- Conflicting official clarification from Polymarket comments or market rules moderators.
- A correction reducing the purchase below 1000 BTC.
- Evidence that the April 13 row was published after 11:59 PM ET, which currently does not appear to be the case.

## What changes if this assumption fails

Confidence would fall sharply and the case would revert to checking for a separate Michael Saylor/company announcement surface explicitly named by the rules.
