# Assumption

The February 2026 Oman/Geneva indirect US-Iran talks would satisfy the market's qualifying-meeting criteria because they appear to have been in-person, publicly acknowledged, and conducted through authorized representatives/mediators.

## Why this assumption matters

This assumption does most of the work behind the variant thesis. If it holds, the market price near 0.76 is materially too low because the event has likely already occurred. If it fails, then the market discount may be justified by rules ambiguity.

## What this assumption supports

- A variant-view probability materially above the market.
- The claim that the market may be stale or overfocused on current war narrative rather than the actual resolution text.
- The interpretation that remaining uncertainty is mostly legal/criteria-based, not factual/event-based.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Reuters/AP titles say talks were agreed, held, and continued.
- The manifest's resolution language explicitly says indirect meetings through mediators can qualify.
- The talks were described as taking place in Oman and Geneva, which points toward in-person diplomatic process rather than purely remote contact.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that the reported talks were not in-person.
- Evidence that the attendees lacked authority to negotiate on behalf of their governments.
- A clear resolution-source interpretation that these contacts do not count because of format details not visible in current reporting.

## Early warning signs

- Rule-focused traders continue to cite the talks as non-qualifying rather than merely uncertain.
- Official-source language characterizes the meetings as technical contact or message-passing rather than diplomatic negotiation.
- A credible consensus emerges that the parties were never physically co-located in a way that satisfies the market rule.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The bullish variant collapses from an "already likely happened" thesis into a normal forward-looking diplomacy forecast. In that case, the 0.76 price may be fair or even high given March war escalation.
