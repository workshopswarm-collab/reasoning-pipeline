# Assumption

The live Oman/Geneva diplomatic channel is real but fragile, and it should not be assumed to convert smoothly into a publicly acknowledged qualifying in-person US-Iran diplomatic meeting within three months.

## Why this assumption matters

A high market price implicitly assumes not only that talks exist, but that the remaining frictions are modest. My lower base-rate estimate depends on the opposite view: hostile-state diplomacy often stalls even after real progress headlines because several political and operational conditions must align at once.

## What this assumption supports

- a probability estimate materially below the 76% market-implied level
- an interpretation that February progress is evidence of possibility, not near-inevitability
- heavier weighting on structural frictions than on headline momentum

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- February reporting shows a real channel and some progress.
- March reporting shows contradictory signals, public denials, and rejected mediation attempts.
- US-Iran diplomacy historically depends on intermediaries, controlled venues, and narrow issue framing rather than smooth bilateral normalization.
- The market resolution standard requires a qualifying meeting that is in-person and publicly acknowledged or credibly reported, which is a narrower endpoint than merely having talks underway.

## What would falsify it

- official confirmation of a scheduled in-person meeting between authorized representatives before June 30
- repeated credible reporting that direct talks are advancing toward a specific venue/date
- public acknowledgment from both sides or from Oman that a qualifying diplomatic meeting has already occurred or is locked in

## Early warning signs

- sustained rhetoric shift from coercion to process language
- public mention of date, venue, or delegation level
- multiple credible outlets converging on the same meeting plan
- evidence that the parties have narrowed the agenda enough to permit a formal encounter

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the channel proves smoother and more durable than assumed, the probability should move sharply upward because the remaining time window is still long enough for one qualifying meeting. In that case the current market may be reasonable or even too low.
