# Summary

AP reported on March 29 that Pakistan publicly said it would host and facilitate meaningful U.S.-Iran talks "in the coming days," while also noting there was no immediate confirmation from Washington or Tehran and uncertainty over whether the talks would be direct or indirect.

## Key facts extracted

- Pakistani Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar said Pakistan would be honored to host and facilitate meaningful talks between the U.S. and Iran in the coming days.
- AP said there was no immediate public confirmation from either Washington or Tehran.
- AP reported Trump said Washington was in discussions with Iran and claimed negotiations were going extremely well.
- AP also reported Iran's parliament speaker dismissed the Pakistan talks, framing them as cover amid military escalation.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Pakistan publicly claims both Iran and the U.S. expressed confidence in Pakistan to facilitate talks.
- Timing language is near-term: "coming days."
- The meeting format was unresolved in the AP report.
- Iranian official rhetoric remained hostile even as mediation reports circulated.

## What is uncertain

- Whether a qualifying in-person meeting will actually occur versus only indirect message-passing.
- Whether the talks, if held, will satisfy the market's exact resolution criteria.
- Whether the relevant deadline is truly June 30, 2026 as the title says, or the March 30, 2026 timestamp shown in the dispatch metadata.

## Why this source may matter

This is the clearest public near-term catalyst for repricing: a named government publicly put a hosting offer and short time horizon on the table. If further official confirmation appears, the market can move quickly.

## Possible impact on the question

Bullish for a Yes resolution and especially bullish for near-term repricing if another government confirms logistics, venue, or participants. But the lack of U.S./Iran confirmation and the unresolved direct-versus-indirect issue keep this from being a clean confirmation signal.

## Reliability notes

AP is a high-credibility wire service. The core claims are attributed to named officials, but the most market-moving part still depends on whether third-party facilitation talk converts into a publicly acknowledged qualifying meeting.
