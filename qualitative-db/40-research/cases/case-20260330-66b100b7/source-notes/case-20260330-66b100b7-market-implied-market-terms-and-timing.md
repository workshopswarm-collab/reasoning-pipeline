# Summary

The market definition is broader than a simple face-to-face summit. It allows meetings conducted indirectly through designated mediators or interlocutors, so long as they are authorized and publicly acknowledged or credibly reported. However, the dispatch metadata contains a notable timing mismatch: the market title references June 30, 2026 while the manifest shows closes_at/resolves_at of March 30, 2026.

## Key facts extracted

- A qualifying diplomatic meeting can be direct or indirect through authorized mediators/interlocutors.
- Brief greetings or chance encounters do not count.
- The meeting must be deliberate, in-person, and publicly acknowledged or reported by a consensus of credible media.
- Dispatch metadata shows a title implying June 30, 2026 but close/resolve timestamps of March 30, 2026.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Indirect meetings via mediators can qualify.
- Public acknowledgment or consensus credible reporting is required.
- The timestamps in the dispatch manifest do not cleanly match the title.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the March 30 timestamps are an internal pipeline error or the actual controlling deadline.
- Whether a mediated process hosted by Pakistan would satisfy the in-person and public-acknowledgment standards.

## Why this source may matter

The broad resolution language is one of the strongest reasons the market can rationally trade high: a mediated diplomatic encounter appears easier to achieve than a fully direct bilateral summit. The timing mismatch is the biggest single caveat because it changes how much runway remains.

## Possible impact on the question

If the true deadline is June 30, a 76% price is easier to defend. If the true deadline is March 30, 76% requires near-immediate execution and looks much more aggressive.

## Reliability notes

This is first-party market-definition material from the assignment and linked market page, so it is highly reliable on wording. The unresolved issue is interpretation of the inconsistent timestamps.
