# Summary
Late-March sources pull in opposite directions. WBUR’s March 24 interview says talks had effectively broken down around the zero-enrichment issue even though some Trump claims suggested weekend contact and “many points of agreement.” Meanwhile, an AP-syndicated March 29 CityNews summary says Pakistan’s foreign minister announced Islamabad would soon host U.S.-Iran talks.

## Key facts extracted
- WBUR says diplomats had recently been meeting in Geneva, but talks centered on a core deadlock: U.S. demand for zero enrichment versus Iran calling that a non-starter.
- WBUR reports Iran publicly rejected claims that significant progress was taking place even as Trump said weekend talks had gone well.
- The AP-syndicated CityNews summary says Pakistan’s foreign minister announced Islamabad would soon host talks between the U.S. and Iran.
- As of March 29, there were still active mediation/hosting efforts rather than total diplomatic closure.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- WBUR: “This week President Trump has said that the U.S. and Iran held talks over the weekend. He claimed the talks went well...”
- WBUR: “Iran has publicly rejected the idea that any significant talks are taking place.”
- WBUR: the talks were centered on zero enrichment, which “Iran has always said ... is a non-starter.”
- CityNews/AP: “Pakistan’s foreign minister says Islamabad will soon host talks between the US and Iran.”

## What is uncertain
- Whether Pakistan-hosted talks are concrete, scheduled, and likely to produce a public in-person bilateral/mediated meeting that resolves this market YES.
- Whether Trump’s progress claims reflect real diplomatic momentum or messaging/positioning amid conflict.
- Whether “host talks” means a resolution-qualifying meeting between authorized representatives in the same place, versus shuttle/indirect contacts or planned talks that never materialize.

## Why this source may matter
This is the clearest stress-test evidence. The market can point to fresh mediation and hosting signals, but the same late-March information set still shows severe disagreement over substance and confusion over whether progress claims are real.

## Possible impact on the question
Supports keeping YES above a pure base-rate floor because diplomacy remains alive. But it argues for discounting confidence because the road to a qualifying meeting still depends on several fragile assumptions all holding at once: talks actually occur, are in person, are between authorized reps, are publicly acknowledged, and survive military/political shocks.

## Reliability notes
WBUR here is mainly useful for interview/transcript framing and reported recent context, not as a formal primary-source readout. CityNews is just a thin AP summary; high value as a timestamped indication that Pakistan publicly claimed hosting intent, low value for operational detail.
