# Summary

ABC reported on March 29 that Pakistani Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar said Pakistan would host and facilitate meaningful U.S.-Iran talks "in coming days." The same live update also quoted Trump saying negotiations with Iran were still ongoing, both directly and indirectly, and that he could see a deal coming "soon."

## Key facts extracted

- Pakistan publicly claimed it would host and facilitate U.S.-Iran talks in coming days.
- ABC reported the announcement followed a meeting in Islamabad involving Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, and Egypt foreign ministers.
- ABC noted neither the U.S. nor Iran had confirmed timing, whether talks would be direct or indirect, or exact participants.
- Trump told reporters negotiations with Iran were still happening "directly and indirectly" and said a deal could come "soon," while also acknowledging a deal might not happen.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- "Pakistan ... would host and facilitate meaningful talks between the two sides in coming days."
- The U.S. and Iran had not confirmed timing, format, or participants.
- Trump said negotiations were still ongoing, directly and indirectly.
- Trump said he could see a deal with Iran coming soon.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the reported talks satisfy the market's exact resolution standard for a deliberate diplomatic meeting.
- Whether any qualifying interaction will be direct or only indirect via intermediaries.
- Whether the market deadline is actually June 30, 2026 as the title suggests or the March 30, 2026 resolve timestamp shown in the dispatch metadata.

## Why this source may matter

This is one of the clearest public indications that the path the market seems to be pricing is not purely speculative: both a third-party facilitator and the U.S. president are publicly describing active negotiations and near-term talks.

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports the market's high implied probability because it suggests diplomatic contact is active and a mediated meeting may already be in planning. It does not fully settle the market because official bilateral confirmation and exact meeting form remain unclear.
