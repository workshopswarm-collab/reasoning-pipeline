# Assumption

A sub-2% upside move from current ETH spot to a Binance 1-minute high of $2,400 is sufficiently common over a six-day window that the market's 76% Yes price is broadly reasonable.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability view depends less on a deep fundamental ETH thesis than on whether ordinary short-horizon crypto volatility is usually enough to touch a nearby upside barrier.

## What this assumption supports

- A roughly agree stance versus the market.
- An own estimate in the high-60s to low-80s range rather than a sharply contrarian call.
- The interpretation that the market may already be efficiently pricing contract mechanics, not just directional sentiment.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ETH was trading around $2,356-$2,365 during research.
- The required move to hit $2,400 was only about 1.5%-1.9%.
- Kraken's reported 24h range already showed a move of well over that size from the daily open.
- The contract resolves on any Binance 1-minute high, which lowers the bar versus requiring a sustained move or weekly close.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp macro or crypto-specific drawdown that moves ETH materially away from the barrier and compresses realized upside volatility.
- Evidence that Binance ETH/USDT trades unusually differently from broad ETH spot during the relevant window.
- New information showing the rules are narrower than the embedded text indicates.

## Early warning signs

- ETH loses the $2,300 area quickly and remains far below it.
- Realized volatility compresses despite the short weekly window.
- Broad risk sentiment turns decisively negative across BTC and ETH.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move down materially, because the case for Yes is mostly mechanical proximity plus volatility rather than a high-conviction directional catalyst.
