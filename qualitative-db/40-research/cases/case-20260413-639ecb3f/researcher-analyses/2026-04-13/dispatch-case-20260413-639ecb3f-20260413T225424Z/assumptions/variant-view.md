# Assumption

The market may be slightly overpricing the final ~1.5-2.0% move from current ETH spot into a Binance 1-minute high at $2,400 because near-threshold weekly hit markets often look easier than they are once short-term breakout attempts fail.

## Why this assumption matters

The variant view depends on distinguishing “close to the threshold” from “likely to print the threshold.” If that gap is small, the market price near 79% is fair; if failed breakout dynamics are being underweighted, the true probability can sit lower even with ETH already trading in the mid-$2300s.

## What this assumption supports

- A modest under-market estimate rather than a strong bearish call.
- The claim that this is not an obvious 80%+ contract despite the small remaining dollar gap.
- The idea that touched-high contracts can still be fragile when the threshold becomes a visible sell zone.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ETH was around $2,358 at review time, so the remaining move was only about $42, but not yet achieved on the governing venue.
- The contract is week-long and touch-based, which mechanically raises Yes odds, but the market may compress the distinction between “very reachable” and “already likely enough to deserve high-70s pricing.”
- Crypto frequently overshoots and reverses around obvious round-number resistance; a market can be directionally right but a little too confident.

## What would falsify it

- Binance ETH/USDT prints $2,400 early in the week.
- ETH establishes sustained trade materially above the high-$2300s soon after review, showing the threshold was not meaningful resistance.
- Additional independent context (flows, macro catalyst, exchange-specific order book evidence) indicates unusually strong continuation odds.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid push through the current daily high into the $2375-$2390 area.
- Broad crypto beta and ETH outperforming BTC on the same day.
- Repeated retests of the upper range without sharp rejection.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The proper conclusion shifts from “slightly under market” to “roughly agree with market or even mild over-market Yes.” The strongest variant angle would disappear because the contract would then look mostly mechanical.
