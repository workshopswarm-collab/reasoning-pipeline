# Assumption

The key assumption is that recent upside momentum and normal crypto intrawindow volatility will create at least one Binance 1-minute ETH/USDT high at or above 2400 before Apr 19 ends ET, even without a single discrete scheduled catalyst.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends more on path and threshold-touch mechanics than on a settled end-of-week close above 2400. If momentum stalls completely, the contract can still miss despite ETH being close in spot terms at assignment time.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above 50% for the 2400 touch.
- A view that current price action itself is the dominant catalyst.
- A conclusion that the market is directionally reasonable but somewhat rich at 0.76.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ETH was already trading in the mid-2350s, leaving only a small additional move to tag 2400.
- Recent 7-day performance exceeded the remaining required move.
- Crypto trades continuously and often overshoots round-number thresholds on momentum bursts.
- The contract resolves on any 1-minute candle high, which is easier to satisfy than a sustained close or end-of-period level.

## What would falsify it

- Rapid reversal back toward low 2200s with no recovery.
- Clear regime change toward risk-off conditions that compress crypto upside for the rest of the window.
- Evidence that the recent rally was a one-off squeeze with exhausted follow-through.

## Early warning signs

- Failure to retest the 2360s after the initial surge.
- Repeated rejection well below 2400 with weakening volume.
- Broader crypto beta fading while ETH underperforms BTC and majors.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move clearly below the market and likely below 50%, because the thesis is not based on a fundamental event calendar strong enough to independently force repricing higher this week.
