# Assumption
A 3,200-theater wide horror launch with mixed reviews and recognizable-but-not-proven reboot/IP appeal is more likely to behave like the $10m–$12m cluster of recent genre comps than like the $24.5m Evil Dead Rise upside case.

## Why this assumption matters
The probability estimate for the 10m–15m bracket depends on treating Evil Dead Rise as the upside exception rather than the central comparable. If that assumption is wrong, the chance of finishing above $15m rises sharply.

## What this assumption supports
- A modest lean toward the 10m–15m bracket as plausible but not overwhelmingly likely.
- A view that the market’s 70% implied probability is somewhat high.
- A capped confidence level because franchise recognition could still lift the film above the base-rate cluster.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- Night Swim, Imaginary, and Abigail all opened near $10m–$12m in similar 3,100–3,400 theater footprints.
- Metacritic/Rotten Tomatoes indicate mixed reception, which is not the profile of a breakout horror event.
- The Mummy branding helps awareness, but this version appears to be a darker, narrower horror reboot rather than a broad action-adventure event.

## What would falsify it
- Strong pre-release Thursday preview number or Friday gross indicating materially higher than comp-set demand.
- Evidence of unusually strong marketing traction or audience urgency closer to release.
- Final weekend gross materially above $15m on The Numbers.

## Early warning signs
- Trade reporting or Friday estimates framing the opening as a breakout.
- Box Office Mojo and The Numbers both showing momentum stronger than the mid-tier horror comp cluster.
- Social or audience response indicating word-of-mouth is much stronger than critics suggested.

## What changes if this assumption fails
The probability for the 10m–15m bracket should fall, with the over-$15m outcome becoming the dominant alternative.
