# Assumption
Pre-release tracking centered around roughly 15m means the decisive risk to the 10m-15m bracket is boundary behavior and late demand, not a complete miss into single digits or a clear breakout well above 20m.

## Why this assumption matters
The market contract is unusually sensitive to a narrow band and explicitly sends exact boundary values to the higher bracket, so a thesis on the 10m-15m range depends heavily on whether the film lands just below or at/above 15m.

## What this assumption supports
- A modestly below-market probability for the 10m-15m bracket.
- Emphasis on preview/Friday reporting as the key repricing catalyst.
- Focus on settlement mechanics rather than broad franchise discourse.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- Same-day contextual tracking clusters around 15m-20m, with one cited range at 14m-20m and point estimate at 15m.
- The official release is imminent, narrowing uncertainty to near-term demand realization.
- Horror openings can move meaningfully on word of mouth and walk-up demand, but those moves commonly shift outcomes by a few million dollars around the central range rather than producing extreme surprises absent a major review/event shock.

## What would falsify it
- Thursday preview or Friday gross signals implying a sub-10m or comfortably above-17m/-18m trajectory.
- A sharp critical/audience reaction swing that clearly changes turnout expectations.
- A more authoritative direct tracking source showing consensus materially below 14m or above 17m.

## Early warning signs
- Weak preview comps or soft Friday matinee reporting.
- Rapid deterioration in review or social sentiment close to release.
- Distribution/showtime signals suggesting fewer screens or weaker premium-format utilization than expected.

## What changes if this assumption fails
If the film trends clearly below 10m or clearly above 15m, the bracket case weakens sharply and the main question becomes which adjacent range captures the opener. The current slight lean against the bracket would need to be revised quickly.
