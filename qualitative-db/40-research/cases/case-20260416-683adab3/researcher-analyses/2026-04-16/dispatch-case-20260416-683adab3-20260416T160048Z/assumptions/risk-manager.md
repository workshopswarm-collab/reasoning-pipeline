# Assumption

The market’s 0.70 pricing is implicitly assuming that a wide-release floor plus recognizable horror/franchise branding is enough to keep the final 3-day The Numbers opening weekend inside the 10m-15m band rather than below it or above it.

## Why this assumption matters

That assumption carries most of the bullish case for the bracket because direct pre-release gross evidence is thin in this run. If the wide-release/franchise-floor assumption is weak, the market is probably too confident.

## What this assumption supports

- A midrange estimate centered near the target band.
- A view that the market is directionally plausible.
- A view that downside to sub-10m is real but not dominant.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Numbers confirms a domestic `Wide` release on April 17, 2026.
- The title has established studio/distributor backing and recognizable horror IP framing.
- Horror openings can cluster in this kind of mid-band when awareness is decent but breakout demand is uncertain.

## What would falsify it

- Credible tracking or previews indicating a very soft start pointing clearly below $10m.
- Evidence of poor theater count, weak marketing, or release-date crowding severe enough to cap demand.
- Stronger-than-expected previews or tracking implying a clear path above $15m.

## Early warning signs

- Lack of broad reporting or tracking mention immediately before release.
- Mixed title/distributor metadata across public sources.
- Weak theater-count visibility or absent exhibitor momentum.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If this assumption weakens, the estimate should move down first because thin pre-release evidence plus a noisy source environment raises the risk of a sub-10m miss more than it supports precision inside the band.
