# Assumption

The market is somewhat overconfident in the $10m-$15m middle bucket because pre-release uncertainty around horror openings is large enough that a narrow-range contract should trade materially below 70% absent strong current tracking evidence.

## Why this assumption matters

The variant case depends less on proving a specific alternative point estimate and more on arguing that the middle bracket is too narrow to deserve a 70% probability without stronger direct tracking support.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market probability estimate for the $10m-$15m outcome.
- A view that the market may be copying an intuitive "modest horror reboot opening" narrative without enough evidence precision.
- A recommendation to treat the current contract price as overconfident rather than obviously wrong-directional.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The contract is a **range** market, not an over/under, so both underperformance and overperformance break the trade.
- The authoritative source has not yet posted opening-weekend data; confidence therefore rests on pre-release inference rather than direct performance evidence.
- Horror openings can be lumpy because marketing resonance, franchise recognition, and opening-night turnout can produce wider-than-expected dispersion around the consensus guess.
- The release is wide through Warner Bros. with recognizable genre branding and major horror producers attached, which creates plausible upside-tail risk above $15m as well as downside-tail risk below $10m.

## What would falsify it

- Credible late-cycle tracking or presales evidence showing a tight, stable consensus centered in the low teens with little disagreement.
- Friday estimates that strongly pin a 3-day result inside the target band.
- A theater-count / preview pattern that meaningfully compresses the likely range around the current market bracket.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple independent trade reporters converge on a narrow low-teens call.
- Friday gross prints in a range that mechanically maps to roughly $10m-$15m after a standard horror weekend multiplier.
- Competitive slate evidence suggests neither breakout upside nor collapse downside is likely.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the likely outcome range is actually tighter than assumed, then the market's 70% price could be reasonable or even slightly cheap rather than overconfident.
