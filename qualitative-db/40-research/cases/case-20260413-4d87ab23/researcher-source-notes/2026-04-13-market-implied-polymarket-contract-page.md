# Summary

This note records the market's operative wording as fetched on 2026-04-13. The most important point is that a qualifying Yes requires not merely model existence or rumor, but a clearly announced next major V-series release that is publicly accessible to the general public by the stated deadline.

## Key facts extracted

- The fetched market page stated the market resolves Yes if the next DeepSeek V model is made available to the general public by April 15, 2026 at 11:59 PM ET.
- Intermediate versions such as V3.5 do not count.
- Versions such as V4 or V5 could count if they are the next major V-series successor.
- Derivative labels like V4-Lite, V4-Mini, V4-Exp, or V4-Preview do not count unless clearly positioned as the new flagship successor to V3.
- Public accessibility can include open beta or open rolling waitlist signups; closed beta or private access does not count.
- The primary resolution source is official information from DeepSeek, with additional verification from a consensus of credible reporting.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The page directly defines both qualifying and non-qualifying release categories.
- The page directly defines source-of-truth hierarchy.
- The page directly defines the timing deadline and public accessibility requirement.

## What is uncertain

- The assignment header referenced May 15, but the fetched market page and provided market description point to an April 15 ET deadline. This discrepancy itself is material and should be treated as a verification issue.
- The page does not define exactly how broad a "rolling waitlist" must be to count if signups are technically open but capacity is constrained.

## Why this source may matter

This contract text governs the resolution mechanics more than generic chatter about DeepSeek progress. In a rule-sensitive market, resolution wording can matter as much as product progress.

## Possible impact on the question

This source raises the bar for Yes. Mere evidence that DeepSeek is close, testing privately, or shipping a preview-style model is insufficient. A market priced around 84.5% is therefore assuming not only imminent development completion, but a specific public-access launch form before the deadline.

## Reliability notes

High reliability for contract interpretation as presented on-market, though final formal resolution could still depend on Polymarket's adjudication process if ambiguity arises.
