# Assumption

The market's 84.5% Yes price is implicitly assuming DeepSeek is close enough to launch its next major V-series flagship publicly within days, and that the launch will satisfy the contract's public-access and naming requirements rather than remaining a V3.x continuation, preview, or limited-access release.

## Why this assumption matters

The current price is difficult to justify unless traders believe both product readiness and contract-compliant public release mechanics are likely in the remaining window.

## What this assumption supports

- A high near-term Yes probability.
- The interpretation that absence of a current public V4 artifact is not especially informative because launch may be imminent.
- The idea that market participants may possess or infer soft signals of pending release cadence stronger than what is visible on public surfaces.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- DeepSeek remains visibly active on official/public development surfaces.
- The homepage prominently advertised an official V3.2 release, showing continued shipping cadence.
- The market may be extrapolating from recent release tempo and the firm's strong frontier-model narrative.
- A contract allowing open beta or open rolling waitlist lowers the operational threshold relative to full open-source weights release.

## What would falsify it

- No official DeepSeek public announcement of a next major V-series successor before the deadline.
- A release that is only preview/experimental/private and therefore non-qualifying.
- Official surfaces continuing to emphasize V3.x with no successor announcement into the final 24-48 hours.

## Early warning signs

- Continued absence of a flagship V4/V5 label on official channels.
- Leaks or chatter pointing to closed testing rather than open access.
- Confusion over whether a release is a successor to V3 or just another V3.x branch.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The high-Yes market case weakens sharply. The market would look overextended because the key embedded assumption is doing most of the work.
