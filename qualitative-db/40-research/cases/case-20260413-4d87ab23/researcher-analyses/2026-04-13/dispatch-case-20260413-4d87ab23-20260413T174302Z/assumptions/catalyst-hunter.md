# Assumption

If DeepSeek intended to launch a qualifying next V-series flagship by the deadline, at least one credible public-accessibility signal would likely already be visible on an official surface or a major public-distribution surface by 2026-04-13.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis leans No mainly because the deadline is very close and there is still no visible qualifying release signal. If DeepSeek is capable of an abrupt same-day public launch with no detectable precursor signal, the bearish timing inference becomes weaker.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market probability estimate.
- The view that the most relevant catalyst is an official DeepSeek public announcement/open-access rollout in the next ~48 hours.
- The interpretation that absence of visible rollout evidence this late is material, not noise.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Major model launches typically leave traces on official announcement channels, product pages, waitlists, API docs, GitHub, Hugging Face, or credible reporting before or at launch.
- Official DeepSeek surfaces currently show V3.x and other-family updates, not a V4 flagship announcement.
- Public-distribution checks do not show a visible V4 artifact.

## What would falsify it

- DeepSeek publicly announces V4 (or another clearly positioned successor to V3) before the deadline and makes it generally accessible through website, app, API, open beta, or open waitlist.
- Credible independent reporting confirms such access and matches official DeepSeek language.

## Early warning signs

- Sudden new official teaser or website/app splash page mentioning V4.
- Open signup/waitlist language on DeepSeek-controlled surfaces.
- New public model card or repo clearly naming the next V-series flagship.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move sharply upward, potentially above market if the launch clearly satisfies both naming/successor and public-access criteria.
