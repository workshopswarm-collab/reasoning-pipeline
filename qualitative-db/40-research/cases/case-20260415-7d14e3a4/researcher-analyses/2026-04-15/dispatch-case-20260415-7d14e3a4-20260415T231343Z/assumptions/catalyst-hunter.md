# Assumption

No major bearish catalyst or exchange-specific disruption large enough to push Binance BTC/USDT below 72,000 at the April 19 12:00 ET one-minute close will occur before resolution.

## Why this assumption matters

The current thesis depends less on BTC needing to rally and more on BTC avoiding a roughly 3.6% drawdown from the sampled spot level into a single specified minute at resolution.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability "Yes" view.
- A conclusion that current price cushion is likely sufficient.
- A view that timing risk is driven mainly by downside catalysts rather than upside catalysts.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot and recent minute / multi-hour context already place BTC materially above 72,000.
- With only four days remaining, the most plausible path to "No" is an abrupt macro, crypto-specific, or exchange-specific selloff rather than gradual drift.
- No concrete scheduled catalyst identified in this run obviously dominates near-term downside enough to overwhelm the current cushion.

## What would falsify it

- BTC/USDT falls below or near 72,000 before April 19.
- A clear bearish macro or crypto-specific catalyst emerges and reprices BTC materially lower.
- Binance-specific trading or data issues create resolution risk around the target candle.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the 74k area and begins trading persistently close to 72k.
- Sharp risk-off moves in correlated macro assets before the weekend.
- Operational issues or unusual dislocations on Binance versus other venues.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The thesis should move from high-probability "Yes" toward a more balanced or even negative view, because the entire edge here is the current cushion plus absence of a strong bearish catalyst.
