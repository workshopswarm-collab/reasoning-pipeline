# Assumption

The main assumption is that no new macro or crypto-specific shock large enough to push Binance BTC/USDT down more than roughly 4% will hit before the April 17 12:00 ET resolution minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC must rally further; it is that the current cushion above 72,000 is likely to survive until the exact resolving minute. That depends more on short-horizon volatility containment than on new bullish catalysts.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above the market-neutral baseline and still high in absolute terms.
- The view that the market is directionally correct to heavily favor Yes.
- The claim that the dominant repricing catalyst is a downside shock rather than a positive scheduled trigger.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was trading near 74.9k at verification time.
- The strike is about 2.9k below spot, giving some buffer.
- The remaining window is short enough that absent a shock, current regime persistence is a reasonable base assumption.
- No high-information scheduled catalyst was identified that would obviously re-rate BTC downward into the deadline.

## What would falsify it

- A fast BTC drawdown that takes Binance BTC/USDT back toward or below 72,000 before noon ET on April 17.
- A sudden macro risk-off event, exchange incident, or crypto-specific negative headline that materially breaks current price support.
- Evidence that Binance-specific pricing dislocates from broader market spot near the deadline.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the mid-74k area and begins spending time near 73k or lower.
- Broad risk assets sell off sharply overnight or into U.S. morning hours on April 16-17.
- Binance experiences operational issues, unusual spreads, or exchange-specific stress.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should fall quickly because this contract resolves on one exact minute, so late weakness matters disproportionately. If BTC trades near the strike into the final hours, the market becomes much more path-dependent and the current high-Yes framing becomes overstated.
