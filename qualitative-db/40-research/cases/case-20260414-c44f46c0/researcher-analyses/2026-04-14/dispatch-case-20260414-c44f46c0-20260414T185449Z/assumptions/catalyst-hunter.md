# Assumption

No near-term macro, regulatory, or exchange-specific shock between April 14 and the April 19 12:00 ET settlement minute will be large enough to push Binance BTC/USDT below 68,000 at the governing close.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC cannot trade down sharply intraday; it is that the probability of a large enough drawdown landing specifically on the governing settlement minute remains modest given the current starting level around 74k.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability despite the contract still being open for several days.
- A view that the market is directionally right to price the strike as very likely to finish above 68k.
- A catalyst-focused framing where only a limited set of negative shocks are material.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot during the run was about 74,093, leaving a cushion of roughly 6,093 above the strike.
- Recent daily Binance closes were consistently above 68k.
- The largest realistic repricing risk over five days usually requires a meaningful macro surprise, severe risk-off move, or crypto-specific operational/regulatory event rather than ordinary noise.

## What would falsify it

- BTC dropping below 68k on Binance before or near April 19 noon ET.
- A major adverse macro release or policy surprise that sharply tightens financial conditions.
- A severe exchange, stablecoin, or crypto-regulatory shock that forces deleveraging.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid loss of the 72k-70k region on heavy volume.
- Funding/liquidity stress and a fast deterioration in sentiment.
- Credible negative regulatory or exchange-operational headlines affecting crypto broadly.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The thesis would move from high-confidence Yes to a much more contested outcome, and the market-implied probability near the mid-90s would likely be too high.
