# Assumption

The base-rate estimate assumes no major exogenous shock hits BTC before the Apr 21 noon ET settlement window and that recent short-horizon volatility remains in the ordinary range implied by the last month of trading.

## Why this assumption matters

The Yes case relies heavily on BTC already trading above the strike. If the volatility regime changes sharply because of macro, regulatory, exchange, or geopolitical shock, the current buffer over 72,000 may be much less protective than the recent outside view suggests.

## What this assumption supports

- A moderate Yes lean rather than a near-certainty.
- Treating current spot and recent one-month path as informative priors for the April 21 noon ET print.
- Interpreting recent 4% downside risk as meaningful but not dominant.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTC/USDT is currently above the strike by about 4%.
- Recent daily path shows volatility, but not a persistent collapse trend into settlement.
- No direct evidence surfaced in this run of a scheduled, highly specific catalyst that clearly dominates the ordinary short-horizon prior.

## What would falsify it

- A large macro shock, policy headline, exchange incident, or crypto-specific event that pushes BTC materially lower before Apr 21.
- A sharp volatility expansion that makes a move below 72,000 substantially more likely than recent path behavior implies.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the low/mid-73,000 area and fails to recover quickly.
- Multiple high-volume daily closes back below 72,000 before settlement.
- Fresh exchange operational stress or market-wide risk-off headlines.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The outside-view probability should move down materially, and the market’s current ~80% Yes pricing could become too high rather than roughly fair.
