# Decision summary

A Bank of Russia key-rate decrease at the 24 April 2026 meeting remains the base case, but the swarm’s below-market view is only partially independently verified; the best synthesis is still below the 91.5% market price, though compressed upward from the swarm’s provisional center because official evidence supports continued easing but does not eliminate a live one-meeting pause risk.

## Why this may matter now

Market-implied probability is 0.915. My syndicated range is 0.82 to 0.88. That points to only a modest, fragile below-market edge rather than the swarm’s larger apparent one. The likely mispricing, if any, is that the market is compressing explicit official conditionality into near-certainty even though a one-meeting pause remains live.

## Shift versus swarm baseline

The provisional swarm center sat around the high-0.7s/low-0.8s, with lane estimates from 0.78 to 0.84. I move somewhat upward from that center because synthesis-stage verification confirmed that the official easing signal is real: the Bank has already cut twice, the March statement explicitly left room for a further cut at upcoming meetings, and the official discussion summary leans more dovish than hawkish overall. But I do not move all the way to market because the large swarm-vs-market gap was not independently verified strongly enough by outside consensus evidence; instead I compress toward market while preserving below-market caution.

## Edge verification status

Independent verification is medium, not high. I independently checked the official March 20 Bank of Russia decision, the governor’s follow-up statement, the 1 April Summary of the Key Rate Discussion, the official decision calendar, and the official key-rate history page. Those checks strongly verify settlement mechanics, the pre-meeting 15.00% baseline, and that official communications support continued easing while preserving pause risk. What remains weak is independent verification of the magnitude of any below-market edge: I do not have a strong truly external analyst-consensus read showing the market is clearly too high by double-digit points. That is why verification quality is medium rather than high.

## Compression toward market

Yes. The swarm’s apparent below-market edge was large enough to trigger heavy skepticism. Official-source verification confirmed the conditionality case, but it did not independently prove that the true probability should sit down near 0.78 to 0.80. Because the external/non-official confirmation of that larger edge is thin, I compressed the final range upward toward market rather than endorsing the full swarm discount.

## Timing and catalyst posture

The dominant catalyst is the 24 April decision package itself: key-rate release, forecast, and governor press conference. Before then, any official inflation, expectations, FX, or fiscal signal could narrow or widen the pause branch. My bias is that the edge, if any, is more likely to decay than widen absent fresh hawkish evidence, because time passing without adverse data tends to favor the continuation-cut narrative already priced by market.

## Key blockers

Main blockers are not contract ambiguity but verification quality and freshness sensitivity. Specifically: limited independent evidence for a large below-market edge; official evidence concentration in the same institution that will decide; and exposure to late pre-meeting data or guidance. There is no major source-of-truth blocker.

## Best countercase

Best surviving countercase: the market may be broadly right because the Bank has already cut in February and March, official communications describe underlying inflation near target-consistent territory net of tax distortions, and most official discussion points toward continued easing unless a new shock emerges. Market-implied and risk-manager represented this best.

## What would change the view

A fresh official signal before 24 April that easing remains likely absent surprise data, or strong broad analyst consensus overwhelmingly expecting a cut with little pause discussion, would move me closer to market. Conversely, hotter inflation, rising expectations, ruble weakness, more proinflationary fiscal news, or hawkish governor guidance would push me lower and validate a larger below-market gap.

## Recommended next action

Collect one strong independent preview/consensus source closer to 24 April, then hand to the decision-maker. If none appears, treat this as a modest below-market lean with limited edge quality rather than a major contrarian opportunity.
