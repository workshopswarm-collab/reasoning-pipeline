# Assumption

The April 24 meeting is more likely than not to deliver at least one additional 25-50 bp cut because the Board already resumed easing in March and the next major information package before resolution is the Bank of Russia's own April meeting/forecast release rather than an intervening shock that clearly argues for a pause.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is priced at an extreme probability, so most of the directional view depends on whether March's pivot is interpreted as the start of a continuing easing sequence or as a one-off adjustment before a pause.

## What this assumption supports

- A high but not near-certain probability of an April decrease.
- A view that the April 24 official decision package is the dominant repricing catalyst.
- A view that the market is directionally right but somewhat overconfident.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Bank of Russia cut in February and again in March, bringing the key rate from 16.00% to 15.00% by late March.
- March materials repeatedly describe cooling demand, easing labor tightness, and underlying inflation around 4-5% SAAR after temporary tax distortions.
- The board explicitly kept further cuts on the table for upcoming meetings.
- The next scheduled decision is only one meeting away, on April 24, and includes a medium-term forecast update.

## What would falsify it

- Official April communication or intervening data indicating February's disinflation was temporary and underlying inflation is reaccelerating.
- A strong external shock, fiscal loosening, or ruble/inflation-expectations deterioration that leads the board to prioritize caution.
- Policymaker guidance implying March was sufficient easing for now and that more evidence is needed beyond April.

## Early warning signs

- Public emphasis on elevated inflation expectations over realized disinflation.
- Messaging that external or fiscal uncertainty has become the dominant constraint.
- Evidence that consumer demand or price growth reaccelerated in March/early April.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability of an April cut would drop sharply toward a pause/no-change base case, and the market's current >90% pricing would look materially too high.
