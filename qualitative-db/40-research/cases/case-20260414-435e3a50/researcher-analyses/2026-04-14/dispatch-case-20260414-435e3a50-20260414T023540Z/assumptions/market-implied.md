# Assumption

The market is assuming that the disinflation and demand-cooling narrative described after the 20 March meeting will still be intact by 24 April, with no new shock large enough to force a pause.

## Why this assumption matters

A 91.5% implied probability is too high unless the March easing logic carries forward mostly unchanged into the April meeting.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability decrease outcome rather than a balanced cut/hold split.
- Treating the market as mostly efficient rather than stale.
- Keeping hold risk in the single digits to low teens instead of much higher.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Bank of Russia already cut in March.
- Official communication described underlying inflation around 4–5% annualised and softer demand.
- The April 1 discussion summary showed most internal discussants broadly saw easing inflation pressure, though with caveats.
- Governor Nabiullina said further cuts would be assessed at upcoming meetings, but not as a matter of course, which is consistent with a likely-but-not-certain cut path.

## What would falsify it

- A meaningful inflation re-acceleration before 24 April.
- Hawkish rhetoric from Bank of Russia officials indicating a likely pause.
- A fiscal-policy change implying materially more proinflationary borrowing/spending.
- A sharp external shock causing ruble weakness or broader inflation concern.

## Early warning signs

- Commentary stressing pause rather than continued easing.
- Evidence that January-February softness was mostly temporary payback after end-2025 purchases.
- Renewed rise in inflation expectations.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The cut would still be plausible, but the market price would look overextended and the hold probability should rise materially.
