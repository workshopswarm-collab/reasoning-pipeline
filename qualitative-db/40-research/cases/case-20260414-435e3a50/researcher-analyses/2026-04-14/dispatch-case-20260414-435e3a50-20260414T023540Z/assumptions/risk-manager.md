# Assumption

The market is implicitly assuming that the Bank of Russia will treat its March 2026 cut and guidance as the start of a continued easing path rather than as a one-off adjustment followed by a pause.

## Why this assumption matters

A market price above 90% embeds not just a directional view but very high confidence that little will interrupt the next step in the easing path before 24 April.

## What this assumption supports

- A high probability estimate for another April cut.
- The interpretation that March guidance is strong enough to carry through one meeting later.
- The view that no-cut risk is small rather than simply nonzero.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Bank of Russia cut in March.
- The March press release explicitly said it would assess the need for a further cut at upcoming meetings.
- Inflation was described as decelerating in a way consistent with additional easing.

## What would falsify it

- Official April-period communication showing that inflation expectations re-accelerated or that external-risk concerns outweighed easing momentum.
- A stronger-than-expected hawkish tone between meetings.
- Any indication that the March cut was mainly a calibration move rather than the start of a near-term easing sequence.

## Early warning signs

- Renewed emphasis on elevated inflation expectations.
- Stronger stress on proinflationary external shocks.
- Language that shifts from "further cut" optionality toward patience or data dependence.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If this assumption fails, the correct risk-manager view shifts from "April cut highly likely" to "cut favored but with meaningful pause risk," and the market's >90% confidence would look too high.
