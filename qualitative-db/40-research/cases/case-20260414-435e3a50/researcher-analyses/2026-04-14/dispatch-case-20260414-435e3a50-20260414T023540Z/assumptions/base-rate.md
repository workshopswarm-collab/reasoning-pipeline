# Assumption

The March 20 easing step reflects a genuine but still conditional easing cycle, so absent a material negative inflation surprise or new external shock the Bank of Russia is somewhat more likely than not to cut again on April 24, but not close to certain.

## Why this assumption matters

The whole base-rate view depends on separating "easing cycle has begun" from "next meeting cut is effectively precommitted." If that distinction is wrong, the market may deserve its >90% confidence.

## What this assumption supports

- A forecast above 50% for another April cut.
- A probability estimate below the market-implied 91.5%.
- The view that a one-meeting pause remains a live tail risk rather than noise.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The official March 20 release explicitly says the Bank will assess the need for a further cut at upcoming meetings, which sounds conditional rather than automatic.
- The April 1 discussion summary shows mixed internal views about whether inflation is durably slowing and whether weak early-2026 demand is temporary.
- Central banks commonly ease in steps but retain optionality meeting to meeting.

## What would falsify it

- Clear official or quasi-official signaling before April 24 that another cut is effectively decided.
- Fresh inflation and demand data showing renewed price pressure or a sharp rebound that makes a pause much more likely.
- Evidence that the Bank of Russia almost never pauses once it starts easing in a comparable inflation regime.

## Early warning signs

- Official commentary stressing inflation expectations or external risks more than disinflation.
- New macro data showing January-February weakness was transitory and March-April demand reaccelerated.
- Public analyst consensus shifting toward hold.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the easing cycle is effectively locked in, the fair probability should move much closer to the market, potentially into the low-to-mid 90s. If instead the March cut was mostly a one-off adjustment, the fair probability could fall materially below 50%.
