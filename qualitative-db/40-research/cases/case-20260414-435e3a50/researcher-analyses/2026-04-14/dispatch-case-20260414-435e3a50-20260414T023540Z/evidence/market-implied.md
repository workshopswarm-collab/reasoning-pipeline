# Summary

The evidence still leans clearly toward another Bank of Russia cut in April, but the official sources preserve enough conditionality that a 100% style reading would be too aggressive.

## Question being evaluated

Will the Bank of Russia decrease the key rate at its 24 April 2026 meeting relative to the pre-meeting level?

## Current lean

Likely decrease.

## Prior / starting view

Start from the market prior of 91.5% for a decrease.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- March 20 Bank of Russia press release: direct, high-weight. The Bank cut to 15.00% and said it would assess the need for a further cut at upcoming meetings depending on inflation slowdown sustainability and risks.
- Governor Nabiullina's 20 March statement: direct, medium-high weight. She described cooling demand and moderate underlying inflation, which is the macro setup that rationalizes another cut.
- April 1 Key Rate Discussion summary: direct-but-backward-looking, medium-high weight. Most discussants saw underlying inflation around 4–5% SAAR net of VAT effects and an economy nearing balanced growth.
- April meeting is scheduled and resolves off official Bank of Russia output, reducing contract ambiguity.

## Evidence against the claim

- Official communications repeatedly stress uncertainty in the external environment and fiscal policy parameters; this is the strongest hold-risk mechanism.
- The April 1 discussion summary shows some participants doubted the data were sufficient to declare durable inflation easing.
- Nabiullina explicitly said future cuts would not occur as a matter of course.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- Weaker consumer demand can mean desirable cooling or merely temporary payback after late-2025 purchases.
- Middle East / external-price effects can support Russian export earnings in the short run while still raising global and domestic inflation risk later.

## Conflict between inputs

The main disagreement is interpretive rather than factual: whether the March/early-April data imply a near-automatic continuation of easing or merely a probable continuation with meaningful pause risk.

## Key assumptions

- No major inflation or ruble shock occurs before the meeting.
- The March disinflation signal remains broadly valid after more complete April data arrive.
- Fiscal-policy news does not materially tighten the Bank's reaction function.

## Key uncertainties

- How policymakers will interpret any new inflation prints before 24 April.
- Whether external and fiscal uncertainties intensify.
- Whether the market is overweighting trend continuation from the prior cuts.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Hawkish official rhetoric ahead of the meeting.
- Inflation data meaningfully above the Bank's comfort zone.
- Ruble weakness or supply-shock headlines that feed imported inflation concerns.

## What would increase confidence

- Fresh official rhetoric reaffirming easing bias.
- Additional evidence that inflation expectations are easing, not merely stable.
- Analyst consensus showing only marginal dissent rather than a split outlook.

## Net update logic

The market prior starts high and official evidence mostly supports it. But the same official materials also leave a plausible pause pathway, so the price looks directionally right yet somewhat overconfident.

## Suggested downstream use

Use as synthesis input for weighing whether the market is efficient versus slightly overextended, not for a strong contrarian override.
