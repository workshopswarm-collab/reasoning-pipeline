# Summary

The evidence leans clearly toward an April cut, but the main risk-manager objection is that the market price appears to convert plausible policy continuity into near-certainty.

## Question being evaluated

Will the Bank of Russia decrease the key rate after the 24 April 2026 meeting, relative to the level before that meeting?

## Current lean

Yes, a decrease is more likely than not and is the base case.

## Prior / starting view

Given the market at 0.915, the starting baseline was that a cut was already strongly expected and needed stress-testing rather than headline confirmation.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- Bank of Russia 20 March 2026 press release: cut the key rate by 50 bp to 15.00% and explicitly preserved room for a further cut at upcoming meetings.
  - Direct evidence.
  - High weight because it is the latest official policy signal before April.
- Same March release described inflation slowdown and a cooling economy.
  - Direct evidence.
  - Medium-high weight because it gives the mechanism for easing continuity.

## Evidence against the claim

- The March release also said uncertainty regarding the external environment had increased considerably.
  - Direct evidence.
  - Medium weight because it preserves a live pause scenario.
- The March release said proinflationary risks still prevailed over disinflationary ones on the medium-term horizon.
  - Direct evidence.
  - Medium weight because it warns that the easing path is conditional, not mechanical.
- The contract is date-specific and resolves only on the April 24 official decision, so any policy-continuation narrative can still fail on timing even if the broader easing cycle continues later.
  - Resolution-mechanics evidence.
  - Medium weight.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- The Bank's statement that it will assess the need for a further cut is supportive, but it is still conditional language rather than forward guidance guaranteeing an April move.

## Conflict between inputs

There is little factual conflict in the available inputs. The main disagreement is weighting-based: whether March guidance should justify a merely high probability or an extremely high one.

## Key assumptions

- March was the start of a near-term easing sequence rather than a one-off adjustment.
- No intervening inflation or external-risk shock will be large enough to force a pause by April.

## Key uncertainties

- How the Bank will weight external-environment risk by April 24.
- Whether inflation expectations stay benign enough to support a second consecutive cut.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Any official communication leaning hawkish on external risk or inflation expectations.
- Any indication the Board sees March as sufficient easing for now.

## What would increase confidence

- An official April-period summary or governor remark clearly signaling continued disinflation and comfort with another cut.

## Net update logic

The official March decision makes a cut the clear base case. The risk-manager adjustment is not to reject that base case, but to mark that a 91.5% market price likely underweights conditionality and timing risk embedded in the same official statement.
