# Summary

This primary source shows that the Bank of Russia already cut the key rate by 50 bp on 20 March 2026 and explicitly left the door open to further cuts at upcoming meetings, including 24 April 2026.

## Key facts extracted

- On 20 March 2026, the Bank of Russia cut the key rate by 50 bp to 15.00%.
- The Bank said it would assess the need for a further key-rate cut at upcoming meetings.
- The Bank linked any further cut to sustainability of the inflation slowdown, inflation expectations, and risks from external and domestic conditions.
- The Bank still said uncertainty regarding the external environment had increased considerably.
- The next key-rate meeting was scheduled for 24 April 2026.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Direct quote anchor: the Bank "will assess the need for a further key rate cut at its upcoming meetings depending on the sustainability of the inflation slowdown, the dynamics of inflation expectations, and the analysis of risks posed by external and domestic conditions."
- Direct quote anchor: "uncertainty regarding the external environment has increased considerably."
- Direct quote anchor: proinflationary risks still prevail over disinflationary ones on the mid-term horizon.

## What is uncertain

- The source does not commit to an April cut; it only preserves optionality.
- The source does not quantify the probability of another cut one meeting later.
- The source does not resolve how much weight the Board would place on worsening external conditions between March and April.

## Why this source may matter

This is the clearest official pre-April policy signal available in the assignment window. It matters because it creates a credible base case for another cut while also preserving obvious failure modes.

## Possible impact on the question

The source supports a high probability of an April decrease, but not certainty. For a risk-manager view, the key point is that the same source that supports the bullish cut thesis also explicitly preserves a no-cut path if inflation expectations or external risks worsen.

## Reliability notes

Very high reliability as the governing central-bank press release for the latest meeting before April. Low ambiguity on what was said; medium ambiguity on how to map that guidance into a one-meeting-ahead probability.
