# Summary

This official Bank of Russia press release is the strongest direct policy signal heading into the April 24 meeting.

## Key facts extracted

- On 20 March 2026 the Bank of Russia cut the key rate by 50 bp to 15.00%.
- The release said the economy was approaching a balanced growth path.
- It said February price growth decelerated after a temporary January acceleration.
- The Bank estimated underlying current price growth remained in the 4–5% annualised range.
- It explicitly said it would assess the need for a further key-rate cut at upcoming meetings depending on sustainability of inflation slowdown, inflation expectations, and external/domestic risks.
- It scheduled the next key-rate meeting for 24 April 2026.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The source directly states both the latest action (a cut) and the conditional forward guidance for another possible cut. It also directly identifies the main caution flags: elevated inflation expectations and uncertainty in the external environment.

## What is uncertain

The press release does not promise another cut. Its language is conditional rather than pre-committed, and it leaves room for a pause if inflation, expectations, fiscal policy, or external conditions worsen.

## Why this source may matter

This is the governing primary policy signal from the same institution that will decide the April meeting. It explains why a market would heavily lean toward another cut while still reserving some probability for no change.

## Possible impact on the question

This source materially supports the market's high implied probability of a decrease, but not quite certainty, because it combines an actual March cut with explicit conditionality for future cuts.

## Reliability notes

High reliability for what the Bank communicated on 20 March. Lower reliability as a deterministic predictor of the 24 April decision because the guidance is explicitly data- and risk-contingent.
