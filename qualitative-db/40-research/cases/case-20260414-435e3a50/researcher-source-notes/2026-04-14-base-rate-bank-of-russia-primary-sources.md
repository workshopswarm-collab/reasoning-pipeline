# Summary

This note captures the official Bank of Russia materials most directly relevant to the April 24, 2026 decision market.

## Key facts extracted

- The governing resolution source is the Bank of Russia’s official April 24, 2026 key-rate publication on the central bank calendar.
- The March 20, 2026 official press release says the Bank cut the key rate by 50 bp to 15.00%.
- The same release says the Bank "will assess the need for a further key rate cut at its upcoming meetings depending on the sustainability of the inflation slowdown, the dynamics of inflation expectations, and the analysis of risks posed by external and domestic conditions."
- The March release also says the next key-rate meeting is on April 24, 2026, with the press release and medium-term forecast scheduled for publication at 13:30 Moscow time.
- The official key-rate history page shows the key rate remained 15.00% through at least April 13, 2026, confirming the pre-meeting reference rate.
- The April 1, 2026 Summary of the Key Rate Discussion shows internal debate was not one-sided: most discussants saw underlying inflation around 4–5% SAAR net of VAT effects, but some argued data were insufficient and that consumption could reaccelerate, preventing further disinflation.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Direct settlement mechanics: the official Bank of Russia calendar and press-release schedule govern what counts.
- Direct policy signal: after the March cut, the Bank explicitly left open a further cut at upcoming meetings rather than locking in another move.
- Direct caution signal: the Bank also highlighted elevated inflation expectations, external uncertainty, and proinflationary risks that could justify a pause.

## What is uncertain

- The official materials do not precommit to an April cut.
- The discussion summary shows mixed internal interpretation of the inflation data, so the path from March to April is still conditional.
- The scraped official publications page was hard to parse cleanly, so this note relies mainly on the specific press release, discussion summary, calendar page, and key-rate history page rather than a reconstructed full meeting history table.

## Why this source may matter

This is the primary source set for both contract interpretation and current policy signaling. It is the best evidence for what the Bank has actually said and what publication will determine resolution.

## Possible impact on the question

These sources support a high probability of another cut because the easing cycle is already underway and the March language explicitly leaves room for another reduction soon. But they also cap confidence below near-certainty because the Bank tied any further cut to continued disinflation and risk assessment, and the April 1 discussion summary preserves a plausible pause scenario.

## Reliability notes

Reliability is high for settlement and for the Bank’s own stated reaction function. Reliability is lower for forecasting the April decision because official communications are conditional and not a commitment.
