# Summary

The Bank of Russia's own March 20 press release, governor statement, April 1 discussion summary, and the official 2026 calendar jointly establish the key catalyst chain for this market: the bank already cut 50 bp in March to 15.00%, explicitly left open further cuts at upcoming meetings, and identified April 24 as the next decision date with the official press release and medium-term forecast scheduled for publication that day.

## Key facts extracted

- On 20 March 2026, the Bank of Russia cut the key rate by 50 bp to 15.00%.
- The March press release says it will assess the need for a further cut at upcoming meetings depending on sustainability of inflation slowdown, inflation expectations, and external/domestic risks.
- The official calendar lists 24 April 2026 as the next Board of Directors key-rate meeting and says the press release and medium-term forecast will be published that day.
- The April 1 summary says most discussants saw underlying inflation around 4-5% SAAR after adjusting for tax distortions, while some thought the data were still insufficient for a confident conclusion.
- Governor Nabiullina said after the March cut that further cuts at upcoming meetings would not happen 'as a matter of course' because uncertainty around the external environment and fiscal-policy parameters had increased.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Direct resolution-relevant timing: April 24 meeting date and same-day publication of the press release/forecast.
- Direct policy-path guidance: further cuts remain possible, but are conditional rather than pre-committed.
- Direct internal split/uncertainty: some policymakers viewed underlying inflation as already around target-consistent levels, while others wanted more evidence because demand could reaccelerate and inflation expectations remained elevated.
- Direct risk framing: external environment and fiscal-rule/budget choices are key uncertainties that could restrain easing.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the incoming April data will confirm that February's disinflation was durable rather than a temporary VAT-related normalization.
- How much weight the board will place on elevated inflation expectations and external/fiscal uncertainty versus the evidence of cooling demand.
- Whether policymakers view the April meeting as too soon for another cut after March's move, despite leaving the door open.

## Why this source may matter

This is the governing primary source set for both the market's resolution mechanics and the immediate catalyst path. It directly states what event resolves the contract, what timing matters, and what conditions the board itself says will govern another cut.

## Possible impact on the question

These materials make a further April cut plausible but not automatic. The most important repricing catalyst before resolution is the April 24 Bank of Russia decision package itself; the main soft pre-catalyst is whether any intervening data or messaging shift the balance between 'continued disinflation/cooling demand' and 'uncertainty/elevated expectations argue to pause.' Given the conditional language, the market should not treat March's cut alone as dispositive proof of an April follow-through.
