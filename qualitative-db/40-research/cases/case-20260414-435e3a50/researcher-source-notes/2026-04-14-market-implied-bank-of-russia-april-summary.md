# Summary

This Bank of Russia discussion summary gives the clearest internal rationale behind the March cut and shows why April is more likely another cut than an automatic one.

## Key facts extracted

- Most discussants thought underlying inflation in January-February was roughly 4–5% SAAR net of VAT effects, with some seeing room for it to approach 4%.
- Some discussants argued data were still insufficient to conclude underlying inflation had durably eased.
- Consumer demand appeared weaker than expected, but there was disagreement over whether that weakness was one-off or persistent.
- Labour-market tightness was easing gradually but not fully gone.
- Fiscal-rule and budget-parameter uncertainty had not yet been resolved.
- The summary notes that output-gap closure may be happening faster than expected, but some participants considered that conclusion premature.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The source directly shows internal debate: baseline disinflation/cooling supports further easing, while uncertainty around consumer demand persistence, fiscal policy, and inflation interpretation leaves a plausible pause scenario.

## What is uncertain

This is a backward-looking summary of the March decision discussion, based on data available as of 20 March 2026. It is not a direct preview of the April 24 vote and may miss new information arriving after 20 March.

## Why this source may matter

It helps decode what the market may be pricing: the committee appears mostly comfortable with further easing but not locked into it. That supports a very high cut probability, though probably not a 99%+ level.

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports a strong lean toward a decrease because the internal policy discussion was already centered on moderating inflation and softer demand, but it also provides the strongest direct official basis for a nontrivial hold probability.

## Reliability notes

High credibility as an official Bank of Russia summary. Moderate predictive completeness because it is anchored to 20 March data and is not a real-time April guidance document.
