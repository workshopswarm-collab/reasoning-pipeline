# Summary

Trading Economics describes the March 2026 Bank of Russia move as a 50bp cut to 15.0%, says this was the seventh consecutive cut from the 2024 peak, and notes a mixed macro backdrop: softer underlying inflation and slower activity supported easing, but the source also flags ongoing proinflationary risks. This matters as contextual evidence because it supports a consensus story that another cut is plausible, while also showing why a hold remains the strongest variant case.

## Key facts extracted

- The page says the Bank of Russia cut the key rate by 50bp to 15.0% in March 2026.
- It frames the March cut as part of a multi-meeting easing cycle from 21%.
- It attributes the cut to slower underlying inflation and slower economic activity.
- It also states that the central bank warned it may not extend the cutting cycle because of proinflationary risks.
- The page's forecast section says analysts/models expect 16.0% by quarter end, which is directionally inconsistent with an immediate follow-on cut and therefore is a useful reminder that secondary aggregators can be internally noisy.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- March 2026 was a cut to 15.0%.
- Easing has already been substantial.
- Inflation/growth dynamics were soft enough to justify the prior cut.
- Proinflationary risks are still present.

## What is uncertain

- The page is a secondary aggregator, not the official settlement source.
- Some explanatory details and forecasts may reflect editorial synthesis or model output rather than a clean official forecast path.
- The apparent inconsistency between a recent easing narrative and a higher quarter-end forecast limits confidence in precise directional inference.

## Why this source may matter

It captures the broad consensus frame likely embedded in a 91.5% market price for "decrease": Russia is already cutting and the easing cycle may continue. At the same time, the same source preserves the most credible alternative narrative: the Bank may pause because remaining inflation risks make an additional immediate cut less automatic than the market implies.

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports a high probability of another cut, but not near certainty. It strengthens the view that "No decrease" is mainly a pause-risk variant rather than a full reversal of the easing regime.

## Reliability notes

Moderate reliability as contextual evidence, not as source of truth. Useful because it reflects the broad macro-consensus narrative, but it is not independent of market framing and contains some internal forecast ambiguity.
