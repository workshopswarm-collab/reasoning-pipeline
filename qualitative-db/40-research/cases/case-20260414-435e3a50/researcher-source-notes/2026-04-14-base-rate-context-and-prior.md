# Summary

This note records the outside-view/context pass used to test whether a 91.5% market-implied probability is too extreme for a one-meeting central-bank move that is not explicitly precommitted.

## Key facts extracted

- The market-implied probability from current_price 0.915 is 91.5% for a decrease.
- The official March 20 Bank of Russia release already delivered a 50 bp cut to 15.00%, so the easing cycle has started.
- However, the official language conditions any further cut on continued inflation slowdown, inflation expectations, and external/domestic risks, which means the April meeting remains a live decision rather than a mechanical follow-through.
- A contextual verification pass using search results surfaced examples from April 2025 coverage showing the Bank of Russia sometimes held rates unchanged even while inflation was easing and market participants were focused on eventual easing. Reuters, Interfax, and Trading Economics snippets all pointed to an April 2025 hold at 21%.
- That prior does not prove April 2026 will be a hold, but it argues against treating the next-meeting cut as virtually locked in.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Polymarket price implies the market strongly expects a cut.
- Secondary contextual snippets indicate there is precedent for the Bank of Russia pausing at an April meeting despite growing easing expectations.

## What is uncertain

- I did not obtain a fully archived independent analyst-consensus page for April 2026, so the contextual pass is lighter than ideal.
- Search-engine snippets are weaker than full-article fetches; they are used only as a secondary verification layer, not as the primary basis of the forecast.
- The exact historical frequency of consecutive Bank of Russia cuts after the first easing step was not reconstructed from a clean downloadable time series in this run.

## Why this source may matter

For a base-rate persona, the main job is to resist overconfidence. The relevant lesson is that central banks often move in conditional increments and can pause even after beginning an easing cycle.

## Possible impact on the question

This context supports trimming probability below the market. It does not overturn the directional case for a cut, but it is enough to reject near-certainty absent stronger evidence such as explicit forward guidance, a broad analyst-consensus check, or fresh April inflation data decisively pointing to another move.

## Reliability notes

Reliability is medium at best. Market price is directly observed, but the historical-comparison layer here is based on contextual reporting/search snippets rather than full primary reconstruction. It is useful as an anti-overconfidence check, not as decisive evidence.
