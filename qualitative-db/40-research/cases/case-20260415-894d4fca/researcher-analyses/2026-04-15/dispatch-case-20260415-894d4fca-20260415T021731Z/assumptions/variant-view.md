# Assumption

The market is over-anchored to a perceived expiration deadline on or before April 19, 2026, even though Public Law 118-49 appears to push the operative sunset to April 20, 2026.

## Why this assumption matters

The bearish variant view depends on urgency being overstated. If Congress and the White House face less real deadline pressure than the market assumes, then passage by the market deadline becomes less likely than the 78.5% price implies.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market probability estimate for Yes.
- The claim that the strongest neglected mechanism is one-day timing/slippage and weaker real sunset pressure.
- The interpretation that the market may be pricing the generic "702 must be renewed" narrative rather than the exact statutory and contract timing.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Public Law 118-49 was enacted on Apr. 20, 2024.
- Section 19 of that law replaces the prior Apr. 19, 2024 sunset reference with "two years after the date of enactment," which maps to Apr. 20, 2026.
- If the legal sunset is Apr. 20 rather than Apr. 19, then a bill passed on Apr. 20 could be legally timely but still miss the market deadline.
- For a controversial surveillance measure, even a one-day reduction in urgency can matter because delay and brinkmanship are common.

## What would falsify it

- Congress.gov or another clearly superior official source explicitly showing the operative expiration is Apr. 19, 2026 rather than Apr. 20, 2026.
- Strong bipartisan leadership commitments or scheduled floor action clearly targeting enactment by Apr. 19, 2026.
- Evidence that the market description's inclusion of Public Law 118-49 effectively already resolves the market Yes or otherwise eliminates the need for new legislation.

## Early warning signs

- Committee or leadership calendars moving a clean extension vehicle before the deadline.
- Credible reporting that leadership has already locked in agreement and floor time.
- Official government guidance repeatedly using Apr. 19, 2026 as the true sunset.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the true urgency is not weaker than assumed, then the main variant thesis collapses and the market's high probability looks more justified. My estimate would move closer to market, likely into the high-70s or low-80s.
