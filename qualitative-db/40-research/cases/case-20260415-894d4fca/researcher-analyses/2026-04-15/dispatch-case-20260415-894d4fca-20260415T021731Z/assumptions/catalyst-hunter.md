# Assumption

The highest-probability path to a Yes resolution is a late clean or near-clean extension vehicle pushed by leadership rather than a fully renegotiated reform package.

## Why this assumption matters

My probability estimate depends heavily on whether Congress can move quickly under deadline pressure. A clean or near-clean extension is procedurally simpler and more plausible on the clock than reopening the full reform fight.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability above 50% despite acknowledged political friction.
- The view that the key catalyst is leadership scheduling rather than new substantive evidence about 702’s intelligence value.
- The view that the market is directionally right but may be underpricing deadline execution risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The House Intelligence chair publicly said on March 19 that the President was seeking an 18-month clean extension.
- Late-stage national-security deadlines often compress into leadership-driven extension vehicles rather than ideal policy bargains.
- The remaining calendar before expiration is short, which favors simpler legislative paths if action occurs at all.

## What would falsify it

- Credible evidence that leadership cannot or will not move a clean extension.
- A public Senate or House blockade making bicameral passage impossible before April 20.
- Emergence of a must-have reform demand that destroys the coalition for a time-limited extension.

## Early warning signs

- No visible bill vehicle or floor plan as the deadline approaches.
- Judiciary/libertarian opposition hardening into a blocking coalition.
- Public statements shifting from extension planning to post-expiry contingency talk.

## What changes if this assumption fails

My estimate would move materially lower because there is not enough time left for a complex bargain. Failure of the clean-extension path would make No the more likely outcome.
