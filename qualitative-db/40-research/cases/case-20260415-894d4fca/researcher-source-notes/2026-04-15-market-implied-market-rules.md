# Summary

The market description says the market resolves `Yes` if legislation that reauthorizes FISA Title VII including Section 702 is passed by both chambers and signed into law by April 19, 2026, and then explicitly adds that qualifying legislation includes `Public Law 118-49`.

## Key facts extracted

- Deadline: `April 19, 2026, 11:59 PM ET`.
- Qualifying legislation includes `Public Law 118-49`.
- Primary resolution sources: `Congress.gov's legislation tracker`, `the Library of Congress (congress.gov)`, and other official U.S. government information.
- Consensus credible reporting can also be used.
- The contract requires legislation to have passed both chambers and become law through normal enactment routes.

## Evidence directly stated by source

Directly from the market page text:
- `Qualifying legislation includes Public Law 118-49.`
- `This market will resolve to "Yes" if legislation that reauthorizes FISA Title VII, including Section 702, is passed by both chambers of the U.S. Congress and signed into law by April 19, 2026, 11:59 PM ET.`

## What is uncertain

- The market text references Congress.gov tracker `house-bill/22`, which appears mismatched with the already-enacted law specifically named as qualifying legislation.
- The note cannot independently verify whether Polymarket intended the market to hinge on a fresh 119th Congress vehicle or merely on whether qualifying legislation existed before expiry.

## Why this source may matter

This is the governing contract language for resolution. It matters more than generic media framing because the named inclusion of Public Law 118-49 is the central reason the market is priced near 79% instead of as a live legislative coin flip.

## Possible impact on the question

The explicit inclusion of Public Law 118-49 makes the market look closer to an interpretation/source-of-truth question than an open legislative forecasting question. That supports the idea that the market is probably pricing a high chance of eventual `Yes` resolution unless the platform interprets its own source references narrowly.
