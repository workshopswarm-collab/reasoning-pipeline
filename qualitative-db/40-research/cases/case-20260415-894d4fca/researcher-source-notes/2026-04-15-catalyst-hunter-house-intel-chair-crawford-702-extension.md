# Summary

This is a direct official signal from the House Intelligence Committee chair about the live legislative push on Section 702 roughly one month before expiration.

## Key facts extracted

- On March 19, 2026, Chairman Rick Crawford said FISA 702 "expires in just over a month."
- He said "The President is seeking an 18-month clean extension" to allow more time to assess implementation of prior reforms.
- He said he was working with the Ranking Member, House Leadership, and Judiciary Chairman Jordan to support that request.
- He framed the issue as an active authorization/oversight matter, not a settled legislative outcome.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- There was an explicit White House ask for an 18-month clean extension.
- Relevant House actors were actively coordinating around that request.
- The timing window was short as of March 19.

## What is uncertain

- The statement does not establish bill text, floor schedule, Senate support, or whether leadership would actually move a vehicle in time.
- It is advocacy from an interested official, so it is stronger on signaling than on passage odds.

## Why this source may matter

It is one of the best official timing/catalyst indicators available because it identifies the concrete near-term catalyst path: a clean extension backed by the President and key House intelligence actors.

## Possible impact on the question

This raises the probability that Congress attempts a late extension vehicle before April 20, 2026, but by itself does not prove bicameral passage and enactment before the market deadline.

## Reliability notes

Official congressional source with direct relevance to timing and legislative intent. Reliability is high for what Crawford said and for the existence of a push; lower for converting that push into enacted-law odds.
