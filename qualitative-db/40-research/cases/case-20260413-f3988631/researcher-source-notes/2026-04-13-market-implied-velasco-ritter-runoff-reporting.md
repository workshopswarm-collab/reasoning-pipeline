# Summary

EL DEBER reported after the first round that Juan Pablo Velasco and Otto Ritter advanced to a second round in Santa Cruz, with Velasco ahead in preliminary counts at the time cited.

## Key facts extracted

- EL DEBER says Velasco and Ritter advanced to the second round for the Santa Cruz governorship.
- The report cites TSE preliminary results (`Sirepre`) and notes these are statistically informative but not official final results.
- In the cited update, Velasco was ahead of Ritter in the preliminary count snapshot.
- The report says the runoff was scheduled for 19 April 2026.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Velasco and Ritter are the two runoff candidates.
- Velasco was ahead in the preliminary count snapshot cited.
- TSE itself warned preliminary results do not replace official results.

## What is uncertain

- This article is about first-round preliminary reporting, not final runoff polling or runoff vote intention.
- It does not prove Velasco is an overwhelming favorite in the runoff.

This is the clearest contextual public evidence found that public reporting had already converged on a Velasco–Ritter runoff with Velasco emerging from the first round in front. That is the strongest visible public case for why the market might price Velasco as the favorite.

## Possible impact on the question
