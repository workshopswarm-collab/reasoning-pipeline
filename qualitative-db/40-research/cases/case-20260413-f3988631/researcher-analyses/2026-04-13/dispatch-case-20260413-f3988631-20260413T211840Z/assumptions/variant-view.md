# Assumption

The market’s very high confidence in Juan Pablo Velasco is partly a narrative/consensus artifact rather than a fully re-verified estimate grounded in independent, current electoral evidence.

## Why this assumption matters

The main variant thesis is not that Velasco is unlikely, but that 80%+ may be too aggressive given limited independently verified evidence in hand and the market’s reliance on consensus reporting before official finality.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below the market.
- A view that the edge, if any, comes from overconfidence and process/timing fragility rather than from a strong alternative candidate call.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market rules themselves emphasize consensus credible reporting, with TSE/OEP official results as fallback in ambiguity.
- The official OEP surfaces confirm the electoral process and candidate/result infrastructure, but the easily retrievable public evidence gathered here does not by itself justify extreme certainty.
- When the market is at ~80% in a date-sensitive election market, thin independent verification can be enough reason to trim confidence without flipping the directional favorite.

## What would falsify it

- Multiple independent, recent, credible reports showing Velasco with a commanding and stable lead.
- Official or near-official first-round / runoff reporting making his victory overwhelmingly likely.
- Clean confirmation that there is no meaningful eligibility, coalition, or counting ambiguity left.

## Early warning signs

- Independent outlets converge on a still-tight contest.
- Administrative or runoff-process ambiguity becomes salient.
- Market price swings sharply on fresh reporting, implying prior confidence was stale.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the market has in fact been well anchored to strong, independent current reporting, then the proper estimate should move up toward the market level and the variant edge largely disappears.
