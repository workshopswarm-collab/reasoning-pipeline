# Assumption

The remaining meaningful catalyst path is mainly official and quasi-official reporting flow from the Santa Cruz second-round process rather than a major new campaign shock that would materially rerate Velasco before resolution.

## Why this assumption matters

The catalyst-hunter thesis depends on the idea that this market is now mostly a reporting-and-confirmation market, not a broad open-ended persuasion market.

## What this assumption supports

- Keeping Velasco as a clear favorite.
- Treating the biggest repricing trigger as official reporting quality and sequencing rather than a new policy or narrative event.
- A probability estimate only modestly below the market rather than a large bearish gap.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The election date in the contract is already in the past, so the market is now primarily about who actually won and how reporting converges.
- The official OEP surface is actively publishing second-round election materials for Santa Cruz.
- Additional light reporting checks did not surface a competing high-salience catalyst or a contradictory consensus headline strong enough to challenge the current favorite.

## What would falsify it

- Credible reporting that Otto Ritter is leading, has claimed victory with corroboration, or that official tallies point away from Velasco.
- A material official challenge, annulment, or reporting disruption affecting Santa Cruz's result path.
- Evidence that the market has misidentified the actual final-round candidate set.

## Early warning signs

- Divergent outlet reports about who is ahead.
- OEP delays, procedural disputes, or legal challenges.
- New publication of official count data inconsistent with the market leader.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case would shift from a confirmation/reporting catalyst frame to a contested-resolution frame, and Velasco's probability should fall materially.
