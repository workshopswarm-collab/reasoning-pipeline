# Assumption

The key case-specific polling and runoff matchup summarized on Polymarket metadata is directionally real and not a scraper or metadata artifact.

## Why this assumption matters

Without that assumption, the evidence base collapses back toward a generic runoff prior and much lower confidence about who is actually favored.

## What this assumption supports

- Treating Juan Pablo Velasco as the real frontrunner rather than merely the market ticker label.
- Keeping the estimate in the mid-to-high 70s instead of closer to a coin flip plus incumbency/coalition noise.
- Interpreting the market price as mostly grounded in real campaign information rather than pure trader reflex.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Polymarket page metadata is detailed and internally coherent: it names the runoff opponent, gives a pollster/media reference, and offers a plausible first-round narrative.
- Wikipedia election pages independently corroborate the runoff date and candidate-name presence in Santa Cruz election sections.
- Official OEP/TSE pages corroborate the existence of a Santa Cruz second round, which makes the overall Polymarket framing structurally plausible.

## What would falsify it

- An official Santa Cruz/OEP candidate page or credible local reporting showing a different runoff pair.
- Evidence that the cited poll or first-round margin is erroneous, fabricated, or about a different race.
- Rapid market repricing driven by correction of the matchup itself rather than normal late-campaign movement.

## Early warning signs

- Conflicting local-media reports about who advanced to the runoff.
- OEP candidate/result materials showing a materially different ranking or alliance structure.
- Sudden disappearance or correction of the Polymarket metadata summary.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The current base-rate view would become too bullish on Velasco. The estimate would likely need to fall materially and the case would need a fresh candidate-level verification pass before any confident directional call.
