# Assumption

The market's high confidence in Juan Pablo Velasco is broadly directionally correct, but it overstates certainty because clean official confirmation and late-runoff fragility are not fully observed in the evidence gathered here.

## Why this assumption matters

The probability estimate depends on separating directional advantage from overconfidence. If this assumption is wrong, the proper risk-manager stance could shift either toward the market or sharply away from it.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below the market price rather than matching it.
- Emphasis on certification/source-of-truth risk and reputational tail risk.
- A conclusion of `roughly agree on direction, disagree on confidence`.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Polymarket places Velasco around 80%, implying strong market confidence.
- Contextual reporting says Velasco reached the runoff, which is consistent with being a real contender.
- The official OEP surface confirms active second-round administration in Santa Cruz, but the evidence fetched here does not provide a clean official winner page.
- In a date-sensitive, consensus-reporting-dependent market, missing clean official confirmation is enough to discount extreme confidence somewhat.

## What would falsify it

- A clean OEP/TSE results page or multiple independent major outlets showing Velasco won decisively and uncontested.
- Evidence that the reputational controversy had no electoral salience or was definitively resolved in his favor.
- Evidence of reporting consensus so strong that official ambiguity is practically nonexistent.

## Early warning signs

- Delayed or messy official reporting.
- Contradictory media calls.
- Legal or procedural disputes around Santa Cruz runoff tabulation.
- Renewed attention to past controversial posts or other candidate-specific scandals.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If strong official confirmation appears, the estimate should move closer to or above the market. If instead reporting becomes disputed or adverse, the estimate should move materially lower because the current thesis relies on a relatively smooth win narrative.
