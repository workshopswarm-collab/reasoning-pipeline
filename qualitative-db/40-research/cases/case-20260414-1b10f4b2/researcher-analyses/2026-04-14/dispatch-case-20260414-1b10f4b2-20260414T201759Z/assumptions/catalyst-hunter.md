# Assumption

Between 2026-04-14 and the 2026-04-20 12:00 ET settlement minute, no scheduled macro catalyst comparable to CPI or an FOMC decision is likely to force a large enough BTC repricing to push Binance BTC/USDT back below 68,000.

## Why this assumption matters

The bullish lean depends less on long-run Bitcoin fundamentals than on the short-horizon catalyst map. If the remaining calendar is relatively light, the market mainly asks whether BTC can hold an already-cleared level rather than whether it can survive a known event-risk gauntlet.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-lean above market-neutral odds
- A view that the most likely repricing path is drift/chop above threshold rather than a forced break below it
- A probability estimate in the high-80s to low-90s rather than something closer to a coin flip

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Fed calendar shows the next FOMC meeting is April 28-29, after resolution.
- BLS CPI schedule shows the next CPI release inside the usual cadence is not before resolution.
- Binance spot is currently about 9% above the threshold, so absent a major catalyst the contract does not require a fresh upside move.

## What would falsify it

- A material macro surprise, geopolitical shock, or crypto-specific regulatory/exchange event before April 20
- A broad risk-off selloff large enough to drag BTC down more than roughly 8-10% from current levels
- Evidence that another scheduled event inside the window is being underweighted and is historically BTC-moving

## Early warning signs

- Fast deterioration in BTC daily closes toward low-70k/high-60k
- Sharp equity or risk-asset selloff before the weekend
- Negative exchange-specific or stablecoin-system headlines
- Abrupt increase in realized intraday volatility with failed rebounds

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case becomes much more about event risk and much less about simple threshold distance. My probability would move down materially, likely into a mid-range zone rather than a high-probability Yes.
