# Assumption

The absence of a major negative macro or crypto-specific shock before April 20 noon ET is sufficient for BTC/USDT on Binance to remain above 70,000.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is less about upside catalysts being needed and more about the threshold likely holding unless a repricing event forces a drop of roughly 6% from current levels.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-but-not-near-certain Yes probability.
- A view that the key catalysts are downside catalysts rather than upside triggers.
- A judgment that timing risk is concentrated in the next scheduled macro data and any exchange-specific disruption.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot is already around 74.4k, leaving a substantial cushion over 70k.
- Recent daily Binance closes have mostly remained above 70k.
- The nearest major scheduled Fed meeting is after resolution, reducing one obvious macro event risk during the window.
- March CPI was already released on April 10, removing one major near-term scheduled macro shock from the immediate calendar.

## What would falsify it

- A renewed risk-off macro shock strong enough to push BTC below 70k by April 20 noon ET.
- A crypto-specific negative catalyst such as major exchange/ETF operational disruption, regulatory shock, or liquidation cascade.
- Binance-specific pricing dislocation or unusual exchange divergence around the settlement candle.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing 72k decisively on Binance with momentum.
- Sharp deterioration in weekend crypto risk sentiment.
- Meaningful exchange outages or large funding/liquidation stress into the settlement window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market’s current 86% may prove too high, and the contract becomes much more sensitive to path-dependent intraday weakness near the noon ET settlement print.
