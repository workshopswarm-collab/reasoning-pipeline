# Assumption

Absent a fresh macro or crypto-specific shock before noon ET on April 17, Bitcoin is likely to remain above the $72,000 threshold because the current spot cushion is several percent and recent Binance trading has mostly held in the mid-74k area.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC is structurally guaranteed to hold 72k, but that the next ~51 hours are short enough that mean reversion below the strike is less likely than continued trading above it unless a distinct risk-off catalyst arrives.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability above the market-implied baseline.
- The view that the main catalyst is downside shock risk rather than upside discovery.
- The interpretation that timing matters more than long-horizon Bitcoin fundamentals for this contract.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot during the run was around 74,040.86, about 2.83% above the strike.
- Recent daily closes include 74,417.99 and 74,131.55 after a temporary dip to 70,740.98, suggesting BTC recently re-established levels above 72k.
- The contract horizon is short: roughly two trading days, which limits the number of scheduled catalysts that can force a repricing.
- Current Polymarket pricing around 81-82% already implies market participants see a substantial cushion.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp macro risk-off event, ETF-flow disappointment, exchange-specific disruption, or crypto idiosyncratic selloff that pushes BTC/USDT back under 72k before or at noon ET on April 17.
- Price action showing persistent trade back below 72k well ahead of resolution.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the mid-73k/74k area and failing to reclaim it.
- A sudden drop toward or through 72.5k within the next 24 hours.
- Cross-asset risk-off signals or headline shocks that hit crypto beta broadly.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case becomes much closer to a coin flip or No-leaning because the contract is a narrow timed print, not a weekly average or end-of-day measure. If BTC is back near the strike, microstructure and intraday volatility matter much more.
