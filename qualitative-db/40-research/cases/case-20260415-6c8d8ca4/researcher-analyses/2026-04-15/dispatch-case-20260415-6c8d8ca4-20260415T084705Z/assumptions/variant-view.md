# Assumption

BTC can absorb normal two-day volatility and still remain above 72,000 specifically on Binance at the April 17 12:00 ET minute close.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis for Yes depends less on long-run BTC optimism and more on whether the current price cushion survives a narrow time-and-venue resolution rule.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above 50%.
- A view that the market is directionally right but somewhat overconfident.
- A variant thesis centered on contract fragility rather than outright bearish BTC fundamentals.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

BTC was about 74,037 on Binance at research time, recent daily closes were above 72,000, and independent contextual data from CoinGecko showed a similar price regime. That gives a live cushion of roughly 2.8% over the threshold.

## What would falsify it

- A material downside move that takes BTC below 72,000 before or near noon ET on April 17.
- A volatility spike showing the current cushion is too small relative to typical event risk.
- Evidence of Binance-specific dislocation versus broader spot markets at the relevant time.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 73k-74k area and spending sustained time near or below 72.5k.
- Macro or crypto-specific negative news creating fast downside momentum.
- Binance price action showing sharper weakness than broader aggregated spot.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case shifts from "market a bit overconfident" to "No is live enough that the market may be materially mispriced." The estimate would move closer to or below the market-implied baseline depending on the severity of the break.
