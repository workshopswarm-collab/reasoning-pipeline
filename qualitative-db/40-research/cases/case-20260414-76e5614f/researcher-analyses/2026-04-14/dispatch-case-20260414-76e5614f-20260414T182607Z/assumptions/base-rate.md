# Assumption

The most important working assumption is that BTCUSDT remains in roughly its recent short-horizon volatility regime through the April 17 noon ET settlement window, without a fresh shock large enough to push the Binance 1-minute close below 72,000.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case for Yes depends less on a bullish thesis than on simple persistence: spot is already above the threshold, so the question is whether BTC can avoid a roughly 3.45% downside move into one specific minute on one specific exchange.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability above 50%
- A view that current market pricing is directionally reasonable
- A conclusion that recent price position carries more weight than older sub-72k frequencies

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Live Binance BTCUSDT was around 74,573 at check time.
- Recent hourly realized absolute moves were usually well under 1% and only rarely approached 2%.
- BTC has printed several recent daily closes above 72,000 after recovering from late-March weakness.

## What would falsify it

- A macro or crypto-specific shock that drives BTC sharply lower before April 17 noon ET
- A visible break back below 72,000 on Binance sustained across multiple hours going into settlement
- Exchange-specific dislocation on Binance relative to broader BTC pricing

## Early warning signs

- Loss of the 74k area followed by failure to reclaim 73k
- A sequence of large downside hourly candles exceeding recent realized norms
- News that materially changes crypto risk appetite or exchange operations

## What changes if this assumption fails

If BTC re-enters a clearly weaker short-horizon regime or trades near/below 72,000 ahead of settlement, the case should move quickly toward a near-coinflip or No view because the market resolves on one exact minute rather than on average price over the day.
