# Assumption

The 0.96 price likely reflects stale, misread, or low-information market positioning rather than a well-supported consensus that GERB-SDS is genuinely 96% likely to finish second.

## Why this assumption matters

Without this assumption, one would need to treat the market price itself as strong evidence that there is hidden information making GERB-SDS an overwhelming runner-up rather than a frontrunner.

## What this assumption supports

- A large disagreement with the market-implied probability.
- A view that the current price is not an efficient summary of accessible public evidence.
- A recommendation to weight contract wording and public ranking context over price alone.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Accessible contextual sources place GERB-SDS ahead of peers rather than narrowly behind a single dominant rival.
- The contract is seat-based and official-source anchored, so a "second place" price should track relative rank, not generic win strength.
- Extreme prices are especially vulnerable to stale books or mislabeled contract intuition when public evidence points in the opposite direction.

## What would falsify it

- A credible, recent, independent Bulgarian poll average or official late-campaign reporting showing GERB-SDS consistently behind exactly one rival but safely ahead of the rest.
- Market microstructure evidence showing deep, active, informed trading around this contract rather than thin stale pricing.
- Authoritative election-night or official preliminary results placing GERB-SDS second.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple late polls converge on another party clearly overtaking GERB-SDS while GERB remains well above third place.
- Credible domestic analysts explicitly frame GERB as the consensus runner-up.
- Liquidity and volume on the contract increase meaningfully with no price correction.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The price would deserve materially more respect as an information-aggregation signal, and my probability estimate for GERB-SDS finishing second should move sharply upward.
