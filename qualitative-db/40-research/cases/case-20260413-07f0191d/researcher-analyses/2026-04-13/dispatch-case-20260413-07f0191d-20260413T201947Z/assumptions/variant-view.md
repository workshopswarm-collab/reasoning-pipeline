# Assumption

The key working assumption is that the polling and reporting signals implying a Progresivna Bulgaria surge represent a real ballot-valid competitor ahead of GERB, while GERB itself remains structurally stronger than the older challenger pack and therefore still likely to place second.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire variant view depends on a specific market structure: GERB can be weak relative to the emerging frontrunner and still be very likely to finish second. If PB is not a real or separately ballot-valid force, or if opposition labels are being double-counted across PB, PP-DB, and DB, then the confidence behind the market may be overstated.

## What this assumption supports

- A high probability that GERB finishes second despite not being the most likely first-place party.
- A view that the market's 0.96 price is directionally sensible but still somewhat too confident because taxonomy and source-of-truth issues remain.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- PolitPro explicitly shows PB first and GERB second, with a meaningful gap over DB, DPS, and Revival.
- Wikipedia independently reflects a more fragmented field and includes PB as a contesting actor in its current-election frame.
- GERB starts from the largest current parliamentary bloc and remains top-tier in an independent POLITICO context snapshot.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that PB is not an actual registered ballot actor or not counted under the market's listed options the way current aggregators imply.
- Evidence that GERB polling has materially collapsed into the same band as DPS / PP-DB / Revival.
- Official or consensus late reporting showing GERB outside the top two in votes or seats.

## Early warning signs

- Clarified official ballot or commission reporting that collapses PB into some other label.
- Independent final polling averages showing GERB no longer clearly ahead of DPS and PP-DB.
- Credible reporting that seat conversion in districts could push GERB below second despite a close national vote margin.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate would need to move down materially, potentially by more than 15 points if ballot taxonomy or challenger consolidation invalidates the apparent PB-first / GERB-second structure.
