# Assumption

The key live assumption is that GERB–SDS remains in a broad top-tier cluster where finishing first is still more likely than finishing exactly second.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is pricing YES at an extreme level, but the contract is about exact rank ordering. If GERB–SDS is still the modal first-place finisher, then a YES bet requires not just strength but a very specific downgrade from leader to runner-up.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market probability for YES.
- A catalyst-focused view that the only high-information events are late credible polling convergence or authoritative election-night counts showing another bloc clearly ahead.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current-seat baseline in accessible election overview material still places GERB–SDS as the largest parliamentary bloc.
- Accessible contextual polling aggregation did not show clean evidence that GERB–SDS had already been durably displaced into second.
- In parliamentary elections, exact second-place contracts are fragile when the apparent favorite for first remains competitive.

## What would falsify it

- Multiple late, independent, methodologically credible polls showing another bloc consistently ahead of GERB–SDS.
- A consensus of credible Bulgarian election-night reporting placing another list clearly first with GERB–SDS clearly second.

## Early warning signs

- Late-campaign momentum visibly consolidating behind one rival bloc.
- Credible media framing shifting from fragmented field to a clear two-way race with GERB–SDS in second.
- Exit-poll or near-final district data placing GERB–SDS behind a rival nationally.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If GERB–SDS is no longer plausibly the modal first-place finisher, the YES probability rises sharply and the market’s extreme pricing becomes more defensible.

## Notes that depend on this assumption

- Main catalyst-hunter finding for this dispatch.
- Any later synthesis that relies on exact-rank rather than broad competitiveness.
