# Assumption

The late-February to early-April 2026 polling order is directionally real enough that GERB-SDS enters election week as the most likely first-place finisher rather than a true second-place contender.

## Why this assumption matters

My probability estimate is driven mostly by outside-view structure: a party polling clearly first across multiple recent polls should rarely be the modal second-place finisher only days before the election.

## What this assumption supports

- A low probability estimate for GERB-SDS finishing exactly second.
- A view that the 0.96 market price likely reflects a labeling, interpretation, or market-structure issue rather than the underlying electoral base rate.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

The polling table compiled on the election page shows multiple firms in March and early April 2026 putting GERB-SDS first and usually comfortably ahead of the field in projected seats. The 2024 baseline also had GERB-SDS first by a wide margin. In proportional parliamentary systems, parties that are the consistent first-place polling leader a week out do not usually become the second-place finisher absent a large late swing or major data error.

## What would falsify it

- A direct poll release from a reputable Bulgarian firm showing GERB-SDS genuinely in a close fight for second rather than first.
- Credible reporting that the polling table used here is stale, erroneous, or misinterpreted.
- A major coalition or ballot-status change that makes the market's GERB-UDF mapping diverge from GERB-SDS seat attribution.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple independent late polls showing GERB-SDS dropping into the low-30s or lower in seats and another party overtaking it.
- Credible analyst reporting that seat conversion or coalition treatment makes GERB-SDS unlikely to finish first despite leading vote intention.
- Resolution-source ambiguity around coalition dissolution or label mapping.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If GERB-SDS is not actually the structural first-place favorite entering election week, then the current low estimate for "finish second" would need a substantial upward revision, possibly by dozens of points depending on the new competitive picture.
