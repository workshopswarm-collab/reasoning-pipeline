# Summary

This source provided the most usable consolidated context for the scheduled election date, the 2024 parliamentary baseline, and a polling table spanning late February through early April 2026.

## Key facts extracted

- The election is scheduled for 19 April 2026.
- The prior October 2024 result had GERB-SDS first on 25.52% and 66 seats, with PP-DB second on 13.75% and 36 seats; Revival was third on 12.92% and 33 seats; DPS was fourth on 11.13% and 29 seats.
- The article's polling table for late February through early April 2026 consistently shows GERB-SDS in first place, with projected seats around the high-50s to around 70.
- In the same polling table, the likely second-place competitors are usually PP-DB, DPS, or less often Revival, not GERB-SDS.
- The article states the electoral threshold is 4% and seats are allocated using proportional representation with largest remainder / Hare quota, which matters because seat ranking, not just raw vote share, determines market resolution.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Sova Harris (2-6 Apr 2026): GERB-SDS 19.0% / 55 seats; PP-DB 11.2% / 33 seats; DPS 9.7% / 28 seats; Revival 7.8% / 23 seats.
- Gallup (20-30 Mar 2026): GERB-SDS 23.4% / 70 seats; PP-DB 10.9% / 33 seats; DPS 10.7% / 32 seats; Revival 6.5% / 20 seats.
- Alpha Research (19-26 Mar 2026): GERB-SDS 21.2% / 64 seats; PP-DB 11.1% / 33 seats; DPS 9.8% / 29 seats; Revival 6.9% / 21 seats.
- MarketLinks (17-21 Mar 2026): GERB-SDS 22.2% / 66 seats; PP-DB 13.3% / 40 seats; DPS 10.5% / 31 seats; Revival 5.5% / 16 seats.
- Earlier March and late-February entries shown on the same page also keep GERB-SDS in first rather than second.

## What is uncertain

- Wikipedia is a secondary aggregator and may lag or contain errors.
- The polling firms are not fully independent from a provenance standpoint because they are mediated through Wikipedia here rather than checked directly at each firm's release page.
- A polling snapshot one week before the election is still not the same thing as official results.

## Why this source may matter

It is the clearest consolidated outside-view source for this exact question: historical standing plus recent polling both indicate GERB-SDS is structurally a first-place party in this election, making a second-place finish an upset rather than the baseline.

## Possible impact on the question

If this page is even roughly accurate, the market price of 0.96 on GERB-SDS finishing second looks badly misaligned with the observed polling structure. The page supports a low probability for GERB-SDS to finish second and a much higher probability that GERB-SDS instead finishes first.
