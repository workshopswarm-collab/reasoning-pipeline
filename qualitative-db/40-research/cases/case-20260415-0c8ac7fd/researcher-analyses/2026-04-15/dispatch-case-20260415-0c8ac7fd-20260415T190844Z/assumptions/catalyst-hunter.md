# Assumption

BTC does not need a fresh bullish catalyst to resolve Yes; it mainly needs to avoid a roughly 3.6% drawdown from the checked spot level into the specific Apr 17 12:00 ET Binance one-minute close.

## Why this assumption matters

My high-Yes view depends more on current cushion and path stability than on any single scheduled macro or crypto event forcing upside before settlement.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above the market-implied 0.87 baseline.
- The claim that the most important near-term catalyst is the absence of a negative shock large enough to push BTC below 72,000 exactly at the governing minute.
- The interpretation that this market is closer to a short-horizon hold-above threshold question than a need-new-upside catalyst question.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance price check showed BTC/USDT at 74,704.
- Recent 1-minute klines show BTC trading steadily in the mid-74k range rather than merely spiking above 72k.
- Secondary contextual price surface from CNBC showed a day low around 73,567, still above 72,000.
- With roughly two days left, the threshold is already cleared by a meaningful margin.

## What would falsify it

- A material risk-off macro shock, crypto-specific negative headline, or exchange-specific disruption drives BTC back below 72,000 before the Apr 17 noon ET close.
- Evidence of elevated volatility or a sharp trend break that makes the noon snapshot unusually fragile.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 73.5k-74k area on Binance.
- Rapid deterioration in broader risk assets or crypto majors.
- Exchange-specific operational or market-structure issues affecting Binance BTC/USDT prints.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market should be treated as much closer to a coin flip than the current extreme price implies, because the thesis would no longer be “hold existing cushion” but “reclaim threshold exactly at the governing minute.”
