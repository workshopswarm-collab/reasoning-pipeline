# Summary
The net is modestly below the market because the calendar still contains one plausible extension catalyst, but the most visible formal negotiation already failed and the contract requires precise public confirmation, not just ongoing de-escalation.

## Question being evaluated
Will there be a qualifying official extension of the April 7 US-Iran two-week ceasefire, or an equivalently well-confirmed official agreement, by 11:59 PM ET on April 20/21 under the market rules?

## Current lean
Lean No versus market, but not aggressively: extension remains live, yet the timing path is narrower than price implies.

## Prior / starting view
Starting view was roughly market-consistent because an intact ceasefire with mediators pushing more talks often yields some rollover probability.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence
- Pakistan and Oman urging continuation of diplomacy cuts both ways: it shows desire for extension, but also signals extension is not done.
- The ceasefire still holding is supportive, but the market needs a documented extension event, not merely absence of renewed fighting.

## Conflict between inputs
- The main disagreement is not factual but weighting-based and timing-based.
- Some evidence supports the existence of a live path to extension; other evidence suggests the available time and unresolved substance make that path narrower than price implies.
- Evidence that would resolve it: official scheduling of a second round, or explicit bilateral statements using extension language.

## Key assumptions
- The next meaningful catalyst is a second formal round rather than a quiet backchannel announcement.
- No-gap timing remains binding under the contract.
- A vague statement about continuing talks would not qualify without extension language.

## Key uncertainties
- Exact expiry timing of the original two-week ceasefire in ET relative to local announcements.
- Whether a narrow rollover is easier than the broader package discussed publicly.
- Whether Iranian official confirmation would appear in channels that are harder to surface quickly in English.

## Disconfirming signals to watch
- White House, State Department, Iranian MFA, IRNA, or IRIB clearly announcing a mutually agreed extension.
- Multiple independent top-tier outlets simultaneously reporting that an official extension has been reached.

## What would increase confidence
- Confirmed date/time for second-round talks before expiry.
- Bilateral public language that explicitly extends the ceasefire duration.
- Independent reporting that a narrow extension is being separated from the broader peace agenda.

## Net update logic
What mattered most is that the most visible negotiation channel already failed once, while the market contract remains strict about what counts. I downweighted generic de-escalation and mediator optimism because those do not settle the contract. I still keep meaningful Yes probability because the ceasefire is intact and a second-round catalyst may still happen inside the window.
