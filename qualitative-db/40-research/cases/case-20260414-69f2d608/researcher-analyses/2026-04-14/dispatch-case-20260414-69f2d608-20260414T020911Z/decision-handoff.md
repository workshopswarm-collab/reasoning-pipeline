# Decision summary

Post-synthesis, the best current view is that a U.S.-Iran ceasefire extension by April 21 remains slightly more likely than not, but materially less likely than the market implies because the clearest direct talks already failed to produce a deal and no qualifying bilateral public extension confirmation is yet visible. The most decision-useful stance is cautious below-market Yes with a contract-sensitive range centered in the high-50s, not the low-70s.

## Why this may matter now

Market-implied probability is 0.705; my post-synthesis range is 0.54 to 0.61. That suggests a moderate below-market edge, but it is timing-sensitive rather than robustly actionable because the range is still slightly-Yes and could move fast on one official announcement. The likely market mispricing is over-collapsing 'ceasefire still holding / talks continue' into 'contract-qualifying extension gets publicly confirmed.'

## Shift versus swarm baseline

There is no major difference from the swarm-implied center; I stay close to it. The swarm median of 0.58 survives synthesis because the additional verification supported both core points at once: diplomacy remains alive enough to keep Yes above 50%, but the contract-sensitive evidence still does not justify anything close to 0.705.

## Edge verification status

Verification quality is medium. I independently rechecked the Polymarket contract text, which clearly confirms that official bilateral confirmation or overwhelming credible-media consensus of an official extension is required. I also reviewed source notes anchored in AP reporting that the April 11-12 talks ended without agreement and that, as of April 13, the ceasefire remained intact with possible follow-on talks proposed. A verification/search pass found no visible official extension announcement. This is enough to verify the market is probably overpricing the formalization step, but not enough for high confidence because the absence-of-announcement evidence is inherently incomplete and the situation is highly freshness-sensitive.

## Compression toward market

No. I did not compress materially toward market because the independent checks did not uncover support strong enough to justify the market's 70.5% confidence. If anything, the verification reinforced the swarm's skepticism: intact ceasefire plus active mediation is real, but the required extension confirmation is still missing.

## Timing and catalyst posture

The next key catalyst is either confirmation of a second formal talks round or an official bilateral extension statement. Before that, the edge is more likely to decay or stay fragile than widen, because the clock is short and the market can still be rescued by a single announcement. Waiting for fresh confirmation likely improves accuracy but may worsen price if a bullish catalyst lands suddenly.

## Key blockers

Main blockers are the lack of official bilateral confirmation, uncertainty over whether follow-on talks happen in time, and wording/no-gap resolution risk under the contract. There is also a smaller blocker around deadline/market-close phrasing mismatch, though it is not currently thesis-changing.

## Best countercase

The best countercase, represented most clearly by market-implied plus catalyst-hunter/variant-view, is that once a ceasefire exists and both sides still prefer avoiding immediate renewed conflict, a narrow procedural rollover may be much easier than the failed broader talks imply. In that view, the market is paying for diplomatic inertia and a last-minute technical extension rather than a full substantive breakthrough.

## What would change the view

The clearest falsifier would be matching official U.S. and Iranian confirmation of an extension, or multiple independent top-tier reports clearly stating that an official extension agreement has been reached. A confirmed second round of talks with explicit extension framing would also push the estimate upward. Conversely, official breakdown statements, no follow-on talks, or renewed hostilities would push it downward fast.
