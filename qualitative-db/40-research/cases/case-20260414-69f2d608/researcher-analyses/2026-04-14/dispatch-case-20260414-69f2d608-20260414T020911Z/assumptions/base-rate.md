# Assumption

A fragile ceasefire that is still holding will not automatically convert into a contract-qualifying extension without explicit public confirmation from both governments or overwhelming independent media consensus before the deadline.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is not asking whether fighting stays mostly paused; it is asking whether a narrow formalization event occurs in time. My probability stays below market only if bureaucratic, diplomatic, and public-confirmation friction remain real obstacles.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market probability estimate.
- The view that informal continuation should be discounted relative to a formally announced extension.
- The view that failed talks on April 11 are meaningful negative evidence rather than temporary noise.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The contract wording is narrow and requires public mutual confirmation or overwhelming credible-media consensus of an official extension.
- AP reported that a major direct negotiation round ended without agreement.
- In conflict settings, formal ceasefire extensions are structurally harder than temporary de-escalation or ambiguous ongoing contact.

## What would falsify it

- Clear official U.S. and Iranian statements confirming extension past the initial two-week term.
- Multiple independent top-tier outlets reporting that a formal extension deal has been reached, even if implementation details remain pending.
- Publication of a broader agreement that explicitly includes a qualifying continuation of the ceasefire with no gap.

## Early warning signs

- Public scheduling of a second negotiation round with named principals and a narrow agenda focused on extension terms.
- Parallel statements from both governments describing an agreed continuation beyond the original term.
- Credible leaks from multiple independent outlets saying text has been finalized pending announcement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If explicit confirmation friction falls away, the probability should move sharply upward because the remaining time window is short and the contract resolves Yes once a qualifying agreement is officially reached, even if later implementation is uncertain.
