# Summary
AP's live reporting gives the clearest current independent evidence that the ceasefire is still holding on April 13 and that additional talks may occur before expiry. This is the strongest practical support for the market's ~70.5% Yes pricing, though it still falls short of official bilateral confirmation of an extension.

## Key facts extracted
- AP reports Pakistan's prime minister said the two-week ceasefire brokered by Pakistan remains intact.
- AP reports Pakistan has proposed a second round of talks between the United States and Iran in the coming days, before the end of the ceasefire.
- AP reports the first talks ended without an agreement, but officials described them as part of an ongoing diplomatic process.
- AP reports the White House was not responsive on whether new talks were being weighed, preserving uncertainty around formal extension prospects.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- "US-Iran ceasefire is holding despite failed peace talks, Pakistan's prime minister says."
- "Efforts are underway to resolve the remaining disputes between Washington and Tehran as a two-week ceasefire brokered by Pakistan remains intact."
- "Pakistan has proposed hosting a second round of talks between the United States and Iran in Islamabad in the coming days, before the end of the ceasefire."
- "The White House was not responsive to queries about whether new talks were being weighed."

## What is uncertain
- AP attributes the holding-ceasefire update to Pakistan's prime minister rather than a direct simultaneous US and Iranian statement.
- The live blog reports discussion of further talks, not a completed official extension.
- It remains ambiguous whether an extension would be publicly confirmed by both governments before the contract deadline.

## Why this source may matter
This is the best near-real-time evidence that the ceasefire has not collapsed and that diplomacy is continuing on a timeline consistent with an extension before April 21.

## Possible impact on the question
This source supports a moderate Yes probability because intact ceasefire conditions and scheduled follow-on talks make extension more plausible than a clean breakdown scenario. It is still not enough to justify very high confidence because the contract demands either official confirmation from both governments or overwhelming media consensus of a reached official extension.

## Reliability notes
AP is a strong wire source and the reporting is highly recent. Independence is only partial because some claims rely on Pakistani officials and anonymous Pakistani sources, so this should be treated as strong contextual evidence rather than final authoritative proof.
