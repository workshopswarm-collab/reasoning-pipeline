# Summary
AP's April 13 live coverage says Pakistan has proposed a second round of US-Iran talks before the ceasefire expires and reports the ceasefire is still intact. For a catalyst-focused view, this is the main near-term event that could force repricing before resolution.

## Key facts extracted
- AP says the two-week ceasefire remains intact as of April 13.
- AP says Pakistan proposed a second round of talks in the coming days, before the ceasefire ends.
- AP says the prior Islamabad round ended without agreement.
- AP says the White House did not respond to questions on whether new talks were being weighed.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- The source headline indicates the parties could be headed toward a second round of talks.
- The linked AP reporting says the ceasefire remains intact.
- The report says Pakistan proposed hosting another round before the end of the ceasefire.
- No official US confirmation of new talks or an extension was cited.

## What is uncertain
- Whether the second round actually occurs on time.
- Whether those talks are about a narrow ceasefire rollover or a broader package that is harder to close.
- Whether even a successful round would produce the bilateral public confirmation needed for contract resolution.

## Why this source may matter
This is the clearest identifiable catalyst on the calendar. If the talks are confirmed and produce public statements from both governments, the market can reprice quickly toward Yes. If the talks slip, stall, or end again without agreement, the market should reprice lower because the time window is short.

## Possible impact on the question
This source supports a live-path Yes thesis, but mainly through timing: the extension chance depends heavily on whether a second negotiating round materializes before the original ceasefire lapses.

## Reliability notes
AP is a strong wire source and highly useful for timing/context. It is not an authoritative settlement source, and its key timing claim relies partly on Pakistani sourcing rather than direct bilateral confirmation from Washington and Tehran.
