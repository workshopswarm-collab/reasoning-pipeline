# Summary
The market is not asking whether fighting remains low in practice. It asks whether there is an official extension of the April 7 two-week ceasefire, with public confirmation from both the United States and Iran, or failing that an overwhelming consensus of credible media that an official extension agreement has been reached.

## Key facts extracted
- A Yes requires a publicly announced and mutually agreed extension to the halt in direct military engagement by 11:59 PM ET on the specified date.
- A new agreement can count only if it takes effect before or at the initial agreement’s scheduled end and there is no gap with no ceasefire in force.
- Informal understandings, unilateral pauses, backchannels, de-escalation, humanitarian pauses, limited operational pauses, and tactical stand-downs do not qualify.
- Official statements from both governments are the primary resolution source of truth; overwhelming consensus of credible media can substitute if they confirm an official extension agreement has been reached.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- The contract explicitly elevates official US and Iranian statements as the main source of truth.
- The contract explicitly excludes mere de-escalation or informal arrangements.

## What is uncertain
- How much media consensus would count as "overwhelming" if official statements remain vague or asymmetric.
- Whether a broader peace framework with ambiguous ceasefire language would satisfy the contract.

## Why this source may matter
This is the governing resolution standard. For risk management, it sharply limits what evidence counts and makes wording risk central.

## Possible impact on the question
This source lowers confidence in any Yes thesis built mainly on hopes of continued diplomacy or practical restraint. The market requires a documented extension event, not just absence of fighting.

## Reliability notes
- Strength: primary governing contract language.
- Weakness: still requires interpretation if real-world statements are vague.
- Best used as the first filter for what evidence matters.
