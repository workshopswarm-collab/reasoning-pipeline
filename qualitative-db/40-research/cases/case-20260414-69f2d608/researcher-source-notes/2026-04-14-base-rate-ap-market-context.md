## What is uncertain

## Why this source may matter

This source is useful as independent contextual confirmation that, as of April 13, observers were still talking about the existing ceasefire as active but fragile, not formally extended. It also highlights the market trap here: a ceasefire can "hold" informally without satisfying the contract's requirement for an official extension.

## Possible impact on the question

The source is mildly supportive of some chance of extension because the ceasefire had not visibly collapsed. But overall it reinforces a base-rate view that continuation of a fragile ceasefire should not be confused with the narrower event required for a Yes resolution: explicit mutual confirmation of an extension.
