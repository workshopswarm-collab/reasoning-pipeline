# Summary

This source is a strong contextual report on the first post-ceasefire direct talks in Islamabad. Its main value is not settlement authority but showing that the April 12 negotiating round ended without an extension deal while both Iranian and Pakistani statements kept diplomacy and the ceasefire framework alive.

## Key facts extracted

- The April 12 Islamabad talks ended without a deal.
- US Vice President JD Vance said the talks ended without an agreement.
- Iranian officials blamed the US for unreasonable demands but did not declare diplomacy over.
- Iranian MFA spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei said, via IRNA citation, that "Diplomacy never ends."
- Pakistan's foreign minister publicly urged both sides to uphold their commitment to the ceasefire and continue engagement.
- The main sticking points were reported as Iran's nuclear programme, Strait of Hormuz issues, sanctions, war reparations, and ending the war.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Direct quote attributed to Vance: no agreement was reached.
- Direct quote attributed to Baghaei: diplomacy remains open.
- Direct quote attributed to Pakistan FM Ishaq Dar: both sides should uphold their commitment to the ceasefire.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the report captures the full private negotiating state.
- Whether later bilateral or mediated talks could produce a qualifying extension before the deadline.
- Whether reported commitment to uphold the ceasefire reflects a mutually agreed formal extension versus only maintenance of the existing two-week arrangement.

## Why this source may matter

It is a meaningful, recent, independent contextual source indicating the obvious bearish headline: no extension deal yet. But it also preserves the variant possibility that market participants may overread "no deal" as "extension path dead" even though official rhetoric still leaves a live diplomatic channel and ongoing commitment to the ceasefire itself.

## Possible impact on the question

On its face this pushes probability down because no extension was reached in the key April 12 round. But because both Iranian and Pakistani statements explicitly keep diplomacy and ceasefire maintenance alive, it does not by itself justify a very low extension probability before April 21.

## Reliability notes

- Useful as contextual reporting, not governing source of truth.
- Contains attributed quotes from named officials and references IRNA, which improves traceability.
- Still a secondary media source and should be paired with official statements or other independent reporting.
