# Summary
Independent reporting says the main direct US-Iran talks held during the current two-week ceasefire ended on April 12 without an agreement, despite 21 hours of negotiations in Islamabad. That is the single clearest near-term disconfirming datapoint for a confident Yes view because the market requires an official extension or overwhelming consensus that such an extension agreement has been reached.

## Key facts extracted
- US Vice President JD Vance said the talks ended without an agreement.
- The apparent sticking points were broad and difficult: nuclear commitments, Strait of Hormuz control, sanctions/assets, reparations, and regional-security questions.
- Iranian officials did not describe the session as a final failure, but also did not claim an extension agreement had been reached.
- Pakistan and Oman publicly urged that the ceasefire be extended and diplomacy continue, implying continuation was desired but not yet secured.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- "The United States and Iran have failed to reach a truce deal after high-stakes talks..."
- Vance said: "The bad news is that we have not reached an agreement..."
- Oman’s foreign minister urged that "the ceasefire be extended and talks continue," which reads as advocacy, not confirmation that an extension already exists.

## What is uncertain
- Whether talks could resume quickly and produce an extension before the current ceasefire expires.
- Whether a narrower extension could be reached even if the broader peace package remains deadlocked.
- Whether official statements from both governments could arrive suddenly after backchannel work.

## Why this source may matter
This is a strong contextual source because it directly addresses the live negotiation process and the absence of a deal as of April 12, only days before the relevant deadline window.

## Possible impact on the question
This source pushes the probability down versus a complacent continuation thesis. It does not resolve the market by itself, but it materially raises path-risk: the most visible formal talks produced no extension, and the unresolved issues are exactly the kind that can break a short ceasefire.
