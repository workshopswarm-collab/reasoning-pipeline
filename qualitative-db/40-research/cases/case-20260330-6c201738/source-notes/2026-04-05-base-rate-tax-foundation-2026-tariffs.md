# Summary

Tax Foundation provides a useful contextual framing for what the broad tariff landscape looks like after legal and administrative changes in 2025-2026. It explicitly distinguishes statutory/applied general tariff rates from effective tariff rates based on revenue and says that while a 10% Section 122 tariff is in effect, the weighted average applied tariff rate is estimated at 10.2% and would be 12.1% at 15%. It also says the Section 122 tariff expires after 150 days.

## Key facts extracted

- Tax Foundation distinguishes statutory/applied tariff rate from effective tariff rate.
- It states that while the 10% Section 122 tariffs are in effect, the applied rate is estimated at 10.2%; at 15% it would be 12.1%.
- It states the rate would fall after the Section 122 tariffs expire.
- It distinguishes Section 122 from earlier IEEPA tariffs and notes those IEEPA tariffs were ruled unlawful.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- A 10% Section 122 tariff is described as the active reference scenario.
- A 15% alternative is presented as a counterfactual/modeling scenario, not clearly as the baseline in force.
- The source consistently frames Section 122 as the general temporary tariff layer that matters for broad average rates.

## What is uncertain

- This is an analytical secondary source, not the governing legal text.
- It discusses average applied tariff levels across imports, not specifically the market's narrower additive rule for China.
- It does not itself settle the exact March 31, 2026 legal rate applicable under the market contract.

## Why this source may matter

This source is useful because it independently reinforces the likely outside-view baseline: a broad temporary 10% general surcharge is the central live scenario, while 15% appears as an alternative or sensitivity case rather than the default. That is relevant to the bracket question (5%-15%).

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports a high probability that the relevant general rate on the target date is in the 10-ish range rather than above 15%, especially if no later official action raises it before March 31, 2026.

## Reliability notes

Medium-to-high usefulness as contextual independent analysis; not authoritative for resolution. Good for rate-calculation logic and base-rate framing, weaker for final settlement than official administration materials.
