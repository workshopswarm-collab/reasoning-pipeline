# Summary

This source note captures the official White House orders most relevant to the market’s resolution logic. Taken together, they show that China was subjected to multiple layers of general tariffs in 2025: a China-specific fentanyl IEEPA tariff raised to 20 percent by March 3, 2025; a general 10 percent reciprocal baseline effective April 5, 2025; and then a China-specific reciprocal rate raised to 125 percent effective April 10, 2025, while other countries’ higher reciprocal rates were temporarily suspended back to 10 percent.

## Key facts extracted

- March 3, 2025 order amended the earlier China fentanyl tariff from 10 percent to 20 percent.
- April 2, 2025 reciprocal tariff order imposed a 10 percent additional ad valorem duty on all imports from all trading partners effective April 5, 2025.
- The same April 2 order set country-specific reciprocal rates effective April 9, 2025 for Annex I countries, including China.
- April 9, 2025 modification order suspended most country-specific reciprocal rates for 90 days but explicitly excluded China from that suspension.
- The April 9, 2025 modification order raised the China reciprocal rate from 84 percent to 125 percent effective April 10, 2025.
- The market contract says the relevant measure is the “general tariff rate,” including any general tariff imposed on all imports plus any general tariff imposed on Chinese imports, while excluding item-specific exceptions or increases.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- White House fact sheet: 10 percent tariff on all countries effective April 5, 2025 at 12:01 a.m. EDT.
- White House April 2 order section 3(a): all articles imported into the customs territory of the United States are subject to an additional 10 percent ad valorem duty beginning April 5, 2025, with an in-transit carveout.
- White House March 3 order section 2: the PRC opioid-related tariff is increased from 10 percent to 20 percent.
- White House April 9 order section 2: other Annex I partners’ higher reciprocal rates are suspended to 10 percent for 90 days, except with respect to the PRC.
- White House April 9 order section 3(b): heading 9903.01.63 for China is amended from 84 percent to 125 percent effective April 10, 2025.

## What is uncertain

- Whether later official action before March 31, 2026 reduces or terminates one or more layers for China.
- Whether any court or statutory development invalidates or sunsets one layer in a way that changes the contract’s operative “in effect” tariff stack by the resolution date.
- How a resolution source would net statutory authority issues if the administration continues to state a tariff schedule while courts constrain collection.

## Possible impact on the question

Absent a later rollback, the official tariff stack is far above the 5 to 15 percent bracket. Even the narrowest plausible official stack from these orders alone is above 15 percent once the 20 percent China fentanyl tariff is included. If the reciprocal China rate remains operative, the market should resolve No by a very wide margin.
