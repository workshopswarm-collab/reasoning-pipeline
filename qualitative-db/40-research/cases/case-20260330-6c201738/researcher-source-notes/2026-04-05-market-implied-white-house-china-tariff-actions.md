# Summary

This source cluster is the governing official chain for the China-specific tariff changes most relevant to the market's resolution mechanics. It shows that the PRC-specific reciprocal tariff escalation was suspended and replaced with a 10% rate starting May 14, 2025, then that suspension was extended through November 10, 2026. Separately, the fentanyl/synthetic-opioid PRC duty rose to 20% on March 4, 2025, then was reduced to 10% effective November 10, 2025.

## Key facts extracted

- White House May 12, 2025 executive order: effective May 14, 2025, PRC imports were subject to an additional 10% ad valorem rate under the reciprocal-tariff regime, with 24 percentage points suspended for 90 days and higher retaliatory-modified rates removed.
- White House August 11, 2025 executive order: the suspension of the heightened China reciprocal tariff continued until 12:01 a.m. EST on November 10, 2025.
- White House November 4, 2025 executive order: the suspension of heightened reciprocal tariffs on PRC imports continued until 12:01 a.m. EST on November 10, 2026; during that suspension, articles of the PRC remained subject to an additional 10% ad valorem rate.
- White House March 3, 2025 executive order: fentanyl/synthetic-opioid PRC duty increased from 10% to 20%.
- White House November 4, 2025 executive order on synthetic-opioid duties: effective November 10, 2025, the PRC duty under that authority was reduced from 20% to 10%.
- USTR Presidential Tariff Actions page independently lists the same China executive-order chain, which is useful as an official index and chronology.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Reciprocal-tariff chain for China: 10% in force during the suspension period, with suspension extended to November 10, 2026.
- Synthetic-opioid tariff chain for China: 20% after March 3, 2025, then 10% effective November 10, 2025.
- The relevant effective-date language is explicit in the executive orders.

## What is uncertain

- Whether any later executive action before March 31, 2026 could modify either the reciprocal 10% or opioid 10% China duties again.
- Whether the market resolver interprets these two general China-wide duties as additive without any later clarifying administrative carveout. The market text strongly suggests yes, but that is still an interpretation step.

## Why this source may matter

These are the closest thing to the market’s primary source of truth because the market says official Trump administration information governs first. They directly answer the critical questions about what was merely announced versus what was actually in effect, and when.

## Possible impact on the question

If the market’s “general tariff rate” means summing general across-the-board China-wide duties that are in effect and excluding item-specific exceptions, then these sources imply a 20% general tariff rate on China at the March 31, 2026 checkpoint: 10% reciprocal + 10% synthetic-opioid duty. That would resolve outside the 5% to 15% band.

## Reliability notes

Very strong on source hierarchy and timing because the materials are official White House executive actions and an official USTR index. Slight remaining ambiguity is interpretive rather than documentary: whether the contract’s “general tariff rate” definitely aggregates these two distinct China-wide duties. The contract’s own example and wording make aggregation look likely.
