# Assumption

The remaining catalyst set before resolution is narrow enough that the market is primarily about whether any trailing scorer can erase McDavid's current lead before the NHL's final official points table is locked.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends on the idea that there is no hidden interpretive or award-voting mechanism left; only late regular-season production and official stat finalization should matter.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability but not absolute yes view on McDavid.
- Emphasis on games remaining and scoring volatility as the main repricing path.
- A view that most remaining information value is concentrated in final regular-season box scores and official leaderboard confirmation.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

The market description says the market resolves according to the player awarded the 2025-26 NHL Art Ross Trophy, with official NHL information as primary source. The Art Ross Trophy is a points-title award, so near-term catalysts are the remaining scoring opportunities and the official publication/final leaderboard.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that the market uses a different final criterion than regular-season points leader.
- A late correction/stat adjustment that materially changes the points race.
- A trailing player rapidly closing or surpassing the lead in remaining games.

## Early warning signs

- Trailing players posting multi-point games while McDavid is idle or limited.
- Official NHL leaderboard updates showing the gap narrowing to one game swing territory.
- Credible reporting about stat corrections or uncertainty in final leaderboard publication.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate would need to drop materially and the memo would shift from "few meaningful catalysts left" to "late scoring volatility still matters more than the current market implies."
