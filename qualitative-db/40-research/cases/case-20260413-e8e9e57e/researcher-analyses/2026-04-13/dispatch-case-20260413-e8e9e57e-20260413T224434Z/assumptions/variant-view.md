## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate depends on treating the race as mostly decided by existing official NHL stats rather than as a live wide-variance scoring contest.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability on McDavid.
- A variant view that is only modestly below the market rather than strongly bearish.
- A focus on completion/announcement/stat-correction risk rather than on pure performance upside for trailing players.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

Official NHL API data shows McDavid at 133 points, ahead of Kucherov at 128 and MacKinnon at 126, with the market already pricing the event around 95%+. At this late date, a five-point margin is usually hard to erase unless meaningful games remain or a correction changes the table.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that multiple regular-season games remain for trailing players with McDavid unavailable or done.
- A late official stat correction cutting the lead materially.
- Official NHL award logic diverging in some unexpected way from the scoring leaderboard.

## Early warning signs

- NHL schedule/news indicating trailing contenders still have meaningful runway while McDavid does not.
- Reporting on pending scoring-change reviews.
- Any mismatch between the NHL points leaderboard and explicit Art Ross winner framing.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market would become less about near-certainty from the current table and more about late-race volatility or resolution mechanics, which would push the estimate down materially.
