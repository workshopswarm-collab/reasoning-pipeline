# Assumption

The key assumption is that official NHL award/finalist communication will align cleanly with the observed league points lead showing Connor McDavid atop the 2025-26 scoring table.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is priced near certainty, so most residual risk is no longer about on-ice performance. It is about whether the contract’s source-of-truth logic and any finalist wording create a settlement edge case.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability Yes estimate rather than a lower estimate driven by contract-mechanics risk.
- The view that the market is directionally correct but slightly too confident.
- The decision to treat sporting uncertainty as largely resolved.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Hockey-Reference shows McDavid leading the league with 133 points, ahead of Kucherov at 128 and MacKinnon at 126.
- The Art Ross Trophy is ordinarily awarded to the NHL points leader.
- The market itself names official NHL information as the primary source, implying standard league records/announcements should govern.

## What would falsify it

- An official NHL page or announcement naming a different Art Ross winner.
- Evidence that the contract’s finalist clause is interpreted in a way that excludes McDavid despite the points lead.
- A late official stat correction overturning the scoring lead.

## Early warning signs

- Lack of any clean NHL finalist/winner communication close to resolution.
- Credible reporting disagreement about whether the Art Ross has a finalist list relevant to settlement.
- Any discrepancy between official NHL stats and major secondary databases.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The residual operational tail would become the dominant risk and the Yes probability would need to be marked down materially, even if McDavid still appears to have led the league in points.
