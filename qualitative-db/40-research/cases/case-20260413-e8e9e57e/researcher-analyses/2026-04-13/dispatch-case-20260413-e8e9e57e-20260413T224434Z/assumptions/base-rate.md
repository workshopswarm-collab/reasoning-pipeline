# Assumption

The Art Ross Trophy will be awarded in the ordinary way to the NHL regular-season points leader, and the market's fallback to consensus reporting will not be needed to override any official NHL designation.

## Why this assumption matters

My probability is high mainly because McDavid appears to have finished first in points across multiple sources. If the contract were to hinge on an unusual technicality, delayed correction, or nonstandard NHL designation process, the near-certainty implied by the leaderboard would be too strong.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability materially above a generic ex ante prior.
- Treating the scoring-leader evidence as the central mechanism.
- Using secondary stats databases as meaningful verification rather than mere noise.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market description explicitly says the market resolves according to the player awarded the 2025-26 Art Ross Trophy.
- In standard NHL practice, the Art Ross goes to the season points leader.
- Two substantial secondary sources agree McDavid leads the 2025-26 points race by 5 points.
- There is no surfaced evidence of a disputed tie, retroactive stat correction, or eligibility issue.

## What would falsify it

- An NHL announcement naming a different Art Ross winner.
- A late official stat correction that removes McDavid from first place.
- Contract guidance clarifying that finalist language or another procedural edge case overrides plain points-leader logic here.

## Early warning signs

- Conflicting leaderboard totals appearing on official NHL channels.
- Credible reporting indicating pending stat corrections or an unresolved tie-break issue.
- Absence of official NHL confirmation unusually close to resolution while other sources conflict.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes probability would fall sharply, and the case would become much more about contract interpretation and source hierarchy than about the outside-view scoring base rate.
