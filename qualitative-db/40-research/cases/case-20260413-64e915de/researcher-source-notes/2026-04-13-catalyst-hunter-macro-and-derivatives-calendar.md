# Summary

The main identifiable scheduled catalysts inside the Apr 13-19 window are macro/positioning related rather than Ethereum-protocol specific. The March 2026 CPI release already occurred on Apr 10, just before the market window, which likely helped frame the strong risk move into Apr 13. There is no FOMC meeting during Apr 13-19; the next one is Apr 28-29. CFTC Commitments of Traders data for the prior Tuesday will be released Friday Apr 17 at 3:30 p.m. ET, and CME documentation highlights weekly Ether options and Micro Ether options as vehicles for trading short-term event risk.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Exact timing of CPI and FOMC calendar events.

## What is uncertain

- CME product pages show the existence of weekly options but not whether those flows are dominant in the present move.

## Why this source may matter

For a one-week ETH threshold market, timing matters. This note narrows the catalyst set: the likely drivers are residual post-CPI risk appetite, ongoing spot/derivatives momentum, and any Friday positioning/flow effects, not a scheduled Ethereum upgrade or FOMC shock during the actual window.

## Possible impact on the question

This source keeps the thesis focused on momentum/liquidity continuation rather than waiting for a missing protocol catalyst.
