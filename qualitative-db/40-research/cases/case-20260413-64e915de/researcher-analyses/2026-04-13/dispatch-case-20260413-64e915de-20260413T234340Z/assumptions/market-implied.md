# Assumption

Because ETH is already trading within about 1% of $2,400 with nearly a full week left, normal short-horizon crypto volatility is sufficient to make a $2,400 touch more likely than not.

## Why this assumption matters

The market's 90%+ pricing only makes sense if a routine weekly move is enough to clear the threshold without requiring a major new catalyst.

## What this assumption supports

- A high probability estimate for the contract resolving Yes on the ↑ 2,400 band.
- A view that the market is directionally right to be confident.
- A rejection of strongly contrarian sub-50% or low-probability views absent bearish regime evidence.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Live spot checks place ETH around 2373-2375 during this run.
- Crypto trades 24/7 and ETH often moves more than 1% intraday over short windows.
- A full week remains before resolution, increasing the number of opportunities for a touch.

## What would falsify it

- Verified resolution rules requiring a stricter source or sustained trade above $2,400 rather than a simple touch/high.
- A rapid bearish regime shift pushing ETH materially away from the level and suppressing volatility upward.
- Evidence that current feeds overstate price relative to the contract's actual governing source.

## Early warning signs

- ETH quickly falls back below roughly 2300 and fails to retest resistance.
- Macro or crypto-specific downside shock reduces the chance of an upside wick.
- Official rules reveal a narrower settlement standard than the assignment wording suggests.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The correct estimate would move down materially, and the case for treating the 90%+ market price as efficient would weaken.
