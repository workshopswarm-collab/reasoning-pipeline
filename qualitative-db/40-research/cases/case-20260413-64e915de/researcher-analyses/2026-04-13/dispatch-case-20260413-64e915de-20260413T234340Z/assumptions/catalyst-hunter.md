# Assumption

The most important assumption is that once ETH trades to within roughly 1% of $2,400 at the start of the window, near-term momentum and liquidity are more likely than not to produce at least one qualifying touch before Apr 19 rather than stall just below the level all week.

## Why this assumption matters

The market resolves on whether the threshold is hit during a narrow period, not on end-of-week close. That makes path dependence crucial. If near-threshold momentum tends to extend into one more wick, the hit probability is high; if resistance and profit-taking dominate, the market's very high implied probability is too aggressive.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate moderately above 50% and potentially in rough agreement with the market.
- A catalyst framing centered on liquidity continuation and short-dated derivatives behavior rather than a single discrete scheduled event.
- The interpretation that being only a few dollars shy of target on day one is genuinely meaningful evidence.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Coinbase spot data showed ETH already reaching $2,395 intraday on Apr 13.
- Hourly candles showed acceleration late in the session rather than exhaustion early in the day.
- No major scheduled FOMC shock sits inside the week; the main macro release, CPI, already passed.
- CME documentation indicates a market structure that supports short-term event trading via weekly Ether options, increasing the plausibility of threshold-seeking flow and volatile extensions.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp reversal that takes ETH materially away from $2,350 and keeps it there for most of the week.
- Evidence that the contract resolves on a source that excluded near-threshold exchange wicks and used a more conservative benchmark.
- A macro risk-off shock or crypto-specific negative headline that changes the regime before a touch occurs.

## Early warning signs

- Failure to revisit the $2,370-$2,395 zone within 24-48 hours after the initial move.
- Visible rejection at prior highs combined with falling intraday volume.
- Broader crypto weakness that pulls ETH back while BTC and risk assets roll over.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If ETH cannot extend after already getting this close, the case shifts from "likely touch on path dynamics" to "market overpaying for a round-number breakout that may never print." That would push the probability materially lower and make the current market price look overstretched.
