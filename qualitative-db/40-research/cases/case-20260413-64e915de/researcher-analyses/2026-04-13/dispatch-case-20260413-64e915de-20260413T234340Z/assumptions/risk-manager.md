# Assumption
Because ETH traded within roughly $5 of $2,400 on the first day of the window, at least one Binance 1-minute candle is more likely than not to print a high at or above $2,400 before Apr 19 11:59 PM ET.

## Why this assumption matters
The forecast is not about end-of-week close; it is a touch market. The entire bullish case depends on near-threshold proximity converting into one qualifying minute-high during the remaining days.

## What this assumption supports
- A high Yes probability close to but below the market.
- The view that path opportunity outweighs directional uncertainty.
- The judgment that current market confidence is somewhat high but not irrational.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- Binance, the governing resolution venue, already printed a 24h high of 2394.71 on Apr 13.
- The remaining threshold gap is small in absolute dollars for ETH.
- The market has almost a full week left, giving multiple volatility windows for a touch even without a sustained breakout.

## What would falsify it
- ETH repeatedly fails just below 2400 while momentum fades and realized volatility compresses.
- Binance-specific order flow diverges downward from broader crypto markets.
- A sharp macro or crypto-specific risk-off move pushes ETH materially away from the threshold early in the week.

## Early warning signs
- Multiple failed pushes into the 2380s-2390s with rejection.
- BTC or broader crypto market loses momentum and ETH underperforms beta.
- Funding/flow conditions deteriorate enough that upside wick probability falls.

## What changes if this assumption fails
The Yes probability should drop materially because the thesis is mostly about path access, not deep fundamental repricing. If ETH loses near-threshold access, the market's current confidence would look overstated.
