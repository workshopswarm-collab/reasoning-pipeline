# Assumption

Absent a fresh negative macro or crypto-specific shock, Bitcoin is unlikely to fall more than ~4% from current Binance levels exactly into the April 20 12:00 ET settlement minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends less on long-run Bitcoin direction than on short-horizon path stability into one specific minute. If a sharp repricing shock arrives near settlement, the market can resolve No even if the broader four-day trend remains constructive.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes probability above 75%
- A view that the market is directionally reasonable but a bit complacent about point-in-time settlement risk
- The conclusion that the dominant catalyst is the settlement timestamp itself rather than a known scheduled event

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance BTC/USDT was around 75,000 during the run, leaving roughly a 3,000-point cushion above 72,000.
- Recent daily closes on Binance were above 72,000 on most recent days after the April 12 dip.
- There was no directly verified scheduled case-specific catalyst in the research pass that obviously dominates the next four days more than ordinary crypto volatility does.

## What would falsify it

- A new macro, regulatory, exchange, or market-structure shock that pushes BTC/USDT below 72,000 before or into noon ET on April 20
- Evidence of weekend or Monday morning momentum reversing sharply toward the April 12 lows
- New verified information showing Binance-specific trading conditions or settlement interpretation create extra downside risk

## Early warning signs

- BTC/USDT loses the recent 74k-75k area and trades back toward low 73k or below
- A risk-off macro headline triggers synchronized selloff across crypto and equities/futures
- Binance venue-specific dislocations or unusual wick behavior appear near key timestamps

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move materially lower, likely toward a near-coinflip or outright No-lean if BTC trades back near or below the strike with less than a day left.
