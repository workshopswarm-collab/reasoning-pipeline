# Assumption

The recent Binance BTC/USDT regime above 72k is informative for the next four days and is not about to be disrupted by a large downside move before the April 20 noon ET minute close.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case for Yes depends less on a special catalyst than on persistence: BTC is already above the threshold, and the question is whether that state usually survives a short remaining horizon.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above 50%
- Treating recent daily closes above 72k as meaningful context
- A view that the market is directionally right but a bit aggressive at 83.5%

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance spot was 75,000 at time of check, around 4.2% above the threshold.
- Recent Binance daily data show multiple closes above 72k in the last several sessions.
- Over a four-day horizon, persistence of an already-established price regime is often the default unless there is a strong contrary shock.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp risk-off move that pushes BTC below 72k and keeps it there into April 20 noon ET.
- Evidence that recent price strength was a transient spike rather than a stable regime.
- A settlement-mechanics issue showing noon ET tends to behave differently than broader daily pricing for this setup.

## Early warning signs

- BTC quickly losing 74k and then 72k on Binance with sustained follow-through.
- Large macro or crypto-specific shock over the next few days.
- Abrupt increase in intraday downside volatility around U.S. morning trading hours.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the regime-persistence assumption fails, the Yes case weakens materially and the market's 83.5% implied probability would look too high by more than a modest margin.
