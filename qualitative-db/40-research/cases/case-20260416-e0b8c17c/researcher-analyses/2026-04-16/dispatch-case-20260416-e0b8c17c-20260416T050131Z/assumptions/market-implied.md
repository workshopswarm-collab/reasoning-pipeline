# Assumption

The market's high Yes price mainly assumes that BTC can absorb normal four-day volatility without falling more than about 4% by the specific Binance noon ET closing minute on April 20.

## Why this assumption matters

This assumption carries most of the case for respecting an 83.5% market-implied probability: the threshold is already below current spot, so the question becomes whether short-horizon downside and point-in-time noise are small enough to leave that cushion intact.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability Yes view rather than a coin flip.
- The interpretation that the market is broadly efficient rather than badly overconfident.
- The claim that the remaining edge, if any, is mostly about settlement-path risk rather than directional BTC thesis disagreement.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance spot during the run was about 75,000, roughly 3,000 above the strike.
- Independent CoinGecko context showed a similar mid-74k to low-75k range over the recent window.
- Only four days remain, so the path to No likely requires either a meaningful downside move or a temporary dip right at the resolving minute.

## What would falsify it

- BTC breaking below the recent mid-74k area and sustaining trade near or below 72k before April 20.
- A sharp risk-off move that widens realized downside volatility materially beyond what current pricing seems to assume.
- Evidence that Binance-specific prints are unusually noisy or prone to settlement-minute deviations versus broader spot.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid deterioration from ~75k toward low-73k/high-72k before the weekend.
- Repeated intraday sweeps below recent support.
- Market repricing in nearby Bitcoin daily-close or above/below contracts toward materially lower odds.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the cushion erodes or noon-minute path risk looks larger than assumed, the fair Yes probability should move down meaningfully and the current market would look overextended rather than efficient.

- The main market-implied finding for this dispatch.
- Any later synthesis that interprets the market as mostly efficient but slightly rich.
