# Assumption

The market is pricing Fenerbahçe partly off broad season strength and squad prestige, while underweighting the fact that Rizespor's recent form is good enough to keep non-win outcomes live in a one-match sample.

## Why this assumption matters

The variant view depends on distinguishing team-quality superiority from single-match win probability.

## What this assumption supports

- A modest under view versus the 0.745 market price.
- An own estimate in the high-60s rather than mid-70s.
- The claim that the best contrarian case is not "Rizespor are likely better" but "the favorite may be a bit overpriced."

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Official TFF standings show Fenerbahçe are clearly stronger over the full season.
- Transfermarkt form table still shows Rizespor with 3 wins in the last 5 league matches.
- In football, draw risk and low-event variance keep even superior teams from converting league-strength edges into certainty.

## What would falsify it

- Credible official pre-match team news showing Rizespor heavily depleted while Fenerbahçe are near full strength.
- Market-wide price convergence materially above the current level with new information I could verify.
- A directly sourced home/away split or head-to-head signal showing a much wider practical gap than the general league table suggests.

## Early warning signs

- Reliable reports of multiple Rizespor absences in defense or goalkeeper.
- Confirmed rotation relief / strongest-available lineup from Fenerbahçe.
- Late sharp move toward an even higher Fenerbahçe win probability.

## What changes if this assumption fails

My probability would move closer to the market or slightly above it, and the variant thesis would collapse into rough agreement with consensus.
