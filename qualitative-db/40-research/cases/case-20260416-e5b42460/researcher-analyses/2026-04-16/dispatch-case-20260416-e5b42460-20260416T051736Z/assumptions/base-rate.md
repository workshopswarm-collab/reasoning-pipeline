# Assumption

Absent major late lineup/injury shocks, a second-place Fenerbahçe side hosting an eighth-place Rizespor side in a normal league match should retain a strong but not overwhelming home-win baseline.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate relies more on structural priors and table strength than on case-specific team news. If late-breaking absences or motivational distortions are large, the base-rate anchor would be too high.

## What this assumption supports

- A win probability modestly below the market price rather than a dramatic fade.
- The view that Fenerbahçe deserves favoritism primarily because of home advantage and stronger season-long standing.
- The conclusion that the market is slightly rich but not obviously mispriced.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Sofascore lists Fenerbahçe 2nd and Rizespor 8th.
- The match is at Fenerbahçe's home stadium in Istanbul.
- For ordinary domestic-league soccer, home favorites with this sort of standings gap win often enough to justify being clear favorites, but draws and occasional away upsets still occur too often to treat the match as near-settled.

## What would falsify it

- Verified reports of major missing Fenerbahçe starters or a heavily rotated lineup.
- Updated odds or credible previews showing the true pre-match price materially below the market-implied 74.5%.
- Evidence that the standings gap masks a much narrower underlying strength gap.

## Early warning signs

- Late injury/suspension news for key Fenerbahçe attackers or goalkeeper.
- A sharp market move down across sportsbooks.
- Reports of unusual fixture congestion, motivational asymmetry, or adverse weather/pitch conditions.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move down, potentially into the high-60s or lower, and the current mild disagreement with the market would become stronger.
