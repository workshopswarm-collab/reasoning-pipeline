# Summary

AAA said on March 26 that the national average had risen to $3.98, up one dollar in a month, and explicitly said it could reach $4/gallon in the coming days. AAA attributed the move to high crude prices linked to the Iran conflict plus rising spring-break gasoline demand.

## Key facts extracted

- AAA said regular gasoline was up **10 cents from the prior week** and **$1.00 from the prior month**.
- AAA said the national average **could reach $4/gallon in the coming days**.
- AAA cited still-high crude prices as the Iran conflict continued.
- AAA cited rising spring-break gasoline demand as an additional pressure source.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- There was a clear near-term catalyst path already visible by March 26.
- Oil-price pressure and seasonal demand were both active inputs.
- AAA itself flagged the exact threshold under debate.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the final pass-through into the AAA average arrives before time runs out.
- Whether crude remains elevated long enough for one more retail increment.
- How much of the March surge had already been fully transmitted by March 30.

## Why this source may matter

This source identifies the catalyst stack the market is likely trading: crude conflict premium plus seasonal demand plus ongoing retail pass-through. It is the strongest direct explanation for why traders became comfortable pricing a high probability.

## Possible impact on the question

Bullish for Yes and especially useful for timing because it says the threshold was expected in the near term, not just eventually.
