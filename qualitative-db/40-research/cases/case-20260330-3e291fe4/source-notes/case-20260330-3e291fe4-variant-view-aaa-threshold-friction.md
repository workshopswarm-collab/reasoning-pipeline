# Summary

The strongest underweighted fact is mechanical: AAA shows the national regular average at **$3.990 on 2026-03-30**, which is still below the market's qualifying threshold. The market rules explicitly resolve by the first two digits / bracket, so this remains a **$3.99** state, not a $4.00 hit.

## Key facts extracted

- AAA Fuel Prices page on 2026-03-30 shows **Current Avg. $3.990** for regular gasoline.
- AAA Fuel Prices page shows **Yesterday Avg. $3.980** and **Week Ago Avg. $3.956**.
- AAA Newsroom on 2026-03-26 said the national average was **$3.98** and **could reach $4/gallon in the coming days**.
- KVUE/AP relay on 2026-03-30 said the national average was **$3.990**, up **1 cent** from Sunday, and that the recent pace had slowed to only a few cents over seven days.
- The market description says resolution uses the **first two digits of the reported price**; e.g., $3.157 resolves to the **$3.15 bracket**.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The current resolution source is still showing a number below 4.000.
- The latest move from yesterday to today was only **+0.010**.
- The broader week-over-week trajectory remains upward, but the most recent reporting says the rise has **slowed** and is merely hovering just below $4.

## What is uncertain

- AAA may print **$4.000 or above** on March 31, which would immediately resolve the market Yes.
- The exact intraday timing of AAA updates is not specified in the sourced snippets here.
- A one-cent move is small enough that any continued upward drift could still be sufficient.

## Why this source may matter

This is the direct resolution-source reality check. The market price near 0.775 can only be right if the remaining move from **3.990 to 4.000+** is much more likely than not over the final day.
