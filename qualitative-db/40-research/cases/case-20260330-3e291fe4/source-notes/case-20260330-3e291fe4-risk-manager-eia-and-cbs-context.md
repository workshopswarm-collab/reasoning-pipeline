# Summary
EIA's March 23 weekly U.S. regular gasoline price was $3.961, confirming a sharp national run-up. CBS, citing AAA and outside analysts earlier in the month, said gas had risen to $3.48 on March 9 and included split forecasts: Eurasia's Ian Bremmer expected average gas to reach $4 in the coming week, while GasBuddy's Patrick De Haan said retreating oil prices would make hitting $4 much less likely and predicted a lower range.

## Key facts extracted
- EIA weekly U.S. gasoline price reached $3.961 by March 23.
- EIA showed a 24.1 cent week-over-week increase nationally.
- CBS reported AAA's national average at $3.48 on March 9, up 48 cents from the prior week.
- CBS quoted Ian Bremmer expecting average gas prices to reach $4 in the coming week.
- CBS also quoted Patrick De Haan saying falling oil would make hitting $4 much less likely and projecting gas only in the $3.50-$3.65 range before settling.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- EIA: “U.S. 3.961” for 03/23/26 regular gasoline.

- CBS: Bremmer “expects average gas prices to reach $4 in the coming week and to hover around that level.”
- CBS: De Haan said if oil prices retreat, “it makes the odds of hitting $4 per gallon much less likely.”

## What is uncertain
