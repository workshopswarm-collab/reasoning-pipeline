# Summary

EIA daily prices show a strong upstream energy backdrop consistent with the market's bullish interpretation. On March 27 close, WTI was $101.26, Brent $121.47, NY Harbor RBOB gasoline $3.26 (+5.6% day/day), Gulf Coast gasoline $3.30 (+6.7%), Los Angeles gasoline $3.75 (+4.9%), and Gulf Coast crack spreads were elevated at $49.35 (+14.9%). This does not guarantee the AAA retail print crosses 4.00 in time, but it supports the idea that retail prices were still under upward pressure heading into the final day.

## Key facts extracted

- WTI crude: $101.26, up 5.3%.
- Brent crude: $121.47, up 7.1%.
- NY Harbor gasoline: $3.26, up 5.6%.
- Gulf Coast gasoline: $3.30, up 6.7%.
- Gulf Coast 3:2:1 crack spread: $49.35, up 14.9%.
- EIA page notes AAA retail data are used in its retail petroleum section, though retail gasoline was NA at the retrieval timestamp.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Upstream crude and gasoline benchmarks were rising sharply late in the month.
- Refining margins were elevated, consistent with stress in retail fuel pricing.
- The near-term direction in the energy complex was supportive of higher pump prices rather than lower ones.

## What is uncertain

- Exact same-day pass-through from spot and futures benchmarks into the national AAA retail average.
- Whether regional spikes are large enough to move the national average one full cent by the next print.

## Why this source may matter

This is one of the strongest public justifications for the market: traders may be pricing not only the current AAA print, but also upstream energy momentum that could push the next AAA reading over the line.

## Possible impact on the question

Supports a high market probability by showing that the market is not only reacting to a near-threshold level, but to an energy complex still moving in the same direction.

## Reliability notes

EIA is a strong primary public data source. The limitation is pass-through timing: wholesale spikes do not map one-for-one into the next AAA national retail print.
