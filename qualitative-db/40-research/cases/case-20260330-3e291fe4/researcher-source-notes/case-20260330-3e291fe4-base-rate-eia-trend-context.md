# Summary

EIA's weekly series confirms that the late-March move has been unusually steep and broad. U.S. regular gasoline rose from **$3.502 on 2026-03-09** to **$3.720 on 2026-03-16** to **$3.961 on 2026-03-23**, a gain of **45.9 cents in two weeks** and **24.1 cents in the latest week**.

## Key facts extracted

- U.S. regular gasoline:
  - 2026-03-09: **$3.502**
  - 2026-03-16: **$3.720**
  - 2026-03-23: **$3.961**
- Change from week ago in latest EIA table: **+24.1 cents** nationally.
- West Coast (PADD5) on 2026-03-23: **$5.262**.
- California on 2026-03-23: **$5.650**.
- Washington on 2026-03-23: **$5.160**.
- Several major cities were already above $4 by 2026-03-23, including Chicago at **$4.178**.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The source directly supports three points:
- the national gasoline series was still climbing hard into the final week of March
- the national average was already within striking distance of $4 as of the last EIA weekly reading
- a number of regions and cities were already far above $4, meaning the national average was being held below $4 mainly by cheaper areas rather than by a broad absence of price pressure

## What is uncertain

- EIA is weekly, while the market resolves on AAA daily national average data.
- EIA does not tell us whether the daily national average definitively crossed $4 on 2026-03-30 or 2026-03-31.
- The final daily move can still flatten or reverse slightly.

## Why this source may matter

This gives the base-rate trend backdrop. The event no longer depends on a sudden regime change; it depends on whether an already-strong national price upswing continues for roughly one more daily increment.

## Possible impact on the question

This increases confidence in a bullish base-rate estimate because the event threshold sits only 3.9 cents above the last EIA weekly print and only 1 cent above the current AAA daily print. Historically, once a broad weekly national upswing of this magnitude is in force, asking for one more cent by the next day is a much easier proposition than asking whether the whole rally starts.

## Reliability notes

High-quality official data. The main caveat is mismatch between EIA's weekly cadence and AAA's daily resolution source, so EIA should be used as structural trend confirmation rather than the final resolver.
