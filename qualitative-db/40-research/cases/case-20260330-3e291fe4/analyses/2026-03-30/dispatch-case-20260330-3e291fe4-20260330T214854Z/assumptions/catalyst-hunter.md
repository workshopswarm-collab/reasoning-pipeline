# Assumption

The market's high probability only works if the remaining upstream and seasonal pressure converts into one more increment in the AAA national retail average before the final relevant print rather than stalling at $3.99x.

## Why this assumption matters

This market is no longer about the broad gasoline thesis. It is about timing of the last marginal pass-through into the exact resolver.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes probability above 50%.
- The view that the next AAA print is the dominant catalyst.
- Confidence that upstream oil strength still matters before expiry.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- AAA is already at $3.990.
- AAA itself said $4 could be reached in coming days.
- EIA shows upstream oil, gasoline, and crack-spread pressure still elevated.

## What would falsify it

- AAA remains below $4.000 through the final relevant update.
- Evidence shows retail prices flattened despite continuing upstream strength.
- A fresh AAA print fails to advance from $3.990.

## Early warning signs

- State-level averages show more flattening than acceleration.
- Commentary shifts from "could hit $4" to "hovering just below $4."
- Upstream oil settles but retail data does not continue rising.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The catalyst case weakens sharply and the contract becomes a clean example of the market being directionally right about pressure but wrong on final-step timing.
