# Assumption
The market's 77.5% YES price appears to assume that being one cent below the threshold on March 30 will convert into an actual AAA print of $4.00 or above by March 31, rather than stalling just short.

## Why this assumption matters
Almost the entire remaining probability mass is about the last tiny step from $3.990 to $4.000+, not about the broader trend anymore.

## What this assumption supports
- A high YES probability near current market.
- The view that momentum and lagged pump pass-through will be enough to push AAA over the line.
- Confidence that no display/measurement quirk leaves the price fractionally short.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- AAA explicitly said the national average could reach $4 in the coming days.
- AAA currently shows $3.990, only one cent short.
- The daily trend from yesterday to today was +$0.010.

## What would falsify it
- AAA prints another sub-$4.00 number on March 31.
- A plateau in pump prices despite elevated crude and recent momentum.
- Clarification that the market's bracket logic effectively requires a stricter threshold treatment than traders assume.

## Early warning signs
- Oil and wholesale prices ease faster than pump prices were expected to pass through.
- AAA moves sideways rather than higher on the final update.
- Analysts emphasizing near-threshold stall risk prove more accurate than the bullish threshold-crossing calls.

## What changes if this assumption fails
The market is suddenly far too high, because a contract sitting one cent below the line with almost no time left can still resolve NO despite a strongly bullish commodity narrative.
