# Summary
The market is directionally right to lean YES, but its 77.5% confidence still looks a bit too high for a threshold contract that remains unresolved one cent below the line with almost no time left.

## Question being evaluated
Will AAA's national regular-gas Current Avg. print at $4.00 or higher on any day by March 31, 2026?

## Current lean
Lean YES, but only modestly above market-neutral and below the current 77.5% price.

## Prior / starting view
Starting from the 0.775 market price, the embedded view is that near-threshold momentum will probably carry the average over the line.

## Evidence supporting the claim
- AAA, the resolution source, already shows $3.990 on March 30. Very high weight; only one cent is left.
- AAA said on March 26 that the national average could reach $4 in the coming days. High weight; direct threshold-specific commentary from the resolving source ecosystem.
- Recent trend was strong and steep: roughly $2.98 one month ago, $3.98 on March 26, $3.99 on March 30. High weight; powerful momentum evidence.
- EIA weekly U.S. regular gasoline price was $3.961 on March 23, confirming broad national pass-through already underway. Medium weight.

## Evidence against the claim
- The contract is not settled by narrative momentum; it still needs an actual AAA print of at least $4.00. High weight; one-cent-away contracts can still fail.
- CBS preserved an important bearish expert view: Patrick De Haan said if oil retreated, the odds of hitting $4 would become much less likely. Medium-high weight; credible near-threshold failure mode.
- There is effectively no remaining time buffer. High weight; timing risk is now dominant.
- Display/measurement mechanics matter more than usual when the current value is $3.990 rather than clearly above threshold. Medium weight.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence
- AAA's “could reach $4 in the coming days” is bullish but not a guarantee.
- A one-cent daily move from yesterday to today is encouraging, but single-day increments near the line are noisy.
- Elevated crude and war-driven fuel stress help, but pass-through into the specific AAA national print may lag or flatten.

## Conflict between inputs
The conflict is mainly timing-based rather than factual. Most sources agree prices surged sharply; the disagreement is whether that surge is enough to guarantee a final threshold breach before time expires.

## Key assumptions
- Near-threshold momentum continues at least one more increment.
- AAA's March 31 reading is the relevant unresolved print and can still move above $4.
- No final-day stall leaves the market resolving NO despite strong macro narrative support.

## Key uncertainties
- Exact March 31 AAA regular-gas print.
- How much overnight or one-day lag remains in national pump-price reporting.
- Whether traders are overconfident because the chart looks almost complete.

## Disconfirming signals to watch
- AAA updates on March 31 and remains below $4.00.
- News of falling crude/wholesale prices accelerates and stalls pump pass-through.
- Any clarification on display precision that cuts against the intuitive “one cent away” interpretation.

## What would increase confidence
- A March 31 AAA print at or above $4.00.
- Additional confirmation that the national average continues its daily upward cadence into the final day.

## Net update logic
I do not need a bearish macro view to sit below market. The risk-manager edge here is about threshold fragility: once the contract is one cent short with almost no time left, the main risk is not the broader commodity thesis but the last-step execution risk.

## Suggested downstream use
Use as a stress-test input for synthesis. The key question is whether the market is pricing “very likely” appropriately, or confusing “almost there” with “already done.”
