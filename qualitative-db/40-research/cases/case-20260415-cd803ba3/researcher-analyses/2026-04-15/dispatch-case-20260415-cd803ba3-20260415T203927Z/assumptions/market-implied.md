# Assumption

BTC is likely to remain above the 74,000 threshold, or at least retake it, by the specific Binance BTC/USDT 12:00 ET one-minute close on April 17 because spot is already above that level by roughly 0.9-1.2% and there is no current sign of venue-specific dislocation.

## Why this assumption matters

The market-implied Yes case mostly rests on persistence around an already-cleared level rather than on the need for a fresh breakout. If current price advantage over the threshold is not durable, the market price is too high.

## What this assumption supports

- A moderate Yes probability above 50%
- A view that the market is roughly efficient rather than clearly stale or overextended
- Treating current spot and cross-venue alignment as meaningful evidence rather than noise

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance settlement venue prices were already above 74,000 during the verification pass.
- CoinGecko and Coinbase were tightly aligned with Binance, lowering concern that the threshold was being cleared only on one venue.
- The required move is not large if BTC simply holds current levels into the next day.

## What would falsify it

- BTC falls decisively back below 74,000 across major spot venues and stays there into the morning of April 17.
- Binance-specific dislocation or abrupt volatility puts the noon ET 1-minute close below 74,000 even if broader BTC spot is near the line.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated hourly failures to hold above 74,000
- Material widening between Binance BTC/USDT and other major spot references
- Broad crypto risk-off move that pushes BTC back through nearby support

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case shifts from a modestly favored Yes to likely No or near coin-flip, because the current positive cushion above the threshold is the main reason the market can rationally sit near 70%.
