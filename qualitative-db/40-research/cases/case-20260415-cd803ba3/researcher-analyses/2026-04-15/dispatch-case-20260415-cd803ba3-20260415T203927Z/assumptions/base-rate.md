# Assumption

The best outside-view approximation is that, absent a new major catalyst, Bitcoin will remain within its recent short-horizon trading regime through the April 17 noon ET resolution window.

## Why this assumption matters

The probability of finishing above 74,000 depends less on long-run Bitcoin fundamentals than on whether the next roughly 44 hours resemble recent realized volatility and level behavior.

## What this assumption supports

- A moderate Yes lean rather than an extreme conviction view.
- Treating recent Binance price distribution around the threshold as more informative than narrative headlines.
- Discounting vivid but unsupported stories that imply a very large directional move before resolution.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

Recent Binance daily data show BTC repeatedly moving through the upper-60k to mid-70k region, with current price modestly above the strike. That makes a stable-regime extrapolation more reasonable than assuming an imminent breakout or crash without fresh evidence.

## What would falsify it

- A large macro or crypto-specific catalyst producing a clear regime shift before April 17 noon ET.
- Sharp movement well outside the recent realized range.
- Exchange-specific disruption affecting Binance BTC/USDT pricing or data availability.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid expansion in intraday realized volatility.
- Sustained move several thousand dollars away from 74,000 before the final day.
- Strong cross-exchange dislocations or Binance-specific operational issues.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the regime changes, the recent-price base rate becomes less informative and the estimate should shift toward the direction implied by the new catalyst and volatility regime.
