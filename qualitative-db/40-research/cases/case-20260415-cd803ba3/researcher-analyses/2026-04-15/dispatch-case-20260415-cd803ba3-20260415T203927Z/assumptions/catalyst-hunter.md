# Assumption

The market is mostly a short-horizon path-dependence question: absent a fresh negative macro or risk-off shock before noon ET on Apr 17, BTC is more likely than not to remain above 74000 because it is already trading modestly above that level.

## Why this assumption matters

The final estimate depends less on a medium-term Bitcoin thesis and more on whether near-term catalysts are strong enough to move BTC by roughly 1% or more at the exact settlement window.

## What this assumption supports

- A slightly bullish but not extreme Yes probability.
- A view that market pricing near 70% is directionally reasonable.
- A catalyst map centered on macro timing rather than crypto-native binary news.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot at research time is already above the strike.
- There is no clearly identified Bitcoin-specific binary event before settlement from the sources checked.
- The visible catalyst calendar is mainly macro data and Fed communication, which can move BTC but are not obviously one-sided.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp risk-off repricing on Apr 16 or the morning of Apr 17 that drives BTC decisively below 74000.
- A crypto-specific negative headline affecting exchange confidence, regulation, or market structure.
- Evidence that current spot is unusually fragile and being held up by transient flow rather than stable demand.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing 74000 well before Apr 17 and failing to reclaim it.
- A material deterioration in broader risk sentiment after macro releases.
- Increased intraday volatility around Fed communication that pushes BTC back into low-73k/high-73k territory.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The thesis would shift from modest Yes edge to No-lean or near-coinflip, because a narrow one-minute settlement contract is highly sensitive to late-window volatility when price is close to the threshold.
