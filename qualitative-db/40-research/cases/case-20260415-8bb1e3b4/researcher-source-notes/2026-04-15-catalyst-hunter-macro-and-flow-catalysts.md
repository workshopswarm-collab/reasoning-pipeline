# Summary

This note captures the near-term catalyst calendar most relevant to whether BTC can remain above 70,000 into April 20. The key finding is that most scheduled high-information U.S. macro catalysts have already passed by April 15, leaving the biggest remaining market-moving forces as ongoing ETF/flow persistence, weekend liquidity/volatility, and any unscheduled geopolitical or risk-off shock.

## Key facts extracted

- Federal Reserve calendar shows the next FOMC meeting is April 28-29, after this market resolves.
- The March 17-18 FOMC minutes were released April 8, already before this research date.
- BLS CPI calendar shows the March 2026 CPI release was April 10, already before this research date.
- CoinDesk reported spot bitcoin ETFs pulled in $471 million on April 6, described as the largest inflow since Feb. 25 and sixth-largest daily total of 2026.
- CoinDesk also noted Bitcoin was still stalling below 70,000 around April 7 despite those inflows, implying flows were supportive but not infinitely powerful.
- CryptoSlate framed April's main macro calendar as employment report, FOMC minutes, Beige Book, and the April 28-29 Fed meeting; by April 15, most near-term scheduled macro event risk before April 20 appears lighter.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Fed official calendar: April 28-29 is the next scheduled FOMC meeting.
- BLS official calendar: March CPI release date was April 10, 2026.
- CoinDesk: ETF inflows of $471 million on April 6 represented a strong positive demand signal.
- CryptoSlate: macro backdrop can still matter for BTC, especially via inflation/Fed sensitivity and geopolitical energy shocks.

## What is uncertain

- Media reports on ETF-flow interpretation and macro sensitivity are contextual, not settlement-authoritative.
- Weekend flow dynamics and headline-driven crypto selloffs are inherently hard to predict on a 4-5 day horizon.
- The source set does not provide a deterministic path for April 20 noon ET; it only clarifies likely catalysts and what has already passed.

## Why this source may matter

This market is highly date-sensitive. If the most dangerous scheduled macro catalysts had still been ahead, the probability of falling back below 70,000 by April 20 would be materially higher. Instead, the calendar implies fewer scheduled repricing events before resolution.

## Possible impact on the question

The evidence modestly supports Yes by showing that BTC is already above the strike and that the remaining path to failure depends more on unscheduled shocks, weakening flows, or weekend volatility than on an obvious imminent scheduled macro release.
