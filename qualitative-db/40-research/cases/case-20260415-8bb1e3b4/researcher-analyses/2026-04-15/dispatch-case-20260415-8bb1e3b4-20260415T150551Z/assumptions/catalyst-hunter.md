# Assumption

The core assumption is that, with most scheduled U.S. macro catalysts already out of the way by April 15, BTC's path into April 20 noon ET will be driven mainly by persistence of existing support and absence of an adverse shock rather than by a fresh scheduled repricing event.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends on timing, not just level. BTC already trading above 70,000 matters less if a major scheduled catalyst is about to hit before the contract window.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-but-not-near-certain Yes view.
- Framing the most likely repricing risk as unscheduled shock / weekend volatility rather than a known scheduled macro print.
- Treating remaining catalyst risk as lighter than it would be earlier in the month.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Official Fed and BLS calendars show the key scheduled releases highlighted in commentary (FOMC minutes, CPI) are already behind us.
- The next scheduled FOMC meeting is after resolution.
- BTC is already materially above the strike, so failure requires a meaningful drawdown over a short remaining window.

## What would falsify it

- Discovery of a still-pending high-information scheduled catalyst before April 20 that the market is underweighting.
- Clear evidence that ETF inflows have sharply reversed or that BTC is losing 70,000 support immediately after this check.
- A geopolitical or exchange-specific shock that creates a rapid weekend drawdown.

## Early warning signs

- BTC falls back below 72,000 and cannot reclaim it.
- Binance spot trades back near or below 70,500 before the weekend.
- Strong risk-off move in equities/oil/rates that spills into crypto.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes estimate should be cut materially because the market would no longer be coasting through a relatively light scheduled calendar; instead, it would face a known repricing trigger or deteriorating support regime.
