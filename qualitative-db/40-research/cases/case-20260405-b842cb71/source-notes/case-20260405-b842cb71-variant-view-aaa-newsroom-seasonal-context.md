# Summary
AAA’s March 19, 2026 fuel-prices article said gas prices had climbed 28 cents week over week, were still trending upward with spring demand, and were not seeing immediate relief from a large announced SPR release because crude prices were still climbing. A February 26 AAA article also said the seasonal rise had begun due to summer-blend gasoline and expected spring-break demand.

## Key facts extracted
- March 19 AAA Newsroom article:
  - "prices at the pump continued to climb by 28 cents since last week"
  - gas prices were "trending upward alongside seasonal demand"
  - the announced release of 172 million barrels from the U.S. strategic reserves had "no immediate relief" because crude prices were still climbing
- February 26 AAA Newsroom article:
  - national average regular gasoline rose above $2.98
  - refineries were beginning to produce more expensive summer-blend gasoline
  - gas demand was expected to increase in March with spring-break travel
