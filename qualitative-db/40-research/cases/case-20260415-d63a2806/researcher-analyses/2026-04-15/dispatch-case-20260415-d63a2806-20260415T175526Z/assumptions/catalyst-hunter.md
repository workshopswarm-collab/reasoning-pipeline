# Assumption

There is no known scheduled catalyst before Apr 17 noon ET that is more likely to determine the contract outcome than ordinary BTC volatility around an already-above-threshold spot level.

## Why this assumption matters

The catalyst-hunter role is supposed to identify near-term repricing triggers. If there were a major known macro print, policy decision, exchange event, or operational risk event inside the window, it could dominate the probability of the exact noon close. Without such a dominant scheduled catalyst, the best forecast leans more heavily on present threshold cushion and normal volatility.

## What this assumption supports

- A high but not extreme Yes probability.
- The view that the main repricing path is BTC drifting with normal macro/crypto risk sentiment rather than waiting on one singular calendar event.
- The conclusion that the key thing to watch next is whether BTC loses the 72k area before the exact governing minute rather than whether one specific scheduled catalyst arrives.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance BTC/USDT level is about 74.1k, already above the threshold.
- Recent 24h and 7d realized ranges include moves both above and below nearby levels, suggesting ordinary volatility is sufficient to move the contract materially.
- Web access for broader news search was partially impaired by bot-detection, so no high-confidence contrary catalyst emerged from independent search during this run.
- The contract is a close-above market at one specific minute, which makes path volatility and exact timing more important than broad long-run thesis quality.

## What would falsify it

- Identification of a specific scheduled event before Apr 17 noon ET with a strong track record of moving BTC by several percentage points.
- A major exchange, regulatory, macro, or geopolitical shock that sharply reprices BTC before the governing minute.
- Fresh evidence that Binance-specific operational issues could distort the quoted close.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 73k and then 72k area on Binance before Apr 17.
- Material risk-off macro headlines or sudden crypto-specific selling pressure.
- Rising divergence between Binance and other major venue pricing, suggesting exchange-specific noise.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a dominant scheduled or surprise catalyst appears, the probability should be re-centered around that event’s downside/upside distribution rather than present spot cushion alone, and the case may need a more event-driven rather than volatility-driven framing.
