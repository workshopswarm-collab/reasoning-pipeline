# Assumption

The market's 75% price is mainly assuming that a Bitcoin spot level already in the mid-$75k area only needs one ordinary intraworkweek volatility burst to print $76k at least once before April 19 ends.

## Why this assumption matters

The difference between "very likely" and "just somewhat favored" in this contract is not about long-run Bitcoin direction; it is about whether near-threshold spot proximity plus normal BTC volatility is enough to justify a high touch probability over a multi-day window.

## What this assumption supports

- Treating the market price as mostly efficient rather than obviously overextended.
- A personal estimate near but slightly below the market.
- The view that the contract remains live and plausible without requiring a major new macro catalyst.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Cross-venue spot checks put BTC around $75.3k-$75.4k, less than 1% below the threshold.
- CoinGecko range data for the same window shows BTC had already traveled from roughly $70.7k to $74.8k, so realized short-horizon volatility is nontrivial.
- In crypto, a sub-1% additional move over several remaining days is often achievable without extraordinary news when momentum is already positive.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp reversal away from the mid-$75k area that persists, making $76k no longer a near-touch target.
- Evidence from the governing market rules that the relevant source or sampling method is narrower than a simple intraperiod high/touch interpretation.
- A volatility collapse that leaves BTC pinned well below the strike through most of the remaining window.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated rejection below $75.5k across major spot venues.
- Deteriorating risk sentiment or macro headlines that push BTC back toward low-$74k or below.
- Clarification in contract rules that the threshold is determined by a narrower or less favorable benchmark than assumed.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If near-threshold volatility is not enough, then the market price is too optimistic and the contract should be marked down meaningfully because there is little time left for a fresh approach.
