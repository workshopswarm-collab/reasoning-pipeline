# Assumption

A weekly BTC threshold-touch contract with the asset already within about 1% of target and nearly six days remaining should usually resolve more often than a same-threshold end-of-week closing-price contract.

## Why this assumption matters

Most of the outside-view case for Yes comes from contract structure rather than a strong directional macro thesis.

## What this assumption supports

- A base-rate estimate above 50%.
- The judgment that the market’s 82.5% is only somewhat rich rather than wildly wrong.
- The view that time remaining plus intraday volatility are the key mechanism.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Resolution depends on any Binance 1-minute high touching 76k, which is easier than holding above 76k at a close.
- BTC had already printed daily highs around 75.4k to 75.5k on checked exchange data.
- BTC routinely makes brief wick highs around round-number levels during volatile periods.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that BTC volatility had collapsed unusually hard relative to recent weeks.
- Evidence that the governing Binance market was structurally constrained or behaving differently from the broader BTC price complex.
- New data showing a sharp regime break lower that makes a 1% upside touch unlikely.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated rejections below 75.5k followed by loss of momentum into the low-70ks.
- Broader crypto risk-off move that shifts BTC materially away from the threshold.
- A narrowing realized intraday range that reduces touch probability.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move down materially, likely toward the mid-60s or lower, because the main structural edge for Yes would weaken.
