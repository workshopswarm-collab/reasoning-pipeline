# Assumption

In a weekly BTC hit market with spot already within roughly 1% of the target, realized volatility and benchmark-specific price prints matter more than any single scheduled macro catalyst.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate depends on treating proximity to the barrier as the dominant mechanism. If instead a specific calendar event is required to generate the move, the probability should be lower.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below but near the 75% market baseline.
- The view that ordinary BTC volatility can still produce a $76,000 print before April 19.
- The claim that timing risk is mostly about whether momentum persists and whether the contract benchmark records the print.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- CoinGecko data showed BTC already around $75.4k.
- The remaining distance to $76k was small in percentage terms.
- BTC had already advanced materially over the prior two days, showing that swings of several thousand dollars were occurring in this regime.
- The fetched macro context did not surface one clearly dominant scheduled event within the window.

## What would falsify it

- A clean reading of the rules showing a restrictive benchmark that materially reduces the chance a broad-market $76k print counts.
- New evidence that a major scheduled downside catalyst or market structure headwind is imminent inside the window.
- Abrupt reversal taking BTC materially away from the threshold, reducing the probability that ordinary volatility is enough.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses momentum and trades back below the low-$74k area.
- Risk assets weaken sharply on new macro headlines.
- Contract-source ambiguity turns out to be more important than assumed.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move lower, and the analysis should shift from "ordinary volatility likely sufficient" toward "needs a specific bullish catalyst and favorable benchmark behavior."
