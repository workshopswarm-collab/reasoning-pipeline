# Assumption

BTC will not suffer a roughly 9% or larger downside move on Binance BTC/USDT by the specific April 19 12:00 ET 1-minute close.

## Why this assumption matters

The core base-rate case for Yes is that current Binance price is materially above the threshold and that a short-horizon move large enough to breach 68,000 is possible but not the most likely baseline path.

## What this assumption supports

- A high probability for Yes rather than certainty.
- A view that the market is directionally right but somewhat overconfident.
- A recommendation to treat the main residual risk as sharp downside volatility into the exact settlement minute.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

Current Binance spot and recent 1-minute candles are around 75,000, leaving a sizable cushion above 68,000. Outside-view reasoning says large-cap crypto can move violently over several days, but a clean >9% downside move into one exact minute is not the default path absent a catalyst.

## What would falsify it

- A material risk-off macro shock or crypto-specific liquidation event before settlement.
- Sustained BTC trade below roughly 69,000-70,000 before April 19.
- Exchange-specific disruption or dislocation on Binance BTC/USDT affecting the noon ET close.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid deterioration in BTC over the next 24-48 hours.
- Expanding intraday realized volatility with downside momentum.
- Major negative regulatory, security, or macro headline affecting crypto broadly.
- Binance-specific outage, liquidity problem, or unusual wick behavior.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability of Yes drops materially, and the contract should be treated less like a cushion/base-rate question and more like a path-dependent volatility and venue-risk question.
