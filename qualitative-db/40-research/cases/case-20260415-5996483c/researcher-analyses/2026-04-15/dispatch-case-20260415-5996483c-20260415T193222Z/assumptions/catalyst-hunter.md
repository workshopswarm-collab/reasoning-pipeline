# Assumption

BTC will remain far enough above 70,000 over the next five days that an exact 12:00 ET Binance one-minute close below 70,000 on Apr. 20 is less likely than the market currently implies.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends less on a new bullish catalyst than on the idea that the current price cushion and recent realized range make a drop through the threshold at the exact settlement minute unlikely.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes probability moderately above 90%
- A view that the market is directionally right but slightly rich
- A catalyst framing where absence of negative shocks matters more than presence of fresh positive news

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot during the run was around 75.25k, more than 7% above the threshold.
- Recent Binance daily closes remained above 70k, including after a notable pullback.
- Recent volatility was substantial, but not enough on its own to make a sub-70k exact-minute close the base case from current levels.
- The remaining window is short enough that regime persistence matters more than long-horizon macro thesis changes.

## What would falsify it

- A macro or crypto-specific shock that quickly sends BTC back toward or below 70k.
- A visible breakdown in Binance BTC/USDT price structure with sustained trading near the threshold before Apr. 20.
- New information that the noon-ET candle mapping or settlement interpretation differs from the expected UTC conversion.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the low-72k to 73k region on Binance before the weekend.
- Sharp risk-off moves in broader crypto without quick reversal.
- Weekend gap-like weakness or liquidity thinning that keeps BTC pinned near the strike.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If BTC revisits the threshold zone before Apr. 20, this market becomes much more path-sensitive and the current high-Yes view should compress materially toward the mid-70s or low-80s depending on realized proximity and volatility.
