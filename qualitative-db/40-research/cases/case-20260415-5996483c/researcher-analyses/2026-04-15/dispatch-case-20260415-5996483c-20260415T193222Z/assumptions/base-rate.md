# Assumption

BTC remains in the post-breakout regime where trading several percent above 70,000 is normal enough that a specific Binance noon minute close on Apr 20 is more likely than not to stay above the threshold.

## Why this assumption matters

My high-Yes view depends less on a single catalyst and more on the belief that the market has already shifted into a level regime above 70k rather than merely spiking through it transiently.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above the raw near-term coin-flip baseline.
- The conclusion that current price cushion and recent daily closes carry real predictive value.
- The judgment that the market is directionally right, though perhaps a bit rich.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTC was about 74.9k on Binance at fetch time, around 6-7% above the threshold.
- Several recent Binance daily closes were already above 70k, suggesting persistence rather than one isolated wick.
- Cross-venue spot checks were tightly aligned, lowering concern that the level is idiosyncratic to one feed.

## What would falsify it

- A rapid drawdown that takes BTC back below 70k and keeps it there into Apr 20.
- New evidence that the recent move was driven by a short-lived distortion rather than broader spot demand.
- Material Binance-specific dislocation near the resolving minute.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated failures to hold above 72k over the next few sessions.
- Macro or crypto-specific shock that quickly compresses BTC back toward the high-60s.
- Growing exchange-specific price divergence or market-structure stress.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move down materially, likely into a much more balanced range, because the case would no longer be about a stable above-70k regime but about whether BTC can re-clear and hold the level at one exact minute.
