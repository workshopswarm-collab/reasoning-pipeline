# Summary

Recent high-visibility draft context is supportive of Reese as a top-three prospect but not cleanly supportive of Reese specifically at No. 2. Independent-ish public analysts split between Bailey at No. 2 and Reese at No. 3, which is the main reason to resist overconfidence.

## Key facts extracted

- NFL.com Daniel Jeremiah rankings 4.0 (Apr. 1) ranks Reese No. 5 overall and Bailey No. 4 overall; Reese is described as a versatile front-seven chess piece but still less polished as a rusher than Bailey.
- NFL.com Mike Band mock 2.0 (Apr. 15) explicitly picks Bailey No. 2 to the Jets and Reese No. 3 to Arizona, while acknowledging Reese is the more versatile player.
- ESPN Mel Kiper mock 4.0 (Apr. 15) also projects Bailey No. 2 and Reese No. 3, framing it as a Bailey-versus-Reese debate and saying the Jets may prefer production over potential.
- ESPN draft hub separately confirms the 2026 draft begins April 23 in Pittsburgh, relevant to the contract timing check.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Reese is a highly rated prospect with elite athletic traits and versatility.
- Bailey is often framed as the cleaner pure edge-rush selection and in at least two current mocks is the projected No. 2 pick instead of Reese.
- The public mock/reporting ecosystem does not show unanimous or near-unanimous Reese-at-2 consensus.

## What is uncertain

- These are still public-facing mocks and rankings, not official team intel or settled draft order.
- NFL.com and ESPN are not fully independent in the causal sense because both reflect a common information environment around the same teams and league rumors.
- Team trade possibilities or late medical/character information could rapidly change the board.

## Why this source may matter

This is the clearest disconfirming evidence against a very high Reese probability. If respected draft analysts still prefer Bailey at No. 2 one week before the draft, then a 73.5% market price may be embedding too much confidence.

## Possible impact on the question

Supports Reese as a legitimate contender and likely top-three pick, but pushes against treating him as an overwhelming favorite for exactly the second slot.

Medium-high credibility and very high recency for contextual draft expectations, but still inherently non-authoritative and somewhat correlated. Good for stress-testing confidence, not for settlement.
