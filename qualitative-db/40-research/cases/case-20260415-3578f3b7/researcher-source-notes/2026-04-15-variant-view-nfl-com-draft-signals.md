# Summary

Recent NFL.com draft content is directionally favorable to Arvell Reese but not unanimous. Daniel Jeremiah ranked Reese No. 5 overall and David Bailey No. 4 on Apr. 1. Lance Zierlein's mock draft 3.0 on Apr. 15 put Reese at No. 2 to the Jets. But Mike Band's mock draft 2.0, also on Apr. 15, sent Bailey to the Jets at No. 2 and Reese to Arizona at No. 3. The official NFL draft order page confirms the Jets own the No. 2 pick.

## Key facts extracted

- Official NFL draft order page lists the New York Jets at pick No. 2 in Round 1.
- Official NFL page says Round 1 begins Thursday, Apr. 23, 2026 at 8 p.m. ET in Pittsburgh.
- Daniel Jeremiah's Apr. 1 top-50 ranking places Bailey at No. 4 and Reese at No. 5.
- Jeremiah describes Reese as an explosive chess-piece defender who projects best as an edge rusher but remains somewhat unpolished.
- Mike Band's Apr. 15 mock gives the Jets Bailey at No. 2, explicitly noting Reese is the more versatile front-seven chess piece but preferring Bailey's cleaner premium-position pass-rush profile and production.
- Lance Zierlein's Apr. 15 mock gives the Jets Reese at No. 2, calling him an upside swing and an unpolished diamond.
- NFL.com player page/video index for Reese includes league-network segments explicitly debating whether the Jets should take Reese at No. 2 and noting both Reese and Bailey took Jets top-30 visits.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Reese is a serious top-of-draft candidate, not a fringe longshot.
- Bailey remains a live competing interpretation for No. 2 even in very recent league-media content.
- Jets-specific public framing emphasizes toughness, physicality, and building from the lines inward.
- Reese's case leans on versatility and traits; Bailey's case leans more on cleaner edge projection and sack/pressure production.

## What is uncertain

- NFL.com mock drafts are informed but non-authoritative and may share overlapping reporting priors.
- The fetched Reese player page did not surface a clean static biography; it mostly exposed related video metadata.
- We do not have a direct front-office statement about Reese vs. Bailey for the 2026 choice, only public team-identity comments from the prior draft cycle.
