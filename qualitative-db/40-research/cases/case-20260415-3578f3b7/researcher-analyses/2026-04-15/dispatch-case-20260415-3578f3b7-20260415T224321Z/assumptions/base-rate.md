# Assumption

The No. 2 market should be treated as a two-player decision tree centered on Arvell Reese versus David Bailey, with no late information likely to collapse uncertainty fully before draft night.

## Why this assumption matters

My estimate is below the market because the outside view says a top-of-board defensive prospect market usually retains meaningful final-week uncertainty unless one player has both ranking and fit consensus.

## What this assumption supports

- Keeping Reese below the market’s implied probability.
- Treating Bailey as the main live alternative rather than noise.
- Framing the market as rich but not wildly wrong.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Recent NFL.com analyst material splits on Reese versus Bailey.
- Jeremiah’s board has Bailey slightly ahead of Reese.
- The Jets’ needs profile allows multiple plausible defensive directions.
- Draft markets often look more certain than the actual distribution of team preferences warrants.

## What would falsify it

- High-credibility pre-draft reporting that the Jets have effectively settled on Reese.
- A broad last-week consensus shift across multiple credible outlets toward Reese at No. 2.
- Credible evidence Bailey is sliding for medical, character, or team-fit reasons.

## Early warning signs

- Reese moving to overwhelming favorite status across major mocks and insider reports.
- Bailey dropping consistently behind other prospects in late boards.
- Specific Jets-linked reporting emphasizing Reese’s versatility as the intended answer.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If late information creates a real Reese consensus, the fair probability should move closer to or above the current market price.
