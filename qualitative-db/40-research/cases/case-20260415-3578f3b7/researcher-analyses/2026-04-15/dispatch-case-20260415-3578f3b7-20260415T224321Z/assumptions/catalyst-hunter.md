# Assumption
The market is materially overconfident if it treats scattered mock-draft consensus or generic prospect ranking strength as equivalent to a stable, team-specific lock for the No. 2 selection.

# Why this assumption is being made
- The market price is 0.735, implying a fairly strong favorite.
- The actual event that settles the contract is a single team decision at second overall, potentially affected by trades, late medical/information flow, and team preference.
- Accessible evidence in this run supported Reese as a serious top-of-board prospect, but not enough to justify treating him as near-settled.

# What would weaken or invalidate it
- Multiple independent late-April mocks converging on Reese specifically at No. 2 to the current holder of the pick.
- Strong team-reporter consensus indicating Reese is the preferred selection.
- Official or near-official reporting that a trade scenario increasing Reese's probability is highly likely.

# Monitoring implications
- Watch final 7-10 day mocks, especially from team-connected reporters.
- Watch for trade chatter involving the No. 2 slot.
- Watch any official NFL publication of final order / compensatory changes only as order validation, not pick prediction.
