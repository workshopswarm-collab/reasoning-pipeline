# Assumption

The market is over-weighting Reese's versatile upside relative to the possibility that the Jets prefer a more conventional high-production edge profile such as David Bailey at No. 2.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire variant case depends on the idea that Reese being the favorite is not the same as Reese being close to locked. If the Jets' actual board strongly prefers hybrid versatility and positional multiplicity, then the market's 73.5% could be justified or even low.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate meaningfully below the market.
- The conclusion that Bailey is the main live alternative rather than background noise.
- The view that split mock-draft evidence should cap confidence rather than merely confirm Reese.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Daniel Jeremiah's Apr. 1 rankings still had Bailey one spot ahead of Reese.
- Mike Band's Apr. 15 mock explicitly chose Bailey for the Jets at No. 2 while acknowledging Reese as a credible alternative.
- Jets public team-building comments emphasize toughness and building from the lines inward, which can support either player and therefore do not uniquely validate Reese.
- Zierlein's Reese-at-2 mock still described Reese as an "unpolished diamond," which implies some projection risk.

## What would falsify it

- Credible late reporting that the Jets see Reese as a clear best-player-available tier above Bailey.
- Multiple independent final mocks converging specifically on Reese at No. 2 for reasoned team-fit or sourcing reasons.
- Reliable reporting that Bailey is more likely to slide than go top two.

## Early warning signs

- Jets-linked reporting shifts from "Reese or Bailey" framing to nearly unanimous "Reese." 
- Additional team-specific reporting says Arizona, not New York, is Bailey's likeliest landing spot.
- More public evidence emerges that the Jets prize off-ball/coverage flexibility in this particular defensive role.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move materially upward toward the market or above it, and the main variant thesis would weaken from "market overconfident" to "market correctly pricing the final week."
