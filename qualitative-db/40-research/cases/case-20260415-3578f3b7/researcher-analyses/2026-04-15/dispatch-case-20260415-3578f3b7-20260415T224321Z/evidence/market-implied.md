# Summary

Reese has enough public support to be a credible favorite at No. 2, but the public evidence does not cleanly support a probability as high as the current market price.

## Question being evaluated

Will Arvell Reese be selected second overall in the 2026 NFL Draft?

## Current lean

Lean yes, but at a lower probability than market.

## Prior / starting view

Starting from the market prior, Reese looked like the name the market believed had already separated from the field.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- Official draft order has the Jets at No. 2, and NFL.com lists edge among their biggest needs. Direct/contextual, medium-high weight.
- Reese is elite on public boards: No. 1 for Eric Edholm and No. 5 for Daniel Jeremiah. Contextual, medium weight.
- Lance Zierlein mock 3.0 sends Reese to the Jets at No. 2. Contextual, medium weight.
- Polymarket volume is nontrivial and price leadership is clear, suggesting some aggregation signal beyond one-off mocks. Market evidence, medium weight.

## Evidence against the claim

- Mike Band mock 2.0 sends David Bailey to the Jets at No. 2 and Reese to Arizona at No. 3, explicitly preferring Bailey as the cleaner premium-position pick. Contextual, high weight.
- Daniel Jeremiah ranks Reese behind Love, Styles, and Bailey overall, showing no consensus that Reese is the obvious No. 2 talent. Contextual, medium weight.
- Trade risk is unresolved because the Jets have multiple early picks and multiple needs. Structural, medium weight.
- Reese is described by Jeremiah as a high-upside but still unpolished projection, which weakens confidence that teams will prefer him over cleaner evaluations. Contextual, medium weight.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- Reese’s versatility can be read either as special upside or as less clean positional fit than a pure edge like Bailey.
- Jets need profile supports defense broadly, but not specifically Reese over Bailey/Styles/other outcomes.

## Conflict between inputs

- Main disagreement is interpretive and weighting-based, not factual.
- Public analysts agree Reese is elite, but disagree on whether he is the best match at No. 2.
- Evidence that would resolve it: credible final-week reporting on Jets preference order or serious trade talks.

## Key assumptions

- Jets keep pick No. 2.
- Jets prioritize upside/versatility enough to prefer Reese.
- No late-breaking information changes Reese’s standing.

## Key uncertainties

- True Jets board ordering.
- Whether public market is using private intel or simply amplifying momentum.
- Whether the No. 2 pick is more tradeable than current pricing implies.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Final-week mocks clustering around Bailey or trade scenarios.
- Reporting that the Jets want a purer edge or offensive help.
- Sharp market drift away from Reese.

## What would increase confidence

- Independent reporting that the Jets view Reese as their preferred defender at No. 2.
- Multiple cross-outlet mocks converging on Reese specifically, not just on a defender.
- Evidence that the Jets are unlikely to move the pick.

## Net update logic

The market prior survives first contact with evidence because Reese clearly belongs in the elite tier and the Jets have a plausible need match. But the anti-market case remains material because public consensus is not cleanly one-sided; the next-best explanations are alive enough that I cannot justify a near-75% price.

## Suggested downstream use

Use as orchestrator synthesis input and as a check against over-crediting market confidence without confirmed Jets-specific intel.
