# Summary

These sources together show what the market is currently pricing and what public campaign context may explain that price. Polymarket shows DMK as a clear favorite; recent campaign coverage suggests AIADMK remains the nearest challenger while Vijay's TVK is salient but still plausibly more spoiler than dominant-seat winner.

## Key facts extracted

- Polymarket showed DMK around 72% and ADMK around 24% on fetch, close to the assignment price of 0.735.
- Polymarket volume was about $329K, which is meaningful enough to treat the price as a nontrivial crowd prior rather than a completely illiquid print.
- Times of India campaign snippets from 11-13 April 2026 describe active campaigning by DMK, AIADMK, BJP, and Vijay's TVK, with at least one item explicitly describing Tamil Nadu as a tight three-way race in some voter segments.
- Those same snippets still frame DMK and AIADMK as the dominant comparison set in much of the campaign coverage.
- The Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK) page shows Vijay's party is new, has no assembly seats, and was founded in 2024 specifically to contest the 2026 election.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Polymarket directly states the current crowd probability split.
- Times of India directly states ongoing campaign themes, the salience of Vijay/TVK, and continued attacks between DMK and AIADMK blocs.
- Wikipedia on TVK directly states that the party is new and holds zero assembly seats.

## What is uncertain

- Campaign snippets are not polling and may overweight attention versus actual seat conversion.
- Polymarket itself is not evidence of the real-world outcome; it is evidence of aggregated trader belief.
- TVK's real vote-to-seat efficiency is highly uncertain and could vary sharply by constituency.

## Why this source may matter

This source set is the best available explanation for why the market might rationally sit in the low-70s for DMK: incumbency and prior seat dominance still matter, AIADMK remains the most obvious alternative winner, and TVK appears disruptive but not yet clearly positioned to win the most seats itself.

## Possible impact on the question

The public evidence seems broadly consistent with the market's shape. It supports a view that DMK should be favored, but not near-certainty, because challenger coordination and spoiler dynamics remain unsettled.

## Reliability notes

Mixed but useful. Polymarket is authoritative for the current implied probability. Times of India snippets are recent but contextual and not independently verified polling. Wikipedia on TVK is useful for party age/baseline but is still secondary.
