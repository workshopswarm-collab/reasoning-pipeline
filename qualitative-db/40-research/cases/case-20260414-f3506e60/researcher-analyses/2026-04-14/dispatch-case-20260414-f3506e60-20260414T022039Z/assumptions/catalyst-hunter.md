# Assumption

The central timing assumption is that no late-breaking catalyst before the 23 April 2026 poll will be strong enough to unify the anti-DMK vote or convert TVK visibility into enough seat-efficient support to push DMK below first place in seats.

## Why this assumption matters

The current thesis leans on DMK retaining a structural seat edge into polling day. If a late catalyst sharply changes opposition coordination or vote splitting, the market could reprice quickly and the seat leader could change even without a huge statewide vote swing.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below but broadly near the market-implied DMK favorite status.
- A view that the most likely repricing path is incremental rather than regime-changing unless a major shock appears.
- A judgment that the remaining catalyst set is mostly campaign-execution and alliance-discipline driven.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- DMK enters as the incumbent with the prior-seat advantage and a still-broad alliance frame.
- The late campaign appears active but not obviously dominated by one opposition-breakthrough event.
- Multi-corner competition, including TVK visibility, can preserve the incumbent’s seat advantage if opposition votes remain fragmented.

## What would falsify it

- Credible late polling or constituency evidence showing a broad anti-DMK consolidation.
- A major alliance or endorsement shift that clearly improves opposition seat conversion.
- A campaign shock, scandal, or administrative issue that creates a measurable late swing against DMK across many constituencies.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated reporting that TVK is taking anti-DMK votes less than expected and instead drawing materially from DMK-leaning urban voters.
- Strong opposition narrative convergence around corruption, governance failure, or anti-incumbency that persists across multiple outlets.
- Reliable seat-level chatter showing AIADMK outperforming in battleground western and urban clusters.

## What changes if this assumption fails

DMK would still not necessarily lose outright, but the estimate should move meaningfully down and the market may have been underpricing opposition seat-conversion risk. The analytical emphasis would shift from structural incumbency to late-swing mechanics.
