# Assumption
DMK's 2021 seat advantage, continued incumbency, and opposition fragmentation remain strong enough in 2026 that normal anti-incumbency and campaign volatility do not erase DMK's lead in seats won.

## Why this assumption matters
Most of the pro-DMK case rests on structural advantage rather than directly observed late-campaign vote shares. If this assumption is wrong, a market price in the low-70s is overstating confidence.

## What this assumption supports
- A view that DMK should still be favored to win the most seats.
- A probability above 50% but below near-certainty.
- A recommendation to treat market confidence as directionally reasonable but somewhat fragile.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- DMK won 133 seats in 2021 versus AIADMK's 66.
- DMK remains the incumbent governing party under M. K. Stalin.
- Available contextual evidence in this run does not show a clear opposition realignment strong enough to erase that structural lead.

## What would falsify it
- Credible late polling or seat projections showing AIADMK or another rival coalition at parity or ahead in expected seats.
- Evidence of major alliance collapse, sharp anti-incumbent swing, or a vote-consolidation dynamic against DMK.
- Official or highly credible reporting of broad seat-level underperformance by DMK in early counting trends.

## Early warning signs
- Repeated constituency reporting centered on anti-incumbent local anger rather than isolated dissatisfaction.
- Major ally defection or seat-sharing conflict in the DMK-led alliance.
- Convergent independent media reporting that AIADMK has rebuilt enough coalition breadth to convert discontent into seats.

## What changes if this assumption fails
The correct estimate would move materially toward a toss-up, and the market's current price would look too complacent rather than fair.
