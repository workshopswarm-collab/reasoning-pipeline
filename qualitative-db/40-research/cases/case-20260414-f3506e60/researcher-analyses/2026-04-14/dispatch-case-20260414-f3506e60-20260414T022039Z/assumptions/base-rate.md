# Assumption

The base-rate view assumes the 2026 Tamil Nadu election remains primarily a two-pole DMK-versus-AIADMK contest and that no late opposition consolidation shock materially changes the seat-conversion landscape before polling.

## Why this assumption matters

If the race stays within the long-running DMK/AIADMK structural frame, then DMK's incumbent advantage and large 2021 lead support a relatively high probability that it again wins the most seats. If that frame breaks, the outside-view estimate should fall.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above the current market's coin-flip region and still clearly above 50%.
- A view that the market's 0.735 pricing is directionally sensible.
- A conclusion that extraordinary case-specific evidence would be needed to make AIADMK or another party the likelier leader in seats.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

Tamil Nadu has been structurally dominated by DMK and AIADMK for decades, and the latest completed assembly result was a decisive DMK win. Current-cycle contextual reporting also still frames the election around Stalin's DMK versus Palaniswami's AIADMK.

## What would falsify it

- Credible late evidence of a broad opposition alliance or wave large enough to erase DMK's seat edge.
- Strong, recent constituency-level polling or high-quality reporting showing AIADMK overtaking DMK statewide in likely seat conversion.
- Major alliance collapse or internal disruption on the DMK side close enough to polling day to matter.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated independent reports that anti-incumbent swing is much larger than public expectations.
- Opposition coordination improving in a way that reduces vote splitting.
- Candidate withdrawals, alliance ruptures, or last-minute scandals concentrated in competitive DMK seats.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The appropriate estimate would move materially lower, potentially into a toss-up range, and a more narrative/case-specific analysis would dominate the base-rate prior.
