# Assumption
The opposition field in Tamil Nadu remains fragmented enough in seat conversion terms that DMK can still win the most seats even if anti-incumbent sentiment is real.

## Why this assumption matters
This is the core mechanism separating a mild contrarian view from a straightforward anti-incumbent story. If opposition coordination is cleaner and more efficient than expected, DMK's current market edge could be overstated.

## What this assumption supports
- A DMK probability modestly above the market baseline rather than below it.
- The interpretation that party-level seat efficiency matters more than broad anti-DMK mood.
- The view that TVK and other multi-corner dynamics may help DMK remain the largest party.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- The contract resolves at the party level, not alliance level, increasing the value of being the single best-converting party.
- Recent contextual reporting highlights both AIADMK-led alliance structuring and TVK disruption as active mechanisms.
- Tamil Nadu contests can be highly alliance-sensitive, so incomplete consolidation can matter disproportionately in seats.

## What would falsify it
- Credible late polling or constituency-level reporting showing AIADMK-led coordination is producing a broad two-corner fight with clear anti-DMK consolidation.
- Evidence that TVK is drawing disproportionately from DMK rather than splitting the anti-DMK or floating protest vote.
- Reporting of a major DMK incumbency shock or alliance rupture shortly before polling.

## Early warning signs
- Multiple reputable polls showing AIADMK overtaking DMK in seat projections.
- Repeated district-level reporting that anti-DMK tactical voting is coalescing.
- Strong candidate withdrawals or local arrangements that reduce three-corner contests.

## What changes if this assumption fails
My estimate would move downward meaningfully, likely toward or below market, because the main reason to see DMK as more resilient than a simple anti-incumbent narrative would weaken.
