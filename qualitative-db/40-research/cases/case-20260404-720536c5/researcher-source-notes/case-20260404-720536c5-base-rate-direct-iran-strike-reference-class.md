# Summary

The narrow market resolution criterion is much rarer than generic "Iran-Israel conflict" rhetoric. Open-source background indicates only a very small number of direct, overt Iranian missile/drone strike episodes against Israeli territory from Iranian territory in the modern record, with the best-known precedent being the April 2024 salvo. Current April 2026 reporting indicates an active Iran-related war environment, but even in that environment many salient actions do not cleanly satisfy this market’s exact resolution rule on a given day.

## Key facts extracted

- The market resolves Yes only for a direct Iranian military drone/missile/air strike from Iranian territory that impacts Israeli ground territory on the specified day.
- Intercepted projectiles do not count; proxy attacks do not count; attacks outside Israel proper do not count.
- The best-known modern reference-class example of a direct Iranian strike on Israel is the April 2024 Iranian missile/drone attack.
- Current April 2026 reporting from Al Jazeera indicates an active US-Israel conflict with Iran and sustained regional war conditions, showing elevated overall conflict context.
- Even in active war context, many events emphasized in coverage concern US-Iran combat, shipping, diplomacy, proxies, interceptions, or broader escalation rather than a clean direct Iranian impact on Israeli territory on a particular day.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Polymarket’s market page explicitly defines a qualifying strike as Iranian military aerial munitions launched from Iranian territory that impact Israeli ground territory, and excludes interceptions, proxies, and non-qualifying attack types.
- Wikipedia’s overview page for Iranian strikes on Israel documents the April 2024 direct Iranian attack as part of the sparse direct-strike history.
- Al Jazeera’s current April 2026 coverage shows active war conditions involving Iran, the US, and Israel, plus sustained conflict in the region.

## What is uncertain
