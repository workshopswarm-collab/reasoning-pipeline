# Assumption

SOL can remain at least modestly above the $80 threshold through the specific Binance 12:00 ET minute close on Sunday, even if it experiences ordinary weekend crypto volatility beforehand.

## Why this assumption matters

The bullish case depends less on a new upside catalyst than on the absence of a sharp downside move large enough to push SOL back below $80 at the exact resolving minute.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability above the raw market baseline.
- A view that the most important near-term catalyst is not a bullish event release, but the passage of time without a material breakdown below $80.
- A view that current spot cushion matters more than narrative chatter.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance spot verification showed SOL/USDT around 84.67, giving a current buffer of about 5.8% above the threshold.
- CoinGecko contextual data showed SOL around 84.73 with positive 24h, 7d, and 14d changes, though weaker over 30d; that is consistent with a still-above-threshold but not runaway setup.
- For a close-above market, a several-dollar cushion with only a few days left is meaningful, though weaker than a touch-market setup.

## What would falsify it

- A sustained downside move that takes SOL/USDT below $80 ahead of Sunday.
- A sharp weekend risk-off move in crypto that compresses SOL more than broad majors.
- Any exchange-specific dislocation on Binance SOL/USDT that leaves the noon-ET minute close under $80 even if broader spot references remain near the line.

## Early warning signs

- SOL losing the low-80s and spending meaningful time near 81 or lower.
- Broad crypto weekend weakness led by altcoins.
- Binance-specific spread or pricing anomalies versus other references.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes probability should fall materially, and the contract becomes a pure timestamp risk problem rather than a comfortable hold-above-threshold setup.
