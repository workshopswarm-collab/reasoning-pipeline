# Assumption

Absent an unscheduled macro or crypto-specific shock, BTC is likely to remain comfortably above 68,000 through the April 20 noon ET settlement minute because there is no obvious high-information scheduled catalyst before then and spot has a roughly 9% cushion.

## Why this assumption matters

The bullish conclusion depends less on a claim that BTC cannot trade below 68k and more on a timing claim: that no catalyst before the exact settlement window is likely to produce and sustain a sufficient selloff at the relevant minute close.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below but broadly aligned with the market's high-Yes view.
- The interpretation that known calendar structure is supportive for Yes.
- The framing that the key risk is unscheduled shock rather than a known scheduled event.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Official Fed calendar shows the next FOMC meeting is after resolution.
- BEA's next major GDP / personal income releases are after resolution.
- Binance spot is around 74.9k, requiring about a 9.2% decline to finish below 68k at settlement.
- Recent realized volatility is meaningful, but the threshold is still materially below spot.

## What would falsify it

- A clear risk-off macro headline before April 20 that drives BTC sharply lower.
- A major exchange, ETF-flow, or crypto credit/liquidity shock.
- BTC failing to hold the low-70k area and beginning a cascading deleveraging move into April 20.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid downside acceleration back toward 70k with elevated liquidation-like price action.
- Adverse weekend geopolitical or macro headlines.
- Exchange outage or market-structure disruption affecting Binance-specific prints.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a genuine downside catalyst emerges and BTC loses the low-70k region decisively, the probability of finishing above 68k should fall quickly because the cushion is no longer large relative to short-term volatility.
