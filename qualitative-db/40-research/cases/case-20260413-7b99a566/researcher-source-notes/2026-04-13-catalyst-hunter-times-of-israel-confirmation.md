# Summary

The Times of Israel reported that the first in-person meeting in the expected negotiations between Israel and Lebanon would take place Tuesday in Washington, and quoted both Lebanese and Israeli public statements acknowledging the arrangement.

## Key facts extracted

- The report explicitly called the planned session the first in-person meeting in the expected negotiations.
- It said the meeting would be hosted at the U.S. State Department on Tuesday, April 14.
- It named the Israeli and Lebanese ambassadors to Washington, plus the U.S. ambassador to Lebanon, as attendees.
- It quoted Lebanon's presidency saying the call was part of diplomatic efforts aimed at securing a ceasefire and launching negotiations, and that both sides agreed to hold the first meeting on Tuesday at the State Department under U.S. mediation.
- It quoted Israeli Ambassador Yechiel Leiter saying Israel agreed to begin formal peace negotiations on Tuesday.
