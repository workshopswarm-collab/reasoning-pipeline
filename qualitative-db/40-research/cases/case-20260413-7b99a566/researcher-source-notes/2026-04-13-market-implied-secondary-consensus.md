# Summary

A Google News aggregation snapshot on April 13 showed multiple outlets independently reporting a near-term official Israel-Lebanon diplomatic encounter, including Times of Israel, CBS/PBS-style summaries, Al Jazeera, and Reuters. Several headlines specifically referenced a first meeting in Washington / at the US State Department on Tuesday, April 14.

## Key facts extracted

- There was broad cross-outlet convergence that Israel and Lebanon were expected to meet in Washington on or around April 14.
- Some headlines described the event as the first meeting / direct talks / US-mediated talks rather than only a proposal.
- This materially strengthens the market’s consensus-reporting path if the meeting occurs as scheduled.

## Evidence directly stated by source

Examples visible in the aggregation included:
- Times of Israel: "First meeting in Israel-Lebanon talks to take place Tuesday in DC – US official"
- CBS/PBS style headline: US to lead ceasefire talks / Israel agrees to talks with Lebanon
- Al Jazeera: Israel rejects ceasefire with Hezbollah before Lebanon talks next week
- Reuters: Israel and Lebanon are expected to hold talks / Lebanon heads to historic Israel talks

## What is uncertain

- Aggregated headlines are not a substitute for reading each original article.
- Some downstream outlets may ultimately trace back to the same originating US-official briefing, so true evidentiary independence is less than headline count suggests.
- Consensus reporting of a scheduled meeting is still not equivalent to confirmed completion of a qualifying in-person meeting.
