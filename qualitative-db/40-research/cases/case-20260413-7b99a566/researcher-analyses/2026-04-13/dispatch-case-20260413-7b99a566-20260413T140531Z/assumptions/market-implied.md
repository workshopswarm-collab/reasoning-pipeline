# Assumption

The market is implicitly assuming that the widely reported US-hosted Israel-Lebanon talks will actually occur in person and will satisfy the contract’s qualification standard for a diplomatic meeting.

## Why this assumption matters

If that assumption is wrong, the current price is too high. The difference between "talks planned" and "qualifying in-person diplomatic meeting publicly acknowledged or credibly confirmed" is the whole market.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate materially above 50%
- A view that the market is mostly efficient rather than stale
- A conclusion that the main remaining risk is execution / wording rather than absence of intent

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Reuters and multiple secondary outlets reported imminent official talks.
- The reported timeframe is very near, which reduces open-ended diplomatic slippage risk.
- US mediation makes an indirect in-person format plausible under the contract wording.

## What would falsify it

- Public cancellation, postponement, or downgrade to calls / remote contact only
- Reporting that no Israeli and Lebanese authorized representatives were physically present in a qualifying format
- A final record showing only shuttle diplomacy without a contract-qualifying in-person mediated meeting

## Early warning signs

- Escalating violence that causes either side to pull back
- Official rhetoric narrowing the encounter into a technical security contact rather than diplomacy
- Reporting emphasizing there was no direct or qualifying mediated meeting, only parallel briefings

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should fall sharply toward No, because the time window is short and the current market premium is mostly about imminent execution rather than broad long-term détente.
