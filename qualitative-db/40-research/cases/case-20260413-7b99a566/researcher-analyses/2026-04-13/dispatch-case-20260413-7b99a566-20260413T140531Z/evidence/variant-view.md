# Summary

The strongest affirmative mechanism is real: credible media now point to an expected Washington meeting involving Israel and Lebanon representatives on April 14, well before the April 19 deadline. The variant view is not “No way.” It is that the market may be overconfident because expected talks, mediated talks, and contract-qualifying in-person diplomacy are not perfectly interchangeable.

## Question being evaluated

Is the market-implied 71.5% probability too high for a contract-qualifying diplomatic meeting between Israel and Lebanon by April 19, 2026?

## Current lean

Slight bearish lean versus the market: Yes is more likely than No, but not by as much as the market price implies.

## Prior / starting view

Starting prior was below 50% because Israel-Lebanon official diplomacy is rare, politically sensitive, and highly dependent on mediators and conflict conditions.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- The rule allows indirect in-person meetings through mediators, which is bullish, but only if the relevant parties are present, which keeps format risk alive.
- If Washington hosts separate but co-located delegations with mediator shuttling, the event may still qualify; if it is only preparatory or remote-linked, it will not.

## Conflict between inputs

- Main disagreement is not factual on whether a diplomatic process exists; it is interpretive and timing-based on whether the reported Washington process is enough to count under the contract.
- Evidence that would resolve this: named delegates, same-room vs shuttle structure, and government acknowledgment after the meeting.

## Key assumptions

- See `assumptions/variant-view.md`.
- The market is pricing expectation of talks too similarly to completed qualifying diplomacy.

## Key uncertainties

- Exact meeting structure.
- Whether authorized representatives from both governments are physically present.
- Whether either government publicly acknowledges the event in qualifying terms.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Official Israeli or Lebanese acknowledgment of an in-person meeting.
- Multiple major outlets after April 14 clearly describing a completed meeting between representatives of both countries.
- Named authorized delegates with venue confirmation.

## What would increase confidence

- Full-text Reuters/AP/AFP-style wire copy with participant and format specifics.
- Government statement from either side.
- Same-day post-meeting reporting confirming co-presence and official capacity.

## Net update logic

What mattered most was not generic “peace talks possible” framing but the specific timing: there appears to be a real Washington event tomorrow. That pushed the probability above 50. What kept me below the market is that the contract is narrower than headline diplomacy language, and the available reporting still leaves enough format/execution ambiguity that I do not want to price it as a 70%+ near-done outcome.
