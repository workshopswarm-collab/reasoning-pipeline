# Assumption

The 0.76 market price is mainly embedding ordinary home-favorite team-strength assumptions rather than special settlement ambiguity or hidden event-specific information.

## Why this assumption matters

If true, the market is probably near a standard bookmaker-style home-win prior and should be treated as moderately efficient absent strong contrary team news.

## What this assumption supports

- A view that the market may already be pricing the main public information correctly.
- A probability estimate close to, but slightly below, the market rather than a sharp contrarian stance.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

This is a standard 90-minute soccer winner market with simple resolution mechanics, and the contract wording does not create obvious hidden asymmetries. In such markets, a mid-to-high 70s home-win price usually implies the market sees Tolima as a clear home favorite and not much else.

## What would falsify it

- Credible pre-match reporting of major Tolima absences or squad rotation.
- Evidence that external odds are materially lower than the market price.
- Evidence that the listed match status, venue, or competition context differs from the market framing.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid price drift downward without a visible contract reason.
- Multiple contextual previews framing the game as closer to even than the market suggests.
- Uncertainty about whether the official governing source recognizes the match status normally.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case would shift from 'market likely efficient' toward 'market stale or overextended', and further verification of team news / external prices would matter more.
