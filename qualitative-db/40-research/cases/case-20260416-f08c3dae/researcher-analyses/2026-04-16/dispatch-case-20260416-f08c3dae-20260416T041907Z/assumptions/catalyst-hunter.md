# Assumption

Tolima's Apr 14 Libertadores match creates some rotation/fatigue risk for Apr 18, but not enough on current evidence to erase its home advantage versus Pereira.

## Why this assumption matters

The main reason not to simply follow the market higher than 0.76 is the only clear near-term catalyst against Tolima: short rest after continental play. If that risk is larger than assumed, the market is too optimistic.

## What this assumption supports

- A mildly bullish but not extreme Tolima win probability.
- Treating late lineup confirmation as the most important pre-kickoff repricing trigger.
- Rough agreement with the market rather than a strong anti-market stance.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ESPN event data shows Tolima played Nacional in Libertadores on Apr 14 and now hosts Pereira on Apr 18.
- The same result block still shows Pereira entering on a weaker domestic sequence, so Tolima does not need to be at full strength to remain the likelier winner.
- Home venue at Estadio Manuel Murillo Toro is explicitly confirmed in the fixture source.

## What would falsify it

- Credible late reporting of multiple Tolima starters unavailable or heavily rotated.
- Evidence of travel/fitness disruption worse than a normal four-day turnaround.
- Official lineups showing a substantially weakened Tolima XI relative to recent league norms.

## Early warning signs

- Club or local match previews emphasizing recovery management.
- Market price drifting materially below the current 0.76 without a broad market move.
- Absence rumors centered on Tolima's core attackers or goalkeeper.

## What changes if this assumption fails

My probability would move down toward coin-flip or below, and the right catalyst framing would shift from "routine home favorite unless team news worsens" to "market underpricing rotation damage."
