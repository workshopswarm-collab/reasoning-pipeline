# Assumption

Absent strong contrary team-specific evidence, the market's strong home-win price is broadly tracking a real underlying favorite setup rather than a major mispricing.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate is intentionally close to, but slightly below, the market. That only makes sense if the market is reflecting genuine team-strength and home-field information not contradicted by accessible evidence.

## What this assumption supports

- A base-rate estimate near the market rather than a large contrarian move.
- The view that CD Tolima is more likely than not to win, but not quite as likely as the 0.76 market price implies.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- For ordinary domestic soccer 1X2-style spots, a team priced this strongly is usually a genuine favorite.
- No accessible independent evidence in this run clearly overturned the market's implied hierarchy.
- The case is labeled low difficulty and low resolution risk, which is consistent with a straightforward favorite-versus-underdog framing rather than a deeply ambiguous contract.

## What would falsify it

- Credible team-news or lineup evidence showing CD Tolima is materially weakened.
- Independent odds or statistical previews showing the true win probability is far lower than the market implies.
- Strong evidence that Deportivo Pereira's current form/availability substantially narrows the gap.

## Early warning signs

- Market price dropping sharply before kickoff.
- Late injury/suspension news for Tolima.
- Independent preview data converging closer to draw / away upset risk.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move down materially and the current near-market posture would become too optimistic.
