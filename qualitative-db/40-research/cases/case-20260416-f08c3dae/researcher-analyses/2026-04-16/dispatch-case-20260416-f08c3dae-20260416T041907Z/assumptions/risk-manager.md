# Assumption

Tolima's strong home/overall edge and Pereira's winless record are more predictive here than generic soccer draw variance.

## Why this assumption matters

The difference between a high-but-not-extreme Tolima win probability and a near-market 0.76+ view depends on whether record asymmetry overwhelms the still-meaningful chance of a draw in a 90-minute soccer contract.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability estimate.
- Agreement or rough agreement with the market's direction.
- A conclusion that Pereira upset risk is lower than draw risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ESPN lists Tolima at 7-6-3 versus Pereira at 0-7-9.
- Tolima's recent home results include several wins by multiple goals.
- ESPN/DraftKings contextual prices also make Tolima a strong favorite.

## What would falsify it

- Credible pre-match information showing Tolima resting key players, severe absences, or lineup disruption.
- A sharp market move away from Tolima across major books without an obvious pricing error.
- Official or credible reporting that Pereira enters with materially improved squad strength or tactical situation not captured in the recent record.

## Early warning signs

- Tolima price drifts notably weaker before kickoff.
- Confirmed absences for Tolima attackers or creators.
- New reporting emphasizing weather, pitch, or rotation conditions that increase draw likelihood.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The fair value should move down toward a more draw-heavy distribution, and the gap between market confidence and justified confidence would widen.
