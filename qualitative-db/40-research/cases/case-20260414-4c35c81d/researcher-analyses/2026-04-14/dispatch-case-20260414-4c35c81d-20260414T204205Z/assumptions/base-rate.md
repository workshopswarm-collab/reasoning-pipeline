# Assumption

Absent strong squad-news or standings evidence, a late-season Saudi Pro League home side like Al Qadisiyah should not be priced as an ~83% outright winner against another established top-flight side.

## Why this assumption matters

This assumption anchors the outside-view estimate and explains why the market price looks somewhat rich unless case-specific evidence clearly overwhelms the generic league prior.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below the market price.
- A cautious stance toward vivid or one-sided narratives without stronger direct evidence.
- Treating draw risk and ordinary league parity as material.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Ordinary top-flight soccer matches have meaningful draw frequency.
- Even clearly better teams rarely deserve extreme outright-win probabilities absent exceptional mismatch, injuries, or motivation asymmetry.
- The contextual fixture source suggests these teams already played a competitive 2-2 league match earlier in the season.

## What would falsify it

- Strong evidence that Al Qadisiyah are dramatically superior in league table, goal difference, or home form.
- Credible team-news showing Al Shabab are missing multiple core starters or are otherwise severely weakened.
- A trustworthy odds screen showing the broader market also converged near or above the current implied probability.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple books or data providers implying a home win well above 75%.
- Official or credible pre-match reports indicating severe Al Shabab absences.
- Table/form data showing Al Qadisiyah performing like a title-level side while Al Shabab are near relegation level.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The base-rate estimate should move materially upward and closer to the current market, with less emphasis on generic draw/parity considerations.
