# Assumption

A pre-match regulation-time home-win price of 83% is too high unless there is strong current evidence of a major team-quality or availability gap that was not legibly established in this run.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire variant thesis depends on treating the market as potentially overconfident rather than correctly reflecting overwhelming underlying team strength.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below market.
- A cautious interpretation of the Yes side despite apparent favorite status.
- The claim that sparse independent evidence should compress confidence rather than justify an extreme price.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Soccer three-way outcomes are structurally draw-prone, so regulation-time win probabilities should be treated carefully.
- The market contract is strict win-only, not draw-no-bet or advancement.
- The additional verification pass in this run did not uncover strong independent match-specific evidence that would clearly support such an extreme favorite price.

## What would falsify it

- Strong, recent standings/form data showing Al Qadisiyah materially above Al Shabab.
- Credible lineup or injury news indicating Al Shabab will be significantly weakened.
- Consensus bookmaker or model prices clustering near or above the market-implied 83% after accounting for margin.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple independent pre-match books moving sharply toward Al Qadisiyah.
- Confirmed absences for key Al Shabab players.
- Newly available official competition context showing a much wider strength gap than assumed.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The variant discount should narrow or disappear, and the case would likely move toward rough agreement with the market.
