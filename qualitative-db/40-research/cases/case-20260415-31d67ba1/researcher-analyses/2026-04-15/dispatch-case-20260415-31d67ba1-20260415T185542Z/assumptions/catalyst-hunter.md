# Assumption

The key assumption is that no major negative macro, crypto-specific, or exchange-specific shock occurs before the April 17 12:00 ET settlement minute that is large enough to push Binance BTC/USDT down more than about 5-6% from the current mid-74k range.

## Why this assumption matters

The case is now mostly about path risk over a short window, not long-run Bitcoin direction. If this assumption fails, the current cushion over 70,000 can disappear quickly and the market could be mispriced even at a 97% implied probability.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability Yes view.
- A thesis that the most relevant catalysts are downside shocks rather than routine bullish continuation.
- Agreement or rough agreement with the market's high Yes price.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance, TradingView, and CNBC context all place BTC in the 74k area on April 15.
- With less than two days to settlement, a move below 70,000 requires a sizable downside swing from current levels.
- No specific imminent scheduled catalyst was identified in this pass that obviously carries that much expected downside information before noon ET on April 17.

## What would falsify it

- A sudden macro risk-off event that hits crypto hard.
- A major regulatory, security, or exchange-operational shock affecting BTC or Binance specifically.
- A rapid BTC breakdown through low-72k and then 70k support before settlement.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 73k area decisively on Binance.
- Sharp risk-off moves in broader crypto or equities.
- News of Binance-specific outages, pricing anomalies, or market-structure issues near settlement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move materially lower, potentially well below market if there is evidence that downside volatility is clustering close to the settlement window.
