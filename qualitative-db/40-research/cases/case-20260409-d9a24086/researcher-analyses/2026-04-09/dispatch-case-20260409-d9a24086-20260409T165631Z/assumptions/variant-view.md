# Assumption

The market’s high YES price overstates the chance that March 2026 CPI-U will print at or above **0.8% on the BLS seasonally adjusted one-decimal headline measure**, rather than merely reflecting a hot inflation narrative.

## Why this assumption matters

The variant thesis depends on the distinction between broad inflation concern and the exact settlement statistic. A threshold this high can fail even if inflation is clearly elevated, as long as the official seasonally adjusted headline print rounds to 0.7% or below.

## What this assumption supports

- A materially lower own probability than the market-implied probability.
- A view that the crowd may be overconfident because narrative heat and contract mechanics are not the same thing.
- Extra caution around rounding and seasonal-adjustment effects.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The contract resolves to the BLS seasonally adjusted monthly CPI-U rounded to one decimal place.
- BLS shows adjusted and unadjusted monthly changes can differ materially.
- BLS seasonal-adjustment documentation says aggregate all-items seasonal factors are dependently derived and not knowable in advance from a simple component sum.
- Cleveland Fed’s inflation nowcasting page provides contextual support that pre-release CPI estimates are model-based and fallible rather than settlement-equivalent.

## What would falsify it

- A credible independent nowcast or market-color source showing very strong confidence that the BLS seasonally adjusted all-items CPI-U will print at 0.8% or above.
- Late-breaking high-frequency price evidence that clearly pushes the official adjusted headline measure above the threshold.
- The actual BLS release printing 0.8% or higher.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple credible preview sources converging on 0.8% or above rather than merely high inflation.
- Clear evidence that energy or other volatile components are large enough to survive seasonal adjustment into the headline print.
- Any indication that traders are pricing the threshold off better private previews than the public information set suggests.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The variant bearish-on-YES view weakens materially, and the case should move closer to the market or even above it if evidence suggests the settlement statistic itself is likely to cross 0.8%.
