# Assumption

The best pre-release directional guide is that the official BLS March 2026 seasonally adjusted CPI-U print will land close enough to the Cleveland Fed April 9 nowcast of 0.84% m/m that the one-decimal-place reported result is more likely than not to resolve at 0.8% or higher.

## Why this assumption matters

The market closes before the official BLS release, so most of the remaining repricing path depends on whether late nowcasts and preview signals are informative about the single governing catalyst rather than just noisy narrative.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly bullish-over-consensus view on the YES side.
- The claim that the dominant catalyst is the April 10 8:30 AM ET BLS release, with Cleveland Fed nowcasting as the most informative late contextual check available on April 9.
- The conclusion that the market is directionally sensible but still leaves some room for upside if traders underweight the latest nowcast versus the high threshold.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BLS is the direct settlement source and reports the relevant CPI-U monthly change to one decimal place.
- Cleveland Fed inflation nowcasting, captured on April 9, shows March 2026 CPI at 0.84% m/m, above the 0.8% threshold.
- Because the market threshold is assessed at one decimal place, a nowcast modestly above 0.8% is more supportive than a borderline 0.80x internal estimate would be in a higher-precision contract.
- The annual BLS seasonal-factor refresh was already incorporated in February 2026, reducing concern that a surprise seasonal-methodology change right before release will create a wholly different interpretive basis.

## What would falsify it

- A credible late preview or leak implying a materially lower seasonally adjusted all-items CPI-U print.
- Evidence that the Cleveland Fed nowcast is referencing a differently defined CPI measure than the market's settlement series.
- The official BLS March release printing 0.7% or lower.

## Early warning signs

- Late selloff in the market despite no new supportive data.
- Independent bank previews clustering materially below 0.8% SA headline CPI.
- Discovery of unusual seasonal-adjustment or rounding treatment that makes 0.84-style nowcasts less informative for the one-decimal settlement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case would shift from a modest YES lean to either near-fair pricing or a NO lean, because the whole thesis is built around a single high-information catalyst and one late contextual verification source rather than a broad mosaic of independent primary indicators.
