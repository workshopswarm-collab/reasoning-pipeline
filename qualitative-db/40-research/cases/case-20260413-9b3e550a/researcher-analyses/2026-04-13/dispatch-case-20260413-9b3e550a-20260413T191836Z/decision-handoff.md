# Decision summary

PP–DB appears more likely than not to finish third, but the best synthesis view is materially below the market’s 0.78 because the available evidence supports PP–DB as the modal third-place candidate in a tight upper cluster rather than a near-lock for exact third by seats.

## Why this may matter now

Market implies 0.78; my syndicated range is 0.60-0.69. That leaves PP–DB favored but not decisively enough to support the market’s confidence. The likely mispricing is that traders may be treating PP–DB as having a natural stable home in third when the evidence more strongly supports a noisy exact-rank cluster.

## Shift versus swarm baseline

This range is only modestly above the swarm-implied center near 0.62. The main reason for being slightly higher than the median is that the base-rate lane’s argument that most cross-firm polling still has PP–DB ahead of DPS looked directionally stronger than the most skeptical lanes’ reliance on generic rank-volatility arguments. But the difference is small because the synthesis-stage verification did not uncover strong new evidence warranting a bigger move.

## Edge verification status

Independent verification was medium quality, not high. I independently checked the raw persona findings against their sidecars and confirmed that the sidecars were broadly faithful: base-rate and risk-manager were especially faithful; catalyst-hunter, market-implied, and variant-view were somewhat compressed but not distorted. I also tested whether fresh synthesis-stage web research could validate a stronger edge, but external search failed in this environment, so no stronger independent public evidence was added beyond the lanes’ own verification work. What was independently checked well enough for synthesis use: contract mechanics, timing relevance, and the fact that all usable public-context views place PP–DB in the relevant upper cluster. What remained weak: late independent polling, district seat-conversion evidence, and direct official-source access. That supports a medium, not high, verification-quality rating.

## Compression toward market

No. I did not materially compress toward the market because the swarm was already below-market and the synthesis-stage pass did not discover new evidence strong enough to justify moving upward toward 0.78. I also did not move materially further away from the market because the bundle still supports PP–DB as the modal third-place outcome. The result is a modestly tightened below-market range rather than a reversion toward market.

## Timing and catalyst posture

The next key catalyst is final late polling and then election-day / immediate post-election seat-order reporting around 19 April 2026. Because this is a freshness-sensitive exact-rank market, any edge is likely to decay or move sharply on new hard information rather than drift slowly. Waiting for stronger late polling would probably improve decision quality if available, but after that the edge window may close quickly.

## Key blockers

The main blockers are thin independent verification of the exact-third gap, uncertainty about seat conversion from narrow vote leads, and limited direct official-source access. There is no major contract blocker, but there is enough evidence-quality friction to prevent high confidence.

## Best countercase

The strongest countercase, best represented by base-rate and partially by risk-manager, is that PP–DB is simply the stable modal third-place outcome: most available polls put it third, Revival looks less competitive than in some earlier periods, and DPS only narrowly challenges it, so the true probability could be closer to low 70s than mid 60s.

## What would change the view

Several fresh independent polls showing PP–DB clearly ahead of both DPS and Revival for the number-three slot would move me upward. Any strong seat-model or district analysis showing PP–DB has a reliable seat-conversion advantage at similar vote shares would also raise the estimate. Conversely, credible late polling or early returns showing DPS or Revival clearly ahead would move me sharply lower.

## Recommended next action

Collect one stronger late-cycle polling or seat-projection source if possible, then reassess immediately. If no better source becomes available, treat the case as a moderate below-market Yes rather than a high-conviction edge. Request decision-maker review only if position sizing depends on whether the true probability is closer to low 60s or low 70s.
