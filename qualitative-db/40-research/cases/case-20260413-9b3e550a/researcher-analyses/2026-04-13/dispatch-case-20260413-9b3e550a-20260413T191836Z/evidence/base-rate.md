# Main claim
PP–DB is the most likely third-place finisher in the 2026 Bulgarian parliamentary election, but with enough competition from DPS that the probability should be below the market's 78% level.

# Supporting evidence
1. **2024 baseline favors PP–DB over immediate rivals**
   - October 2024 recalculated vote shares: PP–DB 14.2, Revival 13.4, DPS 11.5.
   - Supports PP–DB as the strongest incumbent candidate for third once a new top-two entrant displaces it from second.

2. **Recent cross-firm polling usually places PP–DB third**
   - Alpha Research (19-26 Mar): PP–DB 11.1 vs DPS 9.8 vs Revival 6.9.
   - Gallup (20-30 Mar): PP–DB 10.9 vs DPS 10.7 vs Revival 6.5.
   - Sova Harris (2-6 Apr): PP–DB 11.2 vs DPS 9.7 vs Revival 7.8.
   - MarketLinks (17-21 Mar): PP–DB 13.3 vs DPS 10.5 vs Revival 5.5.

3. **Contract structure reduces some ambiguity**
   - Resolves by seat ranking, then valid votes, then alphabetical abbreviation, with CIK fallback if reporting is ambiguous.
   - This makes the event operationally legible even if the third-place gap is narrow.

# Disconfirming evidence
1. **PP–DB margin over DPS is often thin**
   - In Gallup late March, PP–DB leads DPS by only 0.2 points.
   - Thin margins can easily flip from turnout or house effects.

2. **Progressive Bulgaria may cannibalize anti-GERB / reform voters**
   - New party entry changes the competitive structure and may reduce the predictive power of older PP–DB second-place status.

3. **Official-source access was incomplete in this environment**
   - CIK could not be directly fetched due to Cloudflare, so official verification relied on the market's stated fallback rule plus AP-cited date information from secondary reporting.

# Net assessment
- Support is stronger than disconfirmation, so PP–DB should remain favorite.
- But evidence does not support near-certainty; the strongest alternative is DPS, not Revival.

# Decision relevance
A reasonable base-rate estimate is in the upper-60s to low-70s rather than the market's high-70s.
