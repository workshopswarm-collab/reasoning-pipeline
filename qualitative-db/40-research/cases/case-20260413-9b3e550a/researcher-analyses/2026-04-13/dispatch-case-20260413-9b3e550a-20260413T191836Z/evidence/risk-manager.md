# Summary

Net evidence supports PP–DB as a plausible favorite for third place, but not at the confidence implied by a 0.78 market. The main risk is not that PP–DB is irrelevant; it is that fragmented-party ordering can reshuffle exact seat rank near the cutoff.

## Question being evaluated

Will We Continue the Change – Democratic Bulgaria (PP–DB) finish third in the 2026 Bulgarian parliamentary election?

## Current lean

Lean yes, but with meaningful fragility and lower confidence than market.

## Prior / starting view

Starting from the market price, the baseline view was that PP–DB was treated as a strong favorite for exact third place.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- Polymarket contract wording makes third place an exact seat-order question rather than a vague competitiveness question; this helps because PP–DB is at least in the relevant top cluster rather than outside it. Weight: medium. Direct for interpretation, indirect for outcome.
- Outgoing parliamentary seat context places PP–DB on 36 seats versus Revival on 33 and DPS on 29, suggesting PP–DB starts from a real but not huge edge. Weight: medium. Contextual rather than direct forecast evidence.
- POLITICO Bulgaria Poll of Polls page capture confirms PP–DB remains one of the central parties in the current election field and that Revival is a major competitor at roughly 14.0%, reinforcing that PP–DB is in contention for top-three placement. Weight: medium. Contextual polling evidence.

## Evidence against the claim

- The market is about exact third place on seats, and fragmented multiparty contests can convert small polling or regional-efficiency differences into rank-order changes. Weight: high. Structural/risk evidence.
- My contextual polling extraction was partial, not a clean ranked table with fresh independent seat projections, so confidence should be capped. Weight: medium. Source-quality downside.
- The nearest rivals appear close enough that late-campaign movement, turnout asymmetry, or seat-efficiency effects could push PP–DB to fourth. Weight: high. Scenario risk.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- The outgoing-seat ranking supports PP–DB but may not transport cleanly into the next snap election after another government collapse.
- Poll aggregators are informative, but exact seat ranking in Bulgaria can depend on threshold and distribution effects not captured by a single national topline.

## Conflict between inputs

There is no hard factual conflict in the retrieved evidence. The conflict is weighting-based: supportive context says PP–DB is live for third, while the contract structure and fragmented system say confidence should be lower than a simple “favored” label suggests.

## Key assumptions

- PP–DB remains ahead of at least one of Revival and DPS-like competitors on actual seat conversion.
- No coalition-dissolution interpretation materially changes how the contract counts PP–DB.
- Consensus reporting before official certification will broadly match the eventual CIK ordering.

## Key uncertainties

- Clean current ranking of PP–DB versus the nearest third-place competitors.
- Whether national polling gaps translate efficiently into seats.
- How much late movement remains possible before 19 April 2026.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Repeated late polling or credible reporting showing PP–DB fourth.
- Election-night tallies with Revival or DPS-New Beginning ahead on seats.
- Any contract-relevant coalition-counting complication involving PP–DB.

## What would increase confidence

- A direct CIK election calendar/result page capture in this environment.
- A clean recent independent poll table or seat projection showing PP–DB clearly ahead of fourth place.
- Multiple independent contextual sources converging on PP–DB as a stable third rather than merely one of several clustered contenders.

## Net update logic

The evidence did not move me all the way off the market’s direction. It moved me off the market’s confidence. PP–DB looks more likely than not to finish third, but the exact-rank structure, partial source stack, and fragmented-party environment make 78% feel too high.

## Suggested downstream use

Use as decision-maker review input and as a caution against overconfidence in exact-rank election markets where the favorite is plausible but not cleanly separated.
