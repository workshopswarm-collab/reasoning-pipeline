# Summary

The case currently nets out as a disagreement with the market: the available evidence supports PP–DB as a live contender for third place, but not as a near-lock.

## Question being evaluated

Will We Continue the Change – Democratic Bulgaria (PP–DB) finish third in the 2026 Bulgarian parliamentary election?

## Current lean

Lean **no / below-market on PP–DB finishing exactly third**.

## Prior / starting view

Starting from the market price alone, the baseline implied view was that PP–DB had roughly a four-in-five chance of finishing third.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- **Current parliamentary prominence and established national presence**
  - source: Wikipedia election overview and PP–DB page
  - why it matters causally: PP–DB is already one of the largest established blocs and remains one of the obvious competitors for a top-three finish
  - direct/indirect: indirect for the 2026 result
  - weight: medium

- **Poll cluster still keeps PP–DB in the immediate third-place band**
  - source: POLITICO Poll of Polls Bulgaria
  - why it matters causally: PP–DB at 14.5% is not out of the race; it is within the competitive band for third and even near second among the non-GERB pack depending on seat conversion
  - direct/indirect: contextual but highly relevant
  - weight: medium

## Evidence against the claim

- **PP–DB does not appear clearly separated from its nearest rivals**
  - source: POLITICO Poll of Polls Bulgaria
  - why it matters causally: DPS at 14.7% and Revival at 14.0% imply an effectively clustered three-way race, which is inconsistent with a 78% PP–DB probability unless seat-conversion evidence strongly favors PP–DB
  - direct/indirect: contextual but highly relevant
  - weight: high

- **Current assembly ordering is not favorable to the exact-third thesis**
  - source: Wikipedia election overview
  - why it matters causally: the outgoing-seat ordering has PP–DB ahead of Revival, meaning PP–DB has multiple plausible finishing slots, not just third; that makes exact-third a narrower event than general top-three relevance
  - direct/indirect: indirect
  - weight: medium

- **Little time for soft narratives to overwhelm election mechanics**
  - source: election-date verification and contract timing
  - why it matters causally: with the vote days away, repricing should be driven mainly by hard late polls, turnout signals, or official reporting rather than broad image repair
  - direct/indirect: direct on catalyst timing
  - weight: medium

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- PP–DB’s anti-corruption and pro-European identity can help it retain a durable urban/reform vote, but that does not cleanly answer whether it lands second, third, or fourth.
- DPS fragmentation history and coalition complexity could matter, but this run did not establish a clean seat-conversion edge from that alone.

## Conflict between inputs

The main conflict is not factual but **weighting-based and timing-based**:
- market price implies a fairly dominant PP–DB path to third
- contextual competitive data implies a much noisier race among PP–DB, DPS, and Revival

What would resolve it:
- fresh late polls with methodology clarity
- strong seat-model evidence
- election-day returns / consensus reporting

## Key assumptions

- No late catalyst creates a clear PP–DB edge before voting.
- The polling cluster is directionally informative even if not precisely predictive.
- Exact-third pricing should reflect clustering risk, not just broad viability.

## Key uncertainties

- District-level seat conversion
- turnout composition
- late breaks among undecided voters
- whether DPS’s organizational vote turns out more efficiently than toplines imply

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Fresh polls showing PP–DB clearly above both DPS and Revival.
- Analyst/credible media consensus that PP–DB has a seat-efficiency edge.
- Official early results placing PP–DB firmly into third.

## What would increase confidence

- A fresh independent poll average for the final week.
- A credible Bulgaria-focused outlet comparing seat-conversion scenarios.
- CIK election-day reporting confirming actual ordering.

## Net update logic

The key update is from “PP–DB is obviously one of the major blocs” to “PP–DB may be overpriced specifically for exact third.” The market seems to be pricing confidence where the accessible contextual evidence shows clustering. That does not make PP–DB unlikely; it makes the exact-third contract less secure than the market implies.
