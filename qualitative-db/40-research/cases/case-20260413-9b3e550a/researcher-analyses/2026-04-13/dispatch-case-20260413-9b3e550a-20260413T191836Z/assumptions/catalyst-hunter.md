# Assumption

There is no large, credible, late-breaking catalyst in the final pre-election week that sharply improves PP–DB’s odds of landing exactly third relative to Revival and DPS.

## Why this assumption matters

My estimate is below the market because the observed contextual positioning looks like a tight three-way cluster, not a near-locked third-place finish for PP–DB. That conclusion only holds if the remaining time window is too short for a major asymmetric catalyst to emerge in PP–DB’s favor.

## What this assumption supports

- A sub-market probability estimate for PP–DB finishing third.
- A view that election day mechanics matter more than soft late narratives.
- A conclusion that the current market price likely overstates certainty.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The election is only days away.
- The contract resolves on seats, which concentrates importance on the election-day result rather than narrative momentum alone.
- The available contextual polling snapshot puts PP–DB, DPS, and Revival very close together.
- No stronger primary source in this run established a decisive late-cycle shift uniquely favoring PP–DB.

## What would falsify it

- A credible late poll batch showing PP–DB with a clear multi-point edge over both Revival and DPS.
- A major campaign or legal shock that disproportionately damages one of the two main rivals.
- Reliable consensus reporting that PP–DB is structurally advantaged in seat conversion relative to its rivals.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple fresh polls move PP–DB clearly above the other two contenders.
- Sharp turnout narratives favor PP–DB’s urban/pro-reform base.
- Rival-coalition fragmentation, ballot/list complications, or disqualifications materially alter the field.

## What changes if this assumption fails

My estimate would move meaningfully upward, potentially toward the market, because the case would stop looking like a noisy three-way contest and start looking like a more stable PP–DB claim on third.
