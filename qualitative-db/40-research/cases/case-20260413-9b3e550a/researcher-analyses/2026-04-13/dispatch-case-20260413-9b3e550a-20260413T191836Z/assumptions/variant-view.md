# Assumption

The key assumption is that PP–DB's recent support floor is real enough to keep it in the top-four cluster, but not strong enough to justify treating third place as close to locked given the narrow recent margins versus Revival and the possibility of DPS-related ranking shifts.

## Why this assumption matters

The probability estimate depends on whether PP–DB should be modeled as a stable third-place anchor or as one member of a volatile cluster competing for second through fourth. If the latter is true, a 78% market price is likely too high.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market probability estimate for PP–DB finishing exactly third.
- The variant thesis that the market is overconfident because it is compressing a wide distribution of nearby rank outcomes into a single favored outcome.
- Greater weight on recent rank volatility and coalition fragmentation than on a simplistic "PP–DB is usually around third" story.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- June 2024: PP–DB did finish third, but only marginally ahead of Revival.
- October 2024: PP–DB moved up to second, again with only a modest gap over Revival.
- Bulgaria's repeated snap-election environment suggests unstable alignments and noisy rank ordering below GERB.
- The contract resolves on exact ranking by seats, with vote totals and then abbreviation order as tie-breakers, so small shifts can matter materially.

## What would falsify it

- High-quality late polling showing PP–DB has opened a clear and durable gap over both Revival and DPS for the number-three slot.
- Credible campaign evidence that one of the rival parties has structurally collapsed below the competitive range.
- Official or near-official interim results on election night showing PP–DB clearly separated from the nearest competitor for third.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple late independent polls converging on PP–DB well ahead of both Revival and DPS.
- Evidence that DPS fragmentation has made it noncompetitive for upper-rank finishes.
- Strong tactical-voting or coalition-consolidation dynamics favoring PP–DB in urban districts.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If PP–DB is demonstrably more insulated from rivals than this run assumes, the estimate should move upward toward the market or above it, and the variant case would weaken substantially.
