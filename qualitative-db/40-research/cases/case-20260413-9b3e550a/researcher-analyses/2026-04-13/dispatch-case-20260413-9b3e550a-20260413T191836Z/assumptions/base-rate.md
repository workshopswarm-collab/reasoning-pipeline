# Core assumption
The key modeling assumption is that the recent late-campaign polling ordering is directionally informative: Progressive Bulgaria and GERB occupy the top two spots, leaving PP–DB and DPS as the main contenders for third, with Revival now a secondary spoiler rather than the central rival.

# Why this assumption is reasonable
- Multiple March-April 2026 polls from different firms cluster around PP–DB slightly above DPS.
- PP–DB also starts from a stronger 2024 baseline than DPS or Revival in recalculated vote share and current-seat standing.
- The market asks for third in seats, and parties near 10-13% usually convert into a meaningful seat bloc if they clear the threshold.

# What would break the assumption
- If late undecided voters or turnout composition strongly favor DPS.
- If Progressive Bulgaria pulls disproportionally from PP–DB rather than from BSP/other opposition pools.
- If Revival's national vote is understated and it re-enters the third-place race.
- If regional seat conversion materially disadvantages PP–DB versus DPS despite a small national edge.

# Net effect on estimate
This assumption supports making PP–DB the favorite, but only moderately. It does not justify treating 0.78 market odds as obviously too high or too low; it supports a probability somewhat below that level because the PP–DB-over-DPS edge is modest rather than dominant.

# Monitoring implication
Any late evidence showing DPS overtaking PP–DB in several independent polls, or showing strong seat-conversion advantages for DPS in key districts, would be enough to cut the estimate substantially.
