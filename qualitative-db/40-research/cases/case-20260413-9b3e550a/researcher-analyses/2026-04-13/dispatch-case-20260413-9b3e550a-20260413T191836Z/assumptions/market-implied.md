# Assumption

The market's high price on PP–DB finishing third assumes the public three-way cluster around PP–DB, Revival, and DPS is more likely to resolve with PP–DB landing **below one of those rivals but above the rest**, rather than with PP–DB reclaiming second.

## Why this assumption matters

The difference between a fair price near 0.55-0.65 and a stretched price near 0.78 is mostly about whether PP–DB is being treated as an almost locked third-place finisher instead of one member of a noisy second-through-fourth cluster.

## What this assumption supports

- A market-respecting but still skeptical view that PP–DB is a leading candidate for third.
- A probability estimate below the current market price rather than a contrarian bearish collapse view.
- The interpretation that the market may be using tacit structure or recent local information not fully visible in the accessible sources.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Poll aggregation visible through Politico places PP–DB close to Revival and DPS, not far behind both.
- PP–DB held more seats than Revival and DPS in the outgoing assembly, showing the coalition has a real national base and could still convert into second if campaign conditions break well.
- Therefore, a very high third-place probability likely embeds a stronger assumption about ranking stability than the public polling snapshot alone can prove.

## What would falsify it

- Fresh high-quality Bulgarian polls showing PP–DB consistently and materially behind both main rivals for second.
- Reporting that PP–DB coalition structure has weakened enough to make second place implausible.
- Credible seat models or district-level analyses showing PP–DB's vote efficiency is systematically worse than Revival or DPS.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple late polls clustering PP–DB clearly in third rather than tied for second/third.
- Elite or media consensus shifting to treat PP–DB as capped electorally.
- Evidence that PP–DB's urban support is underperforming or inefficiently distributed.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If PP–DB is no longer meaningfully live for second place, the market's 0.78 price becomes easier to justify and my estimate should move upward toward the market.
