# Summary

This note captures the baseline electoral context visible from the 2024 and 2026 Bulgarian parliamentary election pages and the PP–DB coalition page. It is not a primary official result source, but it is useful for establishing the recent ranking pattern and why the market may be overconfident about PP–DB holding third place.

## Key facts extracted

- The 2026 Bulgarian parliamentary election is scheduled for **19 April 2026**.
- The Polymarket contract's election date is therefore directionally aligned with the commonly reported election date.
- In the **October 2024** parliamentary election, **PP–DB finished second with 13.75% and 36 seats**, while **Revival finished third with 12.92% and 33 seats**. The margin between them was small.
- In the **June 2024** parliamentary election, **PP–DB finished third with 13.92% and 39 seats**, while **DPS finished second with 16.56% and 47 seats**, and **Revival was very close behind PP–DB at 13.38% and 38 seats**.
- The 2026 election page reports a fragmented party system, continued snap-election dynamics, and a field where GERB, PP–DB, Revival, and DPS all remain material actors.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Election dates: June 2024 on 9 June 2024, October 2024 on 27 October 2024, next election on 19 April 2026.
- October 2024 ranking by vote share/seats: GERB first, PP–DB second, Revival third, DPS–NN fourth.
- June 2024 ranking by vote share/seats: GERB first, DPS second, PP–DB third, Revival fourth by a narrow margin.
- The 2026 page states the market-relevant ranking rule remains seat-based under Bulgaria's parliamentary system and that the election follows another government collapse.

## What is uncertain

- Wikipedia is not the governing source of truth for settlement.
- The 2026 page includes dynamic/forward-looking material and may lag or inherit errors from news coverage.
- It does not provide a validated late-campaign polling synthesis strong enough on its own to price the contract.

## Why this source may matter

The note matters because it shows the key neglected mechanism for this market: PP–DB is not competing in a stable two-party ranking but in a **very crowded and volatile field where third place has recently been narrow and contestable**. That makes a 78% implied probability look aggressive unless stronger polling or official trend data clearly support it.

## Possible impact on the question

This source pushes toward a more skeptical view of PP–DB as an overwhelming favorite for third. It supports a variant thesis that PP–DB is plausible for second or third, but that the difference between second/third/fourth is thin enough that the market may be overpricing certainty.

## Reliability notes

Useful contextual source, but not authoritative for settlement. It should be paired with the contract language and the Bulgarian Central Election Commission (CIK) as the governing source of truth for official results if ambiguity arises.
