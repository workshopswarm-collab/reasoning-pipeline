# Summary
- Since late 2024, PP–DB has usually polled ahead of Revival and DPS, but often only narrowly.
- In the most recent March-April 2026 polling table, PP–DB is mostly around 11-13%, Revival around 6-8%, and DPS around 9.7-10.8%, while the new Progressive Bulgaria bloc frequently polls first or second.
- That makes PP–DB the modal third-place outcome, but the margin over DPS is thin enough that a nontrivial miss remains plausible.

# Key extracted facts
- October 2024 election baseline in the table: GERB 26.4, PP–DB 14.2, Revival 13.4, DPS 11.5 (recalculated percentages used for seat allocation).
- Early 2025 polls generally had PP–DB second and Revival/DPS competing for third/fourth.
- After Rumen Radev's Progressive Bulgaria entered, recent polls show a new ranking pattern: Progressive Bulgaria first/second, GERB first/second, PP–DB typically third, DPS typically fourth, Revival fifth.
- Recent samples:
  - Alpha Research 19-26 Mar 2026: PB 30.8, GERB 21.2, PP–DB 11.1, DPS 9.8, Revival 6.9.
  - Gallup 20-30 Mar 2026: PB 28.4, GERB 23.4, PP–DB 10.9, DPS 10.7, Revival 6.5.
  - Sova Harris 2-6 Apr 2026: PB 33.6, GERB 19.0, PP–DB 11.2, DPS 9.7, Revival 7.8.
  - MarketLinks 17-21 Mar 2026: PB 29.1, GERB 22.2, PP–DB 13.3, DPS 10.5, Revival 5.5.

# Why this matters
- A base-rate read should weight the repeated ordering across multiple firms more than any single narrative or one-off poll.
- Recent cross-firm evidence says the real contest for third is mostly PP–DB vs DPS, with Revival less likely unless polls are systematically understating it.
- Because PP–DB's edge is often only 0.2-2.8 points over DPS, the probability should be materially below certainty even if PP–DB is favorite.

# Caveats
- Poll table is a secondary compilation, not direct primary poll PDFs for each line item.
- Seat allocation by constituency can slightly distort national-vote ordering, though contract tie-breakers reduce some ambiguity.
- Some older polls predate Progressive Bulgaria's entry and are less relevant to the final ranking environment.
