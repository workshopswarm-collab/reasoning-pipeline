# Assumption

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not just that BTC is currently above the strike, but that the remaining catalyst path before settlement looks relatively benign from a scheduled-event perspective.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability despite short-term volatility.
- A view that time decay favors Yes because the remaining window is short and lacks an obvious routine macro shock date.
- A judgment that the market’s extreme Yes pricing is directionally justified.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Official BLS calendars place payrolls and CPI before the current date.
- Official Fed calendar places the next FOMC meeting after market resolution.
- Current Binance spot price is materially above the strike, so absent a fresh high-information shock, the remaining path to No requires a fairly large downside move.

## What would falsify it

- A major scheduled release or event inside the window that was missed in the initial pass and plausibly changes BTC by several thousand dollars.
- A crypto-specific catalyst such as exchange disruption, regulatory action, or major market-structure event that becomes clearly imminent before April 21.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the low-$70k area on Binance and failing to rebound.
- Risk assets broadly selling off on an unscheduled macro/geopolitical headline.
- Venue-specific issues on Binance that raise operational or settlement risk.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability of Yes should fall materially because the current bullish buffer partly relies on the absence of a large scheduled repricing trigger before settlement.
