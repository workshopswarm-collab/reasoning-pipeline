# Summary

The main obvious scheduled U.S. macro catalysts that often move BTC are not clustered inside the remaining April 16-April 21 window. CPI already printed on April 10, payrolls already printed on April 3, and the next FOMC meeting is April 28-29, after market resolution.

## Key facts extracted

- BLS schedule shows March 2026 CPI released on April 10, 2026 at 08:30 AM.
- BLS schedule shows March 2026 Employment Situation released on April 3, 2026 at 08:30 AM.
- Federal Reserve calendar shows the next 2026 FOMC meeting on April 28-29, after the April 21 market resolution.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Official release dates and times for CPI and Employment Situation.
- Official FOMC meeting dates for 2026.

## What is uncertain

- Unscheduled macro or geopolitical headlines can still move BTC sharply.
- Other scheduled crypto-specific catalysts may exist outside these broad macro calendars.

## Why this source may matter

For a short-dated BTC threshold market, the catalyst question is whether a major information release is still ahead before settlement. These calendars indicate that the largest routine U.S. macro catalysts are mostly behind this market or after it.

## Possible impact on the question

This reduces the odds that a known, high-information scheduled macro event will force BTC down through $68k before noon ET on April 21. It does not eliminate risk, but it weakens the case for a near-term scheduled catalyst-driven repricing lower.
