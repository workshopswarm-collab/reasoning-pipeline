# Assumption

BTC will remain within its recent short-horizon trading regime through the April 16 noon ET resolution minute, rather than experiencing a sharp downside break of more than roughly 3% before that time.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case for Yes depends less on a fresh bullish catalyst and more on the tendency for a benchmark asset already trading materially above a threshold to remain above it over a one-day horizon.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability Yes estimate.
- A view that the market's 90.5% pricing is broadly reasonable.
- Reliance on outside-view persistence rather than narrative-driven upside arguments.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance BTC/USDT is in the mid-74k range, above the 72k threshold by about 2.7%.
- Recent Binance daily-close analogs show high next-day persistence once BTC is already above 72k.
- There is no identified contract-specific exclusion or unusual source ambiguity that would make an ordinary price-persistence read unreliable.

## What would falsify it

- A sudden macro or crypto-specific selloff that pushes BTC below 72k before or at the target minute.
- Exchange-specific dislocation on Binance BTC/USDT even if broader BTC markets stay firmer elsewhere.
- Evidence that recent realized volatility is much larger than this persistence framing assumes.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid overnight loss of the 74k area.
- Binance BTC/USDT trading near or below the 24h low around the next session.
- A widening exchange-specific basis or operational issue affecting Binance price reliability.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability estimate should move down sharply, because the thesis is not that BTC is guaranteed to rise, only that staying above 72k from the current level is usually the default path over the next day.
