# Assumption

BTC/USDT can avoid a roughly 3.6% downside move into the specific Binance 12:00 ET one-minute close on April 16.

## Why this assumption matters

The current price is comfortably but not overwhelmingly above the threshold, so the thesis is carrying meaningful path dependence rather than resting on a huge cushion.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-Yes probability estimate rather than a near-certain one.
- The conclusion that the market is directionally right but slightly overconfident.
- The view that timing and single-minute-close mechanics are the main underpriced risks.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance data currently places BTC/USDT around 74.7k.
- Recent daily closes show BTC has mostly been above 72k in the last several sessions.
- Even the latest 24h low on Binance was about 73.8k, still above the threshold.

## What would falsify it

- A renewed risk-off move or crypto-specific shock that drives Binance BTC/USDT below 72,000 before or at the noon ET settlement candle.
- A sharp intraday wick/reversal that leaves the exact 12:00 ET one-minute close at or below 72,000.
- Exchange-specific operational disturbance or data-display issue affecting the governing candle.

## Early warning signs

- BTC trading back toward 73k or below during the overnight / early morning ET session.
- A broad crypto selloff with hourly momentum accelerating downward.
- Binance-specific irregularities or outages near the resolution window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The correct stance shifts quickly from high-probability Yes to a much tighter or No-leaning view, because this contract is entirely determined by one narrow timestamp rather than by average daily trading level.
