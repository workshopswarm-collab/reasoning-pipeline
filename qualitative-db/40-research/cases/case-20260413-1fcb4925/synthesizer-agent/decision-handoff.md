# Decision summary

Progressive Bulgaria (PB) is plausibly the favorite to win the most seats in the 2026 Bulgarian parliamentary election, but the synthesis-stage review does not support the market’s 95.95% near-certainty. My post-synthesis view is that PB is more likely than not to finish first in seats, yet the remaining uncertainty around late polling quality, seat conversion, and reliance on partially indirect sourcing is too large for a near-lock price.

## Why this may matter now

Market implies 95.95%; my syndicated range is 0.64 to 0.80. That still makes PB the favorite, but the edge is actionable only on the anti-overconfidence side, not as a strong anti-PB call. The likely market mispricing is treating a real frontrunner narrative around Radev/PB as if it were already terminally confirmed in seat terms.

## Shift versus swarm baseline

This range is not materially different from the swarm-implied center around 0.68; it is a modest upward tilt versus the median because the raw findings collectively support 'favorite but overpriced' more than the harsh outside-view skepticism of the base-rate lane. I did not move toward market because the synthesis-stage check did not uncover strong independent evidence for a near-lock, and the failed external search / access limits reinforced caution rather than confidence.

## Edge verification status

Independent verification quality is medium. I independently verified from the bundle that the contract is seat-based with a clear CIK fallback, that PB is a real coalition/campaign and not a mislabeled outcome, and that multiple lanes using different source mixes converged on PB being serious and likely competitive. I also verified that no lane produced strong direct final-week polling or seat-model evidence sufficient to support 95.95%. Verification remains only medium rather than high because direct CIK access was blocked in-run, raw polling materials were often not directly retrievable, and an attempted synthesis-stage web search also hit bot detection rather than adding fresh authoritative evidence.

## Compression toward market

No. The synthesis did not compress toward market because the missing verification cut against trusting the market’s extreme confidence, not in favor of it. If anything, the verification exercise mainly confirmed that the market’s large premium over the swarm should be treated skeptically. I also did not move materially below the swarm center because the upstream evidence still supports PB as a genuine favorite rather than a mere hype story.

## Timing and catalyst posture

This is highly timing-sensitive. The next catalysts are final independent polls, then 19 April exit polls and first credible seat projections, then official CIK allocation if reporting is ambiguous. Before those catalysts, the current edge is more likely to decay or violently reprice than to remain stable, because the market is already near the ceiling. Waiting for fresh polling would likely improve decision quality more than elaborating current narrative arguments.

## Key blockers

Main blockers are: lack of strong direct final-week polling/seat-model evidence; dependence on contextual or tertiary summaries for parts of the case; and real uncertainty about seat conversion in a fragmented threshold-based system. There is no major contract blocker, but there is enough evidence-quality weakness to prevent high-confidence anti-market sizing without caution.

## Best countercase

Best countercase: GERB-SDS still wins the most seats because PB’s apparent lead is narrower than advertised, late undecideds break away, or PB underconverts votes into seats in a fragmented threshold-driven allocation. This countercase was best preserved by variant-view and reinforced by base-rate and risk-manager’s discussion of the narrower Market Links poll.

## What would change the view

I would move materially upward on two or more independent final-week polls clearly showing PB first by a durable margin, a credible seat model showing clear plurality-seat leadership, or exit polls / first returns indicating PB is comfortably ahead in seats. I would move materially downward on credible polling with GERB-SDS tied or ahead, evidence of PB organizational weakness, or early seat projections showing PB underconverting votes into seats.

## Recommended next action

Collect direct final-week Bulgarian polling/seat-model provenance if possible, then reassess immediately before election day. If that is not feasible, wait for exit polls / first credible seat reporting rather than increasing confidence from current materials alone.

## Verification impact

Yes, additional synthesis-stage verification was attempted, including critical comparison of raw findings versus sidecars and a fresh web search. The sidecars were broadly faithful; none hid a major contrary finding. Cross-lane comparison materially strengthened confidence that the market is overpriced on certainty, because even the most pro-PB lanes still stopped far below market. The fresh web search failed due to bot detection, which itself left the core verification gap unresolved rather than closing it.
