# Summary

Evidence supports PB being a real contender, but not enough to justify a near-96% probability that it wins the most seats.

## Question being evaluated

Will Progressive Bulgaria (PB) win the greatest number of seats in the Bulgarian National Assembly in the 19 April 2026 parliamentary election?

## Current lean

Lean no on the market-implied near-certainty, while still assigning PB a meaningful winning chance.

## Prior / starting view

Starting view: a 95.95% market price is unusually extreme for a fragmented parliamentary contest unless supported by overwhelming late polling or quasi-official projections.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- Polymarket contract explicitly lists PB as a valid resolution entity, so this is not a naming error or nonexistent party issue. Direct, high weight.
- Wikipedia election compilation shows PB on the ballot and tracked in polling, often under the "Rumen Radev formation" framing before 2 March 2026. Indirect-to-direct race-structure evidence, medium weight.
- Bulgaria's repeated snap-election instability creates room for outsider realignment and fast swings. Indirect contextual evidence, low-to-medium weight.

## Evidence against the claim

- Contract resolves on seat plurality, which is a harder bar than merely entering parliament strongly. Direct, high weight.
- Available compiled race structure still presents GERB-SDS as the established top-seat baseline from the last parliamentary result and a central polling comparison point. Indirect but important, high weight.
- No authoritative late-stage official source was found directly showing PB as overwhelmingly ahead in seat terms; absent that, a 95.95% probability looks too compressed. Indirect absence-of-confirmation, medium weight.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- The political crisis that helps PB also increases fragmentation, which can either boost a challenger or split anti-incumbent votes inefficiently.
- Consensus-reporting resolution language can create temporary headline overconfidence before official seat allocation is clear.

## Conflict between inputs

- Main conflict is weighting-based: market price implies PB is nearly locked; available evidence supports PB as live and maybe favored, but not obviously to that extent.
- Additional independent late polls or seat models would help resolve this.

## Key assumptions

- The available polling compilation is directionally informative.
- PB has not already opened an overwhelming lead that is simply underdocumented in the sources reviewed.
- GERB-SDS remains the most important alternative winner.

## Key uncertainties

- Exact latest independent polling margins.
- Seat conversion effects under district allocation.
- Whether Radev-linked branding translates into a broad enough parliamentary plurality.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Multiple late polls putting PB clearly first by a durable margin.
- Broad media consensus that PB is now the unambiguous plurality-seat favorite.
- Early official returns showing PB leading nationally and across enough districts to secure first place in seats.

## What would increase confidence

- Direct CIK materials confirming registered list naming and eventual seat allocations.
- Two or more independent late polls from established firms.
- A reputable seat projection or aggregation showing the PB-vs-GERB gap.

## Net update logic

The key update is from "PB exists as a real contender" rather than "PB is obviously mispriced because it is fringe." But the next update is the opposite direction: nothing reviewed justifies treating PB as almost certain in a fragmented parliamentary contest decided by seats. That keeps the final estimate meaningfully below market.
