# Summary

The market case for PB is understandable but overstretched. Available evidence supports PB as a serious contender and perhaps favorite in a fragmented race, but not as a near-certain winner.

## Question being evaluated

Will Progressive Bulgaria win the most seats in the 2026 Bulgarian parliamentary election?

## Current lean

Lean yes, but at a meaningfully lower probability than the market.

## Prior / starting view

Starting from the market, the prior was that PB was likely benefiting from information aggregation around Radev's late but serious entry. The key question was whether public evidence justified confidence near 96%.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- Official campaign site shows a real national campaign, organized lists, and active April messaging.
  - Source: source note on official site.
  - Why it matters: reduces the risk that PB is a paper coalition or an unserious entrant.
  - Direct or indirect: direct on existence/organization, indirect on electability.
  - Weight: medium.

- Analyst commentary treats PB as a credible center-left vehicle around Radev with an identifiable electoral niche.
  - Source: Fakti/Hristova source note.
  - Why it matters: explains why the market may rationally take PB seriously immediately.
  - Direct or indirect: indirect.
  - Weight: medium.

- Fragmented competitive field and proportional rules mean first place may require only pluralistic dominance rather than majority support.
  - Source: election structure/context note.
  - Why it matters: structurally lowers the threshold for a first-place finish.
  - Direct or indirect: contextual.
  - Weight: medium-high.

- Politico's Bulgaria page surfaced a headline that Radev was tipped to win the general election with the new coalition.
  - Source: election structure/context note.
  - Why it matters: independent elite-media signal consistent with market optimism.
  - Direct or indirect: indirect.
  - Weight: medium.

## Evidence against the claim

- No authoritative CIK result exists yet because the election has not happened.
  - Why it matters: this is still a live, uncertain contest.
  - Direct or indirect: direct.
  - Weight: high.

- Publicly retrievable evidence in this run did not yield strong independent polling or seat-model data showing PB near a lock.
  - Why it matters: without that, a 95.95% probability looks too extreme.
  - Direct or indirect: direct observation about evidence quality.
  - Weight: high.

- GERB-SDS enters from the strongest existing parliamentary baseline among named competitors.
  - Why it matters: a new coalition still has to surpass an established leader, not just clear the threshold.
  - Direct or indirect: contextual.
  - Weight: medium-high.

- Secondary sources repeatedly note uncertainty around PB's rapidly assembled structure and candidate-list communication.
  - Why it matters: late-formation execution risk can matter in parliamentary elections.
  - Direct or indirect: indirect.
  - Weight: medium.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- Radev's personal popularity may translate strongly into party-list votes, but the degree of conversion is not well evidenced in the retrieved source set.
- PB's novelty may be a strength in an anti-establishment environment or a weakness against established machines.

## Conflict between inputs

The main conflict is not hard factual contradiction but weighting. Some contextual sources imply PB is the leading story of the race; the absence of strong public polling in this run argues against treating that implication as near-settled.

## Key assumptions

- The market is incorporating dispersed local or language-specific evidence not fully accessible in this run.
- PB's campaign organization is sufficient to convert Radev's profile into constituency-level seats.

## Key uncertainties

- Relative standing versus GERB-SDS in late public polling.
- Whether the market's edge comes from real private information or simply from narrative momentum.
- Whether Polymarket's listed abbreviation and coalition-resolution rules would create any edge-case ambiguity if coalition composition shifts.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Credible polls showing GERB-SDS ahead.
- Reporting that PB underperforms in ground organization or regional list quality.
- Any contract-resolution ambiguity around coalition dissolution or abbreviation mapping.

## What would increase confidence

- Independent polling average or seat model with PB clearly first.
- Direct CIK confirmation of final candidate registration and coalition labeling for ballot purposes.
- Additional independent reporting explaining why PB is favored over GERB-SDS with quantified evidence.

## Net update logic

The market's logic is understandable: fragmented field, strong Radev brand, visible national launch. But the move from "credible favorite" to "95.95% near-lock" is not supported by the evidence retrieved here. The evidence updates toward PB being live and strong, not toward certainty.
