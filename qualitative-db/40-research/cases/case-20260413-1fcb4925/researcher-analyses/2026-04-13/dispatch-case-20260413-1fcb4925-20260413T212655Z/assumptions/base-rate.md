# Assumption

The key assumption is that a newly formed coalition with zero incumbent seats should start from a much lower outside-view probability of winning the most seats than the market's 95.95% price unless strong, independently verified late polling clearly shows that it has already overtaken entrenched incumbents.

## Why this assumption matters

My estimate depends mainly on outside-view structure rather than a vivid case-specific narrative. If PB is in fact already polling clearly first across multiple independent sources, the base-rate objection collapses.

## What this assumption supports

- A sharply below-market estimate for PB winning the most seats.
- Skepticism toward near-certainty for a new coalition in a fragmented parliamentary system.
- Greater weight on structural friction and incumbency baselines than on headline novelty.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The accessible election overview shows PB at **0 current seats** and GERB–SDS at **66**.
- Bulgarian parliamentary politics since 2021 has been fragmented and unstable, but fragmentation usually makes exact first-place seat certainty harder, not easier.
- The market is about **winning the most seats**, a narrower and harder event than being electorally relevant or entering parliament strongly.

## What would falsify it

- Multiple independent late polls or seat models showing PB clearly first nationwide.
- Credible reporting that GERB–SDS or other main rivals have collapsed far below PB.
- Election-night results or consensus reporting placing PB clearly first on seats.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated final-week reporting describing PB as the consensus front-runner rather than a disruptor.
- Seat projections showing PB with a durable lead after constituency conversion.
- Official or semi-official reporting pointing to a first-place PB finish on election night.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move up dramatically, potentially toward or even above the market, because the base-rate skepticism is doing most of the work in the current memo.
