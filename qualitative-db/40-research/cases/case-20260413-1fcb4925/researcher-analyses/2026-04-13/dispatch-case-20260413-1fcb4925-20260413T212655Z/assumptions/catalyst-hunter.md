# Assumption

PB's late entry and Radev's personal popularity are real catalysts, but they are unlikely to fully overcome GERB–SDS's seat-conversion advantage within the remaining days before the 19 April vote.

## Why this assumption matters

The main view depends on separating a genuine repricing catalyst from an overextended terminal-probability claim. If late momentum can still translate into a first-place seat finish, the market near 96% may be justified; if not, PB remains only a live spoiler/runner-up catalyst.

## What this assumption supports

- Own probability materially below market-implied 95.95%.
- A view that the key catalyst is final polling / any credible late-survey confirmation of PB overtaking GERB–SDS.
- A view that PB is a plausible repricing story but not yet the most likely seat leader.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- PB appears to have been formally presented only on 2 March 2026, leaving a compressed window for organization and list-to-seat conversion.
- The available election landscape source still frames GERB–SDS as the established benchmark competitor.
- In seat-plurality markets, late surges can matter, but compressed campaign time and electoral mechanics often favor the entrenched machine unless multiple independent polls confirm the upset.

## What would falsify it

- Multiple late, independent polls showing PB clearly ahead of GERB–SDS rather than merely competitive.
- Credible exit polls on election day showing PB first in votes and projected seats.
- Official or consensus reporting after the vote showing PB clearly won the seat plurality.

## Early warning signs

- Polls showing PB not just crossing into contention but sustaining a lead.
- Media consensus shifting from "new entrant with momentum" to "front-runner".
- Evidence that anti-GERB or anti-establishment votes are consolidating unusually fast behind PB.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The finding should move sharply toward agreement with the market, because the contract is simple: if PB is genuinely first in seats, there is little rule ambiguity left beyond reporting confirmation.
