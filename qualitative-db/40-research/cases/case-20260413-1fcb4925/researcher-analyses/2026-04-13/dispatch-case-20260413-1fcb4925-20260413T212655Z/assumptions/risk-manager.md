# Assumption

Progressive Bulgaria’s polling lead is real enough and broad enough that it will survive normal late-campaign noise and still convert into the largest seat total on the CEC’s final allocation.

## Why this assumption matters

The bullish case for PB winning the market depends less on whether PB is competitive and more on whether its lead is robust after accounting for house effects, undecideds, threshold mechanics, and the possibility that a very new alliance underperforms its polls.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability materially above 50% that PB wins the most seats.
- A view that the market should still lean yes.
- A conclusion that the main risk is overconfidence, not that PB is secretly an underdog.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Multiple March source clusters place PB first.
- Radev’s personal popularity appears to have created an immediate re-sorting of the field.
- The opposition remains fragmented, reducing the chance that one rival cleanly consolidates the anti-PB vote before election day.

## What would falsify it

- A late high-quality poll or multiple independent polls showing GERB essentially tied with or ahead of PB.
- Evidence that PB’s support is concentrated inefficiently or collapses once undecideds break.
- Operational or legal problems that impair PB list performance or ballot presence.
- Early credible reporting on election night indicating GERB has outperformed PB in seat-rich districts.

## Early warning signs

- Continued downward trend in PB support across multiple pollsters.
- Rapid convergence of GERB and PB in final-week polling.
- Clear evidence that smaller anti-system parties are not collapsing and instead are draining PB’s protest vote.
- High variance across agencies without a stable central tendency.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case shifts from “PB likely wins but the market is too confident” to “seat outcome is close enough that extreme yes pricing is seriously wrong.” In that state, the market would deserve a much lower probability than current pricing implies.
