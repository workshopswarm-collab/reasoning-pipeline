# Assumption

Available polling and race structure imply that PB is a serious entrant but still more likely to finish below GERB-SDS in seats than above it.

## Why this assumption matters

The variant view in this case is not that PB is fake or irrelevant; it is that the market has probably overcompressed uncertainty into near-certainty. The final probability estimate depends on treating PB as live but not the modal seat leader.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate materially below the 95.95% market price.
- A "roughly disagree" stance with the market.
- A view that PB can overperform without necessarily winning most seats.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The contract resolves on seats, not vibes or raw anti-incumbent energy.
- Wikipedia's current election compilation shows PB on the ballot and in polling, but within a field where GERB-SDS remains an established top bloc and polling anchor.
- Bulgaria's fragmented snap-election environment increases volatility, but fragmentation can also make it harder for a new entrant to clearly lead all rivals.

## What would falsify it

- Multiple independent late polls showing PB clearly ahead of GERB-SDS in vote share.
- Credible seat models showing PB as the plurality-seat favorite.
- Official or near-official early returns putting PB in first place nationwide.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid convergence of opposition votes around PB.
- Evidence that Radev-backed branding is pulling support directly from several blocs at once.
- Repeated independent reporting that GERB's base is eroding sharply in the final week.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case should move much closer to the market price, potentially sharply upward, because the main current objection to PB near-certainty is that its path to first place still appears competitive rather than dominant.
