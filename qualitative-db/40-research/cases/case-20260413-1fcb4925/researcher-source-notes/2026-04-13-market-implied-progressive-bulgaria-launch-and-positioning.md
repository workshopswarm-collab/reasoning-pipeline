# Summary

This source is a secondary report on Bulgarian analysts reacting to Rumen Radev's new coalition, Progressive Bulgaria, just after its filing with the Central Election Commission.

## Key facts extracted

- Former president Rumen Radev entered the 19 April 2026 early parliamentary election with the newly formed coalition Progressive Bulgaria.
- The coalition documents were submitted to the Central Election Commission.
- Analysts described the project as clearly center-left and targeting the political niche BSP had gradually vacated.
- Analysts also stressed uncertainty around the final structure, public communication, and list formation near the registration deadline.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Progressive Bulgaria was filed with the Central Election Commission.
- The coalition is associated directly with Radev's election entry.
- Antoaneta Hristova called it a center-left project aimed at an identifiable electoral niche.
- The article explicitly notes uncertainty about who would stand next to Radev on the candidate lists and how quickly that information would emerge.

## What is uncertain

- The article does not provide polling numbers or seat projections.
- It is analyst commentary rather than a primary election authority document.
- It does not establish whether Progressive Bulgaria is actually leading the race.

## Why this source may matter

It helps explain why the market might view Progressive Bulgaria as immediately viable despite being a new coalition: it is attached to a nationally known figure and targets a recognizable ideological vacancy.

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports the idea that the market is not pricing an arbitrary long-shot rebrand; it is pricing a serious late-entry vehicle around Radev. But by itself it is insufficient to justify a ~96% probability of winning the most seats.
