# Summary

Recent accessible contextual sources consistently show Progressive Bulgaria (PB), the new alliance associated with former president Rumen Radev, leading the field but with meaningful uncertainty about margin durability, translation of vote share into seats, and reporting quality.

## Key facts extracted

- Balkan Insight (19 Mar) described PB as the frontrunner and cited a Sova Harris poll with PB on 30.9%, ahead of GERB on 19.3% and PP-DB on 12.2%.
- The Sofia Globe (18 Mar), relaying a Market Links poll, showed PB at 21.1%, GERB-UDF at 18.6%, and PP-DB at 12.0%; it also highlighted 19.1% undecided voters and the possibility of a three-party coalition being needed to govern.
- Politico Poll of Polls page for Bulgaria still displayed PP-DB around 14.5% and GERB-led forces ahead in prior European-election style data, which is not directly the same contest but is useful as a reminder that old structural party strength favored established parties rather than PB.
- Google News search results on 13 Apr surfaced multiple apparently independent reports and snippets pointing in the same general direction: Reuters (2 Mar) that Radev’s alliance was expected to lead; TVP World (31 Mar) that Bulgaria faced a fragmented parliament with the Radev alliance leading polls; Sofia Globe (29 Mar) citing Alpha Research with PB at 30.8%; Novinite (early Apr) saying Gallup had PB leading; BTA (23 Mar) on turnout intentions.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- PB was polling first in multiple March source clusters.
- The lead size varied a lot across pollsters: roughly low-20s in Market Links versus low-30s in Sova Harris / Alpha Research coverage.
- There was still substantial undecided vote and broad fragmentation.
- Several smaller parties were near or below the 4% threshold, making seat conversion more volatile than topline vote share alone.

## What is uncertain

- Exact poll quality and house effects across Bulgarian agencies.
- Whether a very new alliance with unclear organization can fully convert personal popularity into seats nationwide.
- Whether late-campaign tightening, anti-Radev consolidation, or threshold effects could compress PB’s seat lead even if it wins the vote.
- Some accessible evidence comes through media summaries/snippets rather than raw poll PDFs or official CEC data.

## Why this source may matter

The market is priced at an extreme probability, so the risk question is less “is PB favored?” and more “is the remaining uncertainty small enough to justify ~96% confidence?” These polling/context sources support PB being the favorite, but they also show enough variation, undecideds, and threshold complexity to argue against near-certainty.

## Possible impact on the question

This source bundle supports a high probability that PB wins the most seats, but not a near-locked one. It pushes toward “favorite, likely yes” while preserving meaningful downside from poll dispersion, seat-conversion mechanics, and late campaign movement.

## Reliability notes

- Independence is medium rather than high because several stories may rely on overlapping domestic pollsters and each other’s summaries.
- Recency is good.
- Best used as contextual and directional evidence, not as an authoritative settlement source.
- Strongest value is in showing both support (PB leads) and fragility (lead size dispersion, undecideds, threshold effects).
