# Assumption

The key working assumption is that no new negative macro, geopolitical, or exchange-specific shock large enough to knock Binance BTC/USDT down more than roughly 3% will hit before the April 17 12:00 ET settlement minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The current thesis is less about discovering a hidden bullish catalyst and more about whether the existing above-threshold cushion can persist through a very short window. If this assumption fails, the market can flip from likely Yes to live No quickly.

## What this assumption supports

- An above-consensus but still cautious Yes-leaning probability.
- The view that the most likely repricing path is modest drift around the current range rather than an abrupt breakdown below 72,000.
- The judgment that recent supportive catalysts already matter more than hypothetical new positives, because the line is below current spot.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot and recent 1-minute closes are already materially above the threshold.
- Recent macro and ETF-flow context has been supportive rather than hostile.
- For this contract, a stable or mildly bullish tape is enough; BTC does not need another breakout.

## What would falsify it

- A renewed macro inflation scare, hawkish-rate repricing, or severe geopolitical risk-off move that materially hits crypto risk appetite.
- Large ETF outflow reversal or visible exchange-specific disruption.
- Binance BTC/USDT losing the 72,000 area decisively before the settlement window.

## Early warning signs

- Failure to hold the mid-74k area and repeated rejection around 75k-76k.
- Rising risk-off tone across equities and crypto after new macro headlines.
- A fast drawdown back toward 73k followed by weak bounce behavior.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move materially lower because the contract is a narrow time-specific close, so a fresh downside catalyst near settlement matters more than medium-term bullish structure.
