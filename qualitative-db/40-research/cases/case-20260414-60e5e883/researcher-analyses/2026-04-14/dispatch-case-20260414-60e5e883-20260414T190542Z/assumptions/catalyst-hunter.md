# Assumption

The market will remain driven mainly by ordinary BTC spot volatility and broad risk sentiment rather than by a single scheduled catalyst large enough to push Binance BTC/USDT from ~74.3k to below 70k by noon ET on April 17.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis for a high Yes probability depends less on predicting a bullish catalyst and more on the absence of a near-term adverse catalyst strong enough to erase a roughly 6% cushion by the exact settlement minute.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below but broadly aligned with the market.
- The view that 70k is currently in-the-money by enough margin that the main remaining risk is path/timing risk.
- The conclusion that the highest-information catalyst is any sharp macro or crypto-specific risk-off shock, not a routine calendar event.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance, the governing source, currently shows BTC around 74.3k.
- Recent daily closes and lows indicate BTC has mostly traded in the low-70s to mid-70s range over the last week.
- Existing case notes and verification checks surfaced no singular scheduled event with obvious expected information value comparable to a multi-thousand-dollar move.
- Polymarket's strike ladder centers materially above 70k, implying the crowd also sees 70k as comfortably below current equilibrium.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence of a major imminent macro catalyst or crypto-specific event likely to move BTC by more than 5-6% before the settlement minute.
- A sharp downside break in Binance BTC/USDT that puts spot back near the 70k line before April 17 noon ET.
- Exchange-specific operational issues affecting Binance pricing or the visibility/finality of the relevant 1-minute candle.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 72k-73k area with momentum.
- Broad cross-asset risk-off behavior or liquidation-driven crypto weakness.
- Any Binance-specific disruption, unusual wick behavior, or confusion around the noon ET candle mapping.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes probability should be cut materially, and the market may shift from a mostly volatility-buffer question to a catalyst-dominated or operational-risk question.
