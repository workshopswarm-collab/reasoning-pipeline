# Summary

Recent reporting frames V4 as awaited and rumored, not released. The strongest contextual signal is that industry observers expected a launch and discussed chips/training, but as of April 11-12 multiple reports still described V4 as not yet publicly visible.

## Key facts extracted

- AFP/Taipei Times reported on April 12, 2026 that "for weeks" the industry had been waiting for a major AI launch from DeepSeek.
- The article explicitly said that despite reports and rumors of imminent release, DeepSeek’s next-generation V4 model was "nowhere in sight."
- The article described V4 as expected to be multimodal and referenced The Information reporting that it might run on Huawei chips.
- The article also framed hardware and engineering constraints as possible reasons for delay and noted lack of comment from DeepSeek and related firms.
- A Google results/news surface on April 13 showed top stories discussing expected late-April launch timing and the Reuters/The Information reporting about chips, reinforcing that public discourse is centered on expectation and reporting, not confirmed official release.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The AFP/Taipei Times piece directly states that V4 was still awaited and not visible as of April 12.

## What is uncertain

- Secondary reports can lag fast-moving launches.
- Some items in the broader news ecosystem appear to recycle the same underlying reporting, so independence is limited.
- Reuters itself could not be directly fetched in this run because of access blocking, though the Google news surface and AFP reporting reference the same general topic.

## Why this source may matter

This is the clearest disconfirming context against a very high Yes price: even recent media coverage treats V4 as expected/imminent rather than already publicly available.

## Possible impact on the question

This contextual evidence argues the market may be over-weighting rumor/imminence. It does not kill the Yes case because launches can happen quickly, but it weakens confidence that a qualifying public release was already effectively underway.

## Reliability notes

Moderate reliability. Good recency and useful context, but not authoritative for resolution. Evidence independence is only moderate because much of the ecosystem appears to rely on overlapping rumor/reporting chains.
