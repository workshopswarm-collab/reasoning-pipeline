# Summary

The Hugging Face model page for `deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V3-0324` independently corroborates that the publicly distributed V-series model visible on a major external platform is still a V3 variant, not V4. It describes V3-0324 as an improved successor to DeepSeek-V3 while retaining the same overall structure.

## Key facts extracted

- The public model page exists for DeepSeek-V3-0324 and describes it as an improvement over DeepSeek-V3.
- The page states the model structure is exactly the same as DeepSeek-V3.
- The page does not position this model as DeepSeek V4 or as the next major V-series flagship after V3.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- `DeepSeek-V3-0324 demonstrates notable improvements over its predecessor, DeepSeek-V3`.
- `The model structure of DeepSeek-V3-0324 is exactly the same as DeepSeek-V3`.
- The repository/model naming is explicitly V3-0324.

## What is uncertain

- Hugging Face is not the governing resolution source; DeepSeek could launch V4 elsewhere first.
- The page does not tell us whether a near-term V4 is pending.

## Why this source may matter

It is a meaningful independent confirmation that DeepSeek's public distribution surface still centers on V3 rather than V4. That matters because a qualifying market `Yes` requires a clearly positioned successor to V3 to become publicly accessible.

## Possible impact on the question

This source reinforces the view that no qualifying V4 public release is already in place and that any `Yes` outcome depends on a new, explicit, near-term public launch catalyst.

## Reliability notes

Good for independent confirmation of what is publicly distributed. Not authoritative for contract resolution on its own.
