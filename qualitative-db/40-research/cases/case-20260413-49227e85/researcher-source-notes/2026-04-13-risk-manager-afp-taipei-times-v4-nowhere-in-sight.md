# Summary

An AFP-reported article published by Taipei Times on 2026-04-12 says the industry has been waiting for DeepSeek's next-generation V4, but that despite reports and rumors of imminent release, "V4 is nowhere in sight." It also highlights operational and hardware-transition frictions that could slow launch timing.

## Key facts extracted

- The article says DeepSeek V4 had not yet appeared publicly as of 2026-04-12.
- It describes weeks of expectation around a major launch without the launch occurring.
- It highlights a potential Huawei-chip transition and associated engineering / performance tradeoff risk.
- It notes DeepSeek did not comment when contacted.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- "despite reports and rumors about its imminent release, DeepSeek’s next-generation 'V4' model is nowhere in sight"
- "that transition can slow development cycles and introduce performance trade-offs"
- "The ongoing wait for DeepSeek V4 points to friction in scaling advanced models without unrestricted access to top-tier Nvidia hardware"

## What is uncertain

- The article is still reporting context rather than a formal resolution source.
- It does not exclude a last-minute launch before the deadline.
- It relies in part on prior reporting and analyst commentary rather than first-party confirmation from DeepSeek.

## Why this source may matter

This is meaningful disconfirming evidence for a near-term YES thesis because it is recent, independent of DeepSeek, and directly says the anticipated model is not yet publicly visible while identifying concrete launch-friction mechanisms.

## Possible impact on the question
