# Summary

Current lean is No / below market because the outside view and available official-family public evidence do not show a qualifying V4/V5 flagship public release yet, while the contract is strict about naming, public accessibility, and official attribution.

## Question being evaluated

Will the next major DeepSeek V model be launched and publicly accessible to the general public by Apr 15, 2026 11:59 PM ET under the market rules?

## Current lean

Lean No.

## Prior / starting view

Starting prior was that a flagship-numbered AI model launch inside a two-day window should be materially less likely than the 75.5% market price unless there is fresh direct official evidence.

## Evidence supporting the claim

- Market price itself at 75.5% indicates broader trader expectation of imminent launch, which may embed off-platform chatter or interpretation not directly observed here. Weight: low-to-medium because provenance is opaque.
- DeepSeek has moved quickly before between V2 and V3 and has a pattern of active public model iteration. Weight: low; relevant but older cadence alone does not settle this narrow window.

## Evidence against the claim

- Official-family public surfaces checked do not show a DeepSeek-V4 or V5 flagship repo/model by Apr 13, 2026. Direct negative evidence; weight: high.
- Hugging Face official org still shows V3.2 line in late 2025 rather than a clean V4 successor, which weakens the idea that a clearly positioned successor is already out. Indirect but relevant; weight: medium.
- Contract requires public accessibility plus official announcement and excludes intermediate/derivative/preview-style labels. This raises the execution bar and reduces chances that ambiguous leaks or closed access would count. Direct rule evidence; weight: high.
- Consensus-reporting dependency means a true qualifying release would likely leave multiple visible traces before resolution. Indirect operational argument; weight: medium.

## Ambiguous or mixed evidence

- GitHub/Hugging Face silence is meaningful but not definitive; DeepSeek could launch via website/app/API only.
- Market could be correctly pricing very recent insider-adjacent expectations, but those are not auditable here.

## Conflict between inputs

The main conflict is between a high market-implied probability and the weaker direct public-evidence picture. This is mostly a weighting and timing disagreement rather than a factual contradiction.

## Key assumptions

- A qualifying release would likely produce visible official artifacts in time for consensus reporting.
- No hidden official launch channel has already opened public access without appearing in the checked surfaces.

## Key uncertainties

- Whether DeepSeek plans a last-minute official announcement within the remaining window.
- Whether official website/app/API channels not checked here already contain qualifying access.

## Disconfirming signals to watch

- Official DeepSeek announcement naming V4 or V5 as successor to V3.
- Public open beta/open waitlist/API access clearly available to general users.
- Multiple credible outlets independently confirming the official public release.

## What would increase confidence

- Direct check of official DeepSeek website/news/product docs showing no qualifying launch.
- Independent credible reporting that no public V4 release has occurred yet.

## Net update logic

The outside-view prior was already lower than market for a narrow deadline flagship release. Strict contract wording plus missing official-family public artifacts kept the estimate below market after verification. I downweighted market confidence because I could not validate the expected public launch traces.

## Suggested downstream use

Use as synthesis input emphasizing contract strictness, negative official-surface evidence, and the possibility that the market is overpricing a last-minute flagship launch narrative.
