# Decision summary

DeepSeek is still more likely than not to miss a contract-qualifying public V4/successor release by Apr 15. Post-synthesis, I land below both the market and the lone lean-Yes lane because direct first-party evidence still points to V3.2 as the public line, while the market’s large Yes premium appears to rely on launch-imminence rumor rather than verified qualifying public accessibility.

## Why this may matter now

Market implied probability is 0.755. My post-synthesis range is 0.34 to 0.50, so the range remains below market but is wider and less aggressive than the swarm median implied. The apparent edge is potentially actionable only if one trusts negative first-party evidence over rumor-implied imminence; it is still fragile because one official launch post could collapse it immediately. The likely mispricing is the market treating 'probably soon' as too close to 'contract-qualifying public release by deadline.'

## Shift versus swarm baseline

My range is modestly above the swarm-implied center around 0.38. The reason is not improved bullish evidence, but synthesis caution about over-trusting negative evidence from absence. The swarm-vs-market gap was extremely large, which raises the verification bar. Because synthesis-stage checks still did not fully cover every official DeepSeek launch surface, I compressed somewhat upward from the swarm center rather than endorsing a very large anti-market edge with high confidence.

## Edge verification status

Verification quality is medium. I independently checked current DeepSeek API docs, the V3-0324 news page, the DeepSeek GitHub org surface, and the DeepSeek Hugging Face org surface. Those checks support the key below-market point that public-facing artifacts still center on V3/V3.2 rather than a visible V4 release. What remains unverified is broader coverage of all official DeepSeek announcement and access surfaces, including any web/app/social/waitlist path not captured in the checked materials. That gap matters because the proposed edge versus market is large and highly timing-sensitive.

## Compression toward market

Yes. The swarm median around 0.38 implied a very large gap versus the 0.755 market. I treated that gap skeptically because the synthesis pass could verify only some, not all, of the relevant official surfaces. The part treated skeptically was the inference from 'no visible qualifying artifact on checked surfaces' to 'low probability overall.' Missing verification of every plausible official launch path led me to widen upward and cap conviction, ending at 0.34 to 0.50 instead of staying tightly around the swarm median.

## Timing and catalyst posture

The next meaningful catalyst is any official DeepSeek announcement or public-access artifact on Apr 14-15 ET. The edge is more likely to decay than widen if no new official evidence appears, because waiting mainly adds binary event risk rather than richer preconfirmation. But once the deadline nears without an official artifact, No should strengthen quickly. Waiting helps only if the goal is to reduce false confidence rather than maximize early edge capture.

## Key blockers

Main blockers are incomplete independent verification of all official release/access surfaces, residual ambiguity around qualifying access mechanics if DeepSeek uses a nonstandard naming or open-waitlist path, and the sheer event-driven nature of the final 48 hours. There is no blocker that invalidates taking a view; they mainly force caution and wider uncertainty.

## Best countercase

Best countercase: the market-implied persona. Its surviving case is that V4/successor launch imminence is real, the market may contain better private-channel information than public researchers can see, and the contract’s allowance for open beta or open rolling waitlist means a narrow but sufficient public-access event could occur suddenly before Apr 15.

## What would change the view

An official DeepSeek announcement naming V4 or otherwise clearly presenting the next major V successor, paired with a public access page, open beta, or open rolling waitlist before Apr 15, would move the view sharply upward. Conversely, continued official silence through late Apr 15 would push the estimate down sharply. Credible reporting of a slip beyond Apr 15 would also materially weaken Yes.

## Recommended next action

Wait for the next official checkpoint, but run one focused recheck on Apr 14-15 ET of official DeepSeek launch/access surfaces. If a qualifying artifact appears, update immediately; if not, confidence in No should rise quickly. No full lane rerun is needed unless new official evidence appears.
