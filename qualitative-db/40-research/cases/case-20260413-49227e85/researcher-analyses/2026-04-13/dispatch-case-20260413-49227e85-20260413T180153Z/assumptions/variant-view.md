# Assumption

If DeepSeek were about to make a qualifying V4 launch by April 15, some clearer official pre-release or launch trace would likely already be visible on DeepSeek-controlled public surfaces by April 13.

## Why this assumption matters

The bearish variant view depends on treating current official-surface silence as informative rather than neutral noise.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market estimate for Yes.
- A view that rumor-based "spotted" claims are being over-weighted relative to official-source requirements.
- A view that deadline risk is larger than the market price implies.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Prior major V-series releases left visible official/open traces.
- The contract requires not merely existence but clear public announcement and general-public accessibility.
- With roughly two days left, a qualifying launch should usually leave stronger official evidence than was found in this run.

## What would falsify it

- An official DeepSeek announcement before deadline clearly naming V4 or another next-major V successor and opening access to the general public.
- A new official repository/model card/product page that plainly positions the model as the successor to V3 and offers public access.

## Early warning signs

- Official site copy changing to mention V4.
- Public waitlist or open beta page appearing on an official DeepSeek surface.
- Major credible outlets independently confirming a public release from official DeepSeek materials.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The No-lean would weaken sharply, and the estimate should move materially upward, potentially above market if the official evidence is clean and contract-compliant.
