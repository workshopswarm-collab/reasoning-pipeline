# Assumption

The market’s high Yes price appears to assume that DeepSeek has a near-ready V4 launch and that public general-access availability can be switched on within the next roughly 48 hours.

## Why this assumption matters

A price around 75.5% is hard to justify unless the market believes launch readiness is already substantially complete and that the remaining uncertainty is mostly timing or announcement friction rather than core product readiness.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes probability materially above 50%.
- The interpretation that rumor volume and supply-chain chatter reflect real launch proximity.
- The view that absence of an official public surface on April 13 is temporary rather than diagnostic.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Recent reporting suggests the industry has been waiting for V4 for weeks and treats it as imminent rather than speculative in the abstract.
- Reporting about chip procurement and deployment planning implies meaningful preparation activity.
- DeepSeek has previously shipped meaningful flagship models and may be capable of a compressed announcement-to-availability window.

## What would falsify it

- April 14-15 passes without an official DeepSeek announcement of public availability.
- A credible report states launch is delayed beyond April 15 or targeted for late April.
- Official surfaces remain inaccessible or unchanged through the deadline window.

## Early warning signs

- Continued absence of any official release note or launch page.
- Contextual reporting continuing to describe V4 as still awaited.
- Evidence that access is limited to private or enterprise channels rather than the general public.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market price should fall materially because the Yes path depends heavily on imminence. If imminence breaks, the contract’s strict public-access requirement becomes much harder to satisfy in time.
