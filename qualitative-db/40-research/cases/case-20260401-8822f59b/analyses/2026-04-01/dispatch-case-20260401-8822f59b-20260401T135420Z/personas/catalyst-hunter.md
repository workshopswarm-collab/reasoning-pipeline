# Claim

MrBeast very likely **did hit 474 million subscribers by the March 31, 2026 deadline**, and the key catalyst was not a new fundamental narrative but the final late-month conversion of already-strong March traffic into the last ~0.5 million subscribers needed.

## Implication for the question

- **Market-implied probability:** 0.82 (~82%)
- **My estimate:** **95% Yes**
- **View vs market:** **Disagree modestly; market looked somewhat too conservative**

Why: by early April 1, third-party real-time counters were already showing MrBeast above 474 million subscribers (web-search snippets surfaced ~474.09M to ~474.18M), while other late-March snapshots still showed him around 473.49M and Polymarket’s own search snippet described him as reaching 473M on March 23 after gaining roughly 4M over the prior 30 days. Given that the assignment timestamp is only about 10 hours after the deadline, that combination strongly suggests the threshold was crossed in the final hours of March 31 or very shortly thereafter. The growth cadence and end-of-month momentum make a pre-midnight ET hit more likely than not, and materially more likely than the market’s 82% baseline.

## Counterpoints

- **Resolution-source risk:** the market resolves off the MrBeast YouTube channel or a consensus of credible reporting. Third-party counters can differ slightly from YouTube’s displayed public count, especially around thresholds and rounding.
- **Timestamp ambiguity:** some available metric snippets are monthly snapshots or search-result extracts, not first-party archived screenshots at 11:59 PM ET.
- **Rounding/display lag:** if YouTube’s visible count lagged a real underlying crossing, there is a small chance of technical ambiguity depending on how resolution is interpreted.

These are real caveats, but they look too small to offset the directional conclusion once post-deadline counters are already above the threshold within hours.

## Key assumptions

- Third-party counters are directionally accurate enough around March 31 to infer whether the threshold was likely crossed before or after midnight ET.
- The late-March growth burst from fresh uploads persisted into March 31 rather than abruptly stalling.
- There was no unusual YouTube display/measurement quirk large enough to keep the official visible count below 474M despite outside counters moving above it.

## Why this is decision-relevant

This market was less about long-run MrBeast growth and more about **deadline timing**. At 82%, the market already leaned Yes, but the live question by late March should have been: is there any material reason the channel misses by a few hundred thousand after sitting in the high-473M range with strong recent momentum? I do not see one. The relevant repricing catalyst was the final 24-48 hour count progression, and that appears to have broken in favor of Yes.

- Consensus credible reporting stating the threshold was only crossed on **April 1 or later**, with specific timestamps.
- Polymarket resolution notes indicating reliance on a display convention that treated the public count as sub-474M at deadline despite third-party counters.
