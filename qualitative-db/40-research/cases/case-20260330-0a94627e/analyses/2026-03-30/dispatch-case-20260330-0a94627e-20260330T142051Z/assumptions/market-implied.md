# Assumption

The reported **$54.5376M** second-weekend figure on The Numbers is close enough to final Box Office Mojo / fallback resolution values that it will not revise down below **$54.0M**.

## Why this assumption matters

This is the key hidden premise under a high YES probability. The market is not asking whether the movie had a strong hold in general; it is asking whether the finalized weekend lands above a very specific cutoff. Because the current apparent margin is only about **$537,595**, small revisions matter.

## What this assumption supports

- A YES probability materially above the current market price of **79.25%**.
- The interpretation that the main remaining risk is operational/finalization risk rather than box-office-demand risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Two The Numbers pages show the same above-threshold weekend gross.
- The film's weekday and second-weekend trajectory are internally coherent with a strong hold.
- The movie remained #1 in its second frame and expanded slightly in theater count, which is consistent with a robust second-weekend outcome rather than a fragile estimate.

## What would falsify it

- Final Box Office Mojo Domestic Daily values for Mar. 27-29 sum to **below $54.0M**.
- The Numbers materially revises the weekend figure downward below threshold.
- Resolver guidance treats the current Box Office Mojo URL anomaly as disqualifying in a way that changes the practical resolution path.

## Early warning signs

- Monday actuals revise down by several hundred thousand dollars.
- Box Office Mojo continues to lack a usable Project Hail Mary page while other industry sources diverge.
- New trade reporting indicates Sunday was softer than currently reflected.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If this assumption breaks, the current YES lean should fall sharply because the market sits very near a hard binary threshold, not a broad performance range.
