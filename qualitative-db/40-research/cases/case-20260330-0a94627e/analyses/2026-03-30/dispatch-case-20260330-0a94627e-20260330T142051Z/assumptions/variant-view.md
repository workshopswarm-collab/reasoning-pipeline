# Assumption

The current BOM estimated weekend total of roughly **$54.54m** will not be revised downward by about **$0.54m** or more when the daily figures are finalized.

## Why this assumption matters

My variant-view disagreement with the market depends on separating **direction** from **confidence**. If a ~1% downward revision is genuinely plausible, then a market price near **79%** likely overstates the safety of the over.

## What this assumption supports

- A directional lean of **Yes**.
- A lower confidence level than the market price implies.
- The claim that the market’s strongest flaw is overconfidence, not outright directional error.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Both BOM and The Numbers currently place the movie above the threshold.
- The gap above $54m is small, so only a modest revision is needed to flip the outcome.
- BOM still labels the figures estimated, which means revision risk is not fully extinguished.

## What would falsify it

- Final BOM daily values lowering the Mar. 27-29 sum to **$54,000,000 or less**.
- Evidence that a weekend estimate this close to the line is historically much more stable than I am assuming.

## Early warning signs

- Any downward revision in Friday or Saturday daily totals before the weekend is finalized.
- Trade or data-source commentary flagging estimate volatility or studio overstatement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the estimate revises under, then the correct variant conclusion would have been stronger than “market overconfident”: the market would have been directionally wrong as well.
