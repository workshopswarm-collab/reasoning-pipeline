# Summary

Trade and mainstream weekend coverage all pointed to a very strong hold for `Project Hail Mary`: Sunday reporting clustered around **$54.5M** second weekend and emphasized unusually good retention versus large-opening comps such as `Oppenheimer` and `Dune: Part Two`. Coverage also highlighted unusually strong audience reception and a favorable near-term release calendar.

## Key facts extracted

From Deadline (Mar. 30, 2026 update):
- Second weekend projected / updated at **$54.5M** on a **-32%** hold.
- Friday: **$14.6M**; Saturday: **$22.7M**.
- Deadline explicitly says the second frame is bigger than:
  - `Oppenheimer` second weekend: **$46.7M (-43%)**
  - `Dune: Part Two` second weekend: **$46.2M (-44%)**
- Deadline says Good Friday / Easter break and school-break patterns could further support strong legs after weekend two.
- Deadline notes the film kept premium-format momentum and saw overperformance in Mountain/West markets.

From AP News (Mar. 30, 2026):
- AP reports `Project Hail Mary` made **$54.5M** in its second weekend according to studio estimates.
- AP says the film dipped only **32%** and kept premium-format screens largely to itself.
- AP notes there was **no significant new competition**.
- AP reports worldwide two-week gross of **$300.8M**.

From Rotten Tomatoes Editorial (weekend box office analysis):
- Opening weekend: **$80.6M**.
- `Project Hail Mary` had **95% Tomatometer** and an **A CinemaScore**.
- RT notes earlier pre-release projections had been much lower before reviews/word-of-mouth boosted confidence.
- RT frames `The Martian` as a relevant sci-fi comp: **$54.3M** opening and over **4x** domestic multiplier.
- RT notes that among March films opening above $50M, very strong final legs are not common outside animation, suggesting the film’s current trajectory is unusually good rather than routine.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Multiple independent reports converged around the same second-weekend number: about **$54.5M**.
- Strong audience and critic scores were explicitly cited as part of the explanation for the film’s hold.
- Lack of meaningful competition and retention of premium screens were cited as structural supports.
- Comp framing versus `Oppenheimer` and `Dune: Part Two` indicates this is an unusually good hold for a large launch, not just an ordinary big-opener fade pattern.

## What is uncertain

- Deadline and AP Sunday pieces are still estimate-based reporting, even if highly likely to be close to Monday actuals.
- Trade comp framing can be selective; `Oppenheimer` and `Dune: Part Two` are informative but not perfect reference classes.
- Rotten Tomatoes editorial analysis mixes statistics with interpretation; useful context, but less authoritative than raw reporting.

## Why this source may matter

This note matters for the outside view because it explains **why** the film could beat a demanding threshold: exceptional word of mouth, limited competition, premium-format retention, and favorable calendar support. Those are the specific conditions that can justify moving far above a normal big-opener decay prior.
