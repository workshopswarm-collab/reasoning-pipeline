# Assumption

ETH can absorb ordinary overnight volatility and still remain above 2200 on Binance at the specific 12:00 ET Apr 17 settlement minute unless a meaningful new bearish catalyst hits.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends less on ETH rallying further than on ETH preserving a current ~4% cushion into a single settlement minute less than 24 hours away.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes probability above the market-implied baseline.
- The view that timing/catalyst risk is mostly about avoiding a sharp downside shock rather than requiring a positive catalyst.
- The conclusion that current price location on Binance is already the dominant fact.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance price is around 2299.7, already above the line.
- Cross-venue prices near 2300 suggest no obvious venue-specific anomaly.
- With less than a day remaining, the threshold distance is meaningful but not enormous; the market mainly faces downside gap/volatility risk.

## What would falsify it

- A sustained drop below roughly 2220-2240 on Binance before tomorrow morning, indicating the 2200 buffer is eroding.
- A new macro or crypto-specific negative catalyst that pushes ETH into a >4% selloff before settlement.
- Evidence that contract timing or candle interpretation differs from the stated noon ET close assumption.

## Early warning signs

- Fast BTC-led risk-off move across majors.
- ETH underperforming BTC materially during U.S. morning trading on Apr 17.
- Exchange-specific dislocation or Binance operational issue near settlement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case would move from a high-probability maintenance problem to a live coin-flip or worse, and the current above-threshold spot check would deserve much less weight.
