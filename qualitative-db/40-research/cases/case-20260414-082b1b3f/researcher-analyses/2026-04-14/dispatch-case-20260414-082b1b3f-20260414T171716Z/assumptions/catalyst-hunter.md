# Assumption

There is no identifiable scheduled catalyst before 2026-04-17 noon ET that is more likely than ordinary crypto volatility to push Binance SOL/USDT down more than about 6% from current levels.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is already pricing an extreme Yes probability, so the key question is not long-run Solana fundamentals but whether a near-term event can force a fast repricing before the exact settlement minute.

## What this assumption supports

- A still-bullish Yes view.
- An estimate modestly below the market because path risk remains real.
- Treating absence of a known catalyst as evidence against searching for an elaborate timing thesis.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance spot is about 85.31, giving a cushion above the 80 threshold.
- Recent trading recovered from the sub-80 prints seen earlier in April.
- No direct scheduled protocol event, exchange change, or clearly dominant Solana-specific catalyst was verified in this run that should overpower ordinary market noise before settlement.

## What would falsify it

- Discovery of a specific scheduled macro, exchange, or Solana event before settlement with credible downside transmission to SOL.
- A rapid market-wide crypto drawdown that meaningfully increases the probability of a sub-80 noon print.
- A Binance-specific operational issue affecting the relevant market or price display.

## Early warning signs

- SOL losing the 83 to 84 area well before April 17.
- Bitcoin or broader alt market breaking lower sharply into risk-off conditions.
- Exchange or network incident headlines creating venue-specific price dislocations.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case becomes less about passive cushion and more about event path dependence, which would justify a materially lower Yes probability and greater disagreement with the current market price.
