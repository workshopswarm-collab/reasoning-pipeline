# Assumption

BTC/USDT will remain in roughly the same short-horizon price regime through Apr. 17 noon ET, with no drawdown large enough to push the Binance one-minute close below $70,000.

## Why this assumption matters

The bullish base-rate case depends less on a fresh rally and more on regime persistence. If the recent trading band holds, the threshold is comfortably in the money.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability materially above 80%
- A view that the market’s 93.5%+ pricing is directionally right but somewhat aggressive
- A decision to treat recent same-venue price history as more informative than broad macro narrative noise over a 2.5-day horizon

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance daily data shows eight consecutive closes above $70,000.
- Recent closes are mostly $1k-$4k above threshold, with highs materially above that.
- Very short-dated BTC threshold markets tend to resolve with the current regime unless a discrete volatility shock intervenes.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp BTC selloff of roughly 6%+ before Apr. 17 noon ET
- Exchange-specific dislocation on Binance BTC/USDT versus broader BTC markets
- Material adverse macro or crypto-specific shock that reprices BTC lower before the settlement minute

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the low-$70k handle on Binance before Apr. 16
- Increasing intraday volatility with repeated tests of 70,000
- Major risk-off moves across macro and crypto simultaneously

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market flips from a comfortable Yes setup to a genuine coin-flip or No-lean outcome because the contract depends on one exact minute rather than average pricing.
