# Assumption

The absence of a major scheduled macro release before Apr. 20 noon ET means the main remaining path to a No outcome is an unscheduled crypto drawdown large enough to push Binance BTC/USDT below 70,000 at the exact settlement minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate relies on timing more than on long-run Bitcoin fundamentals. If a high-information scheduled catalyst were still pending, the current cushion above 70k could be less reassuring.

## What this assumption supports

- A high but not extreme Yes probability.
- Treating current spot buffer and recent range as meaningful.
- Framing the biggest risk as eventless volatility or a sudden risk-off shock rather than a known calendar binary.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Official BLS schedule shows CPI is already past and the next CPI release is after settlement.
- Binance live data shows BTC is currently several thousand dollars above the strike.
- The contract resolves on a single 12:00 ET minute close, so path dependence matters mainly through whether some catalyst can force a large drawdown before that exact minute.

## What would falsify it

- Discovery of a major scheduled macro or crypto-specific catalyst between Apr. 14 and Apr. 20 that materially raises downside event risk.
- A rapid deterioration in BTC market structure, ETF flow narrative, or macro risk sentiment that makes a sub-70k print plausible even without a scheduled release.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the low-70k area and starts closing 1-minute or hourly windows near the strike.
- A major exchange, regulatory, or geopolitical shock hits crypto risk assets.
- A newly identified high-impact event appears on the calendar before Apr. 20.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move materially lower and the market’s current ~85% Yes pricing would look less safe, because a specific repricing catalyst rather than generic volatility would then be underweighted.
