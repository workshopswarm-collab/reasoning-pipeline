# Assumption

The market's ~89% Yes pricing assumes that a spot level around 85 with only three days remaining is enough cushion that ordinary crypto volatility is unlikely to push the specific Binance noon-ET 1-minute close below 80.

## Why this assumption matters

Most of the bullish case is not about a new catalyst; it is about path stability around an already-in-the-money threshold.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability.
- A view that the market is mostly efficient rather than stale.
- A finding that current price level and recent trading range justify market confidence, though not certainty.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was about 84.96 during the run.
- Recent daily Binance closes were mostly above 80.
- The threshold is only about 5 dollars below spot, which is meaningful but not enormous in SOL terms.
- With only a short horizon remaining, the market may be pricing elapsed time plus current cushion more than any fresh directional thesis.

## What would falsify it

- A broad crypto selloff or SOL-specific shock that takes SOL below 80 into the April 19 noon ET measurement window.
- Evidence that recent realized volatility is materially higher than the market seems to be pricing.
- Operational or data issues around Binance that make the settlement minute unusually fragile.

## Early warning signs

- SOL losing the 82-83 area before the final day.
- Large intraday downside swings returning after recent stability above 80.
- Major weekend crypto risk-off move led by BTC/ETH weakness.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market would look overextended rather than efficient, and the correct probability would move materially lower because the contract is resolved on one narrow measurement minute rather than any time during the day.
