# Assumption

The market is overweighting broad Russian offensive intent and underweighting the operational difficulty of converting contested gains on the Huliaipole axis into full municipality capture by a strict map-based deadline.

## Why this assumption matters

My lower-than-market estimate depends on separating "Russia is pressing this sector" from "Russia will complete the exact municipality-level endpoint on time." In war markets, those are often not close substitutes.

## What this assumption supports

- a probability estimate materially below the 86% market-implied level
- a view that contested fronts and counterattacks can delay or break apparently linear capture paths
- heavier weighting on resolution mechanics and battlefield friction than on strategic intent alone

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ISW reporting shows Ukraine has recently counterattacked effectively in the Huliaipole and nearby directions.
- Reported Russian progress west of Huliaipole in mid-March was still slow in kilometer terms.
- The market resolves on full municipality capture on the designated map, which is stricter than merely pressing the town or claiming gains nearby.
- Attritional warfare around fortified and operationally important terrain often produces long stalls and reversals.

## What would falsify it

- direct map evidence showing most or all of Huliaipole municipality already under qualifying Russian shading
- a sudden acceleration in Russian local advances that materially outpaces the March tempo
- repeated credible reporting that Ukrainian counterattacks in this area have failed and Russia has restored maneuver freedom west/northwest of Huliaipole

## Early warning signs

- consecutive ISW / DeepState style updates showing expanding Russian control around Huliaipole itself
- reporting that Ukrainian units are withdrawing from the municipality rather than contesting it
- evidence that Russia no longer has to divert forces defensively elsewhere on the southern front

## What changes if this assumption fails

If Russia is already much closer to full municipality capture than the outside view assumes, or if the local front breaks open, then the market's 86% may be reasonable or even too low. In that case I would revise sharply upward.
