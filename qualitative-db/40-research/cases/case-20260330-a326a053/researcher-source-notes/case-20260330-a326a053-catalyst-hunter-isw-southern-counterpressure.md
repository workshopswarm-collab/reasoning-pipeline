# Summary

ISW's March 9 assessment says Ukrainian counterattacks in the Hulyaipole direction were successfully disrupting Russian plans in southern Ukraine, complicating Russia's intended push toward Orikhiv and later Zaporizhzhia City. For catalyst timing, this matters because the next map cycles can move the market sharply in either direction depending on whether Russian momentum resumes or Ukrainian disruption persists.

## Key facts extracted

- ISW said Ukrainian forces were successfully counterattacking in the Oleksandrivka and Hulyaipole directions.
- ISW said those counterattacks were generating tactical, operational, and strategic effects that may disrupt Russia's spring-summer 2026 offensive plan.
- ISW said Russia had hoped advances in the Hulyaipole direction would complement advances near Orikhiv and eventually support movement toward Zaporizhzhia City.
- ISW said Ukrainian counterattacks in late January greatly complicated Russian plans to advance rapidly from the east.
- ISW said Russia faced a more difficult battlefield situation in southern Ukraine in early March than at the start of 2026.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The Hulyaipole axis is strategically important, not incidental.
- Russian plans in this area are contested and not on autopilot.
- Ukrainian action on this axis is significant enough to alter the broader offensive timetable.

## What is uncertain

- The assessment does not itself give a parcel-by-parcel read of Huliaipole municipality.
- Disruption of Russian plans does not prove Russia cannot still complete the capture by April 30.
- ISW narrative assessments may lag the fastest local tactical shifts.

## Why this source may matter

This source identifies the most important catalyst family for this market: fresh ISW map and text updates showing whether Ukrainian counterpressure is still delaying Russia or whether Russia has reasserted local momentum.

## Possible impact on the question

Bearish relative to a near-certain Yes path, but highly useful for catalyst timing because any evidence that these Ukrainian disruptions are fading would likely force rapid upward repricing.
