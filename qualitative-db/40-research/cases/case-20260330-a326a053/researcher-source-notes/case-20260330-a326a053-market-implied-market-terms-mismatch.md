# Summary

The assignment contains a major internal inconsistency. The title says 'Will Russia capture all of Huliaipole by April 30?' The market description says the municipality must be fully shaded red on the ISW map by **February 28, 2026**. The linked market URL slug also references February 28. But the dispatch metadata shows closes_at and resolves_at of **March 30, 2026**. Regardless of deadline, the resolution condition is strict: the entirety of the municipality must be controlled, not just approached or partially entered.

## Key facts extracted

- Market title references April 30.
- Description references February 28, 2026 at 11:59 PM ET.
- Primary market URL references February 28.
- Manifest closes_at / resolves_at fields show March 30, 2026.
- Resolution requires the entire municipality to be shaded red on ISW and persist through the next full ISW daily cycle.
- Infiltration-area shading does not count.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The resolution standard is much stricter than ordinary 'Russia advances near Huliaipole' reporting.
- A tiny border mismatch is tolerated, but the whole municipality still has to be red.
- Actual control, not just an announcement, is required.

## What is uncertain

- Which date is truly binding for this assignment.
- Whether some traders are pricing the title rather than the detailed description or vice versa.

## Why this source may matter

This is the main reason to resist blindly endorsing the market at 0.86. Even if the front is hot, full-municipality control on the ISW map by a potentially much earlier date is a more demanding event than the headline market title suggests.

## Possible impact on the question

If traders are pricing a later or fuzzier condition than the actual rule, the market may be materially overconfident. If the effective deadline is genuinely later and the front is already hot, the market case strengthens.

## Reliability notes

This is first-party market metadata from the assignment itself, so it is highly reliable on wording. The problem is the wording conflicts internally.
