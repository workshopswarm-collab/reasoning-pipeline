# Summary
ISW’s March 26 assessment says Ukrainian counterattacks in southern Ukraine were making gains in the Hulyaipole direction and had forced Russia to redeploy elite units, complicating Russian spring-summer offensive plans elsewhere.

## Key facts extracted
- ISW says Ukrainian forces made significant tactical gains in southern Ukraine since late January 2026.
- ISW says Ukrainian officials reported liberating over 400 square kilometers in the Oleksandrivka and Hulyaipole directions from late January to mid-March.
- ISW says Russian command had to redeploy elite airborne and naval infantry units to southern Ukraine in early March in response to Ukrainian counterattacks.
- ISW argues Russia is unlikely to make significant progress in other major sectors while contending with Ukrainian successes in the Hulyaipole and Oleksandrivka directions.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- “Ukrainian counterattacks continue to make gains in southern Ukraine...”
- “Ukrainian officials have reported that Ukrainian forces have liberated over 400 square kilometers in the Oleksandrivka and Hulyaipole directions...”
- “The Russian military command had to redeploy elite airborne (VDV) and naval infantry units from Donetsk Oblast to southern Ukraine in early March 2026 in response to Ukrainian counterattacks...”
- ISW says it is “unlikely” Russia can make significant efforts elsewhere while contending with these Ukrainian successes.

## What is uncertain
- The exact boundary status of the Huliaipole municipality on the ISW map as of March 26.
- Whether Russia can reverse these Ukrainian gains quickly enough to satisfy the market’s deadline.
- Whether localized Russian attacks near Huliaipole translate into municipality-wide control rather than continued contested fighting.

## Why this source may matter
This is direct disconfirming evidence against a very high YES price. If Ukraine is recently regaining terrain in the Huliaipole direction and forcing Russian redeployments, then a clean capture of the entire municipality is less automatic than an 86% market price suggests.

## Possible impact on the question
This pushes probability downward by highlighting that the path to full capture is not just “Russia keeps grinding forward.” The area remains operationally contested and Ukraine has recently shown the ability to reverse Russian gains there.
