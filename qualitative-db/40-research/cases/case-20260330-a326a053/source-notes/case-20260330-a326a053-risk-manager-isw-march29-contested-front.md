# Summary
ISW’s March 28 assessment says Russian forces were still attacking around Huliaipole across multiple nearby settlements while Ukrainian forces had recently advanced in the Huliaipole direction. That combination implies an active, fluid front rather than settled Russian control of the entire municipality.

## Key facts extracted
- ISW says Ukrainian forces recently advanced in the Huliaipole direction.
- ISW says geolocated footage indicated Ukrainian advances east of Pryluky, northwest of Huliaipole.
- ISW says Russian forces were attacking near Huliaipole itself and in multiple surrounding directions at the same time.
- The battlefield description is one of ongoing contestation, not completed encirclement or municipality-wide Russian control.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- “Ukrainian forces recently advanced in the Hulyaipole direction.”
- “Geolocated footage published on March 29 indicates that Ukrainian forces recently advanced east of Pryluky (northwest of Hulyaipole).”
- “Russian forces attacked near Hulyaipole itself...” and also in several adjacent localities/directions.

## What is uncertain
- How close Russian forces currently are to controlling all territory inside the municipality boundary used by the contract.
- Whether the Ukrainian advances are tactically meaningful enough to delay or prevent capture by the deadline.
- Whether the market is keying off a map interpretation not fully reflected in text reporting.

## Why this source may matter
The market resolves on the ISW map, so recent ISW language showing both Russian attacks and Ukrainian advances is especially important. A full-municipality capture standard is stricter than “Russia is attacking around the town.”

## Possible impact on the question
This cuts against extreme confidence. The evidence supports that the Huliaipole axis is live and dangerous, but it does not support treating full capture as nearly done or mechanically inevitable.
