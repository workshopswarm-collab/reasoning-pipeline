# Summary

Late-March ISW reporting still points to a contested path rather than a clean sprint. March 26 emphasized Ukrainian gains and Russian redeployments in the Hulyaipole direction; March 29 emphasized broader Russian slowdown and battlefield friction on the spring-summer offensive. For catalyst timing, the next decisive trigger is direct map evidence that Russia is converting pressure into municipality-wide control rather than remaining stuck in attritional advances.

## Key facts extracted

- ISW on March 26 said Ukrainian forces liberated over 400 square kilometers in the Oleksandrivka and Hulyaipole directions from late January to mid-March, and that Russian command had to redeploy elite units to southern Ukraine in response.
- ISW on March 26 said it was unlikely Russia could make significant efforts to advance elsewhere while contending with Ukraine's recent successes in the Hulyaipole and Oleksandrivka directions.
- ISW on March 29 said Russian forces were struggling to advance on the northern part of the Fortress Belt and highlighted recent slowdown and weather/foliage constraints on offensive tempo.
- ISW on March 29 described Russia's spring-summer offensive as real, but not as an unstoppable rapid breakthrough.
