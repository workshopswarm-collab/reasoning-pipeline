# Assumption

The market is mainly pricing that DeepSeek has a near-ready V4 launch internally, but that launch has not yet crossed the contract threshold of a clearly official and generally accessible public release.

## Why this assumption matters

This is the bridge between heavy press expectation and the stricter contract wording. If wrong, the market may be either too low because release is already effectively public, or too high because rumored readiness will not convert into qualifying access in time.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability modestly below the 70% market price rather than a hard bearish view.
- The conclusion that the market is directionally sensible but somewhat aggressive on timing and qualification risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Official DeepSeek surfaces visible in this run still highlight V3.2 rather than V4.
- Reporting consensus indicates a much-awaited V4 and imminent or late-April launch expectations.
- Rule wording requires official announcement plus public accessibility, not just leaks or industry reporting.

## What would falsify it

- A clear official DeepSeek post, website update, app notice, or API release labeling DeepSeek V4 and opening access to the general public.
- Strong evidence that such access already exists on an open waitlist or open beta basis.

## Early warning signs

- Official hints of delay, outage, or rollout limitations.
- Continued media expectation without any matching official public-access artifact.
- Launch framed as preview, invite-only, or private customer access only.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If V4 is already publicly accessible or becomes so imminently with official confirmation, probability should move materially higher. If readiness is overstated and official rollout slips, probability should move materially lower.
