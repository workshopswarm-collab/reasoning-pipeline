# Decision summary

DeepSeek had not shown a contract-qualifying V4 public release on the strongest checked first-party surfaces by 2026-04-13, while most bullish evidence was still anticipation/reporting rather than official public availability; after a bounded synthesis-stage verification pass, the best judgment is still below market for a qualifying release by April 30, though not as low as the swarm median because a late official open-beta/waitlist style launch remains plausible.

## Why this may matter now

Market implied probability is 0.70. My syndicated range is 0.40 to 0.55. That is below market, but the edge is only moderately actionable because the range is fairly wide, crosses a lot of plausible last-minute launch scenarios, and depends heavily on timing/qualification. The likely market mispricing is over-weighting imminent-release reporting relative to the narrower requirement of an officially announced publicly accessible flagship successor.

## Shift versus swarm baseline

This is modestly above the swarm-implied center. The main reason is verification did not strongly support the most bearish lane interpretations beyond confirming absence of visible first-party V4 release evidence as of 2026-04-13. It did, however, reinforce that API docs still mapped public models to V3.2. Because the swarm-vs-market gap was very large, I compressed upward from the swarm median rather than endorsing a huge edge that could not be independently verified strongly enough.

## Edge verification status

Independent verification was medium quality. I re-checked first-party surfaces rather than relying only on the swarm: DeepSeek API docs still described deepseek-chat and deepseek-reasoner as DeepSeek-V3.2, which is meaningful negative evidence against 'V4 already publicly launched.' The main website fetch did not surface a V4 launch artifact, though that check was limited by extraction quality. This supports the below-market case, but not strongly enough to validate a massive edge, because a qualifying launch could appear abruptly on other first-party surfaces and some official surfaces may not be fully captured by lightweight fetches.

## Compression toward market

Yes. The provisional swarm center implied a very large below-market edge, but the synthesis-stage pass did not independently verify that edge strongly enough. What was verified was mostly absence-of-evidence on checked first-party surfaces, not decisive evidence of delay or impossibility. Because a light-touch official open beta/open waitlist could still satisfy the contract quickly, I compressed the final range toward market rather than keeping the swarm near the low 0.40s.

## Timing and catalyst posture

The dominant catalyst is a first-party DeepSeek announcement or public-access page for the next flagship V-series model. Before that catalyst, the edge is likely to decay or stay noisy rather than cleanly widen. Waiting may improve clarity because this is highly event-driven, but it also risks losing price if an official announcement drops suddenly.

## Key blockers

Main blockers are: unresolved operative deadline ambiguity across packet materials; lack of a directly verified official public-access artifact for V4; and the live possibility of a sudden qualifying launch pathway that would invalidate a bearish stance quickly. This is not blocked by total absence of evidence, but by evidence that is incomplete for a high-confidence call.

## Best countercase

The best surviving countercase, best represented by market-implied and partly catalyst-hunter, is that the market is aggregating real near-term launch information and that a qualifying release may not need a full repo or broad product rollout; an official public waitlist/open beta could appear suddenly and satisfy the contract with little advance first-party breadcrumbing.

## What would change the view

A first-party DeepSeek announcement explicitly naming the next flagship V-series model and opening public access, open beta, or open signup before the operative deadline would push the view sharply upward. A clarified earlier deadline with no official artifact would push the view lower. Evidence that a supposed launch is actually private, derivative, or non-flagship would also strengthen the No side.

## Recommended next action

Collect a fresh contract snapshot to resolve the operative deadline, then perform one targeted final check of official DeepSeek announcement/access surfaces near deadline. If no new first-party artifact appears, no further lane rerun is needed; if one appears, request rapid decision-maker review because the market can reprice immediately.
