# Assumption

If DeepSeek has not yet made V4 clearly public on official surfaces by 2026-04-13, the remaining roughly two-day window to the stated April 15 ET deadline is too short for a qualifying general-public flagship launch to still be more likely than not.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate depends heavily on whether late-stage rumor/imminence should outweigh the base rate and operational friction of completing a flagship-model public launch in a very short remaining window.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate materially below the 70% market price.
- Skepticism toward rumor-heavy reporting.
- Weighting contract qualification risk and public-access risk more than narrative momentum.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Major model launches often leak before they launch, but many rumored launch windows slip.
- This contract requires not just announcement but general-public accessibility and clear positioning as the next V-series flagship.
- Official/public DeepSeek surfaces checked on 2026-04-13 did not yet show a clear qualifying release.
- Secondary reports described expectation and gray testing more than completed public rollout.

## What would falsify it

- A DeepSeek official announcement before the deadline explicitly introducing DeepSeek V4 (or V5) as the next flagship successor to V3 and opening access to the general public.
- A public open beta/open waitlist with official labeling that clearly qualifies under the contract.

## Early warning signs

- Official website/app/API documentation naming V4 publicly.
- Public signup flow or rolling waitlist opened by DeepSeek.
- Multiple independent credible reports confirming public access rather than internal testing.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move sharply upward, likely above 80%, because the main current drag is timing/qualification friction rather than lack of any underlying V4 development.
