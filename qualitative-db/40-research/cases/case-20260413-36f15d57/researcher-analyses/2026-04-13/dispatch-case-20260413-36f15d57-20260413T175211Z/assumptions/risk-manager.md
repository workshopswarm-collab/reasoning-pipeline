# Assumption

If DeepSeek had launched a contract-qualifying next flagship V-series model to the general public, that launch would likely be visible on at least one official public surface checked here and/or through clear independent reporting.

## Why this assumption matters

The current bearish view relies heavily on absence of qualifying public-release evidence. If DeepSeek routinely launches major public-access products without leaving visible traces on its website, GitHub, Hugging Face, or broad reporting, then the negative inference weakens materially.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market Yes probability.
- The conclusion that current market confidence likely overstates how much real qualifying evidence exists.
- The risk-manager claim that hidden assumptions in the bullish case are underpriced.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Major flagship model launches usually leave a visible footprint on at least one official company-controlled surface.
- The contract itself requires an official public announcement and general-public accessibility, which should create observable evidence.
- A publicly accessible rollout, even via open waitlist, typically generates at least some credible independent reporting or community trace.

## What would falsify it

- Discovery of an official DeepSeek post, webpage, or product page announcing V4 or V5 as publicly accessible.
- Evidence of open public waitlist signup or open beta access on an official DeepSeek-controlled property.
- Multiple credible independent reports documenting such access with links to official DeepSeek materials.

## Early warning signs

- New official DeepSeek product pages with V4/V5 naming.
- Sudden Hugging Face or GitHub publication of a V4/V5 flagship artifact.
- Reuters/AP/major tech press reporting a general-public launch tied to an official DeepSeek announcement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The current No-lean would weaken sharply and likely flip depending on whether the discovered access meets all contract conditions.
