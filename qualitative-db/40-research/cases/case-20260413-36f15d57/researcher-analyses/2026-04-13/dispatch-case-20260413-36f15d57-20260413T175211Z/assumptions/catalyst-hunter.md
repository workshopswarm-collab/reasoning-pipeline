# Assumption

If DeepSeek intends to qualify this market, it will need to make the next flagship V-series model clearly public on its own official surfaces within days, not merely continue soft preparatory UI changes or rumor circulation.

## Why this assumption matters

The probability estimate depends heavily on timing compression. With the deadline close, the distinction between preparatory signals and an actual public flagship release matters more than the general likelihood that DeepSeek eventually ships V4.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market probability estimate.
- Emphasis on official announcement/public-access catalysts rather than rumor-based anticipation.
- The view that current evidence is still mostly pre-launch rather than launch-confirming.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market contract requires public accessibility and clear public announcement by DeepSeek.
- Public release-surface checks did not show a V4 flagship already available.
- SCMP reported interface changes and anticipation, but not a qualifying flagship release.
- Late-stage release cycles often show stronger official artifacts than what is currently visible if launch is truly imminent.

## What would falsify it

- Official DeepSeek announcement naming V4 or otherwise clearly positioning a successor to V3 as the new flagship and opening access to the general public.
- Public website/app access or open waitlist signups tied explicitly to the qualifying successor model before the deadline.

## Early warning signs

- New official DeepSeek website pages naming V4.
- Public model cards or repos on official DeepSeek channels for a flagship successor.
- Credible reporting converging on a launch date with concrete public-access details.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move materially higher, likely quickly, because a qualifying release under this contract is mostly bottlenecked by official public-access confirmation rather than technical rumor.
