# Summary

Recent reporting supports that DeepSeek V4 was widely expected soon in early April 2026, but the evidence in the checked reports is largely pre-release rumor, leaked interface imagery, and sourcing about development plans rather than confirmation of an actually public, general-access launch. This is important because the market contract is narrower than "likely soon": it requires a public announcement by DeepSeek and general-public accessibility.

## Key facts extracted

- TechNode (2026-04-08) reported DeepSeek V4 "may launch this month" and described a surfaced screenshot from a gray-scale test interface showing new modes including Fast, Expert, and Vision.
- The article says users had awaited an official release since a February test version, but it does not document a public general-access launch already occurring.
- The article explicitly couches much of the timeline as speculation and expectations based on market chatter, crashes/anomalies, and interface screenshots.
- Google News RSS results on 2026-04-13 showed multiple publications covering expected/rumored V4 timing, including Reuters on 2026-04-03 saying the model would run on Huawei chips according to The Information, plus other pieces predicting a late-April launch.
- The Reuters headline visible in the feed supports that credible media believed V4 was in development and newsworthy; however, that still falls short of settlement-grade proof of public release.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- TechNode directly states a full launch was "expected as early as this month" and describes interface screenshot evidence.
- The Google News feed directly lists Reuters/TechNode/other items about anticipated V4 launch timing.

## What is uncertain

- The screenshot could be authentic but still represent internal testing, gray rollout, or non-qualifying preview states.
- Reuters headline alone, without the full article, cannot establish the exact sourcing strength or whether public availability was discussed.
- Some secondary publications may amplify the same underlying leak/report, reducing independence.

## Why this source may matter

These sources are the strongest visible evidence for the bull case that a V4 launch is close. They justify moving above a cold outside-view prior of "major frontier model launches by month-end are rare" but do not justify treating launch as already accomplished.

## Possible impact on the question

This source increases the chance of a qualifying release before the deadline versus a pure no-signal base rate, but only modestly because it primarily supports imminence, not completion under the contract's accessibility and announcement requirements.

## Reliability notes

Medium reliability. TechNode appears recent and specific, but much of the piece is based on leaked/gray-test evidence and interpretation. The Google News feed usefully confirms multiple outlets were discussing V4, but not all reports are independent or settlement-grade.
