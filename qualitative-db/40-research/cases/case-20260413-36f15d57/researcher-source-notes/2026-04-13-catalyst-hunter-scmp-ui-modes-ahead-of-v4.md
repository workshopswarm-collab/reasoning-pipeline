# Summary

SCMP reported that DeepSeek added "instant" and "expert" modes to its website and mobile app on April 8 and framed the change as coming ahead of a much-anticipated V4 release this month.

## Key facts extracted

- DeepSeek added instant and expert modes to the website and app.
- SCMP explicitly described the change as coming ahead of the anticipated release of the next-generation flagship model V4 this month.
- The article did not report that V4 itself was released or generally accessible.
- The report is about interface/product mode changes rather than a flagship foundation-model launch announcement.

## Evidence directly stated by source

Directly stated: UI/product-mode changes occurred. SCMP also asserted temporal proximity to an expected V4 release.

## What is uncertain

- Whether the new modes are backed by a new flagship model or merely routing/front-end changes.
- Whether SCMP's expectation of a same-month release reflects independent sourcing or industry narrative.
- Whether any such release would qualify under the market's exclusion-heavy wording.

## Why this source may matter

It is a real near-term catalyst signal because it shows DeepSeek making visible product changes shortly before the deadline. But it is still only indirect evidence for a qualifying V4 release.

## Possible impact on the question

This source modestly raises the chance of a near-term launch by suggesting preparation activity, but it is not close to sufficient for a Yes view under the contract because neither official flagship labeling nor general-public availability is established.

## Reliability notes

Moderate. SCMP is a recognizable publication and the UI change is testable, but its framing about V4 timing is still secondary reporting, not official DeepSeek confirmation.
