# Summary

This source is the governing contract text for the market. It is decisive for what counts as a qualifying release and for the deadline window.

## Key facts extracted

- The fetched market page says the market resolves Yes if the next DeepSeek V model is made available to the general public by April 15, 2026, 11:59 PM ET.
- Intermediate versions such as V3.5 do not count.
- Derivative or non-flagship variants such as V4-Lite, V4-Mini, task-specialized models, R-series models, and preview/experimental releases that are not positioned as the new V flagship do not count.
- Public accessibility can include open beta or open rolling waitlist signups.
- Closed beta or any private access does not count.
- The release must be clearly defined and publicly announced by DeepSeek as accessible to the general public.
- Primary resolution source is official information from DeepSeek, with additional verification from a consensus of credible reporting.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The contract directly defines both the qualifying model standard and the public-access standard. It also explicitly establishes source-of-truth hierarchy: official DeepSeek information first, then credible-reporting consensus for verification.

## What is uncertain

- The assignment metadata mentions "April 30" while the market page fetch and full contract text point to an April 15 deadline.
- It remains somewhat interpretive what counts as being "clearly positioned as a successor to DeepSeek-V3" if DeepSeek uses adjacent branding rather than a literal V4 label.

## Why this source may matter

This is the core resolution mechanic. Any catalyst analysis is downstream of this wording. A rumored launch, preview, or UI change is not enough unless it becomes a publicly accessible flagship successor release under official DeepSeek labeling or positioning.

## Possible impact on the question

This sharply lowers the value of soft narrative catalysts and raises the importance of official release surfaces: DeepSeek website/app announcements, public-access model listings, or official repositories/accounts showing a clearly successor V-series flagship release before the deadline.

## Reliability notes

High reliability for market rules; lower reliability for the assignment header because it appears inconsistent with the actual fetched contract wording. For forecasting, the contract text itself should dominate.
