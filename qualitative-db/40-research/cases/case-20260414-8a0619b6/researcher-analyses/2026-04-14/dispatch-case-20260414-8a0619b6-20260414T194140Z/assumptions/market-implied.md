# Assumption

The market's ~90% price is mainly assuming that a four-day move from roughly 74.1k down through 70k by the exact Binance noon ET close is possible but less likely than not by a comfortable margin.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire market-implied case depends less on a bullish thesis about Bitcoin and more on the assumption that a roughly 5.5% downside move into a specific narrow time window is not the modal outcome over the next four days.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability Yes view.
- Treating the current market price as mostly efficient rather than obviously overconfident.
- A final estimate only slightly below the market instead of a strong contrarian downgrade.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance BTC/USDT spot is materially above the strike.
- The last 24-hour Binance low was still well above 70k.
- An independent CoinGecko cross-check also placed BTC around 74.2k.
- The contract only needs the final 12:00 ET one-minute close to remain above 70k, not a full-day settlement or a weekly average.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp risk-off move pushing BTC toward or below 70k before April 18.
- New evidence of unusual event risk specific to the April 18 window.
- Clear signs that Binance-specific prints are persistently weaker than broader spot references.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 72k-73k area quickly.
- Large intraday downside volatility increasing into April 18.
- Exchange-specific operational dislocations or abrupt wick risk on Binance.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If BTC trades materially closer to 70k before resolution, the apparently safe cushion disappears and the current ~90% market price would begin to look stale or overextended.
