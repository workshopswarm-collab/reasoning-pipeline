# Assumption

The market’s high Yes probability is mainly assuming that BTC will remain above 70,000 through noon ET on Apr 19 because current spot is already comfortably above that level and no near-term shock is presently evident.

## Why this assumption matters

This assumption carries most of the market-respecting case. If it fails, the current 89.5% implied probability is too high because the event is not about long-run BTC strength but about one exact minute on one exact exchange.

## What this assumption supports

- A view that the current market price is broadly reasonable but somewhat aggressive.
- A probability estimate still above 80% rather than near 50-60%.
- Agreement that the burden of proof is on a bearish case to show a plausible several-day drawdown catalyst.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was roughly 74.3k at check time, around 6% above the strike.
- CoinGecko corroborated that BTC was trading in the same general 74.3k region.
- Polymarket itself priced the 70k Apr 19 outcome around low-90s on-page, consistent with traders seeing the threshold as already in the money absent a meaningful selloff.
- Only about five days remained to resolution, limiting the window for regime change.

## What would falsify it

- BTC falls back below 70,000 on Binance or begins persistently trading near the threshold.
- A macro or crypto-specific shock creates a fast multi-thousand-dollar drawdown before Apr 19 noon ET.
- Binance-specific dislocation or exchange microstructure causes the settlement candle to print below 70,000 despite broader market resilience.

## Early warning signs

- Binance BTCUSDT losing the 72k-73k area and trending down into the low 71k / high 70k region.
- Sharp weekend volatility with elevated downside momentum into the Apr 19 morning window.
- Material divergence between Binance BTCUSDT and other BTC/USD references around settlement time.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case would move from “market probably right, maybe slightly overconfident” to “market overpricing stability.” My estimate would likely drop materially below 80% and possibly much lower if spot approached the threshold.
