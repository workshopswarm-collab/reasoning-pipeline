# Assumption

BTC will continue trading in roughly the recent regime over the next five days, without a large enough drawdown to push the Binance BTC/USDT 12:00 ET April 20 minute close below 70,000.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case for Yes is mostly a continuation argument from current spot being materially above the threshold. If regime conditions break, the outside-view support for Yes weakens quickly.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below but near the market-implied 86%
- The view that current spot cushion matters more than narrative catalysts
- A high-but-not-near-certain Yes stance

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was about 74.6k at the time checked.
- Recent daily data showed multiple closes above 70k and several days in the low/mid-70k range.
- Over a five-day window, a threshold about 6% below current spot is more likely than not to hold absent a notable adverse move.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp risk-off move or crypto-specific selloff that takes BTC back below 70k before April 20 noon ET.
- Repeated trading below 70k in the days immediately before resolution.
- Evidence that Binance-specific pricing is materially weaker than broader BTC/USD references near the fixing minute.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 72k-73k area and failing to recover.
- Elevated intraday downside volatility relative to the recent regime.
- Weekend weakness or exchange-specific dislocations heading into the fixing window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move materially downward, and the market’s current 86% Yes price would start to look too optimistic rather than roughly fair.
