# Assumption

The market price near 72.5% implicitly assumes that the combination of earnings resilience, manageable rates, and supportive risk appetite will still be strong enough to produce at least one additional push to a 6300 intramonth high by the end of March 2026.

## Why this assumption matters

If that combination weakens even modestly, the market can stay healthy but still fail to print a 6300 1-minute high before the deadline. This market does not require a sustained breakout, but it does require a fresh marginal high within a fixed window.

## What this assumption supports

- A bullish probability above 65%
- Confidence that path/timing risk is modest
- The view that a near-record market is likely to complete the final leg to 6300 before the March deadline

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market is already pricing a high probability, implying traders see 6300 as reachable rather than requiring a regime change.
- The resolution condition is a 1-minute high, which lowers the threshold versus needing a monthly close.
- Current macro framing in the vault emphasizes the importance of rates/liquidity/sentiment alignment rather than any single headline.

## What would falsify it

- A renewed hawkish repricing in rates that tightens financial conditions materially
- Clear earnings-growth disappointment from large-cap index leaders
- A growth scare or recession signal that compresses multiples and risk appetite
- A risk-off shock that drives volatility higher and prevents a new marginal index high before the deadline

## Early warning signs

- Markets remaining below prior highs despite otherwise decent news
- Persistent equity breadth deterioration under index-level stability
- Rising real yields or delayed Fed easing expectations
- Defensive leadership replacing cyclical/tech leadership

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move down meaningfully because this market is vulnerable to “almost but not quite” outcomes: the S&P 500 can remain broadly strong while still missing a specific upside threshold by the deadline.
