# Summary
Official macro context remains consistent with a market that would not view 6,300 as an extreme upside threshold by March 2026. The Fed had already held its March 17-18, 2026 meeting, still had six remaining scheduled meetings in 2026, CME FedWatch continues to frame policy expectations via fed funds futures, NY Fed r-star work still points to a structurally positive real-rate regime, and Cleveland Fed inflation nowcasting emphasizes ongoing real-time inflation monitoring rather than a resolved low-inflation end state.

## Key facts extracted
- Federal Reserve lists 2026 FOMC meetings on Jan 27-28, Mar 17-18, Apr 28-29, Jun 16-17, Jul 28-29, Sep 15-16, Oct 27-28, and Dec 8-9.
- CME FedWatch describes itself as showing rate-move probabilities implied by 30-day fed funds futures.
- New York Fed r-star page says the natural rate of interest is the real short-term rate expected when the economy is at full strength and inflation is stable, with current model estimates updated as of Mar 20, 2026.
- Cleveland Fed inflation nowcasting page emphasizes active daily nowcasts for CPI and PCE, implying inflation uncertainty is still live enough to require continuous tracking.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- Fed page directly lists the 2026 meeting calendar and confirms the March 17-18 meeting date.
- CME page directly states FedWatch tracks probabilities of Fed rate changes implied by futures prices.
- NY Fed page directly states r-star is tied to full-strength growth and stable inflation and provides current estimates as of Mar 20, 2026.
- Cleveland Fed page directly states its model provides current-period inflation nowcasts and historically compares well with common benchmarks.

## What is uncertain
- These sources do not directly say what the March 2026 policy path or earnings path for equities should be.
- They do not directly quantify fair value for SPX.

## Why this source may matter
These sources support the market-efficient interpretation that by late Q1 2026 the macro regime is being continuously priced through rates, inflation, and financial-conditions channels. A 6,300 barrier is therefore being evaluated in a fairly mature macro-information environment, not in a vacuum.

## Possible impact on the question
They modestly support trusting the market price as an information-rich prior: if macro conditions were obviously hostile to maintaining or revisiting 6,300, one would expect that to be reflected already through rates and broad risk pricing. Instead, the existence of an active but not obviously broken macro regime is more consistent with 6,300 being a reachable or already reached threshold than with it being a long-shot level.

## Reliability notes
These are authoritative institutional sources for macro process/context, but they are indirect for the exact SPX threshold question. Their role is contextual rather than dispositive.
