# Assumption

BTC will remain sufficiently above $70,000 that the specific Binance BTC/USDT 12:00 PM ET one-minute close on April 20 also prints above $70,000 unless a meaningful risk-off or crypto-specific shock occurs first.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is mostly not about discovering a fresh bullish catalyst; it is about whether the current buffer above the strike survives until one exact settlement minute. That makes path stability and catalyst risk more important than long-run Bitcoin direction.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability above the raw market baseline.
- The view that no special upside catalyst is required for Yes; ordinary price persistence is enough.
- The view that the main risk is a downside catalyst, not failure to find a positive catalyst.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTCUSDT was directly checked near $73,989 on Binance, roughly $3,989 above the threshold.
- Recent Binance daily candles show BTC trading above 70k for multiple recent sessions, with highs as high as 76,038 on April 14.
- For a close-above market, a nearly 5.7% cushion with five days remaining is meaningful, though not decisive.

## What would falsify it

- BTC breaks and sustains below 70k before April 20.
- A macro shock, exchange-specific disruption, or crypto liquidation cascade compresses price into the settlement window.
- Binance-specific price behavior diverges enough from broader spot context to threaten the noon ET close.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing 72k, then 71k, with momentum and volume.
- Sharp weekend or overnight risk-off flows.
- Exchange outage / pricing anomaly concerns around Binance near the resolution window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should fall materially and the case should become a timing-sensitive close-market coin flip rather than a mostly-buffered Yes position.
