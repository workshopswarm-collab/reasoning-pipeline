# Assumption

BSP–United Left remains an active, ballot-qualified coalition whose support does not collapse below the effective 4% parliamentary threshold before or on 19 April 2026.

## Why this assumption matters

The market only asks whether BSP–United Left wins at least one seat. For a proportional parliamentary election with a threshold, most of the probability mass sits on one binary mechanism: above threshold likely means seats; below threshold likely means none.

## What this assumption supports

- A high yes probability despite limited direct late-campaign polling in this run.
- A view that the main catalyst is threshold-risk monitoring rather than coalition formation after the vote.
- A judgment that the market's 73.5% is slightly conservative rather than too rich.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BSP–United Left is an established parliamentary coalition, not a fringe newcomer.
- Contextual evidence used in this run shows it held 19 seats and roughly 7% in the prior parliamentary baseline.
- The coalition is listed as contesting the April 2026 election.
- No contrary evidence of disqualification, withdrawal, or visible collapse was found in the scoped research pass.

## What would falsify it

- Credible late polling showing BSP–United Left consistently below 4%.
- Official evidence that the coalition or key list registration was invalidated or withdrawn.
- Major internal rupture causing ballot fragmentation or a boycott before election day.

## Early warning signs

- Reputable Bulgarian media or wire reports describing BSP–United Left as near or below threshold.
- Candidate-list controversies or registration disputes.
- Sudden defections or coalition splits in the final campaign week.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If threshold safety fails, the probability should fall sharply because the contract is seat-based, not vote-share-based. A sustained move below threshold would likely flip the market toward No.
