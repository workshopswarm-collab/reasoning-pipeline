# Assumption

BSP–United Left remains an operationally intact, ballot-present coalition whose residual support is still comfortably above the 4% parliamentary threshold.

## Why this assumption matters

The market resolves Yes on any seat at all, not on a strong finish. If BSP–United Left is still a functioning electoral vehicle with even modest retained support, the path to at least one seat is much easier than the path to total exclusion.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability materially above 50%.
- A view that the main risk is threshold collapse rather than ordinary underperformance.
- A view roughly in line with, but a bit below, a bullish market price.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BSP–OL won 7.32% and 19 seats in the October 2024 parliamentary election.
- The 2026 election context source indicates the same 4% threshold structure applies.
- BSP's official site shows active campaign communication in April 2026, reducing the chance of a hidden non-participation or organizational breakdown.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that BSP–United Left did not register valid lists or fractured in a way that prevented coherent ballot access.
- Independent polling or credible reporting showing the coalition consistently below 4% nationally.
- Credible reporting of a major late split, boycott, scandal, or legal exclusion that materially impaired vote conversion.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated independent reporting that BSP is polling near or below threshold.
- Sudden candidate-list disputes or coalition member defections.
- Evidence that current campaign activity is hollow relative to competitor mobilization.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should fall sharply, because a threshold miss is the core mechanism for a No resolution. If the coalition is truly at or below 4%, the current market price is too high.
