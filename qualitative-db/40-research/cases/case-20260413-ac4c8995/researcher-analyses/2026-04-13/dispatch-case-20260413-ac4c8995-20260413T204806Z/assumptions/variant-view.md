# Assumption
BSP–United Left will remain an electorally recognizable and ballot-present coalition/party list through election day and will not suffer a late rupture large enough to push it below the 4% threshold.

## Why this assumption matters
The bullish side of the market depends less on BSP winning a large vote share and more on avoiding a threshold miss or ballot/coalition disruption.

## What this assumption supports
- A probability estimate above the current market-implied baseline.
- The view that the main risk is threshold failure rather than complete organizational disappearance.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- BSP–United Left is a current parliamentary actor rather than a speculative entrant.
- The market only requires one seat, a lower bar than strong parliamentary performance.
- In repeated Bulgarian snap-election conditions, incumbency and party machinery still matter for basic entry.

## What would falsify it
- Credible reporting that BSP–United Left is splitting, de-registering, or failing to appear as a coherent ballot option.
- Late independent polling showing it consistently below or near 4% with no offsetting evidence.
- Official candidate-list or ballot developments indicating exclusion or fragmentation.

## Early warning signs
- Reports of leadership rupture or coalition-partner exits.
- Polling deterioration toward the threshold.
- Election-administration disputes affecting registration or ballot placement.

## What changes if this assumption fails
The market should be repriced materially downward because the path to zero seats becomes realistic quickly once a threshold miss or ballot disruption becomes salient.
