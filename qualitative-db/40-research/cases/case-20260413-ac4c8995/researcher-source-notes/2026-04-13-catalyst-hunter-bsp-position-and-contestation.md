# Summary

This note captures the most relevant contextual evidence for whether BSP–United Left is likely to clear the seat-winning threshold: it is an established coalition, represented in parliament, and listed as contesting the 2026 election.

## Key facts extracted

- BSP–United Left is described as a **centre-left electoral alliance in Bulgaria led by the Bulgarian Socialist Party**.
- The 2026 election page lists BSP–United Left among contesting coalitions.
- The same 2026 election page gives BSP–United Left a **2024 result of 7.32% and 19 seats** in the parliamentary-party table.
- In the contesting-parties table, BSP–United Left is again shown on the ballot in 2026, with coalition composition including BSP and allied smaller parties.
- Krum Zarkov is listed as leader/chair in the contextual sources used during this run.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The alliance exists as an electoral coalition and is not a hypothetical label.
- It had a recent parliamentary presence well above the 4% threshold in the prior election snapshot cited on the 2026 election page.
- It is listed as contesting the April 2026 election.

## What is uncertain

- Wikipedia may lag rapid campaign developments.
- This note does not prove current vote share on 13 April 2026.
- The coalition's exact late-campaign strength could still deteriorate materially before election day.

## Why this source may matter

For a market asking only whether the party wins at least one seat, the most important base-rate fact is that BSP–United Left is already an established parliamentary coalition with a recent vote share comfortably above threshold. That raises the burden of proof for a bearish view.

## Possible impact on the question

Absent evidence of collapse, disqualification, boycott, or a sharp late polling drop below 4%, this contextual baseline supports a high probability of winning at least one seat.
