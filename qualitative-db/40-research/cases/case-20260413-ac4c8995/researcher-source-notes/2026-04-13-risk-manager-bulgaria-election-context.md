# Summary

This source was used as a contextual compilation for election timing, electoral rules, and current contest framing. It is not the governing settlement source, but it is useful for quickly cross-checking the date, the 4% threshold, and the fact that BSP–United Left entered the cycle as an already represented parliamentary force rather than a fringe outsider.

## Key facts extracted

- The parliamentary election is scheduled for 19 April 2026.
- The National Assembly has 240 seats and the practical threshold for entry is 4% for parties and coalitions.
- BSP–OL is listed as having won 7.32% and 19 seats in the October 2024 parliamentary election.
- The article frames BSP–OL as one of the parliamentary parties contesting the 2026 election.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Election date: 19 April 2026.

## Why this source may matter

It establishes the key baseline for the risk question: a party that won 19 seats in the prior parliamentary election does not need to surge; it only needs to avoid collapsing below the 4% entry threshold.

## Possible impact on the question

This source pushes against an aggressive bearish case. The main downside path is not "can BSP win many seats" but "can BSP–United Left unexpectedly fall beneath the threshold or fail to translate votes into any seat." That is a narrower failure mode.
