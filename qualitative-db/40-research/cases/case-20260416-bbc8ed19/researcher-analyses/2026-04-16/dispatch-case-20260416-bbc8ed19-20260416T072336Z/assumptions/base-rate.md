# Assumption

The recent BTCUSDT trading regime remains broadly continuous through April 20, such that a current mid-74k spot level is more informative than any one-off narrative shock in determining whether the noon ET Binance 1-minute close stays above 72k.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case for Yes leans on short-horizon threshold retention from an already-above-threshold starting point, not on a claim that BTC must rally further.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability estimate.
- Treating historical 3-5 day downside tolerance as more decision-relevant than directional headline speculation.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTCUSDT is already about 4% above the threshold.
- Historical 3-5 day forward moves in the Binance sample frequently tolerate that size of cushion without ending below 72k.
- No direct evidence in this run suggests an imminent discrete event large enough to dominate the outside-view prior.

## What would falsify it

- A large macro or crypto-specific shock that quickly reprices BTC below 72k.
- A Binance-specific market dislocation producing an exchange-local print below 72k at the exact resolving minute.
- Evidence of an unusually unstable weekend/liquidity setup that makes the noon ET print materially noisier than the daily-base-rate framing implies.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing 72k well before April 20 and failing to reclaim it.
- Rising exchange-specific volatility or outage behavior on Binance.
- A sharp deterioration in spot momentum with repeated lower highs into the resolution window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move materially lower, because the edge from starting above the threshold would no longer be a stable anchor.
