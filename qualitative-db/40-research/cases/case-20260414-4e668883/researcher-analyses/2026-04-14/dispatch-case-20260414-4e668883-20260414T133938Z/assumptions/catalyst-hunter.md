# Assumption

The key nontrivial assumption is that over the remaining Apr 14-19 window, ETH reaching $2,400 depends more on continuation of existing momentum and ordinary crypto volatility than on any single scheduled hard catalyst.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate depends on treating this as a path/timing question, not a deep-fundamentals question. If the market needs a specific catalyst to get the last few dollars, the current extreme price may be too high; if the move can happen from ordinary flow and reflexive continuation, the high price is easier to justify.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate that remains above 50% despite limited identified catalyst calendar detail.
- A view that the most important catalyst is immediate price continuation rather than a scheduled event release.
- A modest discount to the market rather than a strong contrarian stance.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ETH is already trading within a few dollars of $2,400 on independent market-data checks.
- Binance daily highs in the pulled series already reached the mid/high $2,390s.
- For a weekly threshold-touch market, the last small move often depends more on short-horizon liquidity and sentiment than on a new fundamental information release.

## What would falsify it

- Discovery of a restrictive settlement mechanic that makes $2,400 meaningfully harder to count than the market appears to assume.
- A sharp risk-off reversal that moves ETH materially away from the threshold and breaks continuation.
- Evidence that the latest highs were exhaustion rather than setup for another probe.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated failures just below $2,400.
- ETH losing the mid-$2,300s quickly after approaching the threshold.
- Broad crypto weakness or macro risk-off headlines suppressing upside follow-through.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The fair probability should move lower and the market’s extreme confidence would look overstated, because the thesis would no longer be “small remaining move under ordinary conditions” but “needs a specific favorable trigger that may not arrive in time.”

## Notes that depend on this assumption

- Main catalyst-hunter finding for this run.
- Any later synthesis that treats ETH $2,400 as mainly a timing/path problem rather than a catalyst-calendar problem.
