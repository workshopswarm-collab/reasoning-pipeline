# Assumption

A short-dated crypto threshold market can still fail from just below a round-number resistance even when spot has already come within a few dollars of the trigger.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire variant view depends on rejecting the lazy inference that "came close once" is almost equivalent to "will touch soon." In a one-week market, repeated rejection at a psychologically important level can leave a nontrivial miss probability.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly below the market's 89% implied probability.
- The claim that the market may be overconfident rather than directionally wrong.
- The argument that the neglected mechanism is path dependence around nearby resistance, not a large bearish macro thesis.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The contextual source explicitly labeled $2,400 as resistance.
- The accessible snapshot put ETH near but not through the threshold, suggesting the threshold is reachable but not yet achieved.
- Very short windows amplify the importance of failed attempts, weekend liquidity, and timing noise.

## What would falsify it

- A clean, verified trade or high above $2,400 on the governing market source.
- A sustained breakout above nearby resistance levels, especially if accompanied by strong market-wide momentum.

## Early warning signs

- ETH reclaims and holds above the nearby $2,380 resistance.
- BTC and broader crypto beta strengthen enough to drag ETH through round-number resistance.
- Funding, flow, or catalyst news pushes intraday volatility higher in the bullish direction.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The miss probability collapses quickly. If ETH cleanly breaks the nearby resistance stack, the market's high-confidence view looks justified and my estimate should move closer to the high-90s.
