# Assumption

Because ETH is already trading just below $2,400 with several days left, the probability of at least one Binance 1-minute high tagging 2400 is materially higher than the probability of a sustained break and hold above 2400.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is pricing an extreme Yes probability. That only makes sense if traders think the contract's threshold can be cleared by normal crypto intraday volatility rather than requiring a strong multi-day bullish regime shift.

## What this assumption supports

- A high own probability estimate close to the market.
- The interpretation that the market is mostly pricing path mechanics and proximity to trigger, not a deep fundamental thesis about Ethereum.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Polymarket rule resolves on any Binance 1-minute candle high, which is a permissive trigger.
- Binance and CoinGecko spot checks both place ETH around 2388, only about 12 dollars below the trigger.
- Crypto trades continuously and often overshoots nearby round-number levels intraday.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp selloff that moves ETH materially away from 2400 and keeps it there.
- Evidence that Binance-specific highs are not behaving like broader spot prints this week.
- A volatility collapse that makes even a brief wick to 2400 unlikely.

## Early warning signs

- ETH rejects repeatedly below 2395-2399 while momentum weakens.
- Broad crypto risk sentiment turns decisively negative.
- The Polymarket Yes price falls materially despite no rule change.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market would look overextended rather than efficient, and the correct probability would likely drop meaningfully below current market pricing.
