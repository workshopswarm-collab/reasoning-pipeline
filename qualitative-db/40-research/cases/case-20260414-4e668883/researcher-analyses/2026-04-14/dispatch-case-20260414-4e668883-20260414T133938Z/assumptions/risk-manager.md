# Assumption

The market is assuming that ETH being only slightly below $2,400 means a brief touch of the threshold is very likely before April 19 and that no material downside interruption prevents that wick.

## Why this assumption matters

A near-threshold market can look almost settled even though the contract still depends on a time-bounded path event rather than current spot. If that assumption is too strong, an extreme market price can overstate real hit probability.

## What this assumption supports

- A high but sub-market probability estimate rather than full agreement with the 92.35% market price.
- The view that timing/path risk is the main underpriced failure mode.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Independent checks place ETH just under the threshold, so only a small upward move is needed.
- Crypto routinely trades through nearby round numbers intraday.
- The market is likely inferring from proximity plus recent upside momentum.

## What would falsify it

- Verified settlement-source data showing ETH already hit $2,400 during the relevant period.
- A sharp rejection from the high-$2390s followed by sustained downside that leaves little time for recovery.
- Contract rules using a source or methodology that makes a fleeting spot wick insufficient.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated failure near 2390-2399 despite multiple attempts.
- Broader crypto risk-off move or ETH-specific weakness versus BTC.
- Emerging ambiguity about which exchange/index print governs settlement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the assumption fails, the true probability is materially lower than the market price and the risk-manager stance should shift from slight caution to clear disagreement with the market.
