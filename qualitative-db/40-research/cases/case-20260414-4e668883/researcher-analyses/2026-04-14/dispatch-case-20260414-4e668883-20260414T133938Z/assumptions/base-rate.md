# Assumption

ETH will continue to realize enough intraday volatility over the remaining Apr 14-19 window that a sub-1% move from current spot to $2,400 remains materially achievable even without a new major catalyst.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate view is not that ETH is safely above $2,400 already; it is that once spot is trading within roughly half a percent of the threshold with several days left, ordinary crypto volatility alone can create a touch event.

## What this assumption supports

- An own probability estimate above a neutral coin-flip baseline.
- A view that the market’s high implied probability is directionally understandable, though somewhat aggressive.
- A conclusion that lack of immediate catalyst is not by itself enough to make No the better base-rate side.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance candles in the checked period show ETH regularly moving by more than 1% intraday.
- The threshold was only about $11-$13 above checked spot during the verification pass.
- Crypto assets, especially ETH, often touch nearby round-number thresholds within multi-day windows once they are already trading near them.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp volatility collapse with ETH pinned well below $2,400 for multiple days.
- A broad crypto drawdown that moves ETH materially away from the threshold.
- Market microstructure or exchange-specific dislocations that keep Binance ETH/USDT from printing the threshold even if other venues approach it.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated rejection below roughly $2,395-$2,399 on Binance.
- Falling realized volatility and narrowing intraday ranges.
- BTC/crypto-wide weakness dragging ETH back into the low $2,300s or below.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move materially lower, because the bullish case here is mostly a mechanical proximity-plus-volatility argument rather than a strong fresh catalyst thesis.
