# Assumption

The market's high Yes price assumes that being roughly 3 to 4 percent above 72,000 two days early is enough cushion to survive normal BTC volatility through the exact Binance noon ET one-minute close.

## Why this assumption matters

Most of the bullish case is not about needing a further breakout; it is about holding the current zone through a narrow resolution window. If that cushion is too small relative to realized short-horizon volatility, the market is overconfident.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes lean that is still below market confidence.
- The view that the main risk is path and timing, not current spot level.
- The claim that 88% may overstate true confidence even if Yes remains more likely than No.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance spot and kline data show BTC presently above the threshold.
- Independent contextual reporting suggests price is near a resistance or stall zone around 75,000 rather than in uncontested price discovery.
- BTC often moves several percent over a two-day window, so a sub-4% cushion is meaningful but not decisive.

## What would falsify it

- A strong continued breakout well above 75,000 to 76,000 plus stable holding there, making 72,000 much less reachable by noon ET on April 18.
- New evidence that the relevant Binance one-minute close is mechanically less fragile than it appears from the rule wording.

## Early warning signs

- Rejection from the 75,000 area.
- Macro or risk-off moves that hit crypto beta broadly.
- Higher realized intraday volatility on Binance BTC/USDT.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If BTC builds a larger buffer above 72,000, the fair probability should move toward the market or even above it. If the buffer erodes quickly, the market's extreme confidence would look increasingly unjustified.
