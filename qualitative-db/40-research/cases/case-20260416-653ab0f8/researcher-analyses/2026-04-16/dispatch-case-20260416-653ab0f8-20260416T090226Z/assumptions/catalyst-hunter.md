# Assumption

The key working assumption is that no adverse catalyst or Binance-specific operational disruption before Saturday 2026-04-18 12:00 ET will push the final Binance BTC/USDT 1-minute close below 72,000.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is less about long-run BTC value than about surviving a narrow two-day timing window with a roughly 3.5% price cushion. That makes near-term catalyst timing and exchange-specific execution conditions central.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability above the market-neutral baseline.
- The judgment that current distance from strike is meaningful rather than trivial.
- The conclusion that the most likely repricing path is modest drift/chop above 72k rather than a late collapse through the strike.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was around 74.67k during the check, with recent 24h high/low spanning 75.43k to 73.58k, which leaves 72k below the recent low.
- CoinGecko cross-check also placed BTC around 74.7k, suggesting the level is not a single-venue anomaly.
- Tight order book spread and active trading imply normal market functioning at check time.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp risk-off macro shock or crypto-specific negative headline that moves BTC down through 72k before the relevant minute.
- A Binance-specific outage, pricing anomaly, or candle irregularity near settlement time.
- Sustained spot deterioration on Friday that removes most of the current cushion.

## Early warning signs

- BTC falling back toward or below the recent 24h low around 73.6k.
- Rapid widening of Binance spread, visible trading disruption, or exchange incident reports.
- A clear macro risk-off impulse that reprices crypto beta sharply lower before Saturday noon ET.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the cushion is mostly erased or exchange conditions become unreliable, the market should be viewed as much closer to a coin flip than the current high-80s price suggests, because the contract is settled on a single minute close on a single venue.
