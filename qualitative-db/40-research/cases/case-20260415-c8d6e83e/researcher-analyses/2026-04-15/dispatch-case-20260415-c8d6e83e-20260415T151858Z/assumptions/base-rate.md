# Assumption

BTC will not experience a roughly 8% or larger drawdown from current spot into the exact Binance noon ET settlement minute on 2026-04-20.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate yes view depends more on the current cushion above 68,000 than on a claim that BTC will keep rallying. If that cushion disappears before the settlement minute, the high-probability yes thesis breaks.

## What this assumption supports

- A yes probability materially above 80%.
- A view that the market is directionally right but somewhat too confident.
- A base-rate argument based on recent trading range and short-horizon volatility rather than a bullish narrative.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was about 74,044 at the time checked, leaving more than 6,000 of cushion above the threshold.
- The last 30 daily closes were above 68,000 on 22 days, and above 68,000 on 11 of the last 14 days.
- A five-day move from current spot to below 68,000 is a meaningful decline rather than a routine small fluctuation.

## What would falsify it

- BTC trades persistently below roughly 70k before April 20, shrinking the cushion materially.
- A macro or crypto-specific shock causes a fast drawdown into the settlement window.
- Binance-specific pricing deviates materially from broader BTC spot during the relevant minute.

## Early warning signs

- Sharp increase in realized intraday volatility.
- BTC losing the 72k and then 70k area with momentum.
- Exchange-specific operational issues or sudden market dislocation near settlement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move down fast, and the market may no longer deserve a high-confidence yes stance. Near-threshold trading into the final day would make exact minute-resolution risk much more important than broad base rates.
