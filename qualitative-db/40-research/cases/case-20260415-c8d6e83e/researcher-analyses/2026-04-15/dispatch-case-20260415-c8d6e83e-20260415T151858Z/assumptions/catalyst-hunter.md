# Assumption

The main catalyst path is that no major downside shock arrives before April 20 noon ET, so BTC retains enough of its current ~6k cushion above 68,000 to keep the final Binance one-minute close above the threshold.

## Why this assumption matters

The finding depends much more on the absence of a sharp negative catalyst than on the presence of a positive scheduled catalyst. If that assumption fails, the market's current near-certainty can unwind quickly.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability Yes view
- A probability estimate below, but still near, the market
- The claim that the most important catalyst is any event that compresses the current buffer toward 68,000 in the final 2-3 days

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was around 74.1k when checked.
- The threshold is about 8.2% below spot with about five days to go.
- Recent Binance one-minute candles were not trading near 68k.
- No specific scheduled catalyst identified in this run appears large enough on its own to force an 8% downside move.

## What would falsify it

- BTC drops toward or through 70k before April 20.
- A macro or crypto-specific shock produces a fast liquidation-led selloff.
- Binance-specific operational issues create unusual print risk near settlement.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated intraday failures to hold the low-72k to 73k area
- Rising realized volatility in the final 48 hours
- Sudden market-wide crypto de-risking or large risk-off macro headlines
- Noticeable exchange-specific pricing dislocations on Binance

## What changes if this assumption fails

The contract should be viewed as much more path-sensitive, and the probability of Yes should fall materially because the final outcome depends on one narrow settlement minute rather than a broader daily or weekly average.
