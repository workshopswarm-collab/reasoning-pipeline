# Summary

Netflix's official Q4 2025 shareholder letter is the governing primary source for how the company framed the upcoming Q1 2026 quarter before the April 2026 report. It explicitly includes a Q1'26 forecast table showing diluted EPS of $0.76, which exactly matches the market strike described by Polymarket.

## Key facts extracted

- The shareholder letter was furnished via 8-K on 2026-01-20 under Item 2.02 / 9.01.
- Netflix included a Q1'26 forecast table with:
  - revenue: $12.157B
  - operating income: $3.906B
  - operating margin: 32.1%
  - net income: $3.264B
  - diluted EPS: $0.76
- The letter states guidance provided is management's actual internal forecast at the time it reports and that Netflix strives for accuracy.
- The company also forecast full-year 2026 revenue growth and operating margin expansion, consistent with a strong operating backdrop.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Q1'26 Forecast diluted EPS is listed as $0.76.
- Management says Q4 revenue exceeded guidance due to stronger-than-forecast membership growth and ad sales.
- Management presents a constructive 2026 operating outlook with continued growth in membership, pricing, and advertising.

## What is uncertain

- This source does not prove a beat; it only anchors what management itself expected as of January 20, 2026.
- It does not show the current April 2026 sell-side consensus directly.
- It does not confirm the exact Q1 2026 release date beyond showing the prior quarterly cadence.

## Why this source may matter

The contract resolves off GAAP EPS in Netflix's official earnings documents. This source also shows that the strike level is not arbitrary: it matches Netflix's own earlier Q1'26 diluted EPS forecast. That materially supports a high beat probability only if investors believe Netflix had room to outperform or conservatively guided.

## Possible impact on the question

This source supports the market's high confidence insofar as Netflix entered the quarter already forecasting the exact strike, after having just outperformed its own Q4 expectations and described favorable business momentum. But it also creates an important disconfirmer: if Netflix merely lands on its own prior guide of $0.76 after rounding, the contract resolves No because the threshold is strictly greater than $0.76.
