# Assumption

Netflix will report on its normal mid-April earnings cadence, and the official Q1 2026 earnings release will be the single dominant repricing catalyst before market close.

## Why this assumption matters

The catalyst-hunter thesis depends on timing, not just terminal fundamentals. If the report arrives on expected cadence, the market gets a definitive official source before resolution. If timing slips or the release is operationally unusual, both repricing path and resolution mechanics change.

## What this assumption supports

- A view that the most important watch item is the official earnings document rather than softer pre-earnings narrative flow.
- A probability estimate below the market's 94.5% because the decisive catalyst is narrow and still pending.
- The claim that there is limited remaining time for non-authoritative information to change the picture materially.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- SEC EDGAR shows a stable historical cadence of Netflix earnings-related 8-K filings in mid-April, mid-July, mid-October, and mid-January.
- Existing case source notes indicate expected announcement timing around 2026-04-16 from contextual earnings-calendar sources.
- The contract itself is anchored to the next quarterly earnings release and official earnings documents, making the release the natural dominant catalyst.

## What would falsify it

- A company announcement pushing earnings materially outside the expected window.
- Evidence that the market could close before the official earnings document publishes.
- A release format that omits usable GAAP EPS and forces fallback source logic.

## Early warning signs

- No investor-relations date confirmation as the expected date approaches.
- Conflicting aggregator dates across reputable earnings-calendar sources.
- Indications of unusual filing timing or operational disruption.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the timing assumption fails, the catalyst map becomes less about a straightforward earnings print and more about contract mechanics, source-of-truth fallback, and release-window risk. That would push the fair probability lower and make operational-risk more central than earnings-beat logic.
