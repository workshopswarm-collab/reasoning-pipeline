# Assumption

The market's 94.5% Yes price implicitly assumes Netflix is more likely than not to print at least $0.77 diluted GAAP EPS after rounding rather than merely matching its prior $0.76 guide.

## Why this assumption matters

Because the contract threshold is strict (> $0.76), the difference between 'meets guidance' and 'beats by at least one cent after rounding' is the whole market. An extreme Yes price is only rational if traders believe Netflix's prior guide was conservative or already stale versus current expectations.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-probability Yes view.
- A conclusion that the market may be efficiently incorporating stronger-than-guide public information.
- Confidence that management momentum and analyst posture should outweigh narrow threshold risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Netflix's January 2026 shareholder letter showed strong recent execution, including Q4 revenue and ad sales beating guidance.
- The same letter forecast Q1'26 diluted EPS exactly at $0.76, so any Yes thesis requires some belief in upside versus management's own prior line.
- Positive analyst sentiment into April 2026 suggests investors broadly remained constructive on Netflix entering earnings.

## What would falsify it

- A reported diluted GAAP EPS of exactly $0.76 after rounding.
- Evidence that sell-side consensus remained clustered at or below $0.76 rather than above it.
- A late-quarter adverse development that reduced operating leverage enough to erase any upside over the January guide.

## Early warning signs

- Consensus commentary emphasizing 'in-line' rather than upside.
- Any indication that margin, tax, or financing items could compress GAAP EPS versus operating strength.
- Market confidence driven more by generic NFLX bullishness than by contract-specific EPS mechanics.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes case weakens sharply because the contract is binary at a narrow threshold. If Netflix is merely likely to hit guide, a 94.5% price is too aggressive.
