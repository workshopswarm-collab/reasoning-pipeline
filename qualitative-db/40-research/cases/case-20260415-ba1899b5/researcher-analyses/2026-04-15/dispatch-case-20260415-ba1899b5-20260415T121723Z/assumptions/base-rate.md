# Assumption

Netflix's next quarterly diluted GAAP EPS will remain in roughly the recent realized band unless there is quarter-specific evidence of a material profitability step-up.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is implying a near-certain beat of a $0.76 strike. A lower outside-view estimate depends on assuming that recent earnings levels are informative and that a beat above the strike is not automatic absent stronger direct evidence.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate materially below the market-implied 94.5%.
- Greater weight on historical realized EPS and contract mechanics than on generic optimism about Netflix.
- A view that the market may be over-extrapolating favorable narrative momentum.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

Recent historical diluted EPS values available from Macrotrends show Netflix below $0.76 in each listed 2024 and 2025 quarter, with the highest shown value at $0.72. That makes the strike look above recent realized run-rate rather than comfortably inside it. The contract also resolves on reported diluted GAAP EPS, not on narrative business quality.

## What would falsify it

- An official Netflix earnings release showing diluted GAAP EPS above $0.76.
- Credible current-quarter analyst consensus or company guidance clearly above $0.76.
- Evidence that the historical series used for calibration is not comparable to the contract's targeted quarter.

## Early warning signs

- Independent earnings calendars or analyst pages converging on materially higher current-quarter EPS than the strike.
- Strong company guidance or pre-release reporting indicating margin expansion large enough to move diluted GAAP EPS above recent highs.
- Discovery that the strike is stale relative to sharply revised current consensus.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move sharply toward Yes and closer to the market, because the current bearish gap versus market pricing is mainly driven by base-rate resistance to assuming a step-up without direct evidence.
