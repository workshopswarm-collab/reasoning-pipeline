# Assumption

The market is over-extrapolating Netflix's general operating strength into an excessively high probability that diluted GAAP EPS will clear the exact $0.76 rounded strike in the next release.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire variant case depends on distinguishing "good company / likely solid quarter" from "near-certain beat of this exact contract threshold on the next official print." If that distinction is weak, the market's 94.5% price is reasonable.

## What this assumption supports

- A materially lower-than-market beat probability.
- A view that the contract is closer to a modest-favorite yes than a near-lock yes.
- Greater weight on timing, rounding, and exact reported diluted GAAP EPS mechanics.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The contract settles on the company's official earnings documents and an exact threshold, not on broad narrative strength.
- Recent diluted EPS history available through Macrotrends was mixed: 0.66, 0.72, 0.59, 0.56 across 2025 quarters.
- AlphaQuery's contextual consensus estimate is exactly $0.76, meaning the strike appears set around consensus rather than comfortably below it.
- When the strike is aligned with consensus, a 94.5% beat probability can be too aggressive unless there is unusually strong incremental evidence that current consensus is stale and too low.

## What would falsify it

- A direct Netflix pre-release signal or highly credible analyst revision wave showing consensus has moved meaningfully above $0.76 before the print.
- Clear company commentary indicating margins, FX, or revenue are running well ahead of prior expectations in a way likely to lift diluted GAAP EPS above the threshold.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple independent earnings aggregators converging on a materially higher estimate than $0.76.
- Confirmed official release timing with a fresh source and no ambiguity.
- Strong pre-earnings news indicating significant upside in ad-tier, pricing, or margin flow-through.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the market is not over-extrapolating and instead consensus truly moved above the strike with solid evidence, the variant thesis weakens sharply and the correct probability should move closer to the market.
