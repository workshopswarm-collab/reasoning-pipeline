# Summary

This source note captures the immediate backdrop for crypto risk appetite rather than a Solana-specific hard catalyst. The relevant takeaway is that the market is in a risk-on but still fragile regime, which can support a wick through 90 but also means repricing can fail quickly.

## Key facts extracted

- CoinDesk's April 16 markets page reported bitcoin repeatedly failing to cleanly break above the mid-75k area, while other coverage highlighted that derivatives desks still wanted downside protection even as equities traded strongly.
- One CoinDesk headline described bitcoin as "steady as S&P 500 hits record, but options market isn't buying the peace trade," indicating residual hedging demand rather than an unambiguously euphoric crypto tape.
- Another CoinDesk headline noted bitcoin "slides back below $74,000 as breakout to higher levels fails again," showing that the broader crypto complex is still sensitive to failed breakouts.
- CME FedWatch page states it tracks probabilities of Fed target-rate changes implied by fed funds futures, reinforcing that macro-rate expectations remain an active market input even if the extracted page did not provide the numeric probabilities directly.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- CoinDesk page explicitly presents same-day market headlines describing a mixed regime: supportive cross-asset backdrop but skepticism from options and resistance at key crypto levels.
- CME FedWatch explicitly states it is the tool for implied probabilities of upcoming FOMC rate moves and that those probabilities are derived from fed funds futures.

## What is uncertain

- These sources do not provide a deterministic Solana-specific catalyst before April 19.

- The extracted FedWatch page did not expose numerical meeting probabilities in the fetch output.

## Why this source may matter

When a touch market is sitting just below the threshold, broad crypto risk appetite can be enough to produce a brief qualifying wick even without a Solana-specific fundamental announcement. Conversely, a failed BTC breakout or macro wobble can suppress the final few percent needed.

## Possible impact on the question

This context modestly supports a nontrivial chance of a $90 touch because the broader tape is not risk-off, but it also argues against overconfidence because the environment still shows failed-breakout behavior and hedging demand.
