# Assumption

The key working assumption is that SOL, already trading in the high-$88s on Binance, has a better-than-even chance to print at least one 1-minute high at or above $90 before April 19 11:59 PM ET.

## Why this assumption matters

The case is a touch market rather than a close-above market. A small additional move can resolve Yes even if SOL later reverses. The probability estimate depends heavily on whether short-horizon crypto volatility is sufficient to bridge the remaining gap.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above 50%
- A view that the market's 74% implied probability is somewhat high but not unreasonable
- A risk-focused conclusion that path risk is real even though the threshold is nearby

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance, the governing venue, already showed a recent high of 89.15 during the relevant week.
- Spot SOL was still trading around 88.8-88.9 during analysis, so the remaining distance to 90 was modest.
- Crypto assets like SOL can print brief wick highs that exceed round-number thresholds without needing a sustained breakout.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp downside move that takes SOL materially away from 90 and keeps it there into the weekend
- Evidence of deteriorating market conditions that reduce the chance of even a brief upward wick
- Verified Binance 1-minute data showing repeated rejection below 90 without any momentum into the final resolution window

## Early warning signs

- Binance highs stop advancing and remain capped below roughly 89.2-89.5
- SOL underperforms broader crypto beta while the deadline approaches
- Realized volatility compresses sharply, reducing the odds of a threshold-touch event

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move down materially, likely toward a coin-flip or below, because the bull case here is mostly a short-horizon volatility argument rather than a large directional thesis.
