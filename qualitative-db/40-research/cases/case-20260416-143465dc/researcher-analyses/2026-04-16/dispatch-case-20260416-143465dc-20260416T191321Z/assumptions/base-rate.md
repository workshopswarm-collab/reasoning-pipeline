# Assumption

A near-threshold SOL market that is already trading around 88.5-89.0 on Binance has a meaningful chance to print a brief 1-minute High above 90 before April 19 ends, even if it never establishes a durable move above 90 on closing prices.

## Why this assumption matters

The market resolves on an intraperiod touch, not on settlement above the line. That means the probability is carried more by short-horizon volatility and path-dependent overshoot than by whether SOL is fundamentally repriced above 90.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above a naive close-above-90 baseline.
- A view that the contract can resolve Yes from a modest continuation rally or wick.
- Skepticism toward arguments that rely only on daily closes below 90 so far.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance direct data showed SOL already within roughly 1-1.5% of the threshold at research time.
- Crypto majors and large alts routinely print short-lived intraday wicks beyond nearby round-number levels.
- The contract still has multiple remaining calendar days after the check, leaving time for one brief momentum extension.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp reversal that pushes SOL materially back into the low 80s and keeps it there through the remaining window.
- A collapse in realized intraday volatility that makes a 1-2% upside wick unusually unlikely.
- New information showing that the threshold is farther in practice than current Binance prints imply.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated failures near 89 with lower highs.
- Broad crypto risk-off conditions that drag altcoins down before another retest.
- Shrinking intraday ranges despite stable headline conditions.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should fall materially below market, because the main Yes path in this setup is a brief touch rather than a deep structural repricing over several days.
