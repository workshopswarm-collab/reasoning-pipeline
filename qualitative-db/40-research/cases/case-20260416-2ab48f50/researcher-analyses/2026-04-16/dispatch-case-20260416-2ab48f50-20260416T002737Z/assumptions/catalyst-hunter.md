# Assumption

Absent a new macro or crypto-specific shock before noon ET on April 17, Bitcoin is more likely than not to remain above 74,000 on Binance because current spot is already above that threshold and recent trading has spent substantial time in the mid-74k range.

## Why this assumption matters

The core thesis is not that a fresh bullish catalyst is required, but that no major negative catalyst arrives before the narrow resolution minute. That timing assumption carries most of the probability estimate.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly-above-market Yes lean.
- The interpretation that the most material catalyst is actually the presence or absence of a late negative repricing trigger.
- The view that routine noise is less important than discrete macro-risk windows between now and noon ET on April 17.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot at research time is already roughly 794 dollars above the threshold.
- Recent daily closes and intraday prints show BTC operating near or above the target level.
- The contract settles on a single minute, so if spot remains in the current regime, inertia slightly favors Yes.

## What would falsify it

- A clear risk-off catalyst that pushes BTC back below 74,000 and keeps it there into the settlement window.
- Evidence of sustained rejection near 75,000 with repeated failed retests and weakening risk sentiment across correlated assets.
- A Binance-specific dislocation that materially depresses BTCUSDT versus broader spot.

## Early warning signs

- Rapid overnight move back into low-73k or below.
- A broad equity or crypto risk-off open on April 17.
- Negative macro headlines or policy surprises during the US morning.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The thesis should flip toward No or at least toward a more symmetric coinflip if BTC loses the 74k handle ahead of the settlement window.
