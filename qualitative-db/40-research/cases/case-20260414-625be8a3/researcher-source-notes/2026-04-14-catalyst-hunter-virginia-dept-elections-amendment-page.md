# Summary

This is the governing official source establishing that the statewide referendum is scheduled for April 21, 2026 and describing the exact ballot question plus the practical yes/no effects presented to voters.

## Key facts extracted

- The page frames the referendum as a proposed constitutional amendment for the April 21, 2026 special election.
- The ballot question asks whether Virginia should be amended to allow the General Assembly to temporarily adopt new congressional districts.
- The voter explanation says a yes vote would allow the General Assembly to redraw congressional districts in time for the 2026 congressional elections.
- The voter explanation says a no vote would leave authority with the Virginia Redistricting Commission once a decade and keep current districts in place.

## Evidence directly stated by source

Quoted/paraphrased from the page and linked official explanation text:
- “April 21, 2026, Special Election”
- Ballot question: “Should the Constitution of Virginia be amended to allow the General Assembly to temporarily adopt new congressional districts to restore fairness in the upcoming elections, while ensuring Virginia's standard redistricting process resumes for all future redistricting after the 2030 census?”
- “A ‘yes’ vote would allow the General Assembly to redraw Virginia’s congressional districts... in time for the 2026 Congressional elections...”
- “A ‘no’ vote would leave the authority to draw congressional districts with the Virginia Redistricting Commission once a decade and Virginia’s current districts would remain in place.”

## What is uncertain

- The page does not itself provide polling, turnout expectations, or legal-risk probabilities.
- The page does not itself settle whether pending legal challenges could still delay, alter, or moot the vote before it occurs.
