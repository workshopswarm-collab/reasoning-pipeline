# Summary

This source provides historical context for how Virginia voters treated the earlier 2020 redistricting amendment.

## Key facts extracted

- Virginia Question 1 (2020), a redistricting commission amendment, was approved.
- Ballotpedia reports the certified result as **65.69% yes** and **34.31% no**.
- The 2020 amendment transferred redistricting authority from the legislature to a bipartisan commission/citizen structure, with Virginia Supreme Court fallback if the process failed.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- historical ballot result and percentages
- summary of the prior constitutional amendment's purpose and mechanics
- certified-results pointer via VPAP

## What is uncertain

- This is not evidence about the 2026 amendment itself; the 2026 measure is meaningfully different because it partially restores temporary legislative redraw authority under specified conditions.
- Historical approval of a different redistricting reform does not directly imply current voter support for undoing part of that framework.

## Why this source may matter

It supplies a relevant base-rate comparison: Virginia voters have previously approved a redistricting-related amendment by a wide margin, so an outright loss is not the default purely from topic area.

## Possible impact on the question

This context pushes somewhat toward yes by showing prior voter receptivity to redistricting reform, but the causal weight is limited because the current amendment is structurally different and could activate anti-gerrymandering or anti-legislative-control backlash.

## Reliability notes

Useful contextual source with reasonable reliability for historical background, but not independent evidence on the 2026 vote's likely outcome.
