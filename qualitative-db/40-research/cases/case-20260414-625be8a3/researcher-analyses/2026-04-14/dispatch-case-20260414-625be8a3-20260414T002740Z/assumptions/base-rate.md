# Assumption

The referendum will actually be held by the contract deadline and will not be derailed by legal or procedural issues before Virginia reports official results.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is not just asking whether voters would approve the amendment if they vote on it; it also resolves No if the referendum does not occur by November 3, 2026 at 11:59 PM ET or is definitively canceled.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability materially above a coin flip.
- Any inference drawn from ordinary ballot-measure passage rates rather than event-occurrence risk.
- Partial agreement with the market's high implied probability.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market description says the referendum is scheduled for April 21, 2026.
- The Virginia Department of Elections has an official amendment page tied to that special election.
- Once a statewide constitutional referendum is formally scheduled and publicly administered, the base rate for the vote actually occurring is usually high, absent a strong legal block.

## What would falsify it

- A court order stopping or postponing the referendum past November 3, 2026.
- Official Virginia action canceling the referendum with no opportunity to reschedule within the contract window.
- Credible reporting showing the amendment was removed from the ballot or failed a prerequisite needed for the vote to occur.

## Early warning signs

- Court rulings or injunctions referenced by Virginia election officials.
- Official notices changing the election calendar.
- Credible local reporting that the amendment faces serious procedural invalidation.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should drop sharply because the contract explicitly treats non-occurrence by the deadline as No.
