# Assumption

The referendum will in fact occur on or very near April 21, 2026 and will not be derailed by pre-election litigation or administrative disruption.

## Why this assumption matters

The market contract is unusually sensitive to timing and occurrence. If the vote does not happen by the contract deadline framework, or is definitively cancelled, the market can resolve No even if voter sentiment might otherwise have favored the amendment.

## What this assumption supports

- A high yes probability rather than a middling litigation-adjusted one.
- The view that the main remaining catalysts are campaign/turnout-related rather than existential process failure.
- The interpretation that current market pricing mainly reflects expected voter approval rather than event-nonoccurrence risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Virginia Department of Elections is publicly proceeding with the April 21, 2026 special election and ballot question.
- VPAP reports that the Supreme Court of Virginia allowed the referendum to continue and deferred final rulings until after April 21.
- Secondary explainers still describe the measure as on the ballot.

## What would falsify it

- A court order halting or postponing the referendum.
- Department of Elections guidance removing the measure from the special-election schedule.
- Credible reporting of an administrative or legal breakdown that prevents the statewide vote from occurring.

## Early warning signs

- New emergency filings or injunctions in the final week.
- Official election materials changing unexpectedly.
- Major bipartisan media coverage shifting from persuasion/turnout to whether the vote will happen at all.

## What changes if this assumption fails

Own probability should drop sharply because the contract’s path to Yes depends first on the referendum actually being held and producing an approving majority within the allowed window.
