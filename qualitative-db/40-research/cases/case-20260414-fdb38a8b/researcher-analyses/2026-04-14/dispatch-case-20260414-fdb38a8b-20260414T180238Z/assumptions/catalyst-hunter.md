# Assumption

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not just that Bitcoin is strong now; it is that strength persists through a narrow timestamped settlement window. If this assumption fails, current spot cushion is less informative than it appears.

## What this assumption supports

- A yes probability above the market-implied baseline only modestly or roughly in line with it.
- A catalyst view that the main actionable risk is timing/path rather than hidden contract ambiguity.
- A watchlist focused on sharp macro or risk-off shocks before Friday noon ET.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance BTCUSDT spot is materially above the 72,000 strike.
- Recent daily closes have mostly held above or around the low-70s despite fear-heavy sentiment.
- The market only needs one specific minute to settle above 72,000, not a full-day average.

## What would falsify it

- BTC loses the 72,000 area decisively before Apr 17 and cannot reclaim it by the noon ET resolution minute.
- A macro shock or exchange-specific disruption causes sharp settlement-minute dislocation.
- New evidence shows the noon ET candle mapping or settlement interpretation differs from the straightforward reading.

## Early warning signs

- Spot trades back toward the low 72k area with weak rebounds.
- Volatility clusters around US macro headlines or other broad risk-off news.
- Binance-specific operational anomalies or unusual BTC/USDT divergence versus other venues appear.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The contract moves from a high-probability yes to a roughly coin-flip or lower setup because the narrow time-window risk becomes dominant over current cushion.
