# Assumption

Because BTC is already trading in the mid/high-74k area, a one-week Binance 1-minute high of 76k is more likely than not if current short-horizon momentum and volatility persist.

## Why this assumption matters

The case is not about where BTC closes the week; it is about whether price touches a nearby threshold at any point. The probability estimate depends heavily on whether current volatility is enough to produce that touch.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly above 50%
- Respect for the market's 0.73 prior rather than a strong contrarian discount
- The conclusion that the market is directionally reasonable but somewhat aggressive

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Polymarket's rule only requires a single qualifying Binance 1-minute high.
- Contextual price data place BTC within roughly 1.5k of the threshold at research time.
- Recent daily ranges have been large enough that a 1-2% upside extension over several days is plausible.

## What would falsify it

- BTC loses momentum quickly and rejects below the mid-74k area.
- Macro or crypto-specific risk-off conditions compress upside and push spot back toward low-70k or below.
- Realized volatility falls sharply, reducing the chance of a brief threshold touch.

## Early warning signs

- Failure to sustain rallies above recent highs
- Repeated lower highs on liquid venues
- Sharp deterioration in broad crypto risk sentiment soon after market open

## What changes if this assumption fails

The correct estimate would move materially lower, likely below the current market price, because the contract's favorable touch mechanics would no longer outweigh the lack of momentum.
