# Assumption

The market is slightly over-weighting closeness-to-threshold and under-weighting the possibility that BTC stalls below nearby resistance for most or all of the week.

## Why this assumption matters

My variant view only differs modestly from the market because the target is near spot. The main reason to lean below market is that being close to $76k is not the same as clearing it within a dated weekly window.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below the 75% market-implied baseline.
- The claim that the strongest variant thesis is not bearish collapse, but failure to break resistance in time.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance data shows BTC around $74.6k, still below the threshold.
- TradingView context describes repeated rejection near $74k and no confirmed breakout.
- Weekly threshold markets are sensitive to timing and path, not just medium-term directional conviction.

## What would falsify it

- A clean breakout above recent highs early in the Apr 13-19 window.
- Strong continuation momentum that pushes BTC through $76k with volume instead of repeated rejection.

## Early warning signs

- Intraday highs start printing well above $74.9k.
- BTC reclaims and holds above the recent rejection zone instead of fading.
- Additional exchange data show broad-based breakout rather than venue-specific noise.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If BTC cleanly breaks resistance, the correct probability should move above the current market baseline rather than below it, because the distance from spot to threshold would compress quickly.
