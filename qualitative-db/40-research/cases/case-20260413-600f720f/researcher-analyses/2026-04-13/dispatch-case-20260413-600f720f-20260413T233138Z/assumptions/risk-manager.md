# Assumption

A roughly 1.6% move from the observed ~$74.8k spot level to a Binance 1-minute high of $76k is achievable within the April 13-19 window, but not so easy that a 74-75% market probability should be accepted uncritically.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability depends less on long-run BTC fundamentals and more on short-horizon path risk: whether BTC can print a brief spike high on Binance within the specific week.

## What this assumption supports

- A moderate-to-high Yes probability rather than an extreme Yes.
- A slight discount to the market rather than a major directional disagreement.
- Emphasis on timing/path failure as the key underpriced risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Polymarket contract wording only requires a Binance 1-minute high, which lowers the bar relative to a sustained break or weekly close.
- Binance and Coinbase spot snapshots near $74.8k show the target is close enough to be reachable in a normal BTC move.
- The remaining time window is almost a full week, giving multiple chances for a touch.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that BTC momentum has already stalled and is repeatedly rejecting well below $76k.
- A sharp macro or crypto-specific selloff that pushes BTC materially lower and makes a rebound to $76k unlikely in time.
- Updated Binance high/low path data showing recent realized volatility is too weak for a $1.2k upside probe.

## Early warning signs

- Failure to hold the mid-$74k area early in the week.
- Repeated inability to trade above roughly $75k despite broad market strength.
- A widening gap between spot levels and the threshold late in the week.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move down toward a more clearly sub-market Yes probability, with the main thesis shifting from “likely but not cheap” to “market overconfident relative to realized path risk.”
