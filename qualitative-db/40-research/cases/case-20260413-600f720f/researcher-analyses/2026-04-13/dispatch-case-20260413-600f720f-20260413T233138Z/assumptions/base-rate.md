# Assumption

The current weekly trading regime that produced a fast move from roughly $70.7k to the mid-$74k area will not automatically extend another roughly 1.5% to 1.8% upward to the $76k threshold within the remaining contract window.

## Why this assumption matters

The final estimate depends on treating the threshold as reachable but not already likely enough to justify the market's 75% implied probability.

## What this assumption supports

- A below-market estimate for YES on $76,000.
- A view that the market may be overweighting momentum and salience from the sharp Apr 13 rebound.
- A conclusion that the outside-view prior should still discount the chance of one more clean extension leg within days.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Observed contextual pricing shows BTC still below the threshold after a strong daily move.
- Threshold-touch contracts are nonlinear: getting close is not the same as crossing.
- In short-horizon crypto markets, late-stage round-number targets can attract narrative overconfidence.

## What would falsify it

- A verified continuation breakout through $75k followed by sustained strength toward $76k.
- New contextual evidence showing the governing source already printed at or above $76,000.
- A material macro or crypto-specific catalyst that changes the expected volatility regime upward.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple major spot venues printing fresh weekly highs above the current observed range.
- Rapid follow-through after the Apr 13 close rather than mean reversion/consolidation.
- Perpetual funding/open-interest behavior consistent with continued directional squeeze rather than exhaustion.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The correct probability should move materially upward, likely toward or above market, because the main base-rate objection is that the final leg to threshold is not automatic.
