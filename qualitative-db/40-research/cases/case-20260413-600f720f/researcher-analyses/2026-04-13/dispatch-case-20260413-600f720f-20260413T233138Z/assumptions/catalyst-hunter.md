# Assumption
The key nontrivial assumption is that, over a six-day window, Bitcoin reaching $76,000 depends more on immediate market momentum / risk sentiment and the absence of adverse macro or crypto-specific shocks than on any single scheduled hard catalyst.

# Why this assumption is being made
This is a short-dated threshold market. For such markets, the dominant mechanism is often whether price volatility and existing trend strength are sufficient to touch the level at least once during the window. There may be soft narrative catalysts, but they matter mainly insofar as they amplify already-present momentum.

# Evidence supporting the assumption
- The contract is path-dependent over a narrow time window.
- Spot context indicates BTC was below but plausibly within striking distance of $76,000 at review.
- No single clearly dominant scheduled event emerged as an obvious probability-resetting catalyst in the limited-evidence pass.

# Risks / failure modes
- An unobserved scheduled macro event or crypto-specific headline could move odds materially.
- If the market’s governing settlement source uses a specific exchange/benchmark, touch probability may differ from broad aggregated spot data.
- Momentum could fade quickly in a short window, making the market price too optimistic.

# What would change this assumption
A clearly identified, near-dated catalyst with historically high BTC impact, or explicit settlement-source rules tied to a specific venue with known price differences, would reduce reliance on the generic momentum assumption.

# Implication for the main finding
The main finding should frame catalysts as mostly conditional / soft rather than as one decisive scheduled event, and should explicitly note settlement-source ambiguity plus downside shock risk as the main disconfirmers.
