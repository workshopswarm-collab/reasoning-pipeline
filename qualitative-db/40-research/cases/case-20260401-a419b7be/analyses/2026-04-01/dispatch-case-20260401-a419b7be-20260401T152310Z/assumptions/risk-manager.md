# Assumption
The market's high yes price implicitly assumes that the current Middle East/Hormuz disruption keeps enough risk premium in the active WTI contract through the relevant March settlement window, rather than fading quickly as transit resumes and inventories rebuild.

## Why this assumption matters
If the geopolitical outage premium persists, $90+ is plausible even with underlying fundamentals softening later in 2026. If that premium fades faster than expected, WTI can settle back below $90 despite still-elevated headline risk.

## What this assumption supports
- A high probability on the $90+ bracket
- Confidence that current spot/futures strength survives the roll and settlement mechanics
- The view that war-driven supply disruption dominates bearish inventory-building fundamentals over the relevant horizon

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- EIA's March 10 STEO says Brent rose to $94 by March 9, expects Brent to average $91 in 2Q26, and explicitly ties the near-term price spike to the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz and associated shut-in production.
- IEA's March 2026 Oil Market Report says benchmark crude surged to roughly $92/bbl after hostilities began, estimates at least 8 mb/d of crude production curtailed, and notes nearly 20 mb/d of crude and product exports disrupted with only emergency stock draws acting as a temporary buffer.
- The market resolves on the active CME CL settlement, so a sustained disruption into the settlement window would likely keep front-month WTI elevated.

## What would falsify it
- Credible evidence that tanker traffic and insurance coverage through Hormuz normalize quickly and materially before the relevant settlement date
- Visible easing of shut-in production in Gulf exporters faster than EIA currently assumes
- A decisive collapse in front-month WTI relative to Brent/risk headlines, suggesting the risk premium is not sticking in the contract used for resolution

## Early warning signs
- Official or market reports showing resumed large-scale Hormuz transits
- Rapid narrowing of war-risk premia in crude spreads
- OPEC+ supply increases or emergency stock releases proving sufficient to cap front-month prices below $90
- Demand destruction signals intensifying faster than supply normalization risks

## What changes if this assumption fails
The current high market confidence would look overstated. Probability for $90+ should move materially lower because the bearish base of rising non-Middle-East supply, emergency stock releases, and expected inventory builds would reassert itself.
