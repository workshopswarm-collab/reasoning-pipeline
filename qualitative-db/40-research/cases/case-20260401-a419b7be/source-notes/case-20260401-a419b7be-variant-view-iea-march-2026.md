# Summary

IEA's March 2026 report confirms an acute supply-shock narrative: tanker movements through Hormuz nearly halted, benchmark crude surged roughly $20/b to around $92/b, and at least 8 mb/d of crude production was curtailed. But the same report also says high prices and weaker economic conditions are already eroding demand, and that emergency reserve releases are being deployed as a buffer.

## Key facts extracted

- Benchmark crude prices surged about $20/b to $92/b since hostilities began on February 28.
- Nearly 20 mb/d of crude and product exports were disrupted through Hormuz.
- IEA estimates at least 8 mb/d of crude production is currently curtailed, plus about 2 mb/d of condensates and NGLs.
- Several refineries and gas-processing facilities were shut or at risk.
- IEA reduced its forecast for global oil demand growth in March and April by more than 1 mb/d on average.
- IEA cut 2026 global oil demand growth by 210 kb/d to 640 kb/d.
- Global observed inventories were assessed above 8.2 billion barrels.
- IEA member countries agreed on March 11 to make 400 mb of emergency reserves available to the market.

## Evidence directly stated by source

The source directly supports the bullish immediate narrative: a very large disruption at the world's key oil chokepoint can plausibly keep prices elevated. But it also directly supports the underweighted counter-mechanism: high prices themselves are already destroying demand and prompting buffer supply from emergency reserves.

## What is uncertain

- Duration and severity of the shipping disruption.
- Whether emergency stock draws are enough to cap the front-month risk premium.
- Whether demand destruction offsets enough of the physical disruption.
- Whether the official CME active-month settlement relevant to the market remains dominated by immediate risk premium at month-end.

## Why this source may matter

It provides the strongest credible bullish case for $90+, while also containing the seeds of the variant view against overconfidence: shock-driven prices can coexist with demand impairment and policy buffers.

## Possible impact on the question

This source raises the probability of $90+ materially, but not necessarily to the high-80s. It suggests the market should price a substantial chance of persistent disruption, yet it also shows plausible dampeners the crowd may underweight once panic takes hold.
