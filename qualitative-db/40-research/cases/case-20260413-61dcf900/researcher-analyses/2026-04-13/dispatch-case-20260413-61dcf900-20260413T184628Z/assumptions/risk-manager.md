# Assumption

The key assumption is that the Kings' current position on the Western Conference wild-card line with only a few games left is more likely than not to hold through the end of the regular season, even though they had not yet officially clinched at fetch time.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate depends less on whether Los Angeles is currently playoff-caliber in abstract and more on whether its remaining path risk is small enough to justify a high Yes probability before official clinch.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-lean rather than a No-lean.
- A probability estimate above 50% but below near-certainty.
- A view that the market is directionally right but may still be somewhat too confident.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The Kings were listed in the second wild-card slot in the ESPN Western Conference wild-card view on 2026-04-13.
- Multiple lower teams remained behind them rather than ahead of them.
- The season is near its endpoint, which usually compresses the number of remaining paths that can knock a team out once it is already above the line.

## What would falsify it

- An official NHL update showing Los Angeles dropped below the cut line.
- A sudden late-season loss sequence combined with wins by immediate chasers.
- Tie-break disadvantage becoming decisive in a tightly packed final week.

## Early warning signs

- The Kings remain without an official clinch marker while nearby rivals keep winning.
- The gap to the first team out narrows to effectively one game or less.
- Independent standings sources begin showing materially different race interpretation or tie-break framing.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If Los Angeles is no longer clearly favored to retain a spot, the estimate should move down materially and the market's current 73.5% would look too high rather than merely somewhat confident.
