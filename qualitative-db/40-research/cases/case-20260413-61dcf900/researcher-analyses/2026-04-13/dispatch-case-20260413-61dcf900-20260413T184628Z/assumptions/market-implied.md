# Assumption

The market is mainly assuming that Los Angeles converting a one-point cushion plus a game in hand into a playoff berth is more likely than not, but still leaves a substantial path to failure over the final week.

## Why this assumption matters

This assumption is the bridge between raw standings position and a tradable probability near 70-80%. If it fails, the current Yes price could be materially too high.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes probability materially above 50%.
- A conclusion that the market is broadly efficient rather than badly stale.
- A view that current standings plus remaining-game asymmetry matter more than season-long goal differential alone.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Los Angeles is already in the WC2 slot in the official standings.
- The nearest chaser, Nashville, has one fewer game remaining and trails by one point.
- Independent public modeling (MoneyPuck) puts LA near 80% to qualify.
- The Kings have recent positive form, though that should not be overweighted.

## What would falsify it

- Same-day or next-game losses by Los Angeles combined with Nashville wins that erase the cushion.
- Evidence that key tiebreakers or schedule difficulty make the current standing more fragile than it appears.
- A sharper independent model or official clinch/elimination logic implying playoff odds materially below the low 60s.

## Early warning signs

- Los Angeles falls out of WC2 after the Apr 13 slate.
- Nashville defeats San Jose and controls its own path more clearly than the current standings suggest.
- Updated market price or public odds drop sharply despite no obvious news, implying hidden information or misread tiebreak risk.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market-implied case weakens from “roughly efficient favorite” to “overstated favorite,” and a fair probability could drop toward the low 60s or lower.
