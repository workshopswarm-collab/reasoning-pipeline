# Assumption

The core assumption is that Los Angeles is more likely than not to qualify, but the market is overpricing that likelihood because a non-trivial amount of uncertainty remains before an official clinch.

## Why this assumption matters

The entire variant view depends on distinguishing between "favored" and "effectively settled." If the Kings are functionally locked in via remaining schedule/tiebreak math, then the market price is not rich; if not, 0.735 may still be a bit aggressive.

## What this assumption supports

- A modest under-market estimate rather than a hard No view.
- Emphasis on source-of-truth and clinch status over narrative momentum.
- The idea that remaining-path uncertainty still deserves material weight.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ESPN standings show the Kings at 87 points after 79 games and without a clinch marker.
- Hockey-Reference standings also show Los Angeles outside the asterisk-marked playoff teams at the captured snapshot.
- The contract resolves on actual qualification under official NHL rules, not on perceived team strength or hot recent form.
- Negative goal differential and non-clinched status suggest the profile is less secure than a simple recent-form story would imply.

## What would falsify it

- Official NHL confirmation that Los Angeles has clinched.
- Clean reconstruction of remaining games/tiebreaks showing elimination of realistic miss scenarios.
- Credible updated standings showing nearby rivals can no longer catch the Kings.

## Early warning signs

- NHL or broad credible reporting begins referring to the Kings as having officially clinched.
- Competing teams immediately below them lose or are eliminated, collapsing downside paths.
- Updated official standings move Los Angeles into a clearly secured berth.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the Kings are effectively locked in, my estimate should move up toward or above the current market price and the variant thesis should collapse into rough agreement with the market.
