# Assumption

Los Angeles' current one-point edge over Nashville plus one extra game in hand is more likely than not to survive the next 72 hours because the Kings' three remaining opponents are already eliminated and at least one chaser faces direct schedule friction.

## Why this assumption matters

This assumption is carrying the bullish side of the thesis. Without it, LAK's poor full-season goal differential and only modest standings cushion would argue for a much closer to 50/50 view.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above the 73.5% market-implied baseline is not justified; instead it supports only a modestly positive estimate near but below the market.
- The view that the key catalysts are immediate game results rather than slow-moving narrative factors.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Official NHL standings show LAK already occupies WC2 at 87 points through 79 games.
- Nashville has only two games left and tops out at 90 points.
- Winnipeg and San Jose cannot reach more than 88 points.
- LAK's remaining opponents are Seattle, Vancouver, and Calgary, all officially eliminated in the same standings snapshot.
- Nashville and San Jose play each other on Apr. 13, guaranteeing that at least one pursuer fails to bank a clean regulation win path.

## What would falsify it

- LAK loses both Apr. 13 and Apr. 14 while Nashville wins out.
- Anaheim beats Minnesota and Nashville, compressing the race around the line while LAK stalls.
- Tiebreak scenarios break materially worse for LAK than simple points-based intuition suggests.

## Early warning signs

- A regulation loss to Seattle on Apr. 13.
- Nashville beating San Jose in regulation on Apr. 13.
- Evidence that LAK's tie-break position versus Anaheim or Nashville is materially weaker than assumed.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The view would move from "slight favorite but less safe than the market implies" toward "essentially coin-flip or worse," and the market's current premium would look more clearly overstated.
