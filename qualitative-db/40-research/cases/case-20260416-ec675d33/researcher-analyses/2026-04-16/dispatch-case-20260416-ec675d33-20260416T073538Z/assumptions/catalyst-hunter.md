# Assumption

The remaining window into April 20 noon ET will not contain a surprise macro or crypto-specific shock large enough to push Binance BTC/USDT more than roughly 4% lower from current levels.

## Why this assumption matters

The current thesis is not that upside catalysts must arrive; it is that BTC already sits sufficiently above 72,000 that only a meaningful downside catalyst is likely to flip the contract to No.

## What this assumption supports

- An own probability modestly above the market.
- A view that the main catalyst is the absence of a major scheduled negative event rather than the arrival of a positive one.
- A framing that weekend / headline risk is the main disconfirming mechanism.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot is currently around 74.9k, leaving roughly a 2.9k cushion above the threshold.
- Recent daily Binance closes have mostly remained above 72k.
- Official calendar checks show no FOMC decision or CPI release inside the remaining window.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp broad risk-off move in global markets.
- A crypto-specific regulatory, exchange, stablecoin, or security incident.
- A quick BTC drawdown back below the low-72k area before the final observation minute.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the recent 74k-73k area decisively on Binance.
- Weekend gap-down behavior or rising liquidation-driven volatility.
- New negative crypto policy or exchange-operational headlines.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move materially lower because this market resolves off a single minute-close, so a fast drawdown near settlement can dominate a broadly bullish medium-term backdrop.

## Notes that depend on this assumption

- Main catalyst-hunter finding for this dispatch.
- Any later synthesis that treats quiet scheduled calendar conditions as supportive.
