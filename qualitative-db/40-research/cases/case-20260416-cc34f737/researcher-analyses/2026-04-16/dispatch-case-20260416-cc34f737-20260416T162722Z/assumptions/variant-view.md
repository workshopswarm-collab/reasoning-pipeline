## Why this assumption matters

The probability estimate depends on treating the current distance above 2,300 as informative but not decisive. If a major catalyst hits before noon ET tomorrow, current spot context becomes much less predictive.

## What this assumption supports

- A moderate Yes lean rather than an overwhelming Yes view.
- A probability below the market's 71% implied level.
- The variant claim that the market may be overpricing simple spot-above-threshold logic while underpricing path-dependent intraday risk.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current spot on Binance and Coinbase is only modestly above 2,300.
- Binance 24h range already includes prices both above and below the threshold.
- No strong fresh catalyst was required to produce that range, which suggests ordinary crypto volatility alone can decide the contract.

## What would falsify it

- A material macro or crypto-specific catalyst before resolution.
- A sharp trend extension that moves ETH far enough above 2,300 that the threshold becomes trivial by tomorrow noon.

## Early warning signs

- ETH breaking materially above the recent range and holding.
- Large basis opening between Binance ETH/USDT and other major spot venues.
- Event-driven risk headlines that change crypto beta or exchange-specific trading conditions.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a fresh catalyst dominates, the probability should move toward whichever direction that catalyst implies rather than stay anchored to current price distance and realized range.
