# Assumption

Absent a fresh macro or crypto-specific shock before April 21 noon ET, Bitcoin is more likely than not to remain above 72,000 on Binance because current spot is already comfortably above that threshold.

## Why this assumption matters

The probability estimate is driven less by a bullish long-term thesis than by the short remaining time window and the current buffer above the strike.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-Yes estimate moderately above 70%.
- The view that no single scheduled soft narrative catalyst is likely to overpower current spot positioning by itself.
- The judgment that timing risk is mostly about an adverse catalyst arriving before the exact resolution minute.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance spot during research was about 74,990, several thousand dollars above the threshold.
- Independent spot references from Coinbase and CoinGecko were closely aligned.
- Adjacent Polymarket ladder pricing was internally coherent, with higher probabilities below 72,000 and lower probabilities above 72,000.

## What would falsify it

- A sudden macro risk-off event, policy headline, exchange-specific issue, or crypto liquidation cascade that pushes BTC back below 72,000 before April 21 noon ET.
- Evidence that Binance BTC/USDT is trading persistently weaker than broader BTC reference markets.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 74k area decisively and failing to recover.
- A sharp jump in intraday downside volatility over the weekend or Monday morning.
- Negative exchange, regulatory, or ETF-flow headlines that cause cross-venue selling.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case moves from a time-decay / hold-the-line setup toward a coin-flip or No-lean setup, because the margin above the threshold is not large enough to survive a meaningful short-term shock with confidence.
