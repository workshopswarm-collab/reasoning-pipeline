# Assumption

BTC does not suffer a sufficiently large adverse move before April 16 noon ET to push the Binance BTC/USDT 12:00 ET 1-minute close back below 72,000.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends less on upside continuation than on preservation of an existing price buffer above the strike.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes lean despite only one day remaining.
- A view that the market’s high implied probability is broadly justified.
- A catalyst framing where downside shocks matter more than upside catalysts.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance spot checks place BTC around 73.7k early on April 15, already about 2.3% above the strike.
- The 24-hour Binance range still kept price above 72k.
- The clearest identified scheduled macro catalyst in the window is the Fed Beige Book, which can create volatility but is not obviously a deterministic bearish event.

## What would falsify it

- A macro shock, crypto-specific negative headline, exchange/liquidity disruption, or strong risk-off move that pushes BTC/USDT below 72,000 into the April 16 noon ET candle.

## Early warning signs

- Sustained trading back toward 72.5k or lower on Binance.
- Sharp ETF outflow headlines or correlated equity/rates risk-off moves.
- Loss of support across multiple consecutive 1-minute/5-minute Binance closes near the strike.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The case should shift from a buffer-preservation Yes setup to a near-coinflip or No-lean timing setup, because the contract is struck on a single minute close rather than a broader daily average.
