# Assumption

The current BTC/USDT regime near 74k-75k is stable enough that ordinary six-day volatility is more likely to leave Bitcoin above 72,000 than below it at the specific Apr 21 noon ET minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The main Yes view depends less on a special bullish catalyst than on level persistence: if current price regime holds approximately, the threshold is already in-the-money.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability above 50% for Yes.
- A view that market pricing in the low-80s is directionally reasonable but somewhat aggressive.
- A base-rate interpretation that no extraordinary move is required for Yes, but no extraordinary crash is required for No either.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot price at research time is about 75,002, around 4.2% above the threshold.
- Recent daily closes have frequently held above 72,000.
- Over a six-day horizon, level persistence is often stronger than single-day narrative swings unless a major macro or crypto-specific shock intervenes.

## What would falsify it

- A decisive break back below roughly 72,000-73,000 with follow-through before Apr 21.
- A macro or crypto-specific shock that changes regime rather than producing routine noise.
- Evidence that intraday volatility around noon ET is systematically adverse versus the broader price trend.

## Early warning signs

- Consecutive daily closes below 72,000.
- Sharp risk-off move across crypto and high-beta assets.
- Binance-specific operational or pricing issues affecting the referenced candle.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the regime is not stable and BTC slips back into the high-60s/low-70s range, the market should move materially toward No and the base-rate estimate should fall below the current market-implied level.
