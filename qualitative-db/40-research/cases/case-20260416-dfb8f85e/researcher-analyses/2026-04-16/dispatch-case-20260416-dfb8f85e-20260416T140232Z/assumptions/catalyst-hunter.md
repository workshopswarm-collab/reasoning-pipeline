# Assumption

Absent a fresh risk-off macro shock or exchange-specific disruption, bitcoin can absorb normal profit-taking and still remain above the 72,000 threshold by the April 21 noon ET settlement window.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC must rally meaningfully from here; it is that the current cushion above 72,000 is large enough that only a modest adverse move is needed to flip the contract, so the probability estimate depends heavily on whether the next five days are likely to feature an actual catalyst strong enough to cause that drawdown.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate moderately above the market-implied baseline.
- A view that the most important question is catalyst risk, not long-run bitcoin fundamentals.
- A framing where 75k resistance matters less than whether the market breaks down through 72k.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot is currently trading around 73.8k, meaning the threshold is already in the money.
- Recent intraday trading reached above 75.4k, indicating the market has recently tolerated levels materially above the strike.
- Contextual reporting suggests current two-way tension is between institutional demand and short-term-holder supply rather than an already unfolding collapse.

## What would falsify it

- A macro shock or crypto-specific event that quickly pushes BTC below 72k and keeps it there into April 21.
- Clear evidence that the current price is being artificially supported by a transient flow likely to reverse before settlement.
- A Binance-specific operational or pricing disruption relevant to the noon ET one-minute candle.

## Early warning signs

- Spot loses the 73k area decisively and fails to reclaim it.
- Funding/positioning becomes more aggressively bearish while price momentum breaks lower.
- New macro headlines produce broad risk-off moves across equities and crypto together.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The view should shift from modestly bullish-on-threshold to a much more balanced or outright bearish contract assessment, because the contract only needs BTC to print one sub-72k close in the governing minute to resolve No.
