The base-rate case for Yes relies on the threshold already sitting below current spot and below most recent daily closes, so a regime break is the main path to a No resolution.

## What this assumption supports

## What would falsify it

- A sharp multi-day selloff that takes BTC back below $70,000 before April 20.
- New macro or crypto stress that changes the current price regime.
- Exchange-specific disruption around Binance BTCUSDT pricing or market access.

## Early warning signs

- Fast deterioration from the current mid-$74,000 area toward the threshold.
- Elevated volatility with repeated failed attempts to hold above $70,000.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The thesis should move quickly toward No or at least toward a much tighter near-coinflip assessment, because the edge in the current view comes mainly from the threshold being below current spot and recent closes.
