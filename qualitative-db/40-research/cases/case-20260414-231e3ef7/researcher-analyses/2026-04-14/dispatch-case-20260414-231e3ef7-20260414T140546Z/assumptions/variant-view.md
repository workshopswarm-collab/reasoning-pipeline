# Assumption

Even granting that Sindarov is a legitimate elite contender, an eight-player FIDE Candidates event is structurally too competitive and variance-heavy for any single entrant to deserve something close to 99% win probability absent direct official settlement information.

## Why this assumption matters

The whole variant view depends on separating "best or most credentialed entrant" from "near-certain tournament winner." If that structural distinction is wrong, then the market price could be justified.

## What this assumption supports

- A materially lower true probability than the market-implied 99.05%.
- The judgment that the market is overconfident rather than merely bullish.
- The view that source-quality and source-of-truth discipline matter more here than headline consensus.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Candidates tournaments are elite multi-player events rather than head-to-head settled outcomes.
- Chess.com contextual evidence establishes Sindarov as strong and newly qualified, but not as a player with direct official status equivalent to already having won the Candidates.
- The market description itself resolves on the actual winner declared by FIDE, implying that pre-resolution pricing should still reflect tournament uncertainty rather than simple credential recognition.

## What would falsify it

- Official FIDE information showing the field is radically weaker than normal or that the market is effectively already settled due to a rules/format condition.
- Credible evidence that the contract is miskeyed and intended to track a different proposition than actual tournament victory.
- Multiple independent high-quality sources showing Sindarov has become such an overwhelming favorite for field-specific reasons that near-certainty is defensible.

## Early warning signs

- Official publication of the full field or format revealing an unusually lopsided setup.
- A direct FIDE statement or bracket/standings condition that makes Sindarov's win nearly locked before the closing date.
- Broader independent reporting converging on an explanation for the extreme price beyond momentum or copy-trading.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the field structure or resolution mechanics really do make Sindarov close to locked, the variant case collapses and the correct move is to align much more closely with the market.
