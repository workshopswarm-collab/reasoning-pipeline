# Assumption

A two-point lead after 12 of 14 rounds against this remaining schedule implies Sindarov is overwhelmingly likely to convert the event, but not literally certain because a loss to Giri in round 13 or a tie-for-first / playoff path can still matter.

# Why this assumption is being made

The market is pricing Sindarov at 99.05%, which effectively assumes almost all meaningful catalysts are gone. Official FIDE reporting and standings support the broad thrust of that claim, but the remaining direct game against second-place Giri is still a real catalyst, so the final estimate should stay a bit below the market.

# Supporting evidence

- Official FIDE standings after round 12 show Sindarov 9, Giri 7, with two rounds left.
- Official FIDE reporting frames round 13 as Giri's must-win chance.
- Independent contextual check indicates round 13 is Giri vs Sindarov and round 14 is Sindarov vs Wei Yi.

# Key uncertainties / failure modes

- I did not fully extract the official FIDE PDF regulations text for tie-break procedures, so playoff mechanics are context-checked rather than primary-verified.
- If round-13 result is a loss for Sindarov, the event reopens materially.
- If a tie for first occurs, resolution depends on playoff results rather than the current classical standings alone.

# What would change this assumption

- Direct official regulations text showing no playoff path or different first-place determination.
- Official pairing / results information contradicting the contextual pairing sequence.
- New official result from round 13.

# Net effect on the main finding

This assumption pushes the estimate below the market rather than above it: very likely yes, but not quite as close to certainty as 99.05% implies.
