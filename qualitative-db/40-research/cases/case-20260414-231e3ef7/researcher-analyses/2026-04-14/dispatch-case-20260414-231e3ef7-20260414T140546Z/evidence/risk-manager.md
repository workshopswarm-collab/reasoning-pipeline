# Core question
Is Polymarket's ~99% pricing on Javokhir Sindarov to win the 2026 FIDE Candidates materially too high, too low, or roughly fair as of April 14, 2026?

# Evidence supporting the market
1. FIDE round 10: Sindarov led by 2 points with four rounds left.
2. FIDE round 11: he still led by 2 with three rounds left after defending well against Caruana.
3. FIDE round 12: he kept the 2-point margin with two rounds left after a pragmatic draw against Nakamura, while Giri failed to convert against Wei Yi.
4. Polymarket market-context text mirrors the same tournament state and says his implied probability is about 99.2% after round 12.

# Evidence against full certainty
1. FIDE round 12 still treats round 13 as live: Giri has a must-win with White against Sindarov.
2. The market contract resolves to the actual winner declared by FIDE, not to the player who is merely the favorite after round 12.
3. Extreme probabilities can ignore small but real paths: loss in round 13, tie/tiebreak complications, operational disruption, or source/reporting mismatch before official declaration.

# Netting / synthesis
The factual state strongly supports Sindarov as the overwhelming favorite. The risk-manager objection is not a chess-strength objection; it is a settlement-state objection. There is a difference between "almost surely wins from here" and "has already won." That gap is probably only a few percentage points, but at a 99% quote those few points matter.

# Most decision-relevant unresolved item
Direct official confirmation of exact standings/tiebreak consequences after round 12 and then the eventual FIDE winner declaration.

# Resulting lean
Lean: slightly against the market's extreme certainty, but only modestly. High probability yes; near-absolute certainty no.
