# Core question

Will Javokhir Sindarov win the 2026 FIDE Candidates Tournament?

# Resolution-relevant mechanism

This market resolves to the tournament winner. Therefore the important late-stage evidence is not generic player strength but current standings, remaining pairings, and whether any still-live result path or playoff path can prevent Sindarov from ending as winner.

# Evidence for YES

1. **Official FIDE round-12 report + standings image**
   - Sindarov leads with 9/12; Giri is second on 7/12.
   - Only two rounds remain.
   - FIDE quotes Sindarov as intentionally taking a quick draw to conserve energy for round 13.
   - This makes clear the event is in late conversion mode, not open-field mode.

2. **Official FIDE round 9-11 reports**
   - Sindarov's lead persisted through the key stretch and expanded back to two points by round 10.
   - Challenger failures (Caruana in round 11, Giri unable to convert round 12) reduced realistic comeback routes.

3. **Independent contextual confirmation (Wikipedia)**
   - Remaining sequence is round 13 vs Giri, round 14 vs Wei Yi.
   - Event is a 14-round double round-robin.

# Evidence against / disconfirming considerations

1. **Round 13 vs Giri is still a live direct catalyst**
   - Giri is second and gets the immediate head-to-head shot.
   - A Sindarov loss there materially reopens the event.

2. **Possible tie-for-first / playoff path**
   - Contextual source indicates tied first may trigger playoffs rather than immediate resolution by current standings alone.
   - That prevents a clean 100% claim before round 13 is played.

3. **Source-of-truth ambiguity in market text**
   - Market says official FIDE is primary, but credible consensus reporting may also be used.
   - This creates a slight procedural ambiguity if official posting lags while consensus reports a winner.

# Netting / weight

- The official standings are the dominant evidence and strongly support YES.
- The only material remaining negative catalysts are: (a) direct loss to Giri in round 13, and (b) a playoff/tie complication.
- Most other narratives are immaterial at current stage.

# What would most move the estimate

- **Upward to ~99.5-100%:** official round-13 draw or win by Sindarov, or official clinch language from FIDE.
- **Downward sharply:** official round-13 loss to Giri.
- **Minor movement only:** generic commentary about form, nerves, or reputation without new results.
