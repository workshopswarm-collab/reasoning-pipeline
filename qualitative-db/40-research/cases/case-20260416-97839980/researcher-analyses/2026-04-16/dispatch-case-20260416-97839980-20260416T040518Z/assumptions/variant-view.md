# Assumption

The recent Binance trading range around 81.5-86.5 is informative enough that a current spot near 85 implies only moderate, not negligible, risk of a sub-80 noon ET close by April 19.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is priced at an extreme 92% implied probability, so the case for disagreeing slightly depends on treating short-horizon realized volatility as still meaningful even when the threshold is currently below spot.

## What this assumption supports

- A high-but-not-near-certain Yes estimate.
- The variant view that the market may be somewhat overconfident.
- Extra focus on the exact noon ET close rather than broad weekend direction.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

Recent Binance daily closes stayed above 80, but the same sample also showed lows as low as 78.38 and several closes only modestly above 80. That means the threshold is not safely irrelevant; it is merely currently favored.

## What would falsify it

- A fresh sustained rally that moves SOL materially farther above 80, making a noon ET dip less plausible.
- New evidence that realized intraday volatility has compressed sharply.
- Strong market-wide bullish catalyst reducing the probability of a timing-specific drop.

## Early warning signs

- SOL holding above 88-90 into the weekend.
- Broader crypto beta strengthening while SOL outperforms.
- Repeated failed attempts to trade below the low-80s.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The fair probability should move closer to the market or even above it, because the remaining path to a No outcome becomes mostly a tail event rather than an ordinary short-horizon fluctuation.
