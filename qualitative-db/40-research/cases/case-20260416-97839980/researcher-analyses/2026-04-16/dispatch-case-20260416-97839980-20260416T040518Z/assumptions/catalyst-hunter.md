# Assumption

The key assumption is that no new crypto-wide negative catalyst between now and Sunday noon ET will knock SOL down more than roughly 6-7% from the current Binance level and keep it below $80 into the exact resolving minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is already pricing a very high chance of Yes, so the thesis depends less on upside catalysts than on the absence of a sharp downside repricing before the narrow resolution window.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability close to but slightly below the market.
- The view that time decay itself favors Yes if no fresh shock arrives.
- The judgment that current cushion above 80 matters more than speculative upside narratives.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance spot was about 85.37 at the check.
- Recent Binance daily lows were still above 80.
- Only a few days remain until resolution, reducing the window for multiple adverse catalysts to compound.

## What would falsify it

- A crypto-wide risk-off event or exchange-specific disruption that sends SOL back below 80 before April 19 noon ET.
- Evidence that Binance SOL/USDT is trading materially weaker than the broader SOL complex.
- A late-week breakdown in major crypto risk sentiment that removes the current cushion.

## Early warning signs

- SOL losing the 83-84 area on Binance with momentum.
- Bitcoin/ETH weakness causing high-beta alt underperformance.
- Exchange or operational issues affecting Binance price discovery near the window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability of Yes would fall quickly because the contract is path-sensitive to a single exact minute. If SOL trades back into the high 70s or low 80s late in the window, this should move from high-confidence Yes toward a live toss-up.
