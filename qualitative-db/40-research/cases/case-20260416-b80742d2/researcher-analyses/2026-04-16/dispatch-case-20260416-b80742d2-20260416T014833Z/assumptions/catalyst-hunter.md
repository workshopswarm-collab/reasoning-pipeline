# Assumption

Absent a fresh idiosyncratic negative catalyst, XRP can remain above 1.30 through the specific Binance 12:00 ET one-minute settlement window on April 19.

## Why this assumption matters

The case is no longer about whether XRP can ever trade above 1.30; it is already above the strike during this run. The forecast depends on whether timing-specific downside risk between now and the noon ET settlement minute is large enough to overwhelm the existing cushion.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate above the market-implied 95% but not at certainty.
- A catalyst framing in which the highest-information events are negative surprises, not bullish upside catalysts.
- A view that path-to-repricing is more likely to come from risk-off crypto tape, exchange-specific disturbance, or legal/regulatory headlines than from ordinary day-to-day noise.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Live Binance XRPUSDT checks during this run showed spot around 1.4013, materially above 1.30.
- The 24h Binance range checked during this run was approximately 1.3503 to 1.4086, still above the strike.
- Ripple’s recent public communications show a steady stream of institutional-adoption and XRPL ecosystem messaging rather than an obvious near-dated negative company-side event calendar.
- With only a few days to settlement, the base case is persistence unless a clear negative trigger appears.

## What would falsify it

- Binance XRPUSDT materially breaking below 1.30 before or into the April 19 noon ET candle.
- A major adverse crypto-market shock that drags high-beta majors lower across the board.
- A Ripple/XRP-specific legal, regulatory, security, or exchange-access headline that creates rapid de-risking.
- Evidence that the settlement minute should be interpreted differently from the expected noon-ET candle close.

## Early warning signs

- XRP loses the 1.35 area and begins trading near the strike with rising realized volatility.
- Bitcoin/ETH suffer abrupt downside with XRP underperforming peers.
- Exchange-specific disruptions or data inconsistencies emerge around Binance spot feeds.
- Weekend liquidity thins and XRP starts printing larger downward intraday moves.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If this assumption weakens, the case should move from a high-probability Yes toward a far more balanced probability because the contract is resolved by one narrow minute, not a broader average or daily close.
