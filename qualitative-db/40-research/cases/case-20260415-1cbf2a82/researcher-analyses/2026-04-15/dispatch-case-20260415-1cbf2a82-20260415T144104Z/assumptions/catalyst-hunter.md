# Assumption

The main assumption is that, absent a new negative macro or crypto-specific shock, BTC is more likely than not to remain above 72,000 on Binance through the specific noon ET minute on Apr 17.

## Why this assumption matters

The finding is not based on BTC needing to rally; it is based on BTC defending an already-in-the-money threshold over a short horizon. That makes the thesis sensitive to whether the next 48 hours are relatively ordinary versus shock-heavy.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly bullish-to-neutral near-term probability estimate above the market baseline.
- The view that the most important catalyst is the absence or presence of a downside shock, not a fresh upside event.
- The judgment that market pricing is slightly underweighting the fact that current spot is already above the threshold.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance spot was about 74,013 at check time, already above 72,000.
- No scheduled FOMC decision sits inside the remaining window.
- For a short-dated threshold market, no large known calendar catalyst implies the base case is drift/noise around spot rather than a forced repricing event.

## What would falsify it

- BTC falls below 72,000 and stays weak into Apr 17 noon ET.
- A new macro risk-off event, major exchange/product headline, or geopolitical shock causes a >3% drawdown before the cutoff.
- Binance-specific price dislocation meaningfully underperforms broader BTC spot at the resolution minute.

## Early warning signs

- Sharp overnight downside momentum taking BTC back toward low-72k or below.
- A visible risk-off move across equities, rates, and crypto before U.S. cash hours.
- Exchange-specific stress or unusually wide Binance basis versus other BTC references.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability should move materially lower, likely below the current market, because this contract has very little time to mean-revert once price breaks the threshold close to the deadline.
