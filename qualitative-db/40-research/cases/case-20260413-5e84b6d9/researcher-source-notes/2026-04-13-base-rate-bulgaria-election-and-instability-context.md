# Summary

This note captures the strongest contextual evidence used for the outside-view estimate: Bulgaria remains in a prolonged period of electoral fragmentation and repeated snap elections.

## Key facts extracted

- The next Bulgarian parliamentary election is scheduled for 19 April 2026.
- Bulgaria has experienced repeated snap elections and prolonged coalition instability since 2021.
- Caretaker governments have repeatedly filled gaps when parliament failed to form durable coalitions.
- The 2026 election summary page lists Rumen Radev as leader of a party/project labeled Progressive Bulgaria, suggesting some current political relevance rather than pure retirement from partisan politics.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The election date is 19 April 2026.
- The political crisis has featured multiple elections and repeated failures to form durable governing coalitions.
- Various caretaker prime ministers have served during this instability.

## What is uncertain

- Wikipedia is not an authoritative source for final resolution or candidate eligibility.
- The page’s claim that Rumen Radev leads Progressive Bulgaria needs stronger independent confirmation before it should be treated as decisive.

## Why this source may matter

The structural prior in a fragmented parliamentary system is that becoming the next formally sworn-in prime minister is hard, coalition-dependent, and usually flows from party/coalition arithmetic rather than personal popularity alone.

## Possible impact on the question

This context pushes against extreme confidence in any single named outcome unless there is strong coalition evidence. It also creates the main disconfirming consideration for a low-Radev base-rate view: if he now leads a significant electoral vehicle, his path is no longer purely speculative.
