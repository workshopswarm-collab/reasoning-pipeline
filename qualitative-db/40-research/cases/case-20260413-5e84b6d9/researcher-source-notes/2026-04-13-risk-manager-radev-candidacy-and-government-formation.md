# Summary
This source bundle establishes that Rumen Radev is no longer president, is actively leading Progressive Bulgaria into the April 19 election, and is publicly framing the post-election task as forming a majority in parliament. That matters because the market resolves only if he is officially sworn in as Prime Minister, which requires not just electoral popularity but successful government formation.

## Key facts extracted
- Official presidential site says Iliana Iotova took office as head of state on January 23, 2026 after the Constitutional Court terminated Rumen Radev's powers following his resignation.
- BTA reports Radev is leader of Progressive Bulgaria and says the next parliament's top priority is to form a majority.
- BTA quotes Radev acknowledging that much depends on the arithmetic in the National Assembly.
- BTA presents Radev's stated ambition to form a government, but not evidence that a coalition deal is already secured.

## Evidence directly stated by source
- Radev served as president until 2026 and resigned in January 2026.
- Progressive Bulgaria is contesting the election under his leadership.
- Parliamentary arithmetic is central to whether his camp can govern.

## What is uncertain
- Whether Progressive Bulgaria will finish first.
- Whether it can assemble a durable majority or at least a swearing-in coalition.
- Whether anti-Radev or anti-corruption fragmentation leaves Bulgaria without a non-caretaker PM for an extended period.

## Why this source may matter
It directly supports eligibility and candidacy mechanics while also surfacing the main failure mode: winning narrative strength is not the same as commanding a parliamentary majority.

## Possible impact on the question
Positive for Radev relative to a world where he remained president or never ran, but materially weaker than a 90%+ implied probability because the path to resolution runs through coalition formation and formal swearing-in.

## Reliability notes
- Official presidential site is high-quality for the resignation/succession fact.
- BTA is useful for current campaign positioning and public statements, but campaign rhetoric is not proof of post-election majority viability.
