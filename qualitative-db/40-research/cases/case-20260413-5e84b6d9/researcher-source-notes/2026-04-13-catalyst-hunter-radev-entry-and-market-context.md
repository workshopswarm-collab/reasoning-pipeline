# Summary

Recent reporting indicates that former President Rumen Radev resigned, entered the parliamentary race through a new alliance, and is viewed as electorally competitive. But these sources stop short of proving he is the likeliest person to be formally sworn in as prime minister after the election.

## Key facts extracted

- Reuters search result/snippet reports that Radev registered a three-party alliance called Progressive Bulgaria to run in the 19 Apr 2026 election.
- Reuters and other reporting snippets characterize Radev as a leading or popular figure and suggest polls place his alliance near or at the front.
- DW reports Radev announced his resignation and would be replaced by Vice President Iliana Yotova, with a new vote expected in late March or April.
- Wikipedia contextual summary says a caretaker government led by Andrey Gyurov was appointed on 18 Feb 2026 and the election date was set for 19 Apr 2026.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Radev resigned from the presidency and entered active electoral politics.
- A 19 Apr 2026 election date is the operative event horizon.
- Bulgaria remained in a caretaker/interim governing phase before the election.

## What is uncertain

- Poll quality and exact vote/seat margins are unclear from the accessible snippets.
- These reports do not establish a stable governing majority for Radev after the election.
- They do not settle how the market will treat coalition bargaining, failed mandates, or a long post-election deadlock.

## Why this source may matter

These sources establish the main bullish thesis behind a high Radev price: he is not a hypothetical candidate; he is active, prominent, and plausibly the largest single electoral force.

## Possible impact on the question

This source family supports a high probability that Radev is a serious contender and likely to remain the focal name into election week, but it does not by itself justify a >90% probability because formal swearing-in in a fragmented parliamentary system still depends on coalition formation and timing.

## Reliability notes

Useful for current campaign reality and broad direction. Evidence independence is only moderate because multiple outlets may rest on the same underlying public events. Stronger for candidacy status than for exact probability of eventual swearing-in.
