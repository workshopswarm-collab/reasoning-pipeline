# Summary

Official Bulgarian government press releases confirm that Andrey Gurov is serving as prime minister of the caretaker government and that the government was actively preparing the 19 April 2026 snap parliamentary election.

## Key facts extracted

- A 23 Feb 2026 Council of Ministers release says Prime Minister Andrey Gurov opened the first meeting of the new cabinet and adopted the budget framework for preparation and conduct of the upcoming snap parliamentary elections.
- The same release says election funds covered election commissions, public information campaigns and trainings organized by the Central Election Commission, voter-list updates, polling stations abroad, and election-day transparency measures.
- A 1 Apr 2026 Council of Ministers release says the caretaker government would continue to act lawfully and that the Ministry of Interior would ensure the election process is in keeping with the rules.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Direct official confirmation that Andrey Gurov was prime minister as of late Feb/early Apr 2026.
- Direct official confirmation that the election was upcoming and administratively active before 19 Apr 2026.
- Direct official confirmation that the government in office before the election is a caretaker government.

## What is uncertain

- These releases do not by themselves establish who will command a parliamentary majority after the election.
- They do not state when a post-election regular government will be sworn in.
- They do not directly discuss Rumen Radev’s coalition strength.

## Why this source may matter

This is a governing source-of-truth surface for who currently serves as prime minister and whether a PM is caretaker versus regular. That distinction matters because the market explicitly excludes caretaker prime ministers from counting toward resolution.

## Possible impact on the question

This source lowers the risk of confusing the current caretaker PM with the eventual resolving PM. It supports the timing thesis that the relevant catalyst is not the election date alone, but the post-election coalition-formation and swearing-in sequence.

## Reliability notes

High reliability for current-officeholder status and official administrative timing. Low usefulness for seat-count projections or coalition bargaining beyond confirming the state machinery is preparing the vote.
