# Decision summary

Rumen Radev is still the likeliest single named outcome, but the market's 0.9035 price looks materially too high for a parliamentary, coalition-dependent, formally-sworn-in contract with explicit caretaker exclusion and a real deadlock/'Other' path. My post-synthesis view is that Radev is favored but not close to locked.

## Why this may matter now

Market-implied probability is 0.9035; my syndicated range is 0.62 to 0.74. That is a meaningful below-market view and potentially actionable only if one believes public evidence is not missing a major local-information edge. The likely mispricing is favorite-versus-certainty compression: Radev may be the frontrunner, but the market appears to underprice coalition and formal investiture risk.

## Shift versus swarm baseline

This is broadly in line with the swarm-implied center around the high 0.60s, but I narrow away from the base-rate persona's 0.35 because synthesis-stage verification did confirm that Radev is a real, active contender rather than a category-error candidate. I did not move up toward market because the key missing piece—credible coalition-lock evidence—remained missing.

## Edge verification status

Verification quality is medium. The synthesis independently confirmed several critical mechanics: caretaker exclusion, current caretaker status, election timing, Radev's resignation from the presidency, and the parliamentary coalition bottleneck. That is enough to reject a simplistic near-lock interpretation. But verification did not reach the decisive question of whether Radev already has secured coalition support or a clearly dominant investiture path, so the edge is only partially verified.

## Compression toward market

No. I did not compress meaningfully toward the market because the additional verification did not uncover strong pro-market evidence; if anything, it reinforced that the contract is narrower and more coalition-dependent than a 90%+ price suggests. I also did not stay near the most bearish swarm tail because verification did support Radev as a serious, plausible frontrunner.

## Timing and catalyst posture

The next major catalyst is the 2026-04-19 election followed immediately by seat allocation and coalition bargaining. The edge is more likely to compress quickly if credible coalition commitments for Radev appear, and more likely to widen if fragmented results or exclusion coalitions emerge. Waiting for post-election coalition signals is likely to improve decision quality materially.

## Key blockers

Main blockers are lack of independently verified coalition commitments, uncertainty about whether anti-Radev coalitions can coalesce, and the possibility that market pricing embeds local information not visible in accessible sourcing. This is not a blocker to having a view, but it is a blocker to high-conviction anti-market sizing.

## Best countercase

Best countercase: the market's 90%+ price may reflect stronger local information than the accessible sources captured, such as party-level knowledge that Radev is already the accepted coalition focal point and that alternatives are not viable. The catalyst-hunter and variant-view lanes preserved this best.

## What would change the view

This view would move sharply upward on multiple independent reports or official signals that key parties have agreed to back a Radev-led cabinet, or on a post-election seat map that makes his coalition path obviously dominant. It would move downward on explicit coalition vetoes, evidence of an alternative coalition focal PM, or signs that deadlock is likely enough to elevate Other materially.

## Recommended next action

Request a post-election refresh focused narrowly on official results, coalition math, and party commitment statements. If that is not possible yet, proceed with a below-market but medium-confidence view rather than a high-conviction anti-market stance.
