# Assumption

Even if Rumen Radev’s bloc finishes first, the market resolves in his favor only if he can convert electoral strength into a regular post-election government that is formally sworn in before 31 March 2027 ET.

## Why this assumption matters

The contract does not resolve on who wins the plurality or who dominates headlines. It resolves on the next individual officially sworn in as prime minister after the parliamentary election, excluding any caretaker prime minister.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate below the market’s current ~90.35%.
- A catalyst-focused view that coalition formation matters more than election night itself.
- Extra caution around an extreme market price in a fragmented parliamentary system.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market wording explicitly requires formal swearing-in.
- Bulgaria is in a snap-election / caretaker context rather than a stable majoritarian one.
- Recent official government materials confirm the current PM is a caretaker, highlighting the distinction between interim administration and a counting resolution outcome.

## What would falsify it

- Clear official or near-official evidence that Radev already has a durable governing majority lined up and is overwhelmingly likely to be nominated and sworn in quickly.
- Credible reporting that Bulgarian institutional practice makes the leader of the largest bloc the overwhelming favorite to become PM even without a precommitted coalition.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple independent reports of coalition agreements centered on Radev.
- Public endorsements from enough parliamentary actors to indicate a viable governing majority.
- Official scheduling signals pointing toward a swift post-election investiture rather than prolonged bargaining.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If coalition conversion risk is much lower than assumed, the fair probability would move upward materially and could approach the market level.
