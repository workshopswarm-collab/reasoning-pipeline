# Assumption
If Progressive Bulgaria finishes first or near first, Rumen Radev will be able to convert that electoral strength into a formally sworn-in premiership rather than being blocked by coalition arithmetic or prolonged deadlock.

## Why this assumption matters
The market resolves on formal swearing-in, not popularity, candidacy, or even first-place finish. Most of the bullish case depends on crossing this institutional bridge.

## What this assumption supports
- A probability materially above coin-flip that Radev becomes the next PM.
- The view that the market's current favorite status for Radev is directionally justified.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- Radev appears to be a leading electoral contender.
- He has unusually high name recognition and recent momentum after launching Progressive Bulgaria.
- Bulgarian politics has been unstable enough that a fresh vehicle led by a popular ex-president could plausibly become the nucleus of a governing coalition.

## What would falsify it
- Clear evidence that potential coalition partners refuse to back a Radev-led cabinet.
- Election results showing Progressive Bulgaria too weak to assemble a majority path.
- A rival coalition forming around another figure.
- Extended deadlock that increases the chance of an eventual "Other" resolution.

## Early warning signs
- Poll slippage leaving Progressive Bulgaria only narrowly ahead or behind GERB.
- Multiple mid-sized parties above threshold with incompatible preferences.
- Public statements from likely partners ruling out Radev personally.
- Reports emphasizing minority-government or technocratic alternatives.

## What changes if this assumption fails
The probability of Radev being the next formally sworn-in PM drops sharply, and the main risk shifts from horse-race uncertainty to contract-resolution path risk.
