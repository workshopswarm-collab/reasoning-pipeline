# Assumption

The market’s 90%+ pricing assumes that public reporting about Radev’s resignation and active electoral participation is broadly correct and that he is the central coalition-formation focal point after the 19 April 2026 election.

## Why this assumption matters

Without that assumption, the price looks indefensibly high because a sitting or recently resigned president does not become prime minister automatically; he would still need electoral and coalition success plus formal swearing-in under the contract terms.

## What this assumption supports

- Treating the current market price as at least directionally intelligible rather than obviously mistaken.
- Assigning Radev a better-than-even chance of being next sworn prime minister.
- Interpreting the contract as focused on the first non-caretaker post-election premier rather than the current caretaker officeholder.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Public election-page reporting says Radev resigned in January 2026 and formed Progressive Bulgaria.
- The Bulgarian government homepage confirms a caretaker-era current prime minister, which is consistent with the market looking past the current officeholder.
- In fragmented parliamentary systems, markets often concentrate on the actor viewed as the coalition focal point even before formal nomination.

## What would falsify it

- An authoritative Bulgarian source showing Radev is not a registered electoral actor or not eligible to become PM.
- Credible reporting that another coalition leader has clearly overtaken him in government-formation negotiations.
- Official post-election government formation moving to a different sworn PM first.

## Early warning signs

- Polls or reporting showing Progressive Bulgaria underperforming badly.
- Public statements from major parties ruling out coalition support for Radev.
- Resolution-source reporting emphasizing another candidate as the viable compromise PM.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market price should fall sharply. My probability would move from a modestly pro-Radev view toward a much more open field or even “Other,” depending on the alternative coalition map.
