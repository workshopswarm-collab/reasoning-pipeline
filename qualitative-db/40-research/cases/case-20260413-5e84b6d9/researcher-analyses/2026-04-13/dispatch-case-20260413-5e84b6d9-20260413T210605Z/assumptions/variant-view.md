# Assumption
The market is overpricing Rumen Radev because political salience is being conflated with the narrower requirement that he be the first post-election figure formally sworn in as prime minister rather than remaining a presidential or kingmaker figure amid coalition deadlock.

## Why this assumption matters
If this assumption is wrong and Radev is already on a clear path to nomination plus parliamentary support, then the current high market price could be justified. If it is right, the contract wording creates more downside paths than the market implies.

## What this assumption supports
- A probability estimate materially below the market-implied 90.35%.
- Greater weight on coalition mechanics and timing risk.
- Greater attention to the “Other” fallback than the headline price suggests.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption
- The market requires actual swearing-in after the election, not mere expectation.
- Caretaker or interim PMs are excluded.
- Bulgaria’s parliamentary system introduces coalition-formation risk.
- The parliamentary institution, not only presidential status, is decisive for government installation.

## What would falsify it
- Clear official or highly credible independent reporting that Radev is the agreed coalition nominee with secure parliamentary backing.
- Post-election official steps showing he is the imminent and undisputed sworn-in PM.

## Early warning signs
- Broad credible reporting converging on Radev specifically as the coalition compromise PM.
- Major blocs publicly endorsing him before the election.
- Official constitutional steps indicating rapid government formation around his candidacy.

## What changes if this assumption fails
My estimate should move materially upward, likely toward the market, because the main variant mechanism would have been removed.
