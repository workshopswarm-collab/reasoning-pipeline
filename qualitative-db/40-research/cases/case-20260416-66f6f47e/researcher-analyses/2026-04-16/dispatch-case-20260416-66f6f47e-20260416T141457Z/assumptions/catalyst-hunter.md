# Assumption

There is no scheduled macro or crypto-specific catalyst before Apr 21 noon ET likely to move BTC by more than the roughly 2% needed to push it decisively below 72000 from current levels, so current spot level and recent range should dominate the probability.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC is guaranteed to stay above 72000, but that absent a fresh catalyst the market mostly reduces to whether recent spot strength persists for four more days.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability above 50%
- A view that the market is roughly fairly priced to slightly rich, rather than obviously wrong
- Emphasis on threshold distance and ordinary volatility rather than on a specific known event catalyst

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BLS calendar shows the next CPI release is May 12, not inside this window.
- BEA schedule shows the next major GDP / Personal Income and Outlays release is Apr 30, after this market resolves.
- Fed calendar fetch did not surface a clear market-moving Board event in the available extraction.
- Binance recent price history shows BTC already trading above 72000 on several recent days, so the threshold is not far above current spot.

## What would falsify it

- A new macro shock, regulatory shock, exchange-specific issue, or geopolitical event that triggers a rapid crypto risk-off move before Apr 21.
- Evidence of a scheduled but previously missed catalyst landing inside the Apr 16-Apr 21 window.
- BTC losing the 72k area quickly and failing to reclaim it into Apr 21.

## Early warning signs

- BTC closing back under 72k on Binance and failing to hold rebounds
- Abrupt cross-asset risk-off conditions in equities / rates / dollar
- Binance-specific operational issues that could distort or interrupt the governing price surface

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a real near-term catalyst appears or BTC loses 72k cleanly, the current mild Yes lean should compress materially toward coin-flip or No, because this contract is about one exact minute close rather than any intraperiod touch.
