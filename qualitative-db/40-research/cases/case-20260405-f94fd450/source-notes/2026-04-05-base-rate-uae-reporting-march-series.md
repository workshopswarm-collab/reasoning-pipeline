# Summary

Two UAE-focused reports, both citing the Defence Ministry, confirm that Iranian missiles/drones continued throughout March and explicitly describe launches coming from Iran / launched from Iran. This is especially useful for the contract’s attribution requirement, though these particular reports are less decisive on whether every listed barrage produced qualifying impacts.

## Key facts extracted

- Khaleej Times (21 Mar) says UAE air defences dealt with 3 ballistic missiles and 8 drones “coming from Iran” on March 21.
- Khaleej Times says since the start of the attacks, UAE air defences had dealt with 341 ballistic missiles, 15 cruise missiles, and 1,748 drones.
- The National (31 Mar) says the UAE intercepted eight ballistic missiles, four cruise missiles, and 36 drones “launched from Iran” on Tuesday, March 31.
- The National also reports an apparent Iranian drone strike hit a Kuwaiti oil tanker anchored off a port in Dubai and caused a fire, alongside injuries from interception debris in Dubai.

## Evidence directly stated by source

These reports directly support attribution/origin: launches were from Iran. They also support repeated attack frequency throughout March.

## What is uncertain

- Most of the enumerated barrages discussed in these pieces were intercepted, which would not qualify on their own.
- The National’s tanker example is not obviously a strike on UAE soil in the same clean way as Fujairah infrastructure, because the damaged object was a Kuwaiti tanker anchored off a Dubai port.

## Why this source may matter

The contract requires attribution to Iran proper, not proxies. These pieces help satisfy that origin requirement and confirm attacks remained active through the end of the reporting window.

## Possible impact on the question

These reports strengthen the yes case when paired with BBC’s impact reporting, because together they show both attribution to Iran proper and repeated March attacks against UAE territory/infrastructure.
