# Summary

These UAE-focused reports are the best accessible sources for the contract's attribution requirement. They explicitly say missiles and drones were coming from / launched from Iran during March. Their weakness is that much of the same reporting emphasizes interceptions, which do not count by contract.

## Key facts extracted

- Khaleej Times reported on 21 March that UAE air defences dealt with 3 ballistic missiles and 8 drones coming from Iran.
- Khaleej Times also reported cumulative March attack volumes from Iran and casualty counts inside the UAE.
- The National reported on 31 March that the UAE intercepted eight ballistic missiles, four cruise missiles, and 36 drones launched from Iran.
- The National also reported an apparent Iranian drone strike hit a Kuwaiti oil tanker anchored off a port in Dubai and caused a fire.
- Both pieces anchor the activity inside the March reporting window.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- Attribution/origin requirement is directly supported: missiles/drones were coming from or launched from Iran.
- Repeated March attacks on the UAE are directly supported.

## What is uncertain

- Interceptions dominate the reporting, and interceptions are explicitly excluded by the contract.
- The tanker example is not the cleanest qualifying impact because the damaged object was a Kuwaiti tanker rather than obviously UAE soil infrastructure.
- These sources alone do not establish that the specific qualifying impact example most likely to count is the same event with the strongest attribution chain.

## Why this source may matter

The contract requires more than regional war context; it requires Iranian attribution. These reports are the best available support for the proposition that the March UAE attack stream originated from Iran rather than proxies.

## Possible impact on the question

Raises confidence in Yes only when paired with a separate direct-impact source such as BBC. Standing alone, these reports are insufficient because they can still support a false-positive reading driven by interceptions.

## Reliability notes

Useful, especially because they cite the UAE Defence Ministry, but better as attribution scaffolding than as sole settlement evidence. They are materially relevant to the contract but not fully sufficient by themselves.
