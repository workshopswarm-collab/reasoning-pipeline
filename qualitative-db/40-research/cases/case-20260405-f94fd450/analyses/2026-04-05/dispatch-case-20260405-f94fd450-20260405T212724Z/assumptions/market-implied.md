# Market-implied assumption note

The market price of 0.7795 appears to assume at least one of the following:

1. credible reporting already established a direct Iranian-origin impact on UAE soil during March 2026, and the remaining dispute is procedural rather than substantive;
2. the market is heavily weighting the platform-state cue (proposed Yes / disputed / final review) as evidence that a qualifying event probably occurred;
3. traders are extrapolating from broader Gulf attack reporting, assuming UAE was hit in a way analogous to neighboring states without fully separating interception from impact;
4. traders believe the attribution hurdle is effectively satisfied because the broader regional missile/drone barrage was widely understood as Iranian.

The main nontrivial tradeoff is that these assumptions can make price efficiency look plausible even when the public evidence accessible in this run does not cleanly satisfy all contractual conditions. In other words, a high price may reflect information aggregation, but in this case that aggregation may be overconfident if key public reporting actually hinges on excluded mechanisms such as interception or unresolved attribution/date details.

My working assumption is therefore: the market likely knows something real about a UAE-linked Iranian attack episode, but the publicly legible evidence still leaves enough contract-specific ambiguity that the price should not be treated as a near-settlement fact.
