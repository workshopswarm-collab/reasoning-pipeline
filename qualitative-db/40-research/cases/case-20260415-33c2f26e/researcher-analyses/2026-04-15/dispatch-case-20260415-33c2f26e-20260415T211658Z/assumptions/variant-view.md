# Assumption

Absent strong independent evidence on lineup edge, injury asymmetry, or a settled official fixture/odds surface, a 91.5% regulation-win price for a league soccer match should be discounted modestly because draw/upset risk remains structurally meaningful.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate depends on whether the market's extreme confidence is being earned by robust team-specific evidence or is simply a strong-favorite prior carried too far.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly lower probability than the market.
- A view of rough disagreement rather than full opposition.
- Emphasis on evidence-quality limits as the main reason not to endorse 91.5% outright.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

League soccer matches have three regulation outcomes, so even strong favorites retain nontrivial draw risk unless there is unusually strong team-news or form evidence. In this run, additional public verification surfaces were brittle or nonresponsive, so the evidence justifying an extreme number did not look especially robust.

## What would falsify it

- A reliable official competition fixture/result page plus independent sportsbook or reputable stats sources showing Al Nassr priced near the same extreme level.
- Strong confirmed team-news asymmetry, such as Ettifaq missing multiple key starters while Al Nassr is close to full strength.

## Early warning signs

- Multiple independent pre-match pricing sources clustering near or above 90%.
- Official competition pages and reputable data sites cleanly confirming the exact fixture and context.
- Team news that sharply widens the expected talent gap.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The probability estimate should move up toward the market, and the variant angle would largely collapse into agreement with consensus.
