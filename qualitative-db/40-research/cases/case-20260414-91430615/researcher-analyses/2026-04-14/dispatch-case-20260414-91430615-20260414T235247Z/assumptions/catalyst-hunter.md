# Assumption

The key working assumption is that absent a fresh negative macro or crypto-specific shock, bitcoin is likely to remain above 70000 through the specific April 19 noon ET Binance close because the threshold sits comfortably below the current Binance trading regime rather than at its edge.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate depends less on long-run bitcoin direction than on short-horizon path stability into one exact minute. If the current regime is fragile, a five-day window is enough for a 5%+ drawdown that would break the Yes case.

## What this assumption supports

- A high but not near-certain Yes probability.
- The view that the market is directionally right but somewhat overconfident.
- The weighting of near-term catalysts toward downside shock risk rather than upside breakout need.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Current Binance spot is about 74065, well above the 70000 threshold.
- Recent Binance daily closes have mostly been in the 71k-74k area, suggesting 70k is below the center of the recent range.
- Because the threshold is already exceeded by several thousand dollars, the market does not require a new bullish catalyst; it mainly requires the absence of a sufficiently sharp bearish move before noon ET on April 19.

## What would falsify it

- A rapid bitcoin drawdown below 70000 driven by macro risk-off, crypto-specific leverage unwind, or exchange/operational disruption.
- Evidence that current price support is unusually event-driven and likely to fade before the weekend.
- A structural Binance-specific price dislocation versus broader bitcoin markets near resolution time.

## Early warning signs

- BTC closing back below 72k then 71k on Binance before April 19.
- Sharp rise in intraday realized volatility or liquidation-driven selling.
- Weekend liquidity deterioration with repeated failures to hold the low-70k area.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If the regime proves fragile, the fair probability moves materially down because a narrow noon-specific print becomes much more exposed to transient volatility. In that state, the market's current 90%+ pricing would likely be too high by more than a small margin.
