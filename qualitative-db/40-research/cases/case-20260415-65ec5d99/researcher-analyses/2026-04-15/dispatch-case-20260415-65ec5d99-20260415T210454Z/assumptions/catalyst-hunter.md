# Assumption

The most likely event capable of moving this market materially before kickoff is late Real Madrid lineup or injury news rather than any newly discovered baseline-strength information.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability estimate is already built on a large quality and venue advantage for Real Madrid, so the only realistic near-term repricing path is a negative availability or rotation shock that narrows that edge.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate only modestly above the market rather than dramatically above it.
- A catalyst-focused memo centered on late team news, scheduling, and motivation.
- The view that additional generic stats research is unlikely to move the estimate much.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The matchup itself is already known and scheduled.
- Current standings and recent head-to-head context are already strongly favorable to Real Madrid.
- In soccer win markets, late absences, heavy rotation, or a changed incentive environment are among the few common short-horizon catalysts that can materially reprice a favorite.

## What would falsify it

- Credible evidence of a different dominant catalyst, such as a significant rescheduling issue, disciplinary sanction, weather disruption, or market-rule ambiguity.
- Fresh evidence that Real Madrid's baseline edge is overstated for structural reasons not visible in current standings context.

## Early warning signs

- Reports of key Real Madrid absences or squad rotation.
- Signs the title race is no longer live, reducing motivation.
- Unexpected travel, scheduling congestion, or non-league priority changes.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If another catalyst becomes more important than lineup risk, the probability should be recalibrated around that new mechanism and the memo should shift from baseline-quality framing to the newly dominant catalyst.
