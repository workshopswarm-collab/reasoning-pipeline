# Assumption

The market may be slightly overpricing Real Madrid because elite-team reputation and generic superiority are easier to price than the specific probability of converting superiority into a regulation win rather than a draw.

## Why this assumption matters

A 76.5% Yes price is high for a 1X2-style win-only contract; if draw risk is understated, the true win probability can be modestly below the market even while Real Madrid remains the clearly better side.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly bearish variant view versus the market.
- Keeping the estimate below the market rather than matching the full consensus favorite narrative.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The contract is stricter than a casual “avoid defeat” framing because only a Real Madrid win counts.
- Favorite pricing in public markets can inherit prestige and broad team-quality priors faster than it incorporates match-specific drag from rotation, schedule state, or low-event draw pathways.
- Late-season league matches can carry asymmetric incentives, squad-management effects, and variance around motivation or lineup choices.

## What would falsify it

- Strong market-wide bookmaker consensus near or above the current implied level after accounting for overround.
- Clear pre-match lineup/team-news evidence that Real Madrid is likely to field a near-best XI with no meaningful availability or motivation drag.
- Fresh statistical evidence showing Real Madrid's home win-conversion rate versus lower-table opponents is comfortably above the market's implied level.

## Early warning signs

- Price strength toward the high 70s or low 80s that is confirmed by broad external odds markets.
- Team news showing Real Madrid full strength and fully incentivized.
- Alavés entering with poor availability or visibly degraded defensive profile.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The variant edge largely disappears and the correct conclusion shifts toward rough agreement with the market instead of mild disagreement.
