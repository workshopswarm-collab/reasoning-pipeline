# Assumption

Real Madrid will enter the 2026-04-21 match with a broadly representative first-team strength level rather than suffering a late cluster of absences, heavy rotation, or incentive collapse that materially erodes the usual quality gap versus Alavés.

## Why this assumption matters

The base-rate case is mostly driven by the structural class gap between the clubs and by current-season table/goal metrics. If lineup quality or motivation collapses, that prior weakens sharply.

## What this assumption supports

- A win probability above the raw market midpoint.
- Treating season-long standings and goal difference as decision-relevant.
- A view that Real Madrid should be favored by a large but not absurd margin.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Real Madrid's season page indicates they remain 2nd in La Liga as of 2026-04-15, so league points still matter.
- ESPN team metrics show Real Madrid still producing elite league performance on both attack and defense.
- No authoritative late-breaking evidence was gathered in this run showing a major injury crisis or de-prioritization of the fixture.

## What would falsify it

- Confirmed absences for multiple core attackers/defenders or the starting goalkeeper.
- A scheduling or incentive situation that strongly implies rotation.
- Official team news showing Real Madrid deprioritizing the league match.

## Early warning signs

- Credible pre-match reports of multiple stars unavailable.
- Manager comments emphasizing other competitions over league points.
- Market price falling materially without a clear public explanation.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The proper estimate should move down meaningfully, likely toward a more modest favorite range rather than a strong-favorite range.
