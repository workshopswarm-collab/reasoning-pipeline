# Assumption

The market price assumes Real Madrid will field a reasonably strong, motivated side and that no late injury or rotation shock will materially compress the true win probability before kickoff.

## Why this assumption matters

If this assumption is wrong, a price in the mid-70s can be too confident because soccer favorites are fragile to lineup downgrades, congestion, and motivation asymmetry.

## What this assumption supports

- A view that Real Madrid deserve to be clear favorites.
- A probability estimate remaining near, but slightly below, the market.
- A conclusion that the main risk is overconfidence rather than outright mispricing.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Real Madrid’s league position and goal metrics imply materially superior underlying strength.
- Absent a major lineup or incentive shock, stronger teams usually justify favoritism against lower-table opponents.
- The match is still several days away, leaving some nonzero information risk.

## What would falsify it

- Credible late reporting of multiple key absences.
- Strong evidence Real Madrid will heavily rotate because of higher-priority fixtures.
- A meaningful shift in motivation, such as title race irrelevance or competing schedule pressure.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated lineup speculation about resting top attackers or defenders.
- Injury headlines involving core starters.
- Sharp market drift down in the Real Madrid win price without obvious public news.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move down materially, likely into a more modest favorite range, and the disagreement with the market would become larger and more actionable.
