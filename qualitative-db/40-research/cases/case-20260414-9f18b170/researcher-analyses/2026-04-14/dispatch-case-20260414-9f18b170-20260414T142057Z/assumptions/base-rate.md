# Assumption

If BTC is already trading within roughly 0.5% of a round-number threshold with several trading days remaining, the chance of a one-minute Binance wick touching that threshold is materially higher than the market misses imply unless momentum is clearly rolling over.

## Why this assumption matters

The final estimate depends less on long-run valuation and more on short-horizon touch probability once spot is already near the target. If this heuristic is wrong, the high-probability view is overstated.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate modestly above the already-high market price.
- The conclusion that the event is likely but not certain.
- The claim that structural proximity and realized volatility matter more than narrative headlines here.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTC had already rallied from the low $71k area to the mid-$75k area by Apr 14.
- The remaining gap to $76k was small relative to BTC's recent realized intraday range.
- The contract only requires a one-minute high on Binance, not a durable close above the level.

## What would falsify it

- Evidence that near-threshold weekly touch rates are materially lower than implied by recent realized volatility.
- A clear momentum reversal that quickly pushes BTC several percent below the threshold and keeps it there.
- New contract-interpretation evidence showing the target requires a stricter condition than a simple one-minute high.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated failures near $75.7k-$75.9k with momentum fading.
- Broad crypto risk-off move that sends BTC back toward low-$74k or below.
- Exchange-specific dislocations making Binance weaker than the broader market.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move down toward or below market, because the case for a high probability currently leans on threshold proximity plus remaining time rather than a strong fundamental catalyst.
