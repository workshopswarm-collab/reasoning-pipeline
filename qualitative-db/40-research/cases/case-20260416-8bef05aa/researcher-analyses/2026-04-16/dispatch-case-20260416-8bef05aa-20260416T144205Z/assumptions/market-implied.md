# Assumption

BTC’s current trading regime near 74k is stable enough that a drop below 72k at the specific Apr 21 12:00 ET Binance 1-minute close is less likely than not, but still meaningfully possible.

## Why this assumption matters

The market price only makes sense if traders are mostly pricing persistence of an already-achieved level rather than requiring a new upside move.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability above 50% for Yes.
- A market-respecting estimate near the live Polymarket price rather than a sharply contrarian discount.
- The interpretation that 72k is a defendable but not locked-in line.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTC was already around 73.9k-74.0k on Binance and other major references at analysis time.
- The board shape across nearby strike levels looks internally coherent rather than obviously broken.
- The remaining required condition is only that BTC avoid a modest drawdown into one specific close window.

## What would falsify it

- A clear regime break that pushes BTC decisively below 72k before Apr 21.
- Evidence of a Binance-specific dislocation that makes BTCUSDT materially weaker than broad BTC spot.
- Macro or crypto-specific shock that makes a >2.5%-3% decline into the settlement window more likely than currently assumed.

## Early warning signs

- Repeated rejection below 74k followed by loss of 73k.
- Broader crypto risk-off move or sudden macro tightening headline.
- Rising venue-specific volatility or basis instability on Binance.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The fair probability for Yes should fall quickly toward or below coin-flip, because once BTC is back near or under the threshold the contract becomes much more timing-sensitive.
