# Assumption

Bitcoin does not need a new upside catalyst before Apr 21 for this market to resolve Yes; it mainly needs to avoid a roughly 3% drawdown back below 72,000 at the exact noon-ET observation minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The final probability is materially driven by whether the market should be modeled as requiring fresh upside continuation or merely persistence above an already-cleared threshold.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes probability above the market-implied 70.5% baseline.
- The view that current spot level relative to threshold matters more than finding one discrete bullish event.
- The conclusion that the main catalyst is absence of a bearish repricing event rather than presence of a new bullish trigger.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance context shows BTC has already been trading around 74,000-75,000 on Apr 13-16.
- The contract only asks whether the specific Apr 21 noon ET candle closes above 72,000, not whether BTC makes new highs.
- Recent market commentary indicates BTC is near 75,000 and facing overhead resistance rather than sitting below the target and needing a breakout to cross it.

## What would falsify it

- A material macro, geopolitical, regulatory, or crypto-specific shock that pushes BTC/USDT back below 72,000 before Apr 21 noon ET.
- A sharp Binance-specific dislocation or operational issue that makes the governing venue trade materially weaker than broader BTC benchmarks.

## Early warning signs

- BTC losing the 74,000-75,000 zone and closing successive sessions nearer or below 72,500.
- New risk-off headlines that produce a fast cross-asset selloff.
- Binance-specific outage, liquidity, or pricing anomalies.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market should be viewed much more like a coin flip or below if BTC slips back toward the threshold, because the contract is a single-minute close check rather than a broader averaging window.
