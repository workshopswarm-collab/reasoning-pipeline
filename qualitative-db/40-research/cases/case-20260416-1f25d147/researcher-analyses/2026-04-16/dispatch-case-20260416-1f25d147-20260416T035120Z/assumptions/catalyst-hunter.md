# Assumption

No major negative crypto-wide or Solana-specific catalyst arrives before the April 19 noon ET resolution window that is strong enough to push Binance SOL/USDT below 80 at the relevant one-minute close.

## Why this assumption matters

The current price level is already above the strike, so the thesis depends less on needing a fresh bullish catalyst and more on avoiding a sharp adverse move before settlement.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability rather than a merely modest edge.
- The view that timing risk is mostly about downside shock rather than upside discovery.
- The conclusion that the most relevant catalyst is the absence or presence of a late bearish trigger.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

Recent Binance daily data shows SOL trading mostly in the low-to-mid 80s, which means the strike is already crossed. In that setup, the path to Yes is the default if market conditions remain broadly stable. The market-implied probability near 92% is also consistent with traders viewing no major disruptive catalyst as the base case.

## What would falsify it

- A sharp crypto-wide risk-off move before April 19 noon ET.
- Solana-specific outage, exploit, or major negative headline.
- A rapid weekend deleveraging move that pushes SOL/USDT below 80 on Binance into the settlement minute.

## Early warning signs

- SOL losing the low-80s area before the final day.
- BTC / ETH risk-off acceleration spilling into altcoins.
- Exchange-specific disruptions or unusual spread/market-structure behavior on Binance SOL/USDT.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes probability would fall materially, potentially quickly, because the contract is timestamp-specific and gives no credit for trading above 80 earlier in the weekend.
