# Assumption

Without a verified exact 2026 egg-laying timestamp, the best-supported view is that April 11 is plausible and possibly modal, but not so dominant that adjacent dates should be treated as nearly dead.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is pricing April 11 at an extreme confidence level. If exact lay/incubation timing is not independently pinned down, then uncertainty around normal biological variation and "fully emerged" timing can still leave meaningful probability mass on April 12 or April 13.

## What this assumption supports

- A lower probability than the market-implied 94.45% for April 11.
- A variant view that says the market may be directionally right but overconfident.
- Extra caution about treating broad operator context as precise date evidence.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market contract is exacting about what counts: fully emerged, not pip or partial emergence.
- The available operator-provided context that was recoverable in this run gives only a broad March-April hatching window, not exact lay dates.
- A simple incubation-window check shows that a one- to two-day uncertainty in lay/start timing can shift the most likely hatch date across April 11-13.

## What would falsify it

- A reliable nest log or timestamped operator record showing the first egg date clearly enough that April 11 dominates the biological window.
- Direct livestream evidence of a pip / hatch progression already making April 11 overwhelmingly likely relative to April 12.

## Early warning signs

- Discovery of an exact 2026 egg chronology from the operators.
- Clear, timestamped livestream notes indicating the first egg was laid early enough that April 11 should be heavily favored.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If exact egg chronology becomes available and clearly centers April 11, the fair probability should move upward toward the market. If chronology instead points later, April 11 should be marked down sharply.
