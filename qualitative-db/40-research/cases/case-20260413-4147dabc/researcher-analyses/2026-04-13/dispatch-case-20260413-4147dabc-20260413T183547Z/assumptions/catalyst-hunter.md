# Assumption

The market's extreme April 11 pricing assumes the nest's underlying incubation timeline already puts the first **fully visible emergence** near April 11 ET and that no dual-stream outage distorts the recorded date.

## Why this assumption matters

Without a nest-specific incubation anchor, the market's 94.45% price is doing most of the work. For a narrow date contract, the biological timing assumption and the operational-visibility assumption must both hold; if either slips by even part of a day, April 11 can fail despite a nearby hatch.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate that remains high rather than collapsing toward a flatter distribution across April 11-13.
- A view that the highest-information catalyst is the first visible transition from intact egg to full emergence on stream.
- A judgment that timing risk is narrower than normal but not eliminated.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The market is priced at an extreme level, implying traders likely believe they have nest-specific timing information or recent visual evidence not fully recoverable in the lightweight research path used here.
- Wildlife hatch timing often clusters once a nest is close to pipping, so if market participants are tracking that progress, a concentrated date view can be rational.
- The contract's named streams are active and reachable, reducing but not eliminating operational source risk.

## What would falsify it

- Direct nest evidence showing the incubation/egg-lay timeline points materially later than April 11.
- A verified report or stream observation that only pips/partial emergence were seen on April 11, with full emergence later.
- A simultaneous outage of both streams near the actual hatch time that shifts the recorded date under contract rules.

## Early warning signs

- No visible pipping or late-stage hatch behavior by April 11 ET.
- Social/contextual observers discussing delayed hatch progression.
- Stream instability or downtime around the expected hatch window.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market should be repriced downward for April 11 specifically, with probability redistributed mainly into April 12 or later rather than into the entire tail. The case would then become more about narrow contract timing than about whether a hatch is imminent at all.
