# Assumption

Absent a sharp weekend macro or crypto-specific shock, BTC is likely to retain enough cushion above 70,000 to still close the Binance 12:00 ET 1-minute candle above the threshold on April 19.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC cannot trade down, but that the current several-thousand-dollar buffer is large enough relative to normal near-term noise over the next ~5 days.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability estimate above the market-implied baseline only slightly, not dramatically.
- The view that the main remaining risk is event timing rather than ordinary drift.
- The claim that weekend headlines and macro tape matter more than routine intraday noise.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- BTCUSDT is currently around 74k on Binance, materially above 70k.
- The past 7 daily Binance closes were all above 70k.
- Recent realized range has been wide but still mostly above the strike, suggesting the threshold is not sitting at the immediate edge of price.

## What would falsify it

- A meaningful macro risk-off move or crypto-specific selloff that pushes BTC toward or below 70k before the April 19 noon ET candle.
- Evidence that the current price cushion is being held up by a temporary flow that is likely to reverse before the deadline.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses 72k decisively and stays heavy into the weekend.
- Price starts making lower highs after failing to reclaim mid-74k to 76k.
- Broad cross-asset risk-off headlines hit before Sunday noon ET.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market should be viewed as too complacent on timing risk, and the Yes probability should compress materially toward a coin-flip zone if BTC is trading near the low-70s into the final 24 hours.
