# Assumption

The market is slightly over-converting Inter's large season-long strength edge into a single-match home win probability, underweighting draw risk and short-horizon lineup variance.

## Why this assumption matters

My variant view only exists if a strong team can still be overpriced in one specific match. If that calibration assumption fails, the right conclusion is simply that the market's 81.5% is fair.

## What this assumption supports

- A modestly lower own probability estimate than the market.
- Rough disagreement with market confidence, not disagreement on favorite.
- Emphasis on draw/non-win risk rather than upset-win risk for Cagliari.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Inter are clearly superior on table position and squad quality, but 81.5% is a very high bar for a three-outcome soccer match.
- Official club materials show recent strong form but also recent attention to Lautaro Martinez's condition, implying some residual lineup/fitness uncertainty.
- Single-match Serie A win probabilities can be compressed by rotation, game-state randomness, and draw frequency even when one side is much stronger.

## What would falsify it

- Credible pre-match team news confirming Inter are essentially full strength with no meaningful rotation concerns.
- Independent market/odds consensus moving even higher while lineup information improves rather than deteriorates.
- Evidence that contract resolution is functionally equivalent to a settled official result source with no interpretation ambiguity and that pricing across books strongly supports >80% win odds.

## Early warning signs

- Positive lineup news for Lautaro and other key attackers.
- Indications Cagliari are missing multiple core defensive players.
- Broader bookmaker consensus clustering near or above current market price.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The correct view would shift from "Inter favored but a bit overpriced" toward "Inter favored and fairly priced," likely lifting own probability into the high-70s or low-80s.
