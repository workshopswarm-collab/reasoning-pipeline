# Assumption

Inter will field a reasonably first-choice, motivation-intact side such that their season-long strength edge and home advantage remain mostly applicable on matchday.

## Why this assumption matters

The bullish case for an Inter win depends on transferring broad team-quality evidence into this specific fixture. If Inter heavily rotate, deprioritize the match, or lose key attackers/defenders late, the gap versus Cagliari narrows faster than a season record alone implies.

## What this assumption supports

- A high win probability rather than merely "Inter avoid defeat."
- Confidence that Polymarket's favorite pricing is directionally justified.
- The conclusion that major downside comes more from draw/upset variance than from baseline team equality.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- ESPN lists Inter with a 24-3-5 record and home venue advantage.
- Inter's recent form string is positive relative to Cagliari's.
- External sportsbook pricing also treats Inter as a heavy favorite.

## What would falsify it

- Credible pre-match reporting of heavy squad rotation or multiple absences among Inter's core starters.
- Evidence that Inter are prioritizing another competition or materially discounting this league fixture.
- A materially altered venue/competition context.

## Early warning signs

- Late lineup news omitting multiple top attackers or central defenders.
- Sharp odds drift away from Inter without obvious weather or officiating explanation.
- Manager comments signaling rest priorities.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate should move down, likely first into the low-to-mid 70s and potentially lower if multiple negative signals stack at once, because draw probability rises quickly when a favorite's intensity or lineup quality is compromised.
