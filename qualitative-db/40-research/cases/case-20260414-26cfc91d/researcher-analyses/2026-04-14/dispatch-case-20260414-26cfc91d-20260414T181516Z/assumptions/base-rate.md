# Assumption
The main nontrivial assumption is that broad team-strength priors and high-level current-season indicators are sufficient for this low-difficulty match market, without requiring a deeper injury/lineup model at this stage.

# Why this assumption matters
The final estimate is only slightly below market and is driven mostly by structural favorite-vs-underdog logic plus regulation-only draw risk. If that assumption is wrong, the estimate could move several points.

# What supports the assumption
- Inter's long-run club strength is far above Cagliari's.
- Current-season context still points in the same direction rather than contradicting the prior.
- The case is flagged low difficulty, and the market itself already reflects a large favorite.

# What could break the assumption
- major unexpected Inter absences or rotation
- severe schedule congestion or motivational distortion not captured in the broad season context
- a trustworthy odds screen showing materially lower consensus than the current market

# Net effect on the finding
This assumption makes an 80% estimate reasonable as a base-rate memo, but it also argues against overprecision. The estimate should be interpreted as a disciplined outside-view number, not a lineup-sensitive closing projection.
