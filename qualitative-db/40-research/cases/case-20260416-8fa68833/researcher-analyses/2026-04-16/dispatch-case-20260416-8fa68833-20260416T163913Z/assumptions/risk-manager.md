# Assumption

Barcelona's large season-strength edge will carry through into this specific April 22 home league match without a major lineup, fatigue, or motivation shock.

## Why this assumption matters

Most of the bullish case for a Barcelona win comes from team-quality and season-performance gap rather than a direct pre-match lineup edge already confirmed. If that season-strength edge does not transmit cleanly into this specific fixture, the market price can be too confident.

## What this assumption supports

- A probability estimate close to but slightly below the market.
- The view that Barcelona should still be favored to win in regulation.
- The conclusion that the main disagreement with the market is about confidence, not direction.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Barcelona lead LaLiga by a wide margin and have a materially superior goal difference.
- Celta are solid but not at Barcelona's season level.
- The fixture is listed at Barcelona rather than at Balaídos.

## What would falsify it

- Credible pre-match reporting of major Barcelona absences or heavy rotation.
- A scheduling/congestion shock that meaningfully impairs Barcelona relative to Celta.
- Evidence that Barcelona's home edge has eroded or that Celta match up unusually well against them in current conditions.

## Early warning signs

- Injury or suspension news affecting multiple Barcelona attackers or central defenders.
- Reports framing the match as a look-ahead spot or low-motivation spot.
- Market drift downward in Barcelona's win price without a broad league-wide explanation.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The estimate would move down meaningfully, likely into the upper-60s or low-70s, because the main edge in the case is structural rather than contract-based.
