# Assumption

Barcelona's top-of-table status and home edge are real, but the market may still be slightly too aggressive because Celta are good enough to preserve a meaningful draw path and the contract pays No on draws.

## Why this assumption matters

The difference between "Barcelona are better" and "Barcelona win in regulation often enough to justify 77.5%" is the whole case. If Celta are strong enough to draw at a nontrivial rate, the fair Yes price is lower than a casual favorite framing implies.

## What this assumption supports

- A mildly bearish variant view versus the market.
- An own probability estimate modestly below market rather than in line with it.
- Emphasis on contract interpretation and opponent quality over simple team-brand priors.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Barcelona are first in LaLiga, so favoritism is earned.
- Celta are also in European places per LaLiga standings text, which implies stronger underlying quality than a generic mid-lower-table opponent.
- Celta have mixed recent results, but also credible positive away/contextual results like winning at Valencia and Girona and drawing at Betis.
- The market contract resolves No on draws, so any underweighted draw probability matters directly.

## What would falsify it

- Credible pre-match evidence that Celta are materially weakened by injuries, suspension, or full rotation after Europe.
- Strong market or bookmaker convergence toward a substantially higher Barcelona regulation-win probability supported by lineup news.
- Evidence that Celta's European-place standing is misleading because of a temporary or lucky run not reflected in underlying quality.

## Early warning signs

- Reports of key Celta absences or heavy squad rotation.
- Barcelona lineup news showing full-strength availability after a managed schedule.
- Fresh market repricing toward Barcelona with no offsetting negative team news.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The fair Yes probability moves closer to or above the current market level, and the variant case largely collapses into rough agreement with consensus.
