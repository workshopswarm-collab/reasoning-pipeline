The Polymarket contract resolves Yes only if Barcelona win the match in regular time plus stoppage time; any draw or Celta win resolves No. It also identifies official match statistics recognized by the governing body or event organizers as the primary source of truth.

## Key facts extracted

- If FC Barcelona win, the market resolves Yes.
- Otherwise, the market resolves No.
- Only the outcome within the first 90 minutes of regular play plus stoppage time counts.
- If postponed, the market stays open until the game is completed.

## Why this source may matter

It removes a common ambiguity in soccer markets: extra time and penalties do not matter here, and a draw is a No. That matters because a favorite can be much likelier to avoid defeat than to win in regulation.

## Possible impact on the question

The contract wording mildly suppresses the true Yes probability relative to any sloppy framing like "Barcelona to advance" or "Barcelona not to lose." That makes it important not to over-credit favorite status when the underdog is competent enough to draw.
