# Assumption

Absent a new negative catalyst, BTC/USDT on Binance is more likely than not to remain above 70000 through the single settlement minute at 12:00 ET on 2026-04-14.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis depends less on a bullish catalyst appearing and more on the absence of a sufficiently large bearish catalyst or venue-specific dislocation over the remaining time window.

## What this assumption supports

- A high probability Yes view.
- A conclusion that the most relevant catalyst is not a scheduled bullish event but the risk of an adverse overnight repricing event.
- A view roughly aligned with but slightly below the market's strong Yes pricing.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance BTCUSDT was trading around 72200-72380 during the research window, leaving a cushion of roughly 2200+ points above the threshold.
- Resolution is less than one day away, compressing the time available for a large adverse move.
- The contract keys to a single one-minute close, so only a move below 70000 at that specific minute would defeat Yes.

## What would falsify it

- A rapid BTC selloff of more than roughly 3% before noon ET on 2026-04-14.
- Exchange-specific disruption or anomalous Binance print near the settlement minute.
- New information that materially changes crypto risk sentiment before resolution.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the 72000 area and begins trading persistently near or below 71000.
- Broad crypto or macro risk-off headlines during the overnight or morning U.S. session.
- Signs of Binance-specific operational issues around market data or matching.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The market would become much more path-dependent and the current strong-Yes view would weaken quickly, especially if price enters the 70000-71000 zone close to resolution.
