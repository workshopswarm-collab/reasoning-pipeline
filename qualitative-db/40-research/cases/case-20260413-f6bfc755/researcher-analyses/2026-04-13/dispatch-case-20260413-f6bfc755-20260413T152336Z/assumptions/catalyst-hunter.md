# Assumption

Because the governing Netflix US Top 10 movie chart for 4/6/26 - 4/12/26 was not yet published when checked on 2026-04-13, pre-update inference about Thrash depends on soft signals and the market's aggregation rather than direct authoritative ranking evidence.

## Why this assumption matters

The market is already pricing Thrash near certainty before the source-of-truth chart is posted. Any view taken before publication therefore relies on whether those pre-release signals are trustworthy enough to justify a very high probability.

## What this assumption supports

- A slightly-below-market but still high probability estimate rather than a near-100% estimate.
- A catalyst-centered framing in which the Tuesday Netflix chart update is the dominant repricing event.
- Explicit caution that editorial mentions and market conviction are not the same as direct chart evidence.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- The live Netflix Tudum US movies page still showed 3/30/26 - 4/5/26 at time checked.
- The next week 4/6/26 - 4/12/26 was not yet visible on the governing page.
- The weekly recap article provides only prior-week counts plus light contextual Thrash mention, not direct next-week ranking data.

## What would falsify it

- Netflix posts the 4/6/26 - 4/12/26 chart early and Thrash is directly shown at No. 1 or not No. 1.
- A more authoritative pre-release Netflix data surface emerges with direct US weekly movie views for the unresolved week.

## Early warning signs

- The market remains extreme without any directly observable first-party support.
- Another title shows stronger first-party promotional or chart-adjacent momentum than Thrash before the update.
- Resolution wording ambiguity appears around movie list scope or publication timing.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If direct ranking evidence appears before resolution, this dispatch should move from inference-heavy to settlement-surface-heavy analysis and probability should converge sharply toward the posted chart outcome.
