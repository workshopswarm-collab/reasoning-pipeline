# Assumption

The market's high confidence in `Thrash` is relying on off-platform or anticipatory signals that will remain directionally correct when Netflix publishes the official US chart for the week ending 2026-04-12.

## Why this assumption matters

Without the official update, the difference between a 90%+ probability and a merely high-but-not-extreme probability is mostly about whether pre-update information deserves near-settlement confidence.

## What this assumption supports

- A bullish `Thrash` view before publication.
- Acceptance of a market price near certainty despite the source-of-truth page not yet showing the decisive week.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Polymarket had `Thrash` as the heavy favorite.
- The market appears to treat `Thrash` as the obvious consensus title.
- Netflix updates on a known cadence, so traders may be extrapolating from app rank, recent momentum, or other unofficial tracking.

## What would falsify it

- The official Netflix Tudum US movie chart for the week ending 2026-04-12 posts with a different #1 title.
- Credible pre-update tracking indicates another title led US weekly views.

## Early warning signs

- The source-of-truth page still showing only the prior week late into Monday.
- A rival title such as `Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle` retaining meaningful market share.
- Lack of any direct authoritative mention of `Thrash` on the deciding chart before settlement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The current extreme consensus would be revealed as overconfident, and the correct pre-resolution probability should have been materially lower than the quoted market price.
