Thrash is still more likely than not to finish as the top US Netflix movie for the relevant unpublished 4/6/26-4/12/26 week, but the 0.90 market price appears too confident because the governing Netflix chart had not yet published and the bundle does not independently verify the large market-confidence gap strongly enough.

Market implies 0.90; my post-synthesis range is 0.62-0.80. That leaves Thrash favored, but the edge versus market is bearish rather than bullish and not cleanly actionable at high confidence because the range is partly a compression toward market from insufficient independent verification. The likely mispricing is pre-publication overconfidence: traders may be front-running the next Netflix update more aggressively than the visible evidence supports.

This range is modestly above the provisional swarm center of 0.68 at the midpoint level, but still clearly below market. ## Edge verification status

Independent verification of the bearish edge versus market was low. What was independently checked, via the persona work, was mostly source-of-truth mechanics: the governing Netflix Tudum page, the fact that the decisive week was still unpublished, the prior published week showing Anaconda at #1, and the timing of the next update. Those checks are useful and real, but they do not independently establish that Thrash was unlikely to win; they mostly establish that the market's confidence is not directly publicly confirmed. What remained unverified was the most important point: whether Thrash actually led the unpublished 4/6/26-4/12/26 US week. Because that core point stayed unverified, verification quality for the edge is low.

## Compression toward market

Yes. The swarm contained a very bearish outlier at 0.25 and a median around 0.68, but the synthesis-stage review did not uncover strong independent evidence for a large anti-market edge. The main thing verified was absence of publication, not absence of Thrash momentum. That missing verification led me to compress upward versus the more bearish swarm interpretation while still staying below market.

The dominant catalyst is the expected Netflix US Top 10 update on 2026-04-14. Conversely, any credible evidence of another film leading, or any failure of exact title mapping, would move the view sharply downward.
