# Assumption

The core assumption is that no new downside catalyst between now and April 15 noon ET is strong enough to push Binance BTC/USDT down more than roughly 5-6% into the specific resolution minute.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC cannot fall; it is that the remaining time window is short and the current cushion above 70,000 is large enough that only a meaningful downside shock is likely to flip the outcome.

## What this assumption supports

- A high Yes probability despite not treating the outcome as certain.
- A catalyst-focused view that the key question is not long-run BTC direction but whether a large adverse move arrives before the exact noon ET candle.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Direct Binance checks put BTC around 74.1k on 2026-04-13.
- The recent sampled 1-minute history remained above 70k throughout the returned Binance window.
- The most obvious scheduled macro catalyst already visible from primary calendar work, March CPI released on Apr. 10, had already passed.

## What would falsify it

- A clear risk-off macro shock, major crypto-specific negative headline, or exchange/market-structure disruption that drives BTC quickly toward or through 70k before the target minute.
- Evidence that the remaining catalyst calendar contains a major scheduled release the current view underweighted.

## Early warning signs

- BTC loses the 72k-73k area with momentum and broad market stress.
- A sudden spike in downside volatility or large cross-asset de-risking.
- Binance-specific pricing dislocation or operational issues that make the exchange print less representative or more fragile near settlement.

## What changes if this assumption fails

If a real downside catalyst emerges, the thesis should be repriced quickly because this contract is about one exact minute. A sharp pre-resolution drawdown would matter far more than medium-term bullish narratives.
