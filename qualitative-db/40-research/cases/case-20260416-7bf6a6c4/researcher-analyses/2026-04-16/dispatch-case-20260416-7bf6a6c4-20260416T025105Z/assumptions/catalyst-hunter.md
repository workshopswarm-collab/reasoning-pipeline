# Assumption

BTC being already modestly above 74000 the night before settlement is more likely than not to persist through the specific Binance 12:00 ET 1-minute close on Apr 17.

## Why this assumption matters

The thesis is not that BTC will touch or briefly exceed 74000, but that it will still be above the line at one exact settlement minute. That makes persistence through time, not just threshold proximity, the key forecasting assumption.

## What this assumption supports

- A Yes-leaning probability above 50%.
- A view only modestly above the market rather than dramatically above it.
- The judgment that ordinary drift and intraday volatility are less likely than not to push BTC back below 74000 exactly at the governing minute.

## Evidence or logic behind the assumption

- Binance live spot was already around 74900-74950 during research.
- Recent observed Binance range included prices materially above 74000.
- The threshold is close enough that no extraordinary upside catalyst is needed; simple hold-above behavior is sufficient.
- The market itself priced Yes around 73%, implying broad agreement that current conditions favor a hold-above outcome.

## What would falsify it

- BTC falling back below 74000 and remaining weak into late morning ET on Apr 17.
- A sharp risk-off move, exchange-specific dislocation, or macro headline pushing Binance BTC/USDT below the threshold near settlement.
- Evidence that recent strength was a transient squeeze rather than durable spot support.

## Early warning signs

- Loss of the 74000 area during overnight or European-hours trading.
- Rising realized intraday volatility with repeated failures to reclaim above-threshold levels.
- A widening gap between Binance and other major spot venues suggesting exchange-specific noise.

## What changes if this assumption fails

The Yes edge disappears quickly because this market is resolved by one precise future close. If BTC is trading cleanly below 74000 heading into the settlement window, the probability should move down materially and likely below the market-implied baseline.
