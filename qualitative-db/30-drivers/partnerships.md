# Overview summary

Partnerships captures whether a tie-up, distribution relationship, supplier alignment, or strategic collaboration materially expands reach, legitimacy, or execution capacity.

## Why it matters

It matters because some partnerships are mostly narrative while others genuinely change adoption, economics, or strategic positioning.

## Where it applies

Relevant in AI distribution, payments, crypto integrations, sports media rights, and enterprise software ecosystems.

## Common signals

- access to new users, channels, or infrastructure
- integration depth rather than mere announcement value
- economic incentives that make both sides maintain the deal
- evidence the partnership changes distribution or usage behavior

## Common failure modes

- overpricing press-release partnerships with little operational depth
- assuming brand association alone equals demand creation
- underestimating strong partnerships that quietly unlock distribution
