# Overview summary

Development captures whether an entity is genuinely improving, adapting, or compounding capability over time rather than just experiencing a temporary spike.

## Why it matters

It matters because many forecasting errors come from mistaking a good phase for a durable growth trajectory, or from missing real improvements because priors lag.

## Where it applies

Relevant in sports teams and athletes, AI products, companies, and protocols where iteration and learning materially affect outcomes.

## Common signals

- repeated improvement across time windows
- gains holding across different conditions
- evidence that process changes are driving outcomes
- less dependence on one favorable environment

## Common failure modes

- extrapolating one strong phase too far
- missing nonlinear breakthroughs because priors are stale
- confusing hype around development with actual realized gains

## Interactions

Development often interacts with `performance`, `product-launches`, `reliability`, `ecosystem-growth`, and `media-narratives`.
