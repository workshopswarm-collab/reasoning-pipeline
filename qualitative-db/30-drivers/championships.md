# Overview summary

Championships captures the specific logic of title-winning contexts, where playoff structure, endurance, high-leverage execution, and matchup compression matter more than ordinary regular-season quality.

## Why it matters

It matters because title markets are not just stronger versions of ordinary game markets; the path to a championship usually changes which traits deserve the most weight.

## Where it applies

Most relevant in sports leagues and multi-stage competitions with playoffs, knockouts, or season-long title arcs.

## Common signals

- playoff or knockout translation
- depth and resilience under stress
- high-leverage execution and adjustment capacity
- ability to beat multiple elite opponents in sequence

## Common failure modes

- treating regular-season dominance as full title proof
- underweighting matchup compression and late-stage variance
- overpricing one star while underpricing depth and adaptability
