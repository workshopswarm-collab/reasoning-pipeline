# Overview summary

Liquidity captures how easy it is for capital to enter, exit, fund, or support an asset, market, or institution without destabilizing prices.

## Why it matters

It matters because many sharp moves are really liquidity events rather than pure changes in long-run value.

## Where it applies

Relevant in macro, credit, equities, crypto, stablecoins, exchange infrastructure, and venture/IPO environments.

## Common signals

- spread, depth, and funding conditions
- ability to absorb flows without dislocation
- changes in balance-sheet willingness or market-making capacity
- whether apparent strength survives larger size or stress

## Common failure modes

- assuming quoted prices equal real liquidity
- underestimating reflexive unwind risk
- treating strong flows as pure conviction rather than also liquidity-driven

## Interactions

Liquidity often interacts with `macro`, `capital-markets`, `tokenomics`, and `sentiment`.
