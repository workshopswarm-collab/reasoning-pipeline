# Overview summary

Sanctions captures whether financial, trade, or legal restrictions are materially constraining an actor’s access, behavior, financing, or strategic options.

## Why it matters

It matters because sanctions can impair capacity, reshape alliances, and change market access even when they do not produce immediate political capitulation.

## Where it applies

Relevant in Russia, Iran, commodity flows, shipping, defense-linked trade, and cross-border finance.

## Common signals

- enforcement intensity and coalition cohesion
- restrictions affecting finance, shipping, technology, or exports
- adaptation through intermediaries or rerouting
- whether the target is absorbing, evading, or countering the pressure

## Common failure modes

- assuming announced sanctions equal effective sanctions
- ignoring adaptation and sanctions leakage
- expecting fast policy reversal from tools that often work slowly or partially

## Interactions

Sanctions often interact with `trade`, `diplomacy`, `conflicts`, and `energy`.
