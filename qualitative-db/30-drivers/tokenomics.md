# Overview summary

Tokenomics captures how supply, emissions, unlocks, utility, and value-accrual design shape token behavior and market interpretation.

## Why it matters

It matters because protocol relevance and token value are not the same thing; token structure often explains why markets price an asset differently from the underlying product or ecosystem story.

## Where it applies

Relevant in tokens, protocols, stablecoins, and crypto market-structure analysis.

## Common signals

- supply schedule and unlock pressure
- staking, fee, or burn mechanics
- utility versus governance-only role
- whether value accrual logic is credible or weak

## Common failure modes

- equating protocol traction with token-holder value
- ignoring dilution and float dynamics
- overreading utility claims without real demand

## Interactions

Tokenomics often interacts with `liquidity`, `ecosystem-growth`, `governance`, and `sentiment`.
