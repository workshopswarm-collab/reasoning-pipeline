# Overview summary

Reliability captures whether performance, infrastructure, or execution holds up consistently under normal and stressful conditions.

## Why it matters

It matters because markets often overprice peak capability while underpricing consistency and failure risk.

## Where it applies

Relevant in motorsport, AI products, crypto systems, logistics-heavy companies, and any setting where uptime or repeatability matters.

## Common signals

- low failure rates
- stable delivery across time and conditions
- performance that survives stress, scale, or pressure
- limited dependence on ideal conditions

## Common failure modes

- overvaluing best-case capability
- underestimating fragility hidden by good recent luck
- treating one visible failure as proof of structural unreliability without context

## Interactions

Reliability often interacts with `performance`, `development`, `product-launches`, and `capital-markets`.
