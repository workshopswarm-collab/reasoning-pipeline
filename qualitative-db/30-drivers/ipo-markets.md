# Overview summary

IPO-markets captures the specific conditions that make public listings attractive, delayable, or impossible, even for high-profile companies.

## Why it matters

It matters because IPO timing is often driven by window quality, valuation appetite, and market psychology rather than company readiness alone.

## Where it applies

Relevant in private-company listings, marquee tech IPOs, fintech, consumer brands, and underwriting-signaling questions.

## Common signals

- successful or failed recent listings
- valuation tolerance and aftermarket behavior
- underwriting quality and institutional appetite
- whether issuers are delaying despite strong company narratives

## Common failure modes

- assuming a famous company will list simply because it can
- ignoring aftermarket weakness as a negative signal for the whole window
- overreading rumor as execution probability

## Interactions

IPO-markets often interacts with `capital-markets`, `liquidity`, and `sentiment`.
