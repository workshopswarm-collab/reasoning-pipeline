# Overview summary

Conflicts captures active or latent organized confrontation in which military, political, economic, or proxy tools are being used to impose costs or change outcomes.

## Why it matters

It matters because conflicts alter state behavior, commodity pricing, risk appetite, and institutional decision-making in nonlinear ways.

## Where it applies

Relevant in war, proxy confrontation, regional security, sanctions-heavy environments, and conflict-sensitive commodity markets.

## Common signals

- sustained force posture or operational activity
- credible coercive signaling and response cycles
- aid, sanctions, or alliance support affecting battlefield or bargaining leverage
- widening mismatch between political goals and peaceful off-ramps

## Common failure modes

- overreading rhetoric without underlying capability or logistics
- assuming each conflict follows the same tempo or logic
- missing attritional or slow-burning dynamics because headlines focus on spikes

## Interactions

Conflicts often interacts with `deterrence`, `escalation`, `diplomacy`, and `macro`.
