# Overview summary

Macro captures the broad growth, inflation, rates, labor, and financial-conditions environment that shapes many seemingly separate markets at once.

## Why it matters

It matters because asset prices, policy choices, and political narratives often move within the same macro regime even when their immediate headlines differ.

## Where it applies

Most relevant in economics, policy, crypto, rates-sensitive equities, geopolitically exposed commodities, and election environments where cost-of-living, housing, inflation, and growth are shaping voter behavior.

## Common signals

- inflation and growth regime
- labor-market resilience or weakness
- credit conditions and rates path
- whether policy and market expectations are aligned or diverging

## Common failure modes

- treating macro as background noise when it is actually the main forcing variable
- using a generic macro label without identifying which regime feature is binding
- overfitting one data release without regime context

## Interactions

Macro often interacts with `liquidity`, `capital-markets`, `regulation`, and `sentiment`.
