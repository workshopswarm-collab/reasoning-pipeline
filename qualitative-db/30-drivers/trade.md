# Overview summary

Trade captures how tariffs, market access, supply routes, export controls, and cross-border exchange affect growth, pricing power, and strategic leverage.

## Why it matters

It matters because trade conditions can reshape company earnings, national growth, inflation, and geopolitical bargaining power at the same time.

## Where it applies

Relevant in macro, industrials, semiconductors, geopolitics, shipping, commodities, and country-level forecasting.

## Common signals

- tariffs, export restrictions, or market-access changes
- rerouting of supply chains or shipping flows
- visible trade-balance and demand shifts
- firms or states changing sourcing in response to policy pressure

## Common failure modes

- treating trade as only a macro backdrop when it is the direct mechanism
- overreading announced restrictions without enforcement capacity
- underestimating second-order adaptation and rerouting

## Interactions

Trade often interacts with `macro`, `diplomacy`, `sanctions`, and `energy`.
