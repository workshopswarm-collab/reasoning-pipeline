# Overview summary

Legal captures how lawsuits, court rulings, liability exposure, enforcement posture, and formal legal interpretation shape an entity’s room to operate.

## Why it matters

It matters because legal process can change incentives, timing, access, and reputation even before a final resolution.

## Where it applies

Relevant in crypto, platform companies, antitrust, elections, banking, and high-profile public disputes.

## Common signals

- active litigation or investigations
- rulings that change permissible conduct
- rising liability costs or legal uncertainty
- strategic behavior shaped by expected court outcomes

## Common failure modes

- conflating legal and regulatory risk when the mechanism is different
- overreading headline filings without procedural context
- assuming a legal overhang disappears just because headlines cool off

## Interactions

Legal often interacts with `regulation`, `governance`, `operational-risk`, and `capital-markets`.
