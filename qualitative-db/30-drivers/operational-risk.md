# Overview summary

Operational-risk captures whether execution failures, outages, controls problems, logistical breakdowns, or internal process weaknesses can materially disrupt outcomes.

## Why it matters

It matters because many entities fail not because their core thesis is wrong, but because they cannot execute safely, consistently, or at scale.

## Where it applies

Relevant in exchanges, AI products, banks, logistics-heavy firms, protocols, and any institution exposed to execution or control breakdowns.

## Common signals

- repeated outages, failures, or remediation cycles
- weak controls, compliance gaps, or process drift
- dependence on brittle infrastructure or key people
- scaling stress revealing hidden fragility

## Common failure modes

- assuming a strong brand or product offsets weak operations
- treating one incident as isolated when it reflects a pattern
- overgeneralizing from a single outage without understanding severity

## Interactions

Operational-risk often interacts with `legal`, `regulation`, `reliability`, and `sentiment`.
