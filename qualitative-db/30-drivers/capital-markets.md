# Overview summary

Capital-markets captures whether issuance, financing, underwriting, and risk appetite conditions support transactions, valuations, and access to capital.

## Why it matters

It matters because many company and IPO outcomes depend less on company quality alone than on whether the market window is actually open.

## Where it applies

Relevant in IPOs, M&A, credit, venture exits, financing-heavy sectors, and large corporate events.

## Common signals

- deal flow and pricing quality
- investor appetite for new issuance
- financing costs and underwriting confidence
- whether private-to-public transition conditions are supportive

## Common failure modes

- treating a great company story as enough when the market window is shut
- overreading advisory chatter as execution certainty
- missing that access and cost of capital can be the real bottleneck

## Interactions

Capital-markets often interacts with `macro`, `liquidity`, `ipo-markets`, and `sentiment`.
