# Summary

The EAC is a high-yield institution because election administration, voting-system standards, and public trust in election operations increasingly shape political legitimacy.

## What this entity is

A U.S. agency focused on election-administration support, standards, and related operational guidance.

## Why it matters

The EAC matters because even when attention focuses on campaigns and courts, operational election integrity often depends on less-visible administrative capacity.

## Current state

Research should focus on voting-system standards, election-administration support, trust and misinformation resilience, and whether operational election quality is strengthening or under strain.

## Key strengths

- useful bridge between election legitimacy and administrative execution
- strong relevance to state-capacity and trust in democratic processes
- can shape the quality of election operations below the headline legal layer

## Key weaknesses

- public salience is low relative to importance
- much of the impact is indirect through guidance and standards rather than direct power

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful institution for election-administration and legitimacy analysis

## Open questions

- how much future election trust depends on operational competence versus partisan narrative warfare around legitimacy
