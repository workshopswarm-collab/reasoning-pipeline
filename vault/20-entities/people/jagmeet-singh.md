# Summary

Jagmeet Singh is a high-yield political entity because third-party leverage, coalition bargaining, and leadership visibility make him relevant to Canadian election and parliamentary markets.

## What this entity is

A Canadian political leader whose significance comes from leading a nationally relevant smaller party with outsized coalition impact.

## Why it matters

Singh matters because parliamentary systems can make leader-level influence larger than seat share alone suggests.

## Current state

Research should focus on parliamentary leverage, NDP vote share durability, coalition bargaining position, and whether his personal standing remains strong enough to matter beyond party baseline support.

## Key strengths

- can matter disproportionately in minority-government or coalition-sensitive contexts
- useful bridge between leadership optics and parliamentary arithmetic

## Key weaknesses

- influence depends heavily on broader seat math rather than raw personal popularity
- third-party leaders can lose relevance quickly if the field polarizes

## Important recent changes

- his relevance has been shaped less by prime-minister viability than by leverage over broader Liberal-Conservative dynamics

## Open questions

- whether he remains a durable coalition lever or becomes less central as Canadian competition tightens around the two larger parties
