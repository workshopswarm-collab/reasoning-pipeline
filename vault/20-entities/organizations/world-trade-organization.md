# Summary

The WTO is a high-yield institution because trade disputes, multilateral rule legitimacy, and fragmentation of global commerce repeatedly run through it.

## What this entity is

The core multilateral institution for global trade rules and trade-dispute processes.

## Why it matters

The WTO matters because changes in how states use or bypass multilateral trade rules are central to understanding globalization, fragmentation, and economic coercion.

## Current state

Research should focus on dispute processes, rule legitimacy, great-power compliance, reform debates, and whether trade governance is weakening or adapting.

## Key strengths

- central reference point for multilateral trade legitimacy
- useful bridge between trade law and geopolitical fragmentation
- strong relevance to tariff and dispute narratives

## Key weaknesses

- formal rule systems may be weaker than state power in practice
- institutional importance can appear symbolic until major disputes escalate

## Important recent changes

- remains a useful institution for reading whether global trade governance is eroding structurally

## Open questions

- how much rule-based trade governance can endure amid sustained great-power rivalry
