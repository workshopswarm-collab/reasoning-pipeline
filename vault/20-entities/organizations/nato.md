# Summary

NATO is one of the most important geopolitical organizations in the vault because alliance credibility, European security, deterrence, and burden sharing repeatedly run through it.

## What this entity is

A military alliance centered on collective defense among North American and European member states.

## Why it matters

NATO matters because it shapes security guarantees, deterrence signaling, burden-sharing disputes, and alliance coordination in conflict environments.

## Current state

Research should track member cohesion, defense spending, Ukraine support, enlargement, and whether alliance commitments are becoming more or less credible.

## Key strengths

- strong institutional deterrence framework
- major role in European security coordination

## Key weaknesses

- cohesion depends on member politics and burden-sharing credibility
- rhetoric and implementation can diverge

## Important recent changes

- remains a central institution in Russia/Ukraine and Europe-wide security analysis

## Open questions

- how much alliance cohesion strengthens or erodes under prolonged stress and leadership change
