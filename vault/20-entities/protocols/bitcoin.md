# Summary

Bitcoin is the canonical crypto reserve asset and the strongest expression of the digital-scarcity / digital-gold thesis.

## What this entity is

A decentralized proof-of-work cryptocurrency with fixed maximum supply and a strong monetary narrative.

## Why it matters

It is the anchor asset for institutional crypto adoption, ETF narratives, macro sensitivity, and treasury-allocation debates.

## Current state

Research should treat Bitcoin as driven by macro liquidity, regulatory access, ETF/product structure, treasury adoption, mining economics, and custody infrastructure.

## Key strengths

- strongest brand and institutional recognition in crypto
- simple core narrative relative to most alt assets
- clear supply cap and issuance schedule

## Key weaknesses

- energy / mining criticism
- comparatively limited programmability versus smart-contract chains
- still exposed to regulatory and custody bottlenecks

## Important recent changes

- stronger integration with institutional and treasury narratives
- ongoing policy and product-structure relevance

## Open questions

- how durable institutional demand is across market regimes
- whether BTC remains primarily macro beta or strengthens as standalone reserve asset
