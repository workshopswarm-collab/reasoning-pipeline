# Summary

Aave is one of the most important DeFi lending protocols because onchain borrowing, collateral management, and DeFi credit structure are strongly associated with it.

## What this entity is

A decentralized finance protocol for lending and borrowing cryptocurrencies through liquidity pools.

## Why it matters

Aave matters because it is one of the main credit/borrowing layers in DeFi, making it highly relevant for collateral, rates, liquidity, and protocol-fundamentals research.

## Current state

Research should track deposits, borrowing activity, stablecoin dependence, governance, risk parameters, and whether lending demand is durable or market-cycle dependent.

## Key strengths

- strong position in DeFi lending infrastructure
- broad relevance to onchain credit and collateral systems

## Key weaknesses

- highly exposed to smart-contract and collateral/liquidity risks
- demand can be sensitive to broader market-cycle conditions

## Important recent changes

- remains one of the central DeFi lending protocols by scale and visibility

## Open questions

- how durable protocol economics are under different liquidity and rates environments
