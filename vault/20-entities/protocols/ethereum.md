# Summary

Ethereum is the main programmable crypto settlement layer and the core reference point for smart-contract ecosystem analysis.

## What this entity is

A public blockchain centered on smart contracts, dapps, and digital-asset infrastructure, powered by ETH.

## Why it matters

It anchors DeFi, stablecoins, tokenization, L2 ecosystems, and many of the most important crypto infrastructure debates.

## Current state

Research should focus on fees, usage, L2 expansion, stablecoin dependence, developer gravity, competitive pressure, and regulatory treatment.

## Key strengths

- deep ecosystem composability
- strong developer and infrastructure base
- central role in stablecoins and DeFi

## Key weaknesses

- complexity relative to Bitcoin
- scaling and user-experience pressures
- competition from alternative smart-contract chains

## Important recent changes

- growing centrality of Layer 2 scaling
- continued positioning as digital public infrastructure

## Open questions

- whether value capture remains concentrated in ETH as activity moves to L2s
- how durable Ethereum's developer moat is under increasing competition
