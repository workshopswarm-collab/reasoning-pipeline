# Overview summary

Team-dynamics captures how internal relationships, hierarchy, lineup fit, and role clarity affect collective results beyond individual talent alone.

## Why it matters

It matters because many markets misprice teams by summing individual talent while underweighting coordination, role fit, and internal tension.

## Where it applies

Most relevant in team sports, but also useful in politics, companies, and other settings where internal cohesion changes output quality.

## Common signals

- stable role allocation
- clean hierarchy and decision-making
- visible synergy or friction among core members
- whether lineup or roster changes improve or weaken coherence

## Common failure modes

- overvaluing star power without role fit
- underestimating internal conflict or poor hierarchy
- assuming talent automatically compounds in a group setting

## Interactions

Team-dynamics often interacts with `performance`, `development`, `injuries-health`, and `championships`.
