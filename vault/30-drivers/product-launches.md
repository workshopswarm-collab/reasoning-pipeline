# Overview summary

Product-launches captures how new releases, versions, features, or packaging changes alter perception, adoption, competition, and pricing.

## Why it matters

It matters because launches often create short-run narrative spikes, but only some launches create durable product or market change.

## Where it applies

Most relevant in AI, consumer tech, media products, hardware, and other release-driven markets.

## Common signals

- launch-driven shifts in adoption or engagement
- feature changes that materially alter workflow fit
- pricing or packaging changes that affect monetization
- whether launch excitement persists after the novelty window

## Common failure modes

- overreading launch hype as durable adoption
- dismissing launches that actually change category expectations
- confusing benchmark attention with user-value creation

## Interactions

Product-launches often interacts with `development`, `sentiment`, `media-narratives`, and `reliability`.
