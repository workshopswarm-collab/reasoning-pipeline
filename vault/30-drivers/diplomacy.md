# Overview summary

Diplomacy captures whether negotiation, signaling, coalition management, and state-to-state communication are opening or closing pathways to agreement, restraint, or coordinated action.

## Why it matters

It matters because diplomacy often determines whether tensions stay manageable, whether sanctions coalitions hold, and whether military or economic escalation is avoided.

## Where it applies

Relevant in war-risk, sanctions, alliance politics, trade disputes, and international institution questions.

## Common signals

- sustained talks or breakdowns in talks
- coalition cohesion and partner signaling
- third-party mediation or shuttle diplomacy
- visible concessions, off-ramps, or red-line clarification

## Common failure modes

- treating diplomatic contact as proof of progress
- assuming public rhetoric reflects private bargaining reality
- ignoring coalition-management constraints on nominal dealmaking

## Interactions

Diplomacy often interacts with `conflicts`, `deterrence`, `escalation`, and `sanctions`.
