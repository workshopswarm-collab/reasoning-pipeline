# Overview summary

Governance captures how decision rights, institutional structure, accountability, and control shape what an entity can actually do over time.

## Why it matters

It matters because many failures or advantages come not from strategy alone but from who has authority, how decisions are made, and whether the structure is credible under stress.

## Where it applies

Relevant in politics, central banks, AI companies, protocols, DAOs, and public companies.

## Common signals

- clarity or instability of decision-making
- accountability under conflict or pressure
- incentives between leadership and stakeholders
- whether formal structure matches practical control

## Common failure modes

- ignoring governance until crisis reveals it mattered all along
- assuming stated structure equals real control
- over-personalizing outcomes when governance structure is doing the real work

## Interactions

Governance often interacts with `regulation`, `elections`, `capital-markets`, and `tokenomics`.
