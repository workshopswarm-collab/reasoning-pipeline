# Overview summary

Elections captures the recurring mechanics by which candidate quality, turnout, coalition shape, and institutional rules convert political energy into actual power.

## Why it matters

It matters because many political markets are framed around personalities, but election outcomes usually depend on structure, turnout, coalition durability, and timing.

## Where it applies

Most relevant in politics, electoral forecasting, and party competition.

## Common signals

- candidate and party coalition breadth
- turnout intensity and composition
- institutional rules and electoral map
- whether anti-incumbent or continuity energy is broad or narrow

## Common failure modes

- overreading polls without turnout context
- confusing media attention for vote conversion
- underweighting electoral-system mechanics and coalition elasticity

## Interactions

Elections often interacts with `polling`, `sentiment`, `media-narratives`, `leadership-changes`, and `governance`.
