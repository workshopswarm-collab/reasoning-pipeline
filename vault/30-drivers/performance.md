# Overview summary

Performance captures the repeatable on-field or on-court quality that actually drives results beyond raw narrative, reputation, or one-off variance.

## Why it matters

It matters because many sports markets are really questions about whether current output reflects durable underlying quality or temporary form, luck, or weak opposition.

## Where it applies

Most relevant in sports, but the general logic also helps in any system where repeated observable execution matters more than narrative framing.

## Common signals

- underlying efficiency and consistency
- quality versus level of opposition
- repeatability across contexts and time windows
- whether results are supported by process rather than only outcomes

## Common failure modes

- overreading short winning or losing streaks
- confusing reputation with current level
- treating box-score or result-line output as full proof of true quality

## Interactions

Performance often interacts with `development`, `team-dynamics`, `injuries-health`, `seasonality`, and `championships`.
