# Overview summary

Ecosystem-growth captures whether a platform or protocol is attracting enough users, builders, applications, and economic activity to become structurally more important over time.

## Why it matters

It matters because many networks and platforms are valued on the expectation of future ecosystem scale rather than current narrow output alone.

## Where it applies

Most relevant in crypto protocols, AI platforms, developer ecosystems, and app layers.

## Common signals

- user and developer growth
- application diversity and depth
- economic activity that is not purely incentive-driven
- stickiness of participation across cycles

## Common failure modes

- confusing incentive spikes with durable growth
- overvaluing raw activity counts without quality
- missing ecosystem lock-in because current revenue still looks small

## Interactions

Ecosystem-growth often interacts with `development`, `tokenomics`, `liquidity`, and `product-launches`.
