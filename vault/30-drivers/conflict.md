# Overview summary

Conflict captures active or latent organized confrontation in which military, political, economic, or proxy tools are being used to change behavior or control outcomes.

## Why it matters

It matters because conflict changes the decision environment for states, markets, commodities, and institutions in ways that are rarely linear.

## Where it applies

Relevant in war, proxy conflict, sanctions, regional security, and conflict-adjacent commodity markets.

## Common signals

- operational activity and force posture
- sustained coercive signaling
- shifts in aid, sanctions, or alliance response
- whether political aims and military means are aligning or diverging

## Common failure modes

- overreading rhetoric without force or logistics
- assuming every conflict follows the same timeline
- missing attritional dynamics because headlines favor dramatic events

## Interactions

Conflict often interacts with `deterrence`, `escalation`, `macro`, and `media-narratives`.
