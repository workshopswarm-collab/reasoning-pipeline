# Overview summary

Seasonality captures predictable calendar-based timing effects that change demand, form, incentives, attention, or pricing during recurring parts of a cycle.

## Why it matters

It matters because many markets look event-driven when a large part of the movement is actually following a repeatable calendar pattern.

## Where it applies

Relevant in sports seasons, retail and travel demand, elections, commodities, and content or entertainment cycles.

## Common signals

- recurring calendar windows with repeatable effects
- shifts in intensity, volume, or attention tied to the same time each year
- predictable schedule congestion or relief
- repeated mismatch between off-season and in-season behavior

## Common failure modes

- treating every recurring pattern as a law rather than a tendency
- ignoring that seasonality can be overwhelmed by stronger shocks
- overfitting one calendar cycle without comparing multiple years

## Interactions

Seasonality often interacts with `performance`, `championships`, `media-narratives`, and `consumer-demand`.
