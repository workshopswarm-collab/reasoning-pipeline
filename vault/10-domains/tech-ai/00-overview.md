# Synthesis summary

Tech & AI research should separate capability progress from adoption, infrastructure, governance, and commercialization.

## Core conclusions

- Frontier capability and enterprise deployment are related but not identical.
- AI adoption depends on data infrastructure, deployment efficiency, governance, cost, and model choice.
- Open-source vs proprietary model ecosystems are strategic variables.
- Regulated industries can be early adopters when the value proposition is strong and governance exists.

## Main evidence clusters

- model deployment and production use
- open-source vs proprietary adoption
- compute / GPU / vector-database infrastructure
- regulated-industry use cases
- governance and operationalization

## Conflicts between inputs

- capability hype can outpace practical deployment readiness.

## Missing information

- lab and model dossiers
- chip and cloud supply-chain notes
- benchmark tracking

- consumer product adoption notes

## Most fragile assumptions

- that model capability alone drives market outcomes
- that enterprise experimentation is equivalent to durable rollout
