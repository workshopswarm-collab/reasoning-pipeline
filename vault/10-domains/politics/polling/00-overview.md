# Synthesis summary

Polling research should focus on sample quality, bias, turnout assumptions, trend direction, and how polling interacts with narrative and institutional context.

## Core conclusions

- Polls are noisy estimates, not direct forecasts.
- Trend and quality often matter more than any one poll.
- Polling should be combined with structural and institutional information.

## Main evidence clusters

- sample design
- confidence and error
- trend shifts
- turnout assumptions
- poll quality and house effects
