# Overview summary

## Why this subdomain matters

Basketball research should organize around league context, team quality, star-player impact, health/availability, and schedule/playoff positioning.

## Core conclusions

- Basketball is star-driven: elite player availability and form can swing outlook disproportionately.
- Team strength needs to be evaluated through both roster construction and current health/continuity.
- Statistical baselines are strong in basketball, but current-state interpretation still depends heavily on injuries, lineup fit, coaching, and late-game creation.
- For current NBA-style research, teams and star players should be treated as first-class entities.

## Main evidence clusters

- standings and playoff position
- player and team advanced stats
- injury / availability news
- role-player depth and roster fit
- recent form vs long-run baseline

## Conflicts between inputs

- long-run metrics can conflict with short-run health and chemistry.
- team depth can matter less than superstar shot creation in high-leverage moments.

## Missing information

- injury source hierarchy
- lineup and on/off data workflows
- trade / rotation notes
- coaching-style and pace notes

## Most fragile assumptions

- that raw team record fully captures title strength
- that star power alone is enough without context on health and fit

## Recommended next research steps

- maintain canonical dossiers for major contenders and star players
- add injury, rotation, and transaction source notes
