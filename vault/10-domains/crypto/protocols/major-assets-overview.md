# Synthesis summary

The major-asset crypto landscape can be read through four archetypes: bitcoin as monetary reserve asset, ethereum as programmable settlement layer, solana as high-throughput application chain, and dogecoin as sentiment-heavy retail/payment meme asset.

## Core conclusions

- BTC is the clearest macro and institutional anchor asset.
- ETH is the strongest smart-contract and ecosystem-composability anchor.
- SOL is closely tied to performance, app traction, and fast-growth network narratives.
- DOGE is unusually exposed to culture, community, and reflexive attention dynamics.

## Main evidence clusters

- monetary narrative and ETF/treasury adoption
- smart-contract usage and L2 scaling
- performance and app ecosystem traction
- meme and community-driven attention cycles

## Conflicts between inputs

- assets can rally on very different mechanisms even within the same macro environment.
- stronger fundamentals do not always outperform stronger narrative reflexivity.

## Missing information

- fee/revenue and usage metrics
- validator / decentralization comparisons
- wallet and exchange distribution
- regulatory status by jurisdiction

## Most fragile assumptions

- that all majors should be analyzed under the same framework
- that high activity implies durable value capture

## Recommended next research steps

- add independent metrics notes for each asset
- expand to stablecoins and additional majors as needed
