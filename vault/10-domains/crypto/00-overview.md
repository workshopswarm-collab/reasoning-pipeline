# Overview summary

## Why this subdomain matters

Crypto research should be organized around a few recurring pillars: protocols and tokens, market structure, regulation, institutional adoption, and security risk.

## Core conclusions

- Regulation is not peripheral; it is one of the main market-structuring forces.
- Institutional adoption increasingly depends on legal clarity, custody rules, and product availability.
- Stablecoins, ETFs, exchange access, and treasury adoption are major bridge points between crypto-native activity and mainstream finance.
- Security remains a standing drag on the sector, especially in DeFi and infrastructure-heavy segments.

## Main evidence clusters

- U.S. legal/regulatory posture
- EU and non-U.S. jurisdictional divergence
- institutional product growth
- tokenization and DeFi growth
- security and operational vulnerabilities

## Conflicts between inputs

- Clearer regulation can support adoption while simultaneously raising compliance costs and narrowing market structure.

## Missing information

- protocol-specific fundamentals
- onchain activity baselines
- exchange concentration and liquidity maps
- source-quality hierarchy for crypto-native vs institutional reporting

## Most fragile assumptions

- that legal clarity always translates into durable adoption
- that institutional growth implies broad fundamental strength across the sector
