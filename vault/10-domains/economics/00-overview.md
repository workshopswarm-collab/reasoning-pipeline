# Synthesis summary

Economics research should center on macro systems, policy transmission, and measurable indicators that move expectations about growth, inflation, labor, rates, and credit.

## Core conclusions

- Macro variables are interconnected rather than isolated.
- Policy institutions matter because they shape incentives, liquidity, pricing, and expectations.
- Useful economics memory should separate conceptual background from indicator tracking.
- For prediction-market work, the recurring core is inflation, labor, growth, rates, credit, and fiscal/monetary policy.

## Main evidence clusters

- macroeconomic indicators
- central-bank policy
- fiscal policy
- labor markets
- inflation and growth interactions
