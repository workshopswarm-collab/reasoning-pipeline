# Synthesis summary

Inflation research should center on price indices, policy transmission, supply/demand shocks, and expectation management.

## Core conclusions

- Inflation is one of the main macro variables because it drives central-bank reaction functions.
- Expectations matter as much as realized prints in many market contexts.
- Inflation should be studied alongside labor, growth, rates, and energy/supply conditions.

## Main evidence clusters

- CPI/PCE-like measures
- policy response
- supply shocks and energy
- demand conditions
- expectations
