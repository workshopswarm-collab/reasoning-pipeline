# Synthesis summary

Geopolitics research should be built around states, alliances, conflicts, sanctions, diplomacy, and strategic resource systems, especially energy and logistics.

## Core conclusions

- Geopolitical tension often expresses itself through supply fragility, sanctions, alliances, and strategic bottlenecks rather than only direct conflict.
- Energy systems and electrification are now core strategic variables, not side topics.
- State capacity, industrial resilience, and chokepoints matter alongside military and diplomatic signaling.
- China-centered industrial and energy dynamics are likely to recur across many geopolitical topics.

## Main evidence clusters

- conflicts and regional instability
- energy security
- sanctions and fragmentation
- supply resilience
- strategic industrial capacity

## Conflicts between inputs

- abundance of supply does not remove geopolitical risk; it can coexist with it.

## Missing information

## Most fragile assumptions

- that direct conflict is the only high-signal mechanism
- that energy transition reduces geopolitical risk automatically
