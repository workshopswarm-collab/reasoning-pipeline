# Summary

This report frames AI as shifting from experimentation into production deployment, with enterprise adoption driven by model operations, infrastructure, open-source/proprietary model choice, and regulated-industry use cases.

## Key facts extracted

- Enterprise AI moved rapidly from pilots to production deployment.
- Open-source LLMs are widely used alongside proprietary models.
- Highly regulated sectors are meaningful adopters rather than laggards.
- RAG, vector databases, GPU usage, and deployment efficiency are central operational variables.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The report cites sharp growth in production models and vector-database usage.
- It notes strong adoption in finance, healthcare, and manufacturing.
- It emphasizes operational efficiency improvements in deployment pipelines.

## What is uncertain

- The report is vendor-adjacent and enterprise-focused.
- It is less useful for open research or frontier-model geopolitics by itself.

## Why this source may matter

It highlights the operational and adoption variables that often drive tech-and-AI markets more than raw model capability alone.

## Reliability notes

Useful for enterprise-adoption framing; should later be paired with compute, benchmark, model-lab, and regulatory sources.
