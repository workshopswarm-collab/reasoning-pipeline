# Summary

Soccer is a low-scoring, globally dominant sport where match outcomes are sensitive to finishing variance, squad quality, and tournament or league structure.

## Key facts extracted

- Global scale and competition structure matter heavily.
- Match outcomes can hinge on small margins.
- League vs knockout dynamics are important.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a market depends on understanding whether a soccer outcome is league-driven, knockout-driven, or star-driven.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains the basic structure of soccer competition, including leagues, cups, and international tournaments.
- It supports baseline claims about how results and standings contexts are organized.

## What is uncertain

- It does not by itself identify current elite teams, injury context, or tournament-specific edges.
- Soccer valuation still depends heavily on form, depth, and schedule context.

## Why this source may matter

Useful for orienting cross-league and tournament research where competition formats and incentive structures matter.
