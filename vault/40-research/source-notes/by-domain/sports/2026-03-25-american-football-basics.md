# Summary

American football is a high-leverage, possession- and role-structured sport where quarterback play, coaching, injuries, and game-state efficiency usually dominate evaluation.

## Key facts extracted

- The sport is highly structured around downs, field position, and scoring efficiency.
- Roles are specialized and substitutions are extensive.
- Team quality is shaped by scheme, line play, and position-level leverage.

## Why this source may matter

Football markets are often driven by a few high-leverage variables rather than broad roster averages.
