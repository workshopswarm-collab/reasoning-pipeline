# Summary

Streaming television is now a dominant TV-distribution system, making subscriber dynamics, release cadence, licensing, and platform competition core culture-market variables.

## Key facts extracted

- Streaming surpassed traditional television viewing in major markets.
- Subscription and ad-supported models both matter.
- Platform competition shapes content economics and audience behavior.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a culture or media market depends on whether streaming dynamics are likely to matter more than legacy TV or theatrical distribution.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains streaming television as a distribution and consumption model rather than a single platform.
- It supports baseline claims about why streaming has changed release behavior and audience economics.

## What is uncertain

- It does not reveal which services, titles, or monetization models are currently strongest.
- Attention, subscriber growth, and profitability can point in different directions.

## Why this source may matter

Useful as orientation because streaming-related questions often mix platform competition, title performance, and changing release norms.
