# Summary

Streaming media is now a dominant distribution layer for music, video, and live content, making release cadence, platform distribution, and attention capture central to cultural outcomes.

## Key facts extracted

- Streaming is the default delivery mode for many forms of modern media consumption.
- Subscription and platform competition shape distribution and audience reach.
- Live and on-demand formats both matter.
- Streaming turns timing, platform presence, and social amplification into major research variables.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source describes streaming as real-time or near-real-time packetized media delivery.
- It notes widespread adoption since the 2010s for video, music, and live delivery.

## What is uncertain

- This is technical and historical framing, not market-specific audience analytics.

## Why this source may matter

For culture markets, outcomes often hinge on release timing, platform concentration, and narrative spread through streaming ecosystems.
