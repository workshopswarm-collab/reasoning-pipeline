# Summary

Bitcoin is the original decentralized cryptocurrency, organized around proof-of-work security, a 21 million supply cap, and a strong monetary/store-of-value narrative.

## Key facts extracted

- Bitcoin launched in 2009 from Satoshi Nakamoto's design.
- The network uses proof of work and a decentralized blockchain maintained by nodes and miners.
- Supply is capped, with issuance reduced via halvings.
- Research around Bitcoin often turns on monetary narrative, institutional adoption, mining economics, and regulatory treatment.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source describes Bitcoin as the first decentralized cryptocurrency.
- It highlights proof of work, 10-minute blocks, and the supply cap.
