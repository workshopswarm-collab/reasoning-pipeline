# Summary

Stablecoins are core crypto infrastructure rather than a side category: they anchor trading, cross-border flows, and increasingly important regulatory and reserve-risk questions.

## Key facts extracted

- Stablecoins aim to maintain value relative to a reference asset.
- Fiat-backed stablecoins dominate the category.
- Market concentration is high, especially in USDT and USDC.
- Stablecoins create direct links between crypto markets and traditional reserve/risk structures.

## Why this source may matter

Stablecoins are one of the main bridges between crypto market structure, payments, regulation, and systemic-risk questions.
