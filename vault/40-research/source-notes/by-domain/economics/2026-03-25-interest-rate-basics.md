# Summary

Interest rates are a central macro price because they shape borrowing costs, asset valuation, and the policy path.

## Key facts extracted

- Rates affect financing, discounting, and investment decisions.
- Real vs nominal rates matter.
- Central-bank policy rates have broad macro and financial significance.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a market depends on whether a rate narrative is economically coherent or just headline-driven.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains what interest rates are, why central banks move them, and how rate levels transmit through the economy.
- It supports baseline claims about rates as a macro policy tool.

## What is uncertain

- It does not by itself tell you how markets will price the next move or how strongly rates are binding in the current cycle.
- Policy stance depends on inflation, growth, labor, and financial conditions together.

## Why this source may matter

Useful for grounding macro and Fed/central-bank questions before reading live policy signals or market-implied paths.
