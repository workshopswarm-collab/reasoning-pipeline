# Summary

Inflation is one of the core macro variables because it changes purchasing power, policy response, and expectations across the whole economy.

## Key facts extracted

- Inflation is measured via price indices such as CPI.
- It is shaped by demand shocks, supply shocks, policy, and expectations.
- Central banks target low, stable inflation because it interacts directly with growth, labor, and rates.

## Why this source may matter

Inflation is central to rates, recession, consumption, and policy-market research.
