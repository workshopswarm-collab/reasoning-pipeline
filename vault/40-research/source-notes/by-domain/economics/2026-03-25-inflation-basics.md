# Summary

Inflation is one of the core macro variables because it changes purchasing power, policy response, and expectations across the whole economy.

## Key facts extracted

- Inflation is measured via price indices such as CPI.
- It is shaped by demand shocks, supply shocks, policy, and expectations.
- Central banks target low, stable inflation because it interacts directly with growth, labor, and rates.

## Why this source may matter

Useful because inflation is central to macro policy and market interpretation but is often discussed with too little causal clarity.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a market depends on whether inflation pressure is likely to strengthen or weaken rate, growth, or asset-pricing narratives.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains inflation as sustained price-level increase and outlines common causes such as demand, supply, and expectations.
- It supports baseline claims about inflation mechanics.

## What is uncertain

- It does not tell you which inflation driver dominates right now or how persistent the current regime will be.
- Headline inflation and policy-relevant inflation can diverge.
