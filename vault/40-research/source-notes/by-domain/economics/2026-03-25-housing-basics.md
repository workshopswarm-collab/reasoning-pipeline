# Summary

Housing is a major macro system because affordability, rates, supply, household formation, and construction all connect it to inflation, growth, and politics.

## Key facts extracted

- Housing spans shelter, asset value, credit, and policy.
- Supply constraints and financing conditions both matter.
- Housing stress often has broad downstream economic effects.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a macro or policy market depends on whether housing weakness or resilience is likely to matter materially.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains housing as a sector shaped by rates, affordability, supply, and household balance-sheet conditions.
- It supports baseline claims about why housing matters to the broader economy.

## What is uncertain

- It does not show which local or national housing pressures are currently most binding.
- Housing signals can lag rate changes and vary sharply by region.

## Why this source may matter

Useful because housing often sits at the intersection of macro, consumer confidence, and political pressure.
