# Summary

International trade is a core macro system linking growth, industrial policy, geopolitics, and supply-chain dependence.

## Key facts extracted

- Trade enables specialization and cross-border exchange.
- It is shaped by tariffs, comparative advantage, logistics, and policy.
- Trade dynamics often interact directly with geopolitical rivalry.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a market depends on whether a trade move is mostly symbolic, inflationary, growth-negative, or strategically meaningful.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains trade as exchange across borders shaped by tariffs, comparative advantage, and policy rules.
- It supports baseline claims about why trade policy matters economically and politically.

## What is uncertain

- It does not resolve current tariff incidence, supply-chain adaptation, or country-specific vulnerability.
- Headline trade actions often differ from realized economic impact.

## Why this source may matter

Useful because trade questions often mix political signaling with actual economic transmission.
