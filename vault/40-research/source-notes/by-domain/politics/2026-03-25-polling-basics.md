# Summary

Polling is a sampling-based method for estimating public opinion and should be treated as a probabilistic signal, not a direct outcome proxy.

## Key facts extracted

- Polls infer population views from a sample.
- Confidence intervals and sampling design matter.
- Polling failures often come from bias, turnout assumptions, or unrepresentative response.

## Why this source may matter

Polling is central to elections and public-opinion markets, but it has to be handled as measurement rather than truth.
