# Summary

Politics is fundamentally about power, decision-making, institutions, and conflict/cooperation in groups. For research purposes, the highest-signal recurring objects are elections, parties, laws, institutions, leaders, and public opinion.

## Key facts extracted

- Politics spans decisions, power allocation, negotiation, lawmaking, and force.
- It operates from local institutions to sovereign states and international systems.
- Political parties, elections, and political systems structure competition.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source defines politics as activities associated with decision-making in groups and power relations.
- It describes politics as operating across multiple scales and emphasizes parties and elections in modern states.

## What is uncertain

- This is definitional and broad rather than a specialized elections or polling guide.

## Why this source may matter

It establishes the broad ontology for politics markets before drilling into polling, institutions, legislation, or court-driven outcomes.

## Reliability notes

Good baseline framing; later research should rely on electoral data, institutional documents, and polling method sources.
