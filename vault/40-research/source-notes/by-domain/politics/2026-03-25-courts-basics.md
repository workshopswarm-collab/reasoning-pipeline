# Summary

Courts are a core political and market institution because legal interpretation can change institutional power, policy durability, and regulatory boundaries.

## Key facts extracted

- The Supreme Court has final appellate power over major constitutional questions in the U.S.
- Court composition and jurisdictional structure matter for long-run policy interpretation.
- Legal outcomes can matter as much as electoral outcomes in some domains.
