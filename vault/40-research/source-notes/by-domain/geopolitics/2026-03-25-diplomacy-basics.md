# Summary

Diplomacy is the main non-military instrument of foreign policy and should be treated as a core mechanism in geopolitical outcome formation.

## Key facts extracted

- Diplomacy shapes treaties, alliances, negotiations, and conflict-management.
- It is the operational channel of foreign-policy strategy.
- Diplomatic signaling can alter events even without force.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a market depends on whether diplomatic movement is a real de-escalation signal or just tactical theater.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains diplomacy as a mechanism for signaling, bargaining, and conflict management between states.
- It supports baseline claims about how negotiation channels and formal contacts function.

## What is uncertain

- It does not tell you whether current diplomacy is sincere, performative, or likely to produce concessions.
- Talks and statements can mask very different strategic intentions.

## Why this source may matter

Useful for keeping negotiation questions distinct from military action questions and for interpreting ceasefire or summit chatter more carefully.
