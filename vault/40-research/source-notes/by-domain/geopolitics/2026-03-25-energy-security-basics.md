# Summary

Energy security is a core geopolitical issue because energy systems link state resilience, industrial activity, military strategy, and economic stability.

## Key facts extracted

- Energy security involves availability, reliability, and affordability.
- Supply disruptions and chokepoints are strategically important.
- Energy transitions do not eliminate geopolitical risk; they shift its form.

## Possible impact on the question

Helpful when a market depends on whether an energy shock is likely to stay local, become macro-relevant, or feed broader diplomatic pressure.

## Evidence directly stated by source

- The source directly explains the connection between energy supply, transport, vulnerability, and state security concerns.
- It supports baseline claims about why energy disruptions can have geopolitical effects.

## What is uncertain

- It does not by itself show which current actors are most exposed or how fast markets will react in a specific crisis.
- Energy-security logic can vary by region and commodity.

## Why this source may matter

Useful as orientation for linking commodity questions, infrastructure risk, and geopolitical pressure.
